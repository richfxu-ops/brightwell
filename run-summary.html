What got built

The world-architect skill developed a distinctive fictional world from a locked premise (roguelike economy deckbuilder with escalating contracts + a depletable Standing fail-resource), gating every layer on an anti-generic guard and a load-bearing-for-mechanics rule.

In Roundelay, things made while truly attended "wake" and outlast the Paling (the slow forgetting-tide). A maker's gleam — visible trust worn as light — is Standing. Askings (needs towns craft into little objects and hang on lantern-gates) are the escalating contracts. The six Walking Ways are the player archetypes, and a run is one wander-year across a living map, ending in the gentle Quiet Walk.

How it was judged — not vibes

Blind auditsEvery gated metric (trope density, ludic coverage, legibility, depth) was set by a fresh auditor reading the file, never self-scored. L4–L7 used 3-auditor majority panels so borderline elements can't flip on one reader's taste.

Adversarial loopEach layer ran diverge → red-team critics → synthesize → blind audit via a journaled Workflow engine. Issues persist by id until a verifier confirms the fix against the artifact — silence never closes one.

Two human gatesYou approved the L1–3 identity batch, and you ratified the L6 renames that edited locked canon (the Paling, the guild-word retirements, turn→verse).

Canon is checked, not trustedRenames route through a renamed_from registry and a case-sensitive scan; generated views are hash-pinned. Final scan: 0 retired aliases, 0 stale views.
