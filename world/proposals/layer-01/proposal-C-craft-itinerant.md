# Proposal C — craft-itinerant lens

**Layer 1: thematic spine · Proposer C-craft-itinerant · 2026-07-09**

---

## 1. Working name

# ROUNDELAY

A roundelay is a round-song: a melody passed voice to voice around a circle, each singer
carrying it a few bars before handing it on. That is the whole world in one word. Roundelay
is a made world — not made once, long ago, but made *continuously*, by hands, and it only
stays wonderful as long as someone keeps singing their bars.

## 2. The central question

**If the world only stays wonderful because somebody keeps re-making it, how much of your
own light will you put into other people's mornings?**

Play keeps asking this in miniature: every card played is a piece of yourself given to a
made thing; every contract accepted is a promise that a town's colors will not fade on your
watch; every run is one maker's turn carrying the song.

## 3. Core themes and register

**Themes**
1. **Making as world-keeping** — craft is not decoration on top of the world; craft *is* the
   world's physics. A bridge stands because it was made delightfully. When the delight wears
   thin, so does the bridge.
2. **The generosity economy** — everything of value moves as gift, welcome, performance, and
   commission. Nobody in Roundelay hoards; a masterpiece in a locked room goes dim. Value is
   created by being *given well*.
3. **The traveling life** — home is a circuit, not a place. Belonging is something you renew
   town by town, hearth by hearth, and the road itself keeps score of how you travel.
4. **Renewal against the Fade** — beauty is not permanent and that is not tragic. Things
   run down so they can be re-made better; the world's mortality is what gives makers their
   place in it.

**Mood / register**: luminous, warm, itinerant. Early-autumn festival light. The feeling of
arriving in a strange town at dusk and seeing lanterns being hung for you. Miyazaki's
working wonder (Kiki's bakery, Howl's hearth) crossed with Le Guin's craft-seriousness
(true work done with full attention has weight). Picture-book clarity, journeyman's-notebook
texture. Never grim: failure in Roundelay is a dimming, and dimming is quiet and survivable
and even beautiful — but it is *real*, and the world is shy toward the dim.

## 4. Named elements

---

### `waking-of-made-things`

Anything made with complete attention wakes, a little. A well-thrown pot hums when the tea
is right; a well-built door learns the footsteps of the family and swings a half-second
early; a truly great coat will turn its collar up before its wearer feels the wind. Waking
is the ordinary miracle of Roundelay — every child has seen it, and every child has also
felt the small heartbreak of a hasty thing that never woke at all. The old riddle goes:
*the maker gives the thing its shape, but the delight gives it its eyes.*

- **LUDIC HOOK** — this could become: card quality tiers as a *waking* scale. Cards played
  with synergy/flourish "wake" into stronger persistent versions; a run's engine is
  literally a workshop of things you've woken, and degenerate spam-play produces unwoken,
  inert cards.
- **VISUAL HOOK** — woken things carry a thin rim of light along their working edge — the
  lip of the bowl, the blade of the plane, the hem of the coat — a warm gold line like
  late sun on the edge of a table. Unwoken things read matte and slightly desaturated.
  Silhouette rule: woken objects sit at a subtly alert angle, like a listening dog.

---

### `the-fade`

The Fade is the world exhaling. Color leaves things first — a red roof going rose, going
gray — then the knack: wells forget the trick of sweet water, bridges forget exactly how
heavy a cart is, a whole valley's echo goes flat. Nobody fights the Fade and nobody hates
it; it is simply the reason makers exist, the tide that the Rounds were built to answer.
A town three seasons overdue for a maker is called *pale*, and pale towns hang out their
asking-lanterns and wait, patient as winter gardens.

- **LUDIC HOOK** — this could become: the run's escalating pressure clock. Map nodes
  visibly pale over time; contracts grow because the Fade compounds — skip a pale town now
  and its asking is twice the size (and twice the reward, and twice the wonder) when the
  road brings you back.
- **VISUAL HOOK** — desaturation with a direction: the Fade drains color from the edges of
  a scene inward, so pale towns look vignetted in reverse — gray at the rooftops, one
  stubborn ember of color at the hearth-line. Re-made places re-color in visible strokes,
  like watercolor blooming across wet paper.

---

### `makers-gleam` — *the diegetic identity of Standing*

Gleam is the light that gathers on a maker whose work delights. It lives in no one place —
knuckles, hair, the rim of the ears — and everyone can see it at dusk: a lit maker crossing
a square trails a faint shine like a candle carried behind rice paper. Gleam is not virtue
and not wealth; it is closer to *the world's trust*, and the world is frank about it: doors
open a beat sooner for the lit, shy materials consent to their hands, roads shorten under
their boots. When work disappoints — a botched commission, a promise to the work broken,
an audience left unmoved — the gleam dims, gently, the way a room dims when a lamp is
carried out. A maker gone fully dark is not punished; the world simply becomes shy of them
again, as it is shy of everyone at birth, and no material will open until they are re-lit.

- **LUDIC HOOK** — this could become: the Standing fail-resource, exactly. Gleam depletes
  on failed/abandoned contracts and flat performances; at zero, materials refuse the maker
  and the run ends — not death, but the world going quiet. Gleam level could also gate
  soft benefits (shop prices, road-shortcuts, rare material access) so Standing is felt as
  warmth, not just a hit-point bar.
- **VISUAL HOOK** — a rim-light that belongs to the character, not the scene: the maker is
  always lit as if by a small source just behind their shoulder, and the strength of that
  key light IS the Standing meter. Full gleam: honeyed backlight, long soft shadow thrown
  *toward* the viewer. Near-dark: the character rendered in ambient light only, edges
  merging with the background — still present, but the world no longer picks them out.

---

### `the-askings` — *the diegetic identity of escalating contracts*

When a town needs making, it does not post a notice; it *makes the request itself*, because
in Roundelay even a request must be crafted. An asking is a small object that carries the
shape of the need: a tin spoon with a dry bowl (our well), a stitched swallow with one gray
wing (our summer), a hinge that opens onto nothing (our hope, frankly, we're not sure).
Askings are hung on the asking-lantern at the town gate, and accepting one is done with the
hands — you take it down, you carry it, and from then on the little object rides your pack
and warms when you near the work. Larger needs make larger, stranger askings, and the
oldest road-saying covers the rest: *small hands ask for kettles; valleys ask for weather.*

- **LUDIC HOOK** — this could become: the contract system, exactly. Each contract is a
  physical token with legible iconography of its demands; accepted askings sit visibly in
  your pack UI, warm/pulse as deadlines near, and their crafted size/strangeness telegraphs
  contract tier at a glance.
- **VISUAL HOOK** — folk-art objects against dark wood: tin, straw, stitched cloth, carved
  bone-white birch, each with one wrong/faded detail that states the need. The asking-lantern
  itself: a gate-post lantern strung with dozens of small objects turning slowly on red
  thread, backlit at dusk — a chandelier of needs.

---

### `the-long-rounds`

The Rounds are the great circuits the makers walk — not roads so much as *habits the land
has learned*. A Round remembers who travels it well: for a bright maker the miles fold
politely, a day's walk tucking itself into an afternoon, and the same hedge that scratched
you in spring holds out blackberries in fall. Every maker's run is one turn of a Round,
and no two turns visit the same world, because the towns have paled and bloomed differently
each time. Retiring makers say the same thing: you never finish a Round; you hand it on
mid-song.

- **LUDIC HOOK** — this could become: the run/map structure. Procedural circuit (not
  branching-tree-to-a-boss but a loop with a home note), where route choice is the core
  strategic layer: which pale towns to answer, which to trust to the next maker. Road-memory
  = persistent-within-run map buffs earned by how you travel.
- **VISUAL HOOK** — a hand-inked circuit map that draws itself as you walk it, the line
  behind you gaining color and small marginalia (a bird, a kettle, a face) for each town
  answered. The road ahead is a pale pencil line; the road behind is illuminated-manuscript
  bright.

---

### `hearth-right`

Any maker with even a thread of gleam may claim hearth-right: one night's warmth, one meal,
one story owed to the house in exchange. It is the oldest practice on the Rounds and the
most beloved — the knock, the held-up lit hand, the door swinging wide, the whole
household's evening rearranging itself with pleasure around a guest. The story told at the
fire is not payment; it is *how the house learns the world*, and houses that have heard
many travelers' stories wake sooner and stand longer. Refusing hearth-right is almost
unheard of, and a house that does so goes strangely cold for a season, as if embarrassed.

- **LUDIC HOOK** — this could become: the rest-node economy. Camp/inn nodes where the
  resource spent is narrative (choose which of your run's events to "tell"), converting
  run-history into heals, card upgrades, or town relationships — your past choices are
  literally your currency at the fire.
- **VISUAL HOOK** — the doorway shot: night-blue street, a door opened onto deep amber, the
  traveler's silhouette rimmed in gleam, firelight reaching out across the doorstep like a
  hand. Interior palette: hearth-orange, wool-red, shadow-brown, one cold blue window.

---

### `journey-pieces`

A maker's skill is not carried in the head; it is carried in the pack, as pieces. A
journey-piece is a kept fragment of learned craft: a ribbon holding the turn of a dance, a
chip of glaze with a whole kiln's wisdom in its crackle, three bars of a planing-song
knotted into a cord, a joke folded into a paper bird (unfolding it tells it — once). Makers
trade pieces, win them at festivals, and are given them by masters — and to *use* one is to
perform it, out loud, with the hands, in front of the work. A full pack rattling with
pieces is a life's education you can hear coming down the road.

- **LUDIC HOOK** — this could become: the deck itself, exactly. Cards are physical craft
  fragments with craft-discipline suits (song, joinery, glaze, thread, dough, dance...);
  deck-building is diegetic pack-packing, card acquisition is teaching/trading/gifting, and
  "exhaust" effects are pieces given away or performed to destruction (the paper-bird joke).
- **VISUAL HOOK** — each card is a *thing*, painted as an object on worn canvas: ribbon,
  shard, knotted cord, folded paper, all with the gold working-edge of woken things. The
  open pack: a bandolier-style spread, tool-roll leather, each piece in its loop — a deck
  that reads as a beautifully kept toolkit.

---

### `shy-materials`

In Roundelay the materials are alive the way cats are alive: present, opinionated, and not
obliged to come when called. Silver will not take a shape unless it is sung to; a certain
green riverwood opens only for people who are laughing; festival glass must be blown in
front of an audience or it clouds. Getting materials is therefore never mining and always
*courtship* — makers keep notebooks of what the local clay likes, and a stranger's first
question in a new town is not "what do you need" but "what does your stone answer to."

- **LUDIC HOOK** — this could become: resource acquisition as micro-puzzles/conditions
  rather than flat costs — materials that join your pool only when a condition is met this
  turn (played a song card, kept a combo going, have an audience token), making the economy
  feel courted rather than harvested.
- **VISUAL HOOK** — materials with body language: ingots that lean away or lean in, clay
  that ripples toward a singer like iron filings toward a magnet, a shelf of glass jars
  where the contents visibly perk up when the right maker walks in. Color signature per
  material temperament — shy blues, sociable ambers, vain iridescents.

---

### `audience-of-things`

When true work begins, made things gather to watch. Chairs scrape a quarter-turn toward
the bench; lamps lean; somewhere in the rafters the house's oldest woken kettle starts a
low, attentive hum. Makers work better watched — this is measurable, ask any of them — and
the finest workshops are therefore built like small theaters, tools racked in raked rows
like an audience that never goes home. The dread of every maker is not the critic but the
empty room: work done truly alone wakes thin, if it wakes at all.

- **LUDIC HOOK** — this could become: a combo/momentum system. "Attention" builds as you
  chain plays without stalling and is spent as a multiplier on the big card — performance
  as the run's rhythm engine. Persistent artifacts could be woken audience-things that
  grant passive attention.
- **VISUAL HOOK** — the workshop-as-theater: a pool of worklight center-frame, and around
  it, in the half-dark, dozens of small woken silhouettes tilted inward at the same angle —
  jugs, lasts, lanterns, a rocking horse — every gold working-edge pointed at the bench
  like rows of eyes catching stage light.

---

### `the-great-askings`

Once in a while — a generation, a lifetime, never on schedule — the land itself makes an
asking. It uses what it has: a mountain sends an asking of weathered antler and cloudmoss
that no human hands arranged; a fading season sends one as a morning that smells wrong to
everyone at once. Great askings are why the Rounds exist at all, the peak of every maker's
hope and the honest terror at the bottom of the craft: someday something the size of
weather may hang its need on your lantern, having heard, town by town, hearth by hearth,
that your hands are bright enough to ask.

- **LUDIC HOOK** — this could become: the end-run escalation / boss-contract tier.
  Final contracts are re-makings of world-scale systems (a season, a river's voice, a
  mountain's sleep), mechanically demanding the whole engine you've built and diegetically
  *earned* by high Standing — escalation as reputation, not as debt.
- **VISUAL HOOK** — scale inversion of the asking-object: the tiny folk-art token remade
  at landscape size — a valley where the clouds have knotted themselves into red thread,
  a mountainside arranged like objects on a gate-lantern. The maker: one gleam-lit figure
  at the bottom of the frame, pack on, looking up.

---

### `the-quiet-walk`

When a maker goes fully dim, the Rounds have a rite for it, and it is kind. The unlit maker
walks the circuit one more time — no pack, no pieces, no askings — and at every town is
fed, and warmed, and *not asked for anything*, which is the strangest sensation of a
maker's life. Some walk a season and feel the first flicker return in a stranger's kitchen,
laughing at nothing; some walk to the sea and settle there, done, and are honored as the
audience every future maker plays to. Dimming is an ending the way autumn is an ending: it
is one, truly, and also the ground the next brightness grows from.

- **LUDIC HOOK** — this could become: the run-end and meta-progression frame. Standing
  hitting zero triggers the Quiet Walk — a brief, playable epilogue where this run's
  woken works are revisited and one is chosen to persist into the meta-layer (a permanent
  audience-thing, unlock, or starting boon for the next maker).
- **VISUAL HOOK** — the same circuit map, walked in reverse light: dawn-gray palette, the
  maker un-rimmed and ordinary among ordinary people, while everything they woke on the
  way out still carries its gold edge — the light didn't go out; it moved into the work.
  The most emotionally load-bearing image in the game.

---

### `unmarked-wonders`

Every made thing carries its maker's mark somewhere — a thumbprint in the glaze, a
signature bar in the woken hum. But scattered through Roundelay are wonders with no mark
at all: a stone stair a thousand years bright, a river that has never once forgotten its
knack, a bell that wakes other things when it rings. Nobody knows who made them and nobody
agrees whether "who" is even the right question — the cheerful heresy of the Rounds is that
the world may simply have started making *itself*, somewhere, the way a very old workshop
starts finishing small jobs overnight. Makers treat unmarked wonders as colleagues, not
gods: you tip your hat, you leave a journey-piece on the step, you study the joinery.

- **LUDIC HOOK** — this could become: rare discovery nodes and relic-class cards — unmarked
  pieces with rule-bending behaviors that don't fit any suit, found not bought, each one a
  little mystery the collection screen quietly assembles into the game's long riddle.
- **VISUAL HOOK** — the gold working-edge, but *everywhere at once*: unmarked wonders are
  edge-lit along every line, softly, like drawings that haven't been colored in because the
  light got there first. They photograph as slightly too in-focus against a painterly world.

## 5. How escalating contracts and Standing arise — discovered, not installed

Walk one turn of a Round and both halves of the spine simply *happen to you.*

You leave the guild-town in spring with a light pack and a thread of gleam — enough that
doors know you're a maker, not enough that anyone important does. At the first village the
asking-lantern holds small things: a tin spoon with a dry bowl, a cracked whistle. You take
the spoon. You court the local clay (it likes being told about the weather), you re-make
the well-ring in front of six children and an attentive kettle, and the water comes back
sweet, and the color comes back to the square like watercolor blooming — and something
comes to *you*, too. Your knuckles hold dusk-light a little longer. The road out of town
tucks a mile away for you. That's the whole loop, once around, small.

Here is the thing the world knows that no one has to explain: **light is visible.** The
next town heard the well before you arrived — woken things gossip, travelers carry stories
to hearths, and hearths tell their houses. So the next lantern holds a bigger asking, not
because anything demands more of you, but because *need aims itself at brightness* the way
moths do, the way a warm window gathers travelers on a cold road. Being asked is the honor;
the size of the asking is the compliment. Kettles ask small hands. Rivers ask bright ones.
By midsummer you are carrying a stitched swallow with one gray wing, and you understand
with a shiver that a whole summer has heard of you. The contract curve escalates because
reputation is luminous and the Fade is patient — every wonder you wake widens the circle
of things that know your name and dims a little further while it waits its turn.

And the same light is the fail-state, felt from inside as warmth rather than threat. Gleam
is the world's trust made visible, and you spend it every time you work: a flat
performance, a broken promise to the work, an asking carried past its season — each one
carries a little of your light out of the room. Nothing punishes you. The materials just
get shy again. Doors open a beat slower; the blackberry hedge is only a hedge. Fall far
enough and the silver stops answering your songs entirely, and there is nothing to do but
what makers have always done: set down the pack and take the Quiet Walk, warming at
hearths that ask you for nothing, until — sometimes — laughter in a stranger's kitchen
strikes the first spark of the next run. Standing depletes and the run ends, and in
Roundelay that is not a game-over screen; it is autumn.

## 6. Anchor declaration

**Declared anchor: the itinerant-journeyman circuit** (`the-long-rounds` + the guild-town /
traveling-crafter frame). This is a deliberately conventional silhouette — the wandering
journeyman with a pack is folk-tale furniture, and it is *chosen* for exactly that
legibility. It exists to carry three novel elements into instant readability: the
**waking-of-made-things** (why a traveler's work matters cosmologically), **makers-gleam**
(a Standing resource you can read on a character's skin at dusk), and **the-askings**
(contracts as crafted folk-objects rather than postings or debts). The player already knows
how to want to be a traveling maker; everything strange in Roundelay is hung on that
familiar pack-frame. No other conventional elements are relied on: no chosen one (any hands
can brighten), no dark lord (the Fade is a tide, not a villain), no elemental magic (the
magic is attention), and — per the do-not-resemble list — nothing here is owed, filed,
adjudicated, or held aloft by promises. The askings are gifts that request; the gleam is
trust, not credit; the only court in Roundelay is the audience of things.
