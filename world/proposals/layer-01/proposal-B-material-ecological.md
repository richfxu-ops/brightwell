# Proposal B — Material / Ecological Lens

## Working name: **SOLMERE**

*The land of the light-meres — a watershed where light behaves like slow water and ripens like fruit.*

---

## 1. The natural philosophy (the one strange law)

In Solmere, light is a substance with habits. It falls from the sky as everywhere else, but it does not vanish when the sun moves on: it **settles**. It runs downhill in bright rills, gathers in hollows, and stands in the valley-bottoms as **meres of pooled light** — lakes of standing gold you can wade into up to your knees. Pooled light is alive the way a river is alive: it has currents, moods, and — this is the law everything else grows from — **it learns routes**. Light flows more readily where light has flowed before. A path walked with a full lantern becomes, over a season, a path light chooses on its own.

And pooled light **ripens**. Fresh-fallen light is thin and green as new cider; left to stand and fed by what grows around it, it deepens through gold, to amber, to the rare slow violet that only the oldest meres hold. Every color has different properties, different appetites, different creatures that come for it. The whole economy of Solmere is a hydrology and a horticulture of light: tend the watershed, ripen the pools, meet what the brightness draws.

Nothing in Solmere is owed. Things are **drawn** — the way moths are drawn, the way water is drawn downhill. That distinction is the soul of this proposal.

---

## 2. Central question

**When your tending makes the season bloom grander than you are, do you grow to meet it — or let the brightness flow on to someone else?**

Every run keeps asking it: each success brightens your valley, and brightness *draws* larger wonders that need more of you.

## 3. Core themes & register

1. **Reciprocity, not transaction** — the land and its keeper feed each other; care is an ecology, never a ledger.
2. **Ripening and impermanence** — everything luminous is seasonal; light hoarded goes grey, light spent well comes back deeper in color.
3. **Being known by a place** — the deepest wealth in Solmere is recognition: the land remembering your hands.
4. **Success invites the sublime** — doing well does not make life safer; it makes life *bigger*, and that is the world's gift and its test.

**Mood/register:** luminous pastoral. Hushed dawns, festival dusks, weather you can cup in your hands. Strange physics delivered with picture-book calm — Miyazaki's megafauna gentleness, Le Guin's sense that magic is husbandry. Never grim, never clerical; the worst thing that can happen to you is that the world politely stops noticing you.

## 4. Named elements

### `light-meres`
The standing lakes of pooled light in every valley-bottom, each with its own current, temper, and depth of color. A mere breathes: it swells after clear days, thins under cloud, and ripples away from strangers but toward hands it knows. Villages are built on mere-shores the way ours are built on harbors.
- **LUDIC HOOK — this could become:** the run's resource nodes — tappable pools that regenerate on a rhythm, with over-drawing a mere thinning it (and your Standing) for turns to come; deck cards that dredge, divert, or deepen specific meres.
- **VISUAL HOOK:** a green valley cradling a lake of gold that has *shorelines of shadow*; wind-ripples of glare crossing it like fish; a child skipping a stone that leaves rings of brightness climbing the grass.

### `the-hueing`
Light ripens through colors as it stands: **green** (new-fallen, quick, thin), **gold** (table light, the daily coin of life), **amber** (slow, sweet, heavy — it drips), and **violet** (decades old, kept in the deep meres, almost a being). Ripening can be coaxed — certain reeds, certain songs, certain patient stirrings — but never rushed; forced light goes grey.
- **LUDIC HOOK — this could become:** resource maturation as a core economic verb — cards and holdings that *age* between turns, a hold-vs-spend tension where every color is a different resource tier, and "forcing" as a tempting efficiency card that risks grey (dead) yield.
- **VISUAL HOOK:** an orchard where light hangs in drops from lattice-frames, a visible gradient down the rows — hard green glints, warm gold globes, amber sagging like honey, and at the row's end one violet drop the size of a plum that everyone lowers their voice near.

### `bright-shadow`
Everyone in Solmere casts two shadows: the ordinary dark one from the sun, and opposite it a **bright shadow** — a faint figure of colored light thrown by the land's recollection of you. It deepens in saturation where you have tended well; it washes toward grey when you take without tending, force light, or let a guest go hungry. Keepers read each other's bright shadows the way sailors read weather. **This is Standing.** When a bright shadow goes fully grey, the light no longer knows that person: meres close like eyes at their approach, lanterns will not ripen in their hands, and the season simply — kindly, completely — moves on without them.
- **LUDIC HOOK — this could become:** the Standing fail-resource made visible on the player avatar itself — a saturation meter with diegetic feedback (shops literally dim toward you, pools recede from your feet as it drops); run-end is the grey walk home under ordinary daylight.
- **VISUAL HOOK:** a figure on a ridge at noon casting one black shadow east and one rose-and-gold shadow west; as the run falters, the bright twin fades frame by frame like a watercolor left in the rain.

### `guesting-seeds`
The contracts of Solmere are planted, not signed. A **guesting-seed** is a bud of condensed light, traded at mere-markets, that grows over days into a great blossom — and when it opens, something *arrives* through it: a flight of glass moths, a walking river's detour, a hue-heron rookery, each needing to be fed, watered in light, and seen safely onward. Fulfilled well, an arrival departs scattering seed of the **next size up** — because light learns routes, and every satisfied guest brightens the way to your valley for something grander. **These are the escalating contracts**, and their growth is as lawful and as unresented as fruit following blossom.
- **LUDIC HOOK — this could become:** the contract system itself — you *choose and plant* your obligations, water them to influence timing and size, and each completion seeds the next tier; failed guestings don't fine you, they grey your bright-shadow and un-learn the route (contract tree resets downward).
- **VISUAL HOOK:** a fist-sized bud staked in a dooryard swelling day by day into a gate-sized blossom of layered light; on arrival-morning it opens like a slow firework and something enormous and gentle steps or pours through.

### `walking-rivers`
The great migrations of Solmere are rivers of light that travel overland at a walking pace — molten-gold ribbons a mile long that follow the routes light has learned, pausing at deep meres to drink and to breed the little fish of pure glare that live inside them. Their itineraries shift with the brightness of the land: a well-tended valley may find a walking river turning up its lane like an honored, catastrophic, magnificent guest.
- **LUDIC HOOK — this could become:** the run map — a route structure that literally re-draws itself based on your accumulated tending; late-run high-tier contracts are hostings of a walking river's overnight stay (huge intake, huge demand, transforms the board).
- **VISUAL HOOK:** a ribbon of daylight moving across dusk hills like a slow river out of its bed, hedgerows lighting up as it passes, leaping glare-fish leaving streaks like shooting stars at knee height.

### `moth-barges`
Cargo in Solmere travels on the backs of glasswing moths the size of barns — placid, wordless megafauna that drink amber light and navigate by the ripeness of the meres below. Keepers rig soft rope ladders to their thoraxes and load panniers between the wing-roots; a moth-barge will only alight where the bright-shadows below are deep enough to trust.
- **LUDIC HOOK — this could become:** logistics and hand-size — transport capacity as a run stat, moth-barge cards gated by Standing thresholds (the world's trust made mechanical), high-capacity routes that open only to saturated bright-shadows.
- **VISUAL HOOK:** a barn-sized moth at dusk, wings like cathedral windows lit from within, wing-dust falling as slow gold snow on the upturned faces of a village; silhouette of a keeper climbing a rope ladder into the glow.

### `dusk-drink`
Every evening the pooled light drains: the world drinks. Meres spiral softly downward into the root-mouths of trees with a sound like a far-off choir exhaling, and whatever a keeper has not harvested, hued, or fed to a guest by dusk goes down with it. It is not loss — it is the land's supper — but it makes every day a shape with an edge.
- **LUDIC HOOK — this could become:** the turn structure — an end-of-round drain that clears unspent basic resources, forcing tempo decisions; deliberately letting good light go down with grace could even *feed* rootglow (below), turning the timer into a second economy.
- **VISUAL HOOK:** dusk as a visible slow whirlpool in every pool, gold threading down into the black soles of oaks; the moment after, when the valley is dark but every tree's roots glow faintly through the turf like veins.

### `rootglow`
What the world drinks, the world keeps. Beneath Solmere runs a slow reservoir of swallowed light — the rootglow — and a keeper whose bright shadow is deep enough may kneel at a root-mouth and *ask*: sing a measure, open a well, and be answered with old light the color of embers. The roots answer generosity with generosity; they answer greed by drinking the asker's own bright shadow a little.
- **LUDIC HOOK — this could become:** a banked emergency resource — withdrawals scale with current Standing and damage Standing when over-used, an elegant press-your-luck valve for desperate turns.
- **VISUAL HOOK:** a keeper kneeling in night grass at a knothole glowing like a hearth; ember-light rising out of the ground into her cupped hands, and the grass around her knees momentarily printed with veins of gold.

### `grey-weather`
Solmere's only adversary is weather made of shadow: soft silver fogs that drift in off the unlit country beyond the watershed and *unripen* whatever they touch — gold back to green, green to nothing, bright shadows washed a shade paler. Grey-weather is not evil; it is winter's cousin, lawful and patient, and keepers speak of it the way farmers speak of frost.
- **LUDIC HOOK — this could become:** the hazard deck — forecastable events that decay resources and chip Standing, with mitigation as a whole equipment/practice suite (fog-fences of amber, chime-lines, hue-hardened stores).
- **VISUAL HOOK:** a fogbank like poured pewter sliding down a bright valley, the gradient line where gold goes grey moving visibly across a hillside; dew afterward like beads of dull glass on every web.

### `mere-keepers` — **DECLARED ANCHOR**
The player is a **mere-keeper**: an itinerant tender of the light-watershed, part shepherd, part orchardist, part harbor-master, walking a circuit of valleys with a staff, a seed-satchel, and a lantern that is really a portable mere. *This is a deliberately conventional figure — the wandering pastoral tradesperson — and it is chosen as the anchor precisely so that everything strange (light-hydrology, the hueing, guesting-seeds, the bright shadow) is met through hands and tools a player already understands: tend, carry, plant, harvest, host.*
- **LUDIC HOOK — this could become:** the run avatar and deck identity — keeper schools/traditions as starting archetypes (still-water keepers who ripen, rill-keepers who move light fast, seed-keepers who contract aggressively).
- **VISUAL HOOK:** a small figure with a wide hat and a shoulder-slung glass lantern half-full of pooled gold, crook-staff trailing a thread of light through wet grass; big landscape, small silhouette, two shadows.

### `chimeglass`
The trade-token of Solmere: droplets of ripened light blown into glass beads that ring faintly when brightness passes near. Spending chimeglass means cracking the bead and letting the light out to do something — so every purse is also a held breath, and a rich market sounds like rain on bells.
- **LUDIC HOOK — this could become:** the currency layer with color denominations (green/gold/amber beads = tiered coinage), plus "cracked-bead" one-shot effect cards where money and spell are the same object.
- **VISUAL HOOK:** strings of glowing beads in graded hues hung at a market stall, each swaying bead ringing a different soft note; a payment shown as a bead cracked between finger and thumb, releasing a firefly-swirl.

### `the-high-bloom`
Once a season, all the learned routes converge: walking rivers braid, moth-barges gather in flotillas, every guesting-blossom in the watershed opens on the same night, and the valley that has tended best hosts the **High Bloom** — a night when light rises instead of falling, and the whole landscape lifts briefly into flower. It is the grandest guesting of all and the natural summit of a keeper's season.
- **LUDIC HOOK — this could become:** the run's climax structure — a final convergence contract whose size is the sum of every route you brightened, win condition as *hosting* rather than defeating; scoring as how much of the Bloom your valley could hold.
- **VISUAL HOOK:** an inverted lightfall — meres emptying *upward* in slow columns, blossoms of light the size of houses opening across a dark valley, moth-barges circling inside the columns like leaves in an updraft.

## 5. How escalating contracts and Standing arise — naturally, wondrously

A keeper's first guesting-seed is small: a knuckle of warm light bought with two green beads, planted by the doorstep, watered from the lantern. It opens in three days onto a drift of glass moths no bigger than sparrows, who drink a saucer of gold and leave. That is the whole contract — no signature, no schedule, nothing owed. Just: something came because you made it bright here, and you fed it, and it went on its way glad.

But light learns routes. The moths' path home is a hair brighter now for their having drunk well, and brightness *draws*. The seed the moths scatter as they go is a size larger than the one you planted, because the way to your valley now carries their satisfaction in it like a scent. Plant that one, and what arrives is a hue-heron with her whole rookery, needing a week of amber. Host *her* well and the walking river itself begins, mile by mile over the following days, to lean toward your lane. **This is the escalation, and no one imposes it: it is phototropism.** The world's grandeur grows toward whoever feeds it, the way sunflowers grow toward morning. A keeper watching her guesting-buds swell larger every cycle feels what a gardener feels watching a sapling become a tree that will one day be bigger than the house — awe, and the sweet pressure of rising to it. The question is never *can you pay* — it is *can you be big enough, bright enough, ready enough for what your own tending has invited.*

And Standing is simply the other face of the same law. The land that learns routes also learns hands. Your bright shadow — the colored twin the world throws behind you — *is* the land's memory of your tending, and it is spent the way soil is spent. Strip a mere to meet a guest and your bright twin pales a shade. Force green light to gold and it pales two. Let an arrival stand hungry at an open blossom and the route un-learns you: somewhere out in the hills, a river leans away. None of this is punishment; nothing scolds you; there is no ledger anywhere in Solmere. It is only that recognition is a living thing, and living things can starve. A keeper whose bright shadow greys out entirely is not exiled or ruined — she is *unremembered*. The meres close like eyes as she walks. The moth-barges pass over. The season, that grand converging festival of light, flows around her valley like water around a stone, and she walks home under plain sunlight casting one shadow, an ordinary person in an extraordinary world — until, perhaps, next season, when the light falls new and green and unprejudiced, and a keeper can begin again with a lantern and a seed.

Escalating contracts: **the world blooming toward you.** Standing: **the world's memory of your hands.** Both are the same law of light seen from two sides, and both feel — because they are — like weather, growth, and being known.

## 6. Anchor declaration (summary)

One anchor is used, declared in its element entry: **`mere-keepers`** — the conventional itinerant pastoral tender — chosen so that the genuinely novel systems (light-as-watershed, the hueing, guesting-seeds, bright shadows) are all encountered through the immediately legible verbs of tend / carry / plant / harvest / host. Anchor share: 1 of 12 elements.

## 7. Compliance notes (D-002 / D-003)

- **No debt/obligation cosmology:** nothing in Solmere is owed, promised, or held aloft by a vow; contracts are planted ecological events, escalation is phototropism, and the text repeatedly forecloses ledger-readings ("no ledger anywhere in Solmere").
- **No courts, filings, paperwork, guardianship; no estuary-mud economy; no sky-city, no name-syllable currency.**
- **No twee defaults:** no candy, no godmothers, no wishes on stars; the megafauna are wordless.
- **Spine intact:** escalating contracts (`guesting-seeds` → `the-high-bloom`) and depletable Standing (`bright-shadow`) are load-bearing, diegetic, and wondrous — failure is being forgotten by the light, not being punished by it.
