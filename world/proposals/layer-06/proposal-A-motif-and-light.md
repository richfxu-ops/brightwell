# Layer 6 — Proposal A · MOTIF AND LIGHT

**Lens:** lead with the picture. Build the visual system out of canon's own light-behaviors — gleam grain, lantern dark, dawn reissue, the seasonal palettes, the two persistences — into one grammar an art team can paint from and a style audit can check any frame against. Naming follows the pictures.

**Obeys:** all Layer 1–5 register bans at full strength (vocabulary, channel, restraint, riddle, reservoir, transit-light, pedigree, folk-scale, timescale, membership, officiant, quarrel ration, holdings, standing-never-spends, stealth, mint, commerce-vocabulary, matter-is-ordinary, courier, salvage, stage-slang, almanac, weather-danger, crown guard, stretch reservation). Twee-guard throughout: elegance here is rationing — one red line, one gold edge, saturation earned, dark kept warm.

---

## 0. The picture first

Roundelay is painted in **one light and three brushes**, and everything in this layer is that sentence unpacked. Light is attention; the brush tells you whose it is. Scenery takes the **wash** — wet watercolor that blooms when regard returns and drains, rooftops first, hearth-line last, when it goes. People take the **rim** — a key-light from a small source just behind the shoulder, and the strength of that light *is* Standing. Things take the **edge** — gold, and only in the two lawful signatures of the two persistences: the still line of the fired, the breathing skin of the held.

Seven restraint rules govern the whole surface, stated here once and enforced by the named laws below:

1. **Gold is rationed.** It appears only as the two persistences permit, and no thing wears both signatures at once.
2. **One red line per frame.** Last-red and red thread are the palette's only full-saturation note; a composition holds one red gesture the way a sentence holds one em-dash.
3. **Saturation is earned.** The fullest color in any scene belongs to what is most attended — dawns over pale floors, a re-making mid-bloom, a hearth. Default scenery sits two steps back from the swatch.
4. **Dark is warm.** Night-blue, shadow-brown, rafters' dark. Darkness is where the world keeps what it is not showing you yet; it is never a threat (weather-danger law, kept visually).
5. **Light in motion is almost never seen.** Changes are *found*, not watched — the transit-light restraint made cinematography.
6. **Nothing lerps; everything settles.** The world's one animate verb is attending, so the motion grammar is the tilt, the sway, the settle — never the bounce, the slide, the pop.
7. **No cursive, no candy, no christening.** Folk-craft lettering is a workman's round hand; the palette is dyer's and limner's stock, named from things; and no object in the world bears a personal name.

The rest of this proposal is the motif bible (section 1), twelve named style laws (sections 2–5, canon element format), the naming language with samples (section 3), the card voice with samples (section 4), and the sanctioned renames, executed (section 5).

## 1. THE VISUAL MOTIF BIBLE

Seven systems, each given palette words, light behavior, shape language, a motion signature, and what the camera loves. Every claim below is derivable from Layers 1–5; nothing contradicts a channel.

### 1.1 Gleam — Standing worn as light

- **Palette:** honeyed backlight at full; amber through rice-paper at middle; ambient-only at the bottom, edges merging with the ground. Never white, never halo — gleam is dusk-colored, the warm end of daylight, because it is *read at dusk*.
- **Light behavior:** a key-light that belongs to the character and not the scene, thrown from a small source just behind the shoulder; strength of key = current Standing; length and softness of the thrown shadow = the meter's second read (full gleam throws a long soft shadow toward the viewer). Grain rides *inside* the rim: long calm spans (bridge-grain), water-ripple (song), freckles (kitchen-laughter), dapple (Fairwright rings), slantwise furrow-light (Gleaner), watered silk (Mannerly) — tree-rings for a walking life, readable in any portrait.
- **Shape language:** gleam clings where surfaces end — knuckles, hair, ear-rims, the hem of a sleeve in wind. It is dew, not armor: it never outlines the whole figure (that is the unmarked wonders' uncanny register and no one else's).
- **Motion signature:** the **cling** — gleam arrives as dew arrives, found on the skin rather than seen landing, except in the room at peak overflow (see 1.4 and `found-not-watched`). When Standing falls, the rim does not flicker or drain like a bar; the *scene's* ambient rises to meet it — the world stops picking the maker out.
- **The camera loves:** a lit maker crossing a blue square at dusk, shine trailing like a candle behind rice paper; the loading-screen portrait close enough to read grain; the near-dark maker still present, un-lost, merely unremarked.

### 1.2 Askings and lanterns — contracts as crafted need

- **Palette:** tin, straw, stitched wool, bone-white birch, one wrong faded detail per object — the grey wing, the dry bowl — against dark wood and warm lantern dark. Red thread the only saturate.
- **Light behavior:** backlit at dusk, the canonical read: dozens of small objects turning slowly on red thread, a chandelier of needs. Tier is *height*: door-nail (no lantern at all), low hooks, eye-height, rafters — and above tonight's showing, exactly one tier previews in silhouette before the dark closes (Layer 5's lantern-mile, kept as the interface law).
- **Shape language:** folk-object silhouettes, each one legible as its need at ten paces — the demand iconography is the object itself, never a badge on it. Great askings invert scale: the land's found arrangements (antler and cloudmoss in the shape of falling water) read as *landscape that composed itself*, red thread tied where it lies.
- **Motion signature:** the **slow turn** on thread; the **warming** — an accepted asking rides the pack and warms as its season shortens, a color-temperature shift from tin toward ember, never a timer (almanac ban, kept visually: nothing counts down; things *ripen*).
- **The camera loves:** the lantern-mile — ridge-top dusk, the gate-lantern glowing like a dropped constellation; the Merrylees hour — a bright-rimmed maker looking up into rafters that will not lower what she asked for smaller; the tired scale-inversion of a great asking, one gleam-lit figure at the bottom of the frame, pack on, looking up.

### 1.3 Handsels and the courting — money and materials

- **Handsels:** graded glow, dull → warm → singing, worn as a full-surface **breathing skin** (see `the-two-golds`); sound is the second channel — a good market at noon sounds like rain on bells, and a bright maker walking chimes. The signature close-up of the whole economy: a dull button brightening the moment a pleased hand closes over it.
- **The courting:** materials have body language and temperament palettes — shy blues (singing-silver's moth-wing sheen, leaning away from tuneless hands), sociable ambers (apprentice-stuffs swelling gently toward anyone: floury whites, wet-straw gold, riverbank brown), green-gold that widens its grain when the room laughs (riverwood), crowd-confetti caught motionless in festival-glass, vain iridescents for the proudest stock. Materials never speak; they lean, stiffen, ripple, perk — a cat's vocabulary, never a face (restraint ban kept: no eyes, no mouths, ever, on anything).
- **Shape language:** stalls strung dull-to-singing like lanterns tuning; the pack as a garden — bandolier loops, net-purse, shavings-jar glowing like banked embers, everything visibly tended or visibly going ordinary.
- **Motion signature:** the **sway-and-ring** for held things (pendular, wind-and-walk driven, idle motion that is never idle animation — it is the meter); the **lean** for materials (toward, away, minute and unmistakable).
- **The camera loves:** two hands over a stall-board at the exact moment one token brightens; the quietest corner of a loud fair, where everyone near the silver is humming.

### 1.4 The seep and the dawn — the Paling cycle (renamed; see section 5)

- **Palette going:** the reverse vignette — grey enters at the rooftops and edges, the hearth-line ember holds last; paling greys are dove, ash-rose, bone, oatmeal — *beautiful* greys, undyed-wool greys, never sludge. A pale town is a town rendered in silverpoint with one coal still lit.
- **Palette returning:** watercolor bloom — re-made places re-color in visible strokes across wet paper, the one lawful moment the wash is seen moving. Dawn is the other: rose-gold, sized by the country's paleness, longest and latest on the heights, immense and almost embarrassing over pale floors.
- **Light behavior:** dawn as the map's emotional weather-report — the greyer the evening, the redder the morning. Underground, the gold veins running valleyward appear **only in naturalist's plates** (diagrams, exempt from the transit-light restraint), never in scenes.
- **Shape language:** knack-loss has physical tells before color goes — the well-rope at the wrong angle, birds refusing a bridge; chalk spirals ringing gateposts like patient tree-growth, the outermost ring still wet.
- **Motion signature:** **none, by law.** The seep is never animated. Grey is found, gold is found; the world changes between looks (`found-not-watched`). The two sanctioned exceptions are the bloom of a woken re-making and the Moth Fair condition — overflow running visibly up the strings at peak, before a crowd, the game's fireworks and never its wallpaper.
- **The camera loves:** a grey valley at the end of night with a row of waiting figures, then the rose-gold coming up off the whole floor at once; the alley behind the fair-stage, generations deep in accidental color.

### 1.5 The four lights — the year as palette bible

One hill, painted five times (the canon four-panel plate, plus winter's coda):

- **The Green Going:** new-leaf green under rain-grey; thin quick dawns; wet chalk on dark stiles; kettle-sized askings in tin and straw. Line quality: soft pencil, unfinished edges — the year sketched.
- **The Long Light:** white-gold and dust; short noon shadows; bunting and festival brass; crowds as pooled color. Line quality: crisp, full ink.
- **The Deep Gold:** honey raking sideways; long shadows; the first enormous dawns; gleaning country opening in dove-grey and last-red. Line quality: heavy, warm, gilded at the edges.
- **The Red Walk:** madder on grey; rose-gold mornings at full size; red thread everywhere like first strokes of paint; the Fair's dust riding the seam of the seasons. Line quality: spare — few marks, each certain.
- **The Wintering (coda):** blue snow-light at the window, amber everywhere else; the world's palette folded to two colors and a fire.

Act transitions telegraph entirely by palette and weather, never by title card (Layer 5's rule, adopted as grade discipline).

### 1.6 The map — the inked round as art object

- **Palette:** oiled canvas, iron-gall ink, chalk white, one madder thread; the walked line illuminated-manuscript bright, the road ahead pale pencil.
- **Light behavior:** route-light as the run's autobiography — marginalia (a bird, a kettle, a face) accruing color behind the maker; shed-lines as faint crown-ridges; paling as true grey wash on the canvas itself, spirals copied forward in chalk.
- **Shape language:** a loop with a home note, never a tree; nodes, roads, and country kept as three drawing grammars (inked beads, ruled-and-wandering lines, washed regions).
- **Motion signature:** the **listening tilt** — one corner of the unrolled canvas lifting, very slightly, toward the road where the maker's tellings are walking. The map attends; it never acts.
- **The camera loves:** worn canvas across two knees at firelight; the Quiet Walk replaying the same canvas in reversed light, dawn-grey, every woken marginal figure still gold-edged.

### 1.7 The two persistences — the glance law (I-016)

Resolved and stated as style law at `the-two-golds` below; summarized here because every system above touches it: **fired regard wears the still line; held regard wears the breathing skin; no thing wears both.**

## 2. NAMED ELEMENTS — THE STYLE LAWS OF LIGHT AND MOTION

#### `the-three-brushes`

Limners on the Rounds — hearth-taught, academy-free, per the folk-scale guard — carry three brushes and a rule older than any of them: *the brush tells you whose the light is.* The **wash** is scenery's: wet, blooming, drained from the edges inward when a place lets go. The **rim** is people's: one dry bright stroke where the world's trust catches a figure, from a small source just behind the shoulder. The **edge** is things': one drawn line of gold on the working edge, or the low breathing sheen of the held, and nothing else. A painting that rims a doorway or edges a face is called *wrong-brushed*, and a limner would sooner scrape the board than hang it — not from rule-keeping but because it reads as a lie the way a wrong note reads: everyone can hear it, even the children.

- **This could become:** the art direction's master QA law and the render architecture in one — three shader/material disciplines (scenery owns bloom and desaturation; characters own rim and grain; props own edge-line and sheen), so the channel ban is enforced by the pipeline rather than by review; any frame, screenshot, or card illustration is auditable against one question per pixel: *which brush?*
- **Visual:** the style-guide's first plate: one dusk square painted three times — once all-wash (a ghost town), once all-rim (a stage), once all-edge (a shop window) — then the true frame, all three at once: grey town, bright maker, alert kettle, legible in one glance.

#### `the-two-golds`

*(Resolves I-016.)* Gold is one channel with two signatures, and every market-day teaches the difference. **Fired waking wears the still line**: one thin, crisp gold along the working edge and nowhere else — the lip of the bowl, the blade of the plane, the hem that does the keeping-warm. It is born in a single glaze-crackle bloom at the bench, the one moment of firing you ever see, beautiful and slightly final, like a signature watched drying; after that it never moves again, and it weathers only by softening, centuries-slow, into the gone-gentle gilding of the twice-benched. **Held waking wears the breathing skin**: a full-surface sheen with a slow tempo — about the length of a breath — that answers the moment: it brightens visibly under a glad hand, swells in company, dulls in a shut box toward matte. Three glance-tests, teachable to a child: *edge or surface; still or breathing; born once or fed always.* And the ration that makes both legible: **no thing wears both.** The one lawful breach is the unmarked wonders — edge-light along every line at once — which is exactly why they photograph as slightly too in-focus against a painterly world: they are the ration broken by something the ration cannot reach.

- **This could become:** the two persistences read at arm's length everywhere they matter — a woken card carries the still line on its leading edge (the edge that faces the play area), fired for the run; handsel tokens, shavings, and the glad-load's seams carry the breathing sheen, animated by the economy's actual state (idle coins visibly slowing); artifact-vs-currency is thereby never a tooltip, always a glance; the crackle-bloom is the waking moment's one VFX, reserved and unmistakable.
- **Visual:** the second style plate: a woken plane and a singing coin side by side on one bench — the plane's blade holding one still hairline of gold, the coin breathing over its whole face — and beneath them the same two objects gone old: the plane's line softened like worn gilt, the coin lapsed to button-matte.

#### `found-not-watched`

The world keeps its changes between looks, and the road has a saying for it: *the lane is found brighter, never caught blushing.* No eye has ever seen the seep move, or the spilling land, or a combing finish; grey arrives while you slept and gold arrives at dawn, and the two lawful sights of light in motion — the watercolor bloom of a woken re-making, and overflow running up the strings at peak before a gathered crowd — are each a festival precisely because they are all anyone ever gets to see. Makers live comfortably inside this discretion; it is why the world feels kept rather than haunted. What changed while you were gone is the road's way of talking to you.

- **This could become:** the cinematography and VFX budget as one discipline — world-state changes resolve on cuts, returns, and dawns (the node you left is *found* different), never on ambient tweens; the two sanctioned light-in-motion effects are reserved for the two fictions that earn them (re-making bloom; Moth Fair overflow at peak public turns), making the game's biggest mechanical moments also its only fireworks; everything else communicates by before/after, which is cheap, legible, and uncanny in exactly the right dose.
- **Visual:** a two-frame motif used throughout — same composition, hours apart: the stage at dusk with the crowd, then the empty alley at dawn a shade more watercolor than it was; nothing seen leaving, nothing seen landing, the difference doing all the talking.

#### `the-settling-tilt`

Nothing in Roundelay acts, so nothing in Roundelay's picture *moves* — it settles. The quarter-turn of a chair toward the bench, the degree of lean in a shelf of jars, the pack's pieces tilting like grass under one wind toward the road a telling walks: the world's whole animate vocabulary is the compass needle's — a small overshoot, a tremble, and rest, always toward what matters. Its companion is the sway: held things ride the day pendularly, on thread and net and walking hips, moved by wind and gait and never by themselves. Two motions, and the third thing is stillness, which is what the fired do best. Sentimental strangers looking for the pot that pours itself go home disappointed; everyone else learns to read a room's opinion in half a degree.

- **This could become:** the UI animation grammar entire — panels, cards, and cursors settle by tilt (toward the playable, the affordable, the newly relevant) rather than sliding, bouncing, or pulsing; hand-cards lean a half-degree toward legal plays; the pack UI tilts toward inbound tellings as the forecast; idle motion exists only as sway on held/hanging objects — so the interface literally attends, and the restraint ban (attend, never act) becomes the product's feel.
- **Visual:** the workshop-as-theater shot as motion reference: a pool of worklight, and in the half-dark dozens of small silhouettes arriving at the same angle by slow degrees — jugs, lasts, a rocking horse — every gold edge pointed at the bench like rows of eyes catching stage light.

#### `named-from-things` *(anchor — declared: dyer's and limner's pigment-naming — madder, ochre, bone-black — is real workshop furniture every artist already owns, kept deliberately familiar so the strange cargo reads at once: Roundelay has NO abstract color language anywhere in its diegesis — every color in the world is the name of a thing in the world, so the palette is itself a tour of the setting.)*

Ask a limner for purple and you will be asked *which* purple — plum, or the slate a wet roof goes? The Rounds name color the way they name everything: from what's to hand. Madder and ember and hearth-red; moth-wing, kingfisher, bottle-green; new-leaf, riverbank, wet-straw, bone, dove, oatmeal; the rose-gold that only means dawn. There is no word on any road for *red itself* — a child asking for it would be handed something red and told what it was. The habit keeps the world's speech concrete and its paintings honest: a palette list reads as a pack inventory, and a pack inventory reads as a poem nobody meant to write.

- **This could become:** the art bible's palette specification AND the in-game text standard in one — swatches shipped under thing-names (a build's colors are auditable as vocabulary: if a hue has no thing-name, it has no place); rarity and grade language never resorts to abstract tiers (no "epic," no color-coded borders — grade reads as material and light, per section 3); accessibility note: because every color is a thing, every color has a shape and a texture synonym, so no read in the game is hue-only.
- **Visual:** the palette bible painted as a limner's fold-out roll: small swatch-cards in loops, each swatch a tiny still-life of its namesake — a madder root, a moth's wing, a curl of new leaf — the game's color script and its world introduced on one page.

#### `the-lantern-dark`

Dark in Roundelay is the warm kind: the rafters' dark where the great askings wait, patient as stored apples; the night-blue street outside an amber doorway; the shadow-brown of an inn's beams; winter's blue window with the fire's gold everywhere else. Nothing hunts in it (the road's dangers are weather and staleness, never teeth), so the eye is taught early that darkness is *where the world keeps what it is not showing you yet* — the unlowered asking, the unwalked road, the season not yet come. The rafters do not menace the dimming maker; they hold her sized work the way an attic holds a wedding coat.

- **This could become:** the UI's treatment of the unavailable and the unknown — locked and out-of-tier content hangs as warm silhouettes in rafters' dark (never greyed-out, never padlocked); fog-of-war is pale pencil on canvas, not black; menus and rest screens live in night-blue-and-amber; the one dark the game never uses is cold black, so even the run's worst evening is painted like a kept house after lamps-out.
- **Visual:** interior grammar plate: hearth-orange, wool-red, shadow-brown, one cold blue window (canon's hearth palette) — beside it the lantern interior: tin and straw silhouettes at eye-height in glow, and above them shapes in the warm dark, present, patient, unlit.

#### `dusk-reads-dawn-gives`

The road's day has two reading lights and every maker keeps their hours. **Dusk is when the world is legible**: gleam shows plainest, lanterns are backlit, stalls string their glow, grain can be read across a square — the hour of arriving, appraising, deciding. **Dawn is when the world gives**: the reissue, loose and unclaimed, sized to the country's patience — the hour of income, and of standing in what the grey saved up. Between them, noon is for work (flat, honest, shadowless worklight; the bench does not flatter) and night is for fires (amber pools in blue; the telling hour). A turn of play — dawn, road, work, fire — is therefore also one full circuit of light, and a maker who has lost track of the season can read the hour off the color of the world like everyone else on the Rounds.

- **This could become:** the game's lighting-and-screen-flow law — approach and preview screens graded dusk (the lantern-mile as the information architecture); income, rewards, and reveals graded dawn; the bench UI in flat worklight; rests and tellings in fire-amber — so the loop's phases are told entirely by grade and the interface needs no clock, calendar strip, or countdown anywhere diegetic (the almanac ban honored by cinematography).
- **Visual:** four thumbnails of one town, one turn: rose-gold gate at dawn; flat bright square at noon; backlit lantern and rimmed figures at dusk; one amber doorway in blue at night — the whole game-feel storyboarded in four grades.

#### `chalk-thread-and-gold` *(lineage, declared: this element spatializes no new furniture — it gathers Layer 3's road-marks, Layer 4's spirals and red thread, and the two golds into one notation standard, stated here so the interface has a single law instead of four habits.)*

The road already drew its own interface, and it uses exactly three marks. **Chalk** is information: spirals, birds, waves, tallies — white, temporary, anyone's, renewed by whoever passes and honest because it was never decreed, only checked. **Red thread** is minding: what hangs an asking, ties a combing, bookmarks one knack against a return — chosen, persistent, one per lane, and never a bond, only a thought kept. **Gold** is waking: the two persistences, earned at the bench or fed by hands, and counterfeit-proof because the world itself does the gilding. Chalk says *know this*; thread says *this is minded*; gold says *this is awake* — and nothing on any gatepost, stall, or pack has ever needed a fourth mark.

- **This could become:** the entire UI iconography standard — every interface mark in the game is drawn as chalk (info layers, map intel, tutorial annotations, tallies-never-digits in diegetic frames), thread (active contracts, pins, bookmarks, the year-string as run history), or gold (waking states, per `the-two-golds`); the HUD becomes unmistakably in-world at zero fiction cost, and any proposed icon that is not chalkable, tieable, or gildable is by law redesigned.
- **Visual:** one gatepost as the UI style sheet: whitewash spiral with a wet outer ring, a chalk bird pointing outward, one madder knot at the lane's elbow, and on the lantern above, tin shapes with their red threads and the town's one gold-edged kettle in a window behind — every interface element the game will ever need, photographed in situ.

## 3. THE NAMING LANGUAGE

#### `the-craft-of-names`

A name on the Rounds is a small made thing: two beats, plain stuff, worn smooth by handing on. The world coins the way it makes — from what's to hand — and its morphology fits in a walking-song: **processes are gerunds wearing the definite article** (the courting, the vouching, the combing, the gleaning, the spilling, the telling, the wintering — and the Paling, see section 5); **made things and practices are two-beat compounds of plain craft-words** (asking-lantern, journey-piece, year-string, glad-load, road-mark, hearth-right, even-morrow, waymeet) — hyphen-joined, Latin-free, with no invented syllables anywhere, because a coined phoneme is a thing no hand ever held; **grades and tiers are read off behavior and place, never counted** (dull, warm, singing; door-nail, low hooks, eye-height, rafters); **people wear hedgerow names** — a plant, craft, or weather word and one short surname (Immy Ash, Sorrel Vane), and no one anywhere wears a title; **places take the land's own suffixes** (-by, -down, -ford, -fell, -lees, -mere, -wold, vale) with workshop-inventory first elements, and **roads are named for their manners**, not their destinations. What is never named at all is its own law (see `the-unchristened`), and the register bans hold in the lexicon itself: the Rounds have no diegetic words for the banned registers — failure's whole vocabulary is ecological (dim, pale, quiet, stale, out of season), and a scan of every proper noun in this layer will find nothing owed, filed, adjudicated, or enforced.

- **This could become:** the content pipeline's naming validator and the writers'-room style card — two beats, plain stuff, gerund-or-compound, grade-by-behavior — applied to every card, node, material, and event name the GDD generates, so procedurally assembled names (town generators, card upgrades) stay in-register by construction rather than by review.
- **Visual:** names as lettering: a workman's round hand in iron-gall ink, chalk caps on signposts, stitched samplers for the way-songs — never blackletter, never cursive flourish; the letterforms themselves plain stuff, worn smooth.

**Sample names, a working set:**

*A starter hand of journey-pieces* (cards are named as the thing in the loop — plain noun phrase, one adjective at most, "of the" only when it names ground):
1. **First Luck-Bird** (whittling — the handsel faucet)
2. **Three Bars of a Planing-Song** (joinery/song)
3. **Weather-Talk** (clay courting — you tell it about the weather)
4. **A Knot of Bright Thread** (thread)
5. **Kiln-Chip** (glaze — a whole kiln's wisdom in one crackle)
6. **The Quarter-Turn** (audience — the chairs decide to listen)
7. **Dough-Mother** (dough — a patience passed hand to hand)
8. **Ribbon of the Turn** (dance, kept in ribbon)
9. **Hem-Stitch** (thread — the hem does the keeping-warm)
10. **The Folded Joke** (paper — exhaust: unfolding it tells it, once)

*Early acquisitions beyond the starter, for register:* **The Pour-Song** (singing-silver, one song first heat to last pour); **A Jar Already Friendly** (an introduction-jar, taught by a Mannerly).

*Towns* (workshop-inventory + land-suffix): **Kettleby, Swallowdown, Chalkford, Emberlees, Withyfold, Braidmere, Wickworth, Dovewold** — sitting comfortably beside canon's Ferrow, Merrylees, Whistledown, Setterby, Hask, Brindle-on-the-Wold, Quill Vale, Wray Fell, Wader's Ford.

*Roads* (named for manners): **the Blackberry Mile** (generous on the return leg), **the Dawnside Road** (a Gleaner stretch of sky, walked sunwise), **the Two-Sheds Road** (a crown-path with the long sight), **the Long Dapple** (hedge-shade the whole way), and canon's **Wold Road**, broad and sociable.

*Material grades* (the ladder named by manner of asking, never by number): **apprentice-stuffs** (canon — chatter to anyone) → **the fair-spoken** (local characters with easy terms: bell-bronze, hearth-slate) → **the proud** (vouching first, always: singing-silver, laughing-riverwood, festival-glass) → **the light-led** (rare by where light has been, never by what ground withholds: last-red, the twice-benched, festival-glass again from its other side). Handsel grades stay canon's own and perfect: **dull, warm, singing.**

*Asking tiers* (named by lantern anatomy — height IS tier): **door-nail work** (a household's supper-grade need, no lantern, no reading) → **the low hooks** (kettle-size) → **eye-height** (a journeyman's season) → **rafters-work** (canon — the great readings, waiting) → and past any town's crafting, **work the size of weather** (the folk phrase for a great asking, which is otherwise named only by its ground: *the Wray Fell asking*, never anything grander).

#### `the-unchristened`

No thing in Roundelay bears a person's name. The oldest woken kettle in a rafters is *the house's kettle*; the disputed bell is *the ferry-bell at Hask*; a masterwork is *the well-ring at Kettleby* to everyone including its maker, whose mark is a thumbprint in the glaze and a signature bar in the hum, never letters. Christening an object — giving it a small name of its own, as if it had a self to answer to — is how sentimental strangers get the world wrong twice in one word: things have *use*, not selves; they attend, and attending needs no name to do it. Introducing your plane by name would embarrass a Roundelay workshop the way introducing your hammer would embarrass anyone. The practice runs deeper than manners: a name is regard aimed at a *who*, and aiming who-regard at a what is exactly the sentimentality the waking law is precise against — the reason the canon riddle says *watching gives the thing its eyes*, and never a voice. Great askings likewise take only their ground's name; ways are never anyone's surname; and the unmarked wonders, whose whole mystery is a swallowed signature, are the law made monument: the world's finest things are the ones attribution cannot reach.

- **This could become:** the item-text and relic-naming guardrail — no legendary items with personal names, no "Berta the Kettle" NPCs-as-objects, no named weapons register anywhere in the pool; relic-class unmarked cards are named by ground and behavior (*the Setterby Chip*, *a Stair-Worn Piece*), which quietly teaches the long riddle every time the collection screen is read; flavor text may quote a maker but never christen a thing, keeping the anti-twee line enforceable at the copy-edit level.
- **Visual:** a museum-of-none: the collection screen as a porch shelf of unlabelled, ground-named things, each with its chalk tag in the road's shorthand — a place-word and a work-word — and not one nameplate anywhere.

## 4. THE CARD-FLAVOR VOICE

#### `the-round-book` *(anchor — declared: the journeyman's notebook is folk furniture every player owns — the field diary, the recipe margin, the trail log — kept deliberately familiar so the strange cargo reads instantly: the game's entire text surface is ONE practice — many hands, quoting each other, hearth to hearth — so flavor text is never a narrator and never lore; it is craft-advice with provenance, the world speaking as it actually speaks.)*

Every maker keeps a round-book: part manners-notes (what this valley's clay likes), part tally kept for the joy of counting, part draft of the winter-telling, inked at the same fires as the map. Round-books are lent, copied, argued with in the margins, and quoted at benches three ways from where they were written; the Mannerly's great notebooks are round-books grown communal, and the light-followers' four-word findings live in everyone's. So when a card speaks, it speaks as an entry — somebody's hand, somewhere on the road, telling you the practice — and it says whose: a round-book, porch-talk, a walking-song, a finding, a line chalked on a stall-board. The voice's rules are the road's: craft-advice in the imperative or the plain observation; concrete nouns, two clauses, full stops; the world never explains its physics, it hands you the practice and lets the windowsill do the rest; wonder arrives as understatement — the strange thing said the plain way; humor is dry and kind, never winking; no exclamation marks, no archaism, and nothing speaks that should only attend.

- **This could become:** the flavor-text system with provenance as a mechanic-adjacent surface — every card carries an entry plus an attribution line, and attributions quietly do work (way-affinity reads as whose books you're quoting; the tutorial is the player's own round-book filling in, per Layer 2's `the-noticing-game`; the end-of-run review is the winter-telling read bead by bead in the same voice) — one register from first tooltip to final screen.
- **Visual:** card text set as an inked entry on the card's canvas — round workman's hand, iron-gall brown, the attribution smaller and set off by a long dash; here and there a marginal correction in a second hand, because a round-book that was never argued with was never lent.

**Sample flavor lines (the voice, demonstrated):**

1. *Weather-Talk* — "Tell the clay about the weather. It has been under it all year."
2. *The Pour-Song* — "One song, first heat to last pour. Sing like you mean to finish; the silver can tell."
3. *First Luck-Bird* — "Every coin was somebody's glad afternoon. Keep it moving."
4. *The Folded Joke* — "Worth one telling. Keep it for a cold room."
5. *The Quarter-Turn* — "You will know the room is yours by the chairs. — a Fairwright's round-book, copied everywhere"
6. A rafters asking — "The rafters are not empty. They are patient. — porch-talk at Merrylees"
7. A dawn card — "Pale country gets the best mornings. Arrange to be standing in one. — a Morning-Gleaner's round-book, past the last hearth"
8. An inbound-telling tick — "You've light on the road from Wader's Ford. Dusk after next, I'd say."

Each line is wonder-by-understatement, zero twee: no talking objects, no coziness adjectives, no exclamation, and every one of them is *advice* — the register of a world that teaches rather than narrates.

## 5. RENAMES — SANCTIONED FLOW, EXECUTED

### I-002 · 'the Fade' → **the Paling**

*Candidates weighed, all in the world's own register:*

1. **the Paling** — canon already half-coined it (pale towns, the paling-arithmetic, "a town three seasons without a maker is called pale"); a gerund with the definite article, exactly the world's process-morphology (the courting, the combing, the gleaning); and the hedger's pun is free and beloved — a paling is also a fence-stake, so the road can say *the Paling is what the world's patience is fenced with*.
2. **the Seep** — canon's own mechanism-word; honest, but it names the plumbing rather than the weather, and the layer needs the weather.
3. **the Out-Breath** — the cosmology's tenderest figure; kept as the Paling's poetic register ("the out-breath" survives as its folk synonym), but as a primary name it over-explains.
4. **the Greying** — right morphology, wrong precision: canon is exact that grey is the symptom and forgetting the disease.
5. **the Wane** — a moon-word in a sun-and-hearth cosmology; pretty, unearned.

**Pick: the Paling.** It is the only candidate the world has already been saying. Execution across canon (terms.json: `the-paling`, `renamed_from: the-fade`):

- "the Fade" → **the Paling** at every diegetic occurrence, Layers 1–5 (L1 theme 4 becomes "Renewal against the Paling"; the grammar-of-light law reads "Places pale. The Paling takes knack and color from scenery…"; element id `the-fade` → `the-paling`).
- Derivatives: "Fade-prone" → **paling-prone**; "the Fade's tide" → **the Paling's tide**; "Fade-grey" (channel ban) → **paling-grey**; "Fade rate" (non-diegetic hooks) → **paling rate**.
- Untouched, because already in-register: pale towns, the paling-arithmetic, "saving up," the out-breath (kept as folk synonym), the seep (kept as mechanism-word).
- Canon-scan tokens for the pipeline: `Fade`, `fade` (case-checked against legitimate uses: "places fade" may stand as plain verb where it names the channel — recommend keeping the verb *to pale* for scenery to make the scan trivial: "places pale, people gleam, things wake," which is also simply better).

#### `the-paling`

The world exhales, and the road's word for the exhale is the Paling — kin to the pale towns that wait through it, to the paling-arithmetic chalked in spirals on their gateposts, and, the hedgers insist, to the fence-stake, because patience needs fencing and the Paling is what the world's patience is fenced with. Nobody fights it and nobody hates it; it is the tide the Rounds were built to answer, and its visual identity is the tenderest desaturation in games: the reverse vignette — grey entering at the rooftops and the scene's edges, draining inward, while the hearth-line ember holds last of all, one coal of color the living re-bind nightly just by gathering near it. Paling greys are dove, ash-rose, bone, oatmeal — undyed-wool greys, the Gleaners' own cloth — and they are never dirty and never dark, because the Paling takes the *trick* of things first and leaves their dignity; a pale town in silverpoint with one lit coal is among the most beautiful frames the game owns, which is the register doing its deepest work: the pressure clock is painted as patience, not as rot. What "fade" would have called loss, the Paling's whole picture-language calls *saving up* — and the immense rose-gold dawn over a pale floor is the same picture, the morning after.

- **This could become:** the pressure system's complete visual identity — node paling as reverse-vignette state layers (rooftops → walls → lanes → the held hearth-line), spiral-count as the diegetic difficulty read, and the paired payoff (grey evening, red dawn) as the reward telegraphing — so escalation and income read as one weather system in one palette, no meters anywhere.
- **Visual:** the four-state paling plate of one town: kept (full color, ordinary dawn); one ring (grey at the rooftops); five rings (silverpoint, the ember-line, an immense dawn behind the ridge); answered (watercolor blooming back across wet paper, strokes visible, children out watching it happen — the one lawful sight of the wash in motion).
