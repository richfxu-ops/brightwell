# Layer 4 Proposal B — Flows and Fairs: The Traffic of Light

*Proposer lens: FLOWS AND OCCASIONS. The economy of Roundelay is not a list of stuffs; it is a circulation you can stand inside. This proposal is written the way a lantern-keeper would explain money to a child: by following one bright thing all the way around.*

---

## The economy in one breath

Nothing in Roundelay is scarce the way ore is scarce. Matter is ordinary and everywhere; straw and clay chatter to anyone; the world's one conserved stuff — regard — falls free on every eye each dawn. What is scarce is scarce the way a *good hour* is scarce:

- **A morning can only be stood in once.** The dawn-draw is the day's whole income of working attention, and no maker holds more than a day of it.
- **A bright coin must keep moving.** Handsels are worth what they are awake, and the held waking must be fed — wealth in Roundelay is a juggling, never a pile.
- **Proud stuff consents, and consent is gated by shine.** The finest materials will not so much as state their terms to hands the world is not currently holding in mind.
- **A crowd is an occasion, not a faucet.** Deep waking needs gathered watchers, and watchers gather at fairs, hearths, fair-answers, and red-thread days — the calendar is a resource.
- **A season only ripens once.** Askings warm, then stale; pale towns compound on the gatepost spirals; the Round-Fair is always a week ahead of autumn and will not wait.

So the maker's craft-economy is a *timing* economy: attention, coin, consent, crowd, and season, all of them flows, none of them hoardable, every one of them beautiful to look at. Nothing anywhere in it is owed. Prices are read, not enforced — and the whole apparatus of enforcement is replaced by one law every child has tested on a windowsill: **what stops moving, dims.** A miser is not punished; a miser is simply a person slowly turning money back into buttons.

## Named elements

### 1. `the-dawn-draw`

The day's income arrives before the towns wake. Makers on the road rise into first light and simply *attend* it — breakfast taken facing east on a stile, tools laid out on a wall, ten minutes of doing nothing with the whole body — because a living creature gathers loose regard through itself, and dawn is the reissue at its most gatherable, before ten thousand town-eyes begin aiming it elsewhere. Pale country pours the richest draws (its gloaming-table is fullest), which is why Morning-Gleaners walk into the grey on purpose and why an ordinary maker crossing faded hills wakes up already half paid for the risk. The draw cannot be banked — living light is a current, never a pool — so a wise day is planned at sunrise, around what the sunrise gave.

- **This could become:** the per-turn attention income, exactly as canon seeds it — flat universal base + local-table share, granted at turn start as "the morning"; unspent attention returns loose at end of turn and comes back in tomorrow's local draw. Route valuation becomes dawn-forecasting.
- **Visual:** a maker motionless on a stile at the grey-gold hinge of the day, steam rising from a tin cup, the valley's color coming up like watercolor flooding a wet page — over kept country a brief ordinary blush, over pale country an immense rose-gold spill that takes minutes to cross the frame. The player's income animation *is* the weather.

### 2. `apprentice-stuff`

The gregarious materials — straw, dough, river clay, withy, tallow, chalk, patch-wool — that answer anyone, dim or bright, and never state a term more demanding than *use me while I'm fresh*. They are the floor of the whole economy: every child's first conversation with the world, every dimmed maker's road back, and every journeyman's daily bread-work between great commissions. Nothing made of them ever astonishes, and everything made of them serves, and the Rounds run on that: kettle askings, mended latches, festival dough-birds, and the blanks from which handsels are whittled.

- **This could become:** the ungated base resource — always available at every node, zero acquisition condition, low waking ceiling. The floor that makes Standing-loss survivable (at zero gleam, apprentice-stuff still answers) and the raw stock of handsel-crafting.
- **Visual:** sociable ambers and oat-golds; open bins and warm clutter; clay that ripples faintly toward any passing hand like a dog's glance, straw that leans into being picked up. The one shelf in the market that is never behind anything.

### 3. `the-proud-stock`

The shy materials, met as an economy: sung silver, the green riverwood that opens only for the laughing, festival glass that clouds without an audience — and with them bell-bronze, which will only pour while a crowd holds one breath, and hearth-slate, quarried sweetest while a story is being told over the work. Two gates, in strict order, and the whole market knows them: first *consent to negotiate*, which is gleam's business and no coin's; then *terms stated must be met*, and the terms are the material's own — none of it speech, all of it read in behavior and temperament, the way a cat's terms are read. Courted proud stock travels in cradles and introduction-jars, and a jar already courted — carried to you by a Mannerly, or pressed on you in a glad-load — is among the most valuable things a pack can hold, because it collapses weeks of courtship into a gift.

- **This could become:** gated high-power resources: Standing widens which materials will state terms this run (gate one, never a spend), and each stated term is a legible micro-condition — sing first, keep the chain unbroken, hold an audience token, work at dusk (gate two). Introduction-jars are tradeable acquisition-skips.
- **Visual:** body-language stock: ingots that lean away or lean in, jars whose contents visibly perk when a bright maker enters, silver with a held-breath stillness until it is sung to. Temperament palette — shy blues, vain iridescents, the deep green of wood deciding whether you're funny.

### 4. `bright-shavings`

Great work leaves warm leavings. When proud stock is worked before a gathered room, its offcuts — silver curls, glass drops, slate flakes, the knot-ends of a woven commission — come off the bench carrying a residue of the room's regard: not fired (an offcut has no use for the light to set into), only *held*, a faint sheen that will lapse in a season if no one delights in it. So makers keep a shavings-jar, and the Rounds have a use for warm leavings that is nearly as old as benches: they are the blank-stock of handsels and small journey-pieces, luck already half awake before the whittling starts. The finest coins in circulation began as the floor-sweepings of famous work, and everyone who receives one is told so, which is half of why they stay bright.

- **This could become:** a crafting byproduct resource — big attended plays yield shaving tokens, convertible at rests and fairs into fresh handsels or minor pieces; a lawful bridge from the bench economy into the money and deck economies.
- **Visual:** a squat glass jar in the pack's side-loop, glowing faintly like banked embers — curls of silver, beads of glass, a ribbon-end — each with the low breathing sheen of held light, never the crisp gold edge of the fired.

### 5. `the-open-purse`

Nobody on the Rounds carries money shut away, because a strongbox is where shine goes to seep. Makers' purses are open-work by ancient craft-sense — net-purses, bandolier loops, coins strung visibly at the belt — so that every handsel rides the day in the world's attending: glanced at by market crowds, chimed by walking, admired at hearths. A coin in an open purse holds its waking; a coin in a shut box dulls in a season, its lapsed light seeping downhill to the gloaming-table like any unregarded wash — nothing taken, nothing lost, next-morning light for somebody. Purse-craft is real craft: a well-made net-purse is itself usually woken, and the sound of a bright maker walking — that faint rain-on-bells — is the sound of their savings staying alive.

- **This could become:** the currency container as a design surface — a visible wallet with a soft cap (loops, not a number), idle dimming per rest for coins that didn't move, and purse upgrades as fired artifacts that slow the lapse. Anti-hoard pressure worn as costume, not rulebook.
- **Visual:** close-up of a hip: netted coins in graded glow swaying against worn leather, chiming; beside it, in a dim interior, the counter-image — a strongbox ajar, its coins matte as buttons, and the floorboards beneath it just perceptibly warmer in color than the rest of the room.

### 6. `the-glow-price` *(anchor — declared: the market stall with marked prices is furniture, kept deliberately familiar — rails of goods, a keeper, a cost you can see — so that its three strange cargoes read at a glance: the price is a GLOW, not a number; change comes back BRIGHTER when the buying delights; and nothing anywhere enforces any of it.)*

A stall on the Rounds names its price by hanging a handsel of the asking brightness on the rail: this basket is a *warm* basket; that courted jar of sung silver is a *singing* jar. Any coin bright enough serves, and the reading is public — a child can price the whole fair by ear. What keeps it honest is not a keeper's eye but the physics of delight: pay meanly and the trade moves nothing, and unmoved things dim; pay *well* — with a story, with the coin's own history told, with the little ceremony of a handsel handed rather than dropped — and the stall-keeper's pleasure wakes the change, so that what comes back to your purse comes back a shade brighter than the price strictly asked. Buying beautifully is, mildly and lawfully, profitable, and every market on the Rounds therefore sounds like a party, because the economy pays people to delight each other and has no other police.

- **This could become:** shop pricing in wakefulness denominations (dull / warm / singing) with a spend-into-delight kicker: purchases made under attention (chained plays, audience present, a telling attached) refund brighter change — tempo and money braided into one decision.
- **Visual:** stall-fronts as rails of small tokens dull-to-singing, the price-handsel hung at eye height like a tuning fork for the whole stall; a bargain painted close-up as two hands and one coin visibly warming between them at the moment of the handing.

### 7. `the-glad-load`

No town pays a maker, and no maker would know what to do with a wage; what happens instead cannot be helped, the same way a fed traveler cannot help telling. A town whose asking is answered *delights*, and delighted hands give: they press the maker's pack full at the gate — handsels rung bright that very evening by the whole square's glad palms, a journey-piece taught by whoever the town loves best, an introduction-jar of the local proud stuff already courted, bread and wax and wool, and road-marks chalked a day's walk ahead so the next hills already know. The glad-load scales with the gladness, and the gladness scales with the need: a three-ring town loads a pack, a five-ring town half-fills a cart, and what follows a great asking is less a load than a season. It is the single richest flow in a maker's economy, and not one dram of it was ever demanded.

- **This could become:** the contract-payoff bundle — reward scaling read directly off the gatepost spiral (rings in = load out), paid across every sub-economy at once: bright handsels, one taught card, one material introduction, map-intel chalked outward from the town. Skipped-town compounding thus raises reward and difficulty in the same visible number.
- **Visual:** the gate at dawn, the whole town out, a pack being loaded past sensible fullness — small hands pressing tokens into side-loops, an elder tying an introduction-jar with red-threaded knots — the maker lit warm, laughing, trying to refuse and failing, the load itself faintly glowing at the seams.

### 8. `the-waymeet-table`

Where the great circuits cross, on ground no town keeps, there is always a plank table under the fingerboard sign, and on it: whatever the last hundred walkers chose to leave. The custom needs no rule because the physics writes it — anything you are not using is dimming on you, so the rational fate of an idle piece, a spare jar, a coin gone quiet, is to be *given*, and the table is where the road's whole liquidity pools. You take what you will keep bright and leave what you won't; a thing taken from the table arrives already storied ("this whistle has crossed the country twice"), which is a head start on waking; and nobody has ever kept accounts on a waymeet table, because there is nothing to keep — the table is never owed anything, only fed, the way a fire is fed.

- **This could become:** free swap nodes at circuit crossings — leave-a-card/take-a-card, resource conversion without a vendor, deck-thinning as gifting (with the gifted thing's small blessing following the giver a leg down the road). The road's answer to a shop with no shopkeeper anywhere in it.
- **Visual:** a weathered plank table under a many-fingered signpost at a crossroads, odd silhouettes in low sun — a whistle, a jar, three strung coins, one folded paper bird — every object wearing a different history of sheen, and every walker in the frame rearranging it slightly in passing.

### 9. `the-tellings-worth`

Hearth-right is free — one night's warmth, one meal, nothing asked — and yet the fireside is one of the great markets of the Rounds, because what moves there moves in the only coin a fed and firelit traveler cannot keep: the telling. The story told at the fire is how a house learns the world, and houses grow rich on it — waking sooner, standing longer against the Fade — so hosts compete in kindness for reasons the kindness entirely conceals. And the teller is paid too, in the world's own way: the event retold is regard renewed, a cup refilled and run over again to its pourer; a maker who tells *well* of their own road feels the night warm exactly the deed they chose to keep alive. Selection, never expenditure: the run's past is an instrument the fireside teaches you to play.

- **This could become:** the rest-node economy as canon seeds it — rest and healing free by right; the player picks one event from the run's history to tell, and that event's tag becomes the night's boon (upgrade, relationship, small blessing), while the house's own waking quietly compounds the node for future runs.
- **Visual:** the doorway shot canon owns — night-blue street, amber interior — carried one beat further: around the teller mid-story, every woken thing in the room tilted one degree closer, and on the hearth-stone, next morning, a bloom of color the width of a story.

### 10. `spill-boons`

When work dies in front of a crowd, the slackened gleam slides off where the maker stands and soaks loose into the flagstones — and the Rounds, being the Rounds, have a warm name for what it leaves: spill-boons, the small unowned brightenings a later walker finds in a lane that grief painted. But every maker learns the steepness of that trade the first time they die on a stage, and the principle is stated on every quiet-porch on the circuit: **the lane keeps the color, never the cause.** Spilled light is loose light — its lines of cause gone slack — and loose light on a place is the weakest holding in the world: a passer-by gathers from it what a morning-glance gathers, a sip, never a cup, and none of it will ever again answer to the one who spilled it. What a square forgets of you is worth incomparably more on your skin than in its stones; no maker has ever painted their way bright by falling, and the old toast — *to the lanes we've painted* — is drunk precisely because everyone at the table knows it.

- **This could become:** the Standing-loss compost — each gleam point lost re-colors the node as ambient micro-boons, claimable only by later visitors and future runs, sized well below a Standing point's marginal run value (**I-012, answered:** the in-fiction law is *sip-never-cup, and never for the spiller* — a place-wash holding is categorically the weakest in the cosmology, so the boon's ceiling sits far under the gleam's worth by physics, not by tuning; exact numbers are game-architect's, with a napkin-sim check that deliberate failure is never worth it).
- **Visual:** the alley behind the fair-stage, generations deep in accidental watercolor — heartbreak pigment, lovelier than anything painted on purpose — and a next-morning walker pausing in it, warmed a little, not knowing by whom.

### 11. `the-walking-light`

Overflow travels at a hearth's pace, and the Rounds can *see it coming* (**I-015, answered**). Delight earned three towns back walks the hearth-lines as tellings, and the murmur-net runs just ahead of the light itself: woken things murmur what the hearths have heard, so a maker's own pack turns weathervane — pieces tilting, by slow degrees and nothing more, toward the road a tale is walking — and porch elders will glance at a bright pack and say, comfortably, "you've light on the road from Wader's Ford; dusk after next, I'd say." The landing is therefore never a surprise and always an occasion: the shine arrives at dusk, source-known ("the Ford still tells of the reel"), and only *then* does any lantern read it, because a lantern reads hands, not rumors of hands — what is in transit is visible to everyone and counted by no one until it lands.

- **This could become:** the delayed-Standing UI contract, in-fiction: incoming gleam ticks visible on the map in transit (the tale walking, source-stamped, with an arrival-dusk estimate read from pack-tilt and porch-talk); peak-Standing and lantern sizings update only on landing, and the next asking-tier silhouette previews in the rafters before any accept decision — the ratchet never fires off-screen.
- **Visual:** dusk camp: every piece in an open bandolier tilted the same way, like grass under one wind, and the maker looking back down that road with the particular smile of someone whose good deed is out walking; the landing itself painted as knuckles taking dusk-light a shade early.

### 12. `the-stilled-country`

Rarely — more rarely than a great asking; the Rounds count stillings in lifetimes, not seasons, and a walker may cross one in a whole walking life or never — a stretch of country simply stops breathing (**I-014, answered**). The Fade stills, as it did in Quill Vale forty years back; nothing pales, nothing re-colors, the dawns come grey and thin because the light has nowhere to return from, and the light-followers note, carefully, that every remembered stilling lay near a near-closed wonder, and say no more than that. A maker inside a stilled country works in held breath: no morning worth rising for, no spiral turning on any gatepost, boons staling on the branch — the one landscape in Roundelay that teaches, by absence, why the Fade is the friend. And when it clears — always at a turning of the season, never predictably which — the held-back reissue returns all at once: a Quill-Vale Morning, a season of dawns poured into one, the richest single draw a maker will ever stand in, worth the crossing's whole grey purse and a little more, and not a dram beyond what the stillness withheld.

- **This could become:** a rare route anomaly (roughly once per several runs): zero dawn income and zero paling pressure while active, staling boons, then a release-burst dawn on clearing sized as compensation-plus-a-little, never as profit — the detour teaches the master loop by turning it off.
- **Visual:** waxy, over-bright, unripening color — fruit-bowl colors with no air in them — under thin grey mornings, birds singing confused at noon; then the clearing: the vastest rose-gold dawn in the game's art, walkers arriving from three towns away to stand in it.

### 13. `the-fresh-verse`

Between one maker's autumn and the next maker's spring, the world runs its long forgetting — hearths stop carrying last decade's tales, shines thin in grey retirements, lanterns meet hands they have never read — and this proposal rules plainly what that means for the game (**I-013, answered: clamped**): *every re-spark is a fresh maker, walking a fresh verse.* The song keeps the verse and forgets the singer; a run is one turn of a Round, shorter by far than the forgetting's clock, so no decay is ever played — the long forgetting is not a meter but a *door*, the reason there is always room on the road for new hands, the reason every lantern's rafters are patient and every dawn is unclaimed. (The cosmology's lawful continuation — a maker returned out of the Quiet Walk to lanterns that remember her fired sizings — stays on the shelf as a possible named high-stakes mode for game-architect; the base game clamps, because the economy's meta-scale dawn is precisely that each new maker arrives unread.)

- **This could become:** the meta-layer rule: no between-runs Standing or reputation decay to manage — each run starts a fresh maker at baseline gleam, inheriting only what physics lets the world keep: woken works, porch elders, road-marks, spill-boons, and the annotated country every past run painted.
- **Visual:** the same circuit map across two runs — the line of the old walk faded to pale pencil, its marginalia (a bird, a kettle, a face) still faintly inked, and a new bright line beginning at the guild-town in spring, crossing its own predecessor's ghost at the first stile.

## The systems map

Six interacting systems. Every arrow below is traceable in the element fictions above (and in locked canon where noted). Each system ends with what an artist paints.

**S1 — The Round of Light (master circulation).**
`gloaming-table` -> reissues as -> `the-dawn-draw` (turn income) -> spent at the bench on asking-work -> splits three ways by conservation: **sets** into the work (fired waking; the card wakes) + **overflows** to the maker (gleam / Standing, sometimes delayed via `the-walking-light`) + **returns loose** to the node (re-coloring); unspent surplus and all loose wash -> seep downhill -> `gloaming-table` -> richer local dawns.
*Paint it:* the schoolroom chalk-circle (sun, eye, hand, bowl, roof, pool, sun), animated by the game's actual light-flows.

**S2 — The handsel round (money).**
`apprentice-stuff` + `bright-shavings` -> whittled into -> fresh handsels (near-dull: anyone can make money; nobody can make it worth anything alone) -> woken by circulation through `the-glow-price`, `the-glad-load`, `the-tellings-worth`, `the-waymeet-table` (each glad handing brightens; delighted buying returns change a shade brighter) -> idle coins in violation of `the-open-purse` -> dim -> lapsed shine seeps to -> `gloaming-table` -> tomorrow's `the-dawn-draw`. Money's sink is literally the income system.
*Paint it:* a coin's biography in five vignettes — floor-sweepings, whittling knife, market palm, netted purse at a hip, and (the cautionary panel) a strongbox with a warm floor.

**S3 — The courtship ladder (materials).**
`apprentice-stuff` work + performances -> delight -> gleam rises -> gate one opens: `the-proud-stock` states terms -> terms met by performing journey-pieces before audiences (gate two) -> proud material worked into asking-answers -> offcuts return as -> `bright-shavings` (-> S2), while the finished work fires (-> S1's setting) and its overflow feeds gleam back to the top of the ladder. Introduction-jars (via `the-glad-load` and Mannerly lending) short-circuit gate two as gifts.
*Paint it:* the shelf of jars perking as a bright maker walks in; the two gates as one image — silver silent before a dim hand, singing-still before a lit one.

**S4 — The asking cycle (contracts).**
The Fade pales towns -> `the-paling-arithmetic` compounds skipped need (canon) -> lanterns size by fired peak-readings and show by tonight's gleam (canon; previews guaranteed by `the-walking-light`) -> answered askings -> node re-colors (-> S1) + `the-glad-load` pays across every other system (handsels -> S2, taught piece -> S5, introduction-jar -> S3, road-marks -> map intel) + overflow gleam raises the next sizing; stale or flubbed askings -> gleam spills -> `spill-boons` -> the node keeps the color (-> S6); seven rings unanswered -> the combing -> a great asking (canon boss tier).
*Paint it:* one gatepost spiral, ring by widening ring, and the same town painted at three, five, and seven rings — plea, poem, weather.

**S5 — The pack economy (pieces / the deck).**
Pieces enter the pack by teaching, winning, gifting: `the-glad-load`, fairs and fair-answers, season-shadowing's parting gift (canon), `the-waymeet-table`, porch elders -> pieces are performed (spending dawn-draw attention, satisfying proud-stock terms, delighting audiences -> gleam) -> some exhaust — performed to destruction, the paper-bird pattern — their last telling running full to the pourer -> some are given away (waymeet table, partings), thinning the pack and warming the road behind; retired and Fade-prone pieces -> Evener last lightings -> their held wash returns to the `gloaming-table` -> richer dawns on this very route (-> S1).
*Paint it:* the bandolier open on tool-roll leather, one loop conspicuously empty, a paper bird unfolding in firelight for the only time.

**S6 — The compost loop (failure and retirement).**
Flat performances and stale askings -> gleam slides off -> lanes found brighter -> `spill-boons` for later walkers and future runs (sip-never-cup, never for the spiller) -> claimed boons feed early-leg economies of the *next* maker (-> S2/S5 small starts), while unclaimed wash seeps -> `gloaming-table` (-> S1); deliberate retirement runs the same pipe on purpose — last lightings converting the worn-out into a season of richer valley dawns; `the-stilled-country` is this loop clamped shut, and the Quill-Vale Morning is its release valve; `the-fresh-verse` is the same composting at meta-scale: the world clearing its rafters so every new maker arrives unread.
*Paint it:* the slumped fiddler and the brightening alley in one frame; forty years later, a stranger warming their hands in it.

Every system drains into S1 and is fed by it: the economy has one water table, and the game's "where it went" readout is the cosmology's first law wearing a UI.

## The pack, leg by leg

*(The circulation diagram, lived from inside.)*

**First leg — spring, kept country.** A light bandolier: six or eight apprentice-tier pieces, a net-purse of mostly-dull handsels, a lump of river clay, chalk, an empty shavings-jar. Income is the thin ordinary dawn of well-kept country plus whatever spill-boons past walkers left in the lanes. The lantern shows kettles. Everything you make, you make of what chatters.

**Second leg — the engine turns.** First glad-loads: a taught piece in the third loop, an introduction-jar riding the pack's crown, coins that chime now when you walk. Gleam has opened two proud terms; the shavings-jar glows; the fair is two towns ahead and you are routing to catch it; at dusk the pack's pieces all tilt toward the road behind you, where something you did is still being told.

**Third leg — abundance under pressure.** The purse sings and cannot be allowed to sit; the rafters have lowered work that needs bell-bronze and a crowd; you are choosing pale roads on purpose for their enormous mornings and reading gateposts like an almanac, because two towns you skipped in spring are at five rings and one country westward is quietly gathering toward seven. Everything you own is bright, and nothing you own will stay bright unless it moves — the late run is rich the way a juggler is rich: only mid-air.

## The run's economy arc, diegetically

Early scarcity is *shyness*: the world has not heard of you, so only the gregarious floor answers, and the dawns over kept country are polite and small. The mid-run engine is *circulation achieved*: glad-loads feeding purse, purse feeding courtship, courtship feeding the bench, the bench overflowing onto your knuckles, and every flow visibly braided at the fair. Late abundance-under-pressure is the ratchet felt as honor: sized rafters that never lower their reading, coins that must keep moving, ripened five-ring poems on every side road, and a combing gathering where your spring shortcuts used to be — wealth as weather you must keep walking through, never a pile you could sit on. And the end, either way, composts: an autumned run spills and seeds the lanes; a triumphant one hands the verse on mid-song.

## Carried obligations — summary of answers

- **I-012 (spill-boon ceiling):** answered in `spill-boons`. In-fiction law: *the lane keeps the color, never the cause; a sip, never a cup, and never for the spiller.* Place-wash is the cosmology's weakest holding, so boon value sits categorically below any Standing point's marginal worth — by physics, not tuning. Exact numbers deferred to game-architect with a napkin-sim check that deliberate failure is never EV-positive.
- **I-013 (between-runs forgetting):** answered in `the-fresh-verse`. **Clamped**: every re-spark is a fresh maker; runs are shorter than the forgetting's clock, so no meta-decay is ever played. The long forgetting's mechanical home is the *reset itself* — unread lanterns, baseline gleam, an unclaimed world. The remembered-maker continuation stays shelved as an optional named mode for game-architect.
- **I-014 (stilled regions):** answered in `the-stilled-country`. Rarity: counted in lifetimes, not seasons — roughly once per several runs; zero income and zero pressure while active; the release burst is a Quill-Vale Morning sized as the stillness's withheld dawns returned — compensation plus a little, never profit.
- **I-015 (delayed gleam visibility):** answered in `the-walking-light`. Incoming light is readable in transit — murmur-net, pack-tilt, porch-talk, source-stamped with arrival estimates — and lanterns read only landed light, with next-tier silhouettes previewing before any accept. The ratchet never fires unpredictably.
- **I-016 (held vs fired art signatures):** deferred to Layer 6 as assigned; this layer feeds it and does not contradict it — throughout the above, fired things carry the static gold working-edge and held things (handsels, shavings, glad-load glow) carry a full-surface breathing sheen, never a crisp line.

## Anchor declaration (this layer)

One anchor, declared inline at `the-glow-price`: the market stall with visible prices is deliberately familiar furniture, kept so that its novel cargo reads in one glance — **price as glow rather than number, change returned brighter when the buying delights, and no enforcement existing anywhere in the world**. Anchor share this layer: 1 of 13 elements. (`the-waymeet-table` is defused in its own text: it is a shop with no shopkeeper *and no accounts*, which no player has furniture for.)

## Register check

Nothing above is owed, promised, filed, or enforced; failure composts and never fines; Standing gates and is never spent; materials behave and never speak; woken things tilt and never act; prices are read, not enforced; and every flow in the layer can be painted — most of them at dawn.
