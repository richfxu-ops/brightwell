# Layer 4 proposal A — material-catalogue

## THE MAKINGS AND THE MEANS — the material economy of Roundelay

**Lens:** lead with the substances. Every named stuff below is a station in the Round of Light — a particular way regard is held, moved, or given back — and every scarcity is lawful, felt, and paintable at a fair stall.

---

### The market's grammar — what can be traded at all

The three settlings and the one binding sort everything a maker will ever handle into four classes, and only three of them can change hands:

- **The current cannot be traded.** Living attention is a current, never a pool; nobody has ever sold a morning's regard, only earned it by delighting. This is the quiet floor under the whole economy: the one thing everything else is made of has no stall, no jar, and no substitute — you gather it by being worth watching, or not at all.
- **The loose is courted.** Raw stuff — clay, silver, riverwood, glass-sand, pigment — carries the wash of wherever it lay, and it changes hands the way it leaves the ground: by manners, met on its own terms.
- **The set is given and taught.** Works and journey-pieces carry fired regard; they move by gift, teaching, trading, and winning, and their worth is the knack they hold.
- **The held is the money.** Handsels carry loose light bound by circulating delight; their worth is how awake they are, and awake is a thing anyone can hear.

And one law over all the stalls, the first thing a master tells an apprentice at their first fair: **scarcity in Roundelay is manners, not tonnage.** The hills are full of silver; the rivers are full of clay; what the world is not full of is hands the proud stuff will answer. Nothing is mined, because nothing needs to be wrestled — everything worth having is *asked*, and the rarity of a material is exactly the difficulty of its asking. Three stuffs are the lawful exceptions, rare by geography of light rather than by manners — last-red is found only where the Fade has worked long, festival-glass only where crowds gather, the twice-benched only where something was lovingly retired — and even those are rare because of *where light has been*, never because of what the ground withholds.

*(Register note, kept per canon I-003: materials never speak, here or anywhere. Their "terms" are read through behavior and temperament, the way a cat's terms are read — a lean, a stiffening, a ripple toward a singer. Nothing in this layer talks.)*

## Named elements

### `the-apprentice-stuffs`

Straw, dough, river clay, withy, tallow, coarse wool — the gregarious tier, the stuffs that answer any hands at all, dim or bright, because they live where the wash lies deepest: dooryards, kitchens, riverbanks, the valley-floors the gloaming-table runs shallow beneath. They are every newborn's first courtship and every Quiet Walker's way back, and their abundance is their dignity: no fair has ever wanted for straw, and the straw-corner of any market is where children and dim old masters work side by side without a gate between them. Their one limit is honest — apprentice work holds only the loose wash, wakes thin, and pales fastest — so the whole tier is a doorway, never a destination.

- **This could become:** the floor resource — zero-gate basic material for starter and comeback cards, always purchasable, always courtable, keyed to the Quiet Walk re-entry loop; its works carry the fastest Fade-decay in the game, making the upgrade path felt rather than told.
- **Visual:** floury whites, wet-straw gold, riverbank brown; a material that leans *toward* everyone — dough swelling gently under any palm, clay rippling at a child's hum; silhouettes of round loaves and coiled withies, the warmest still-life shelf in the game.

### `singing-silver`

The proud metal: silver that will not take a shape unless it is sung to, one unbroken song from first heat to last pour — the braid stiffens the instant the song breaks, and stiffened silver must be courted all over again another day. The lodes are common as any hill's; what is rare is the singing, and rarer still the vouching before it, for singing-silver will not so much as ripple for hands the world is not currently holding in mind. At a fair it passes the only way it can: the smith sings the last bars *into the buyer's keeping*, the way bakers pass a dough-mother — proud stock travels with its courtship live, and the buyer keeps the terms warm or loses them.

- **This could become:** the high tier of a two-gate material market — visible gleam gate, then a legible micro-condition (an unbroken play-chain with no stall); Mannerly-affine; inventory that must be "kept warm" (a light upkeep condition, never a fee) or it reverts to ordinary stock.
- **Visual:** moth-wing sheen, shy blue-white; it ripples toward a singer like iron filings toward a magnet and leans away, minutely and unmistakably, from tuneless hands. The stall of singing-silver is the quietest place in a loud market — everyone near it is humming.

### `laughing-riverwood`

The green riverwood grows where the living come to be glad — swimming holes, ferry landings, fair fords — leaning out over generations of holiday wash, and it opens only for people who are laughing: the grain splits clean under genuine laughter and tears ragged under a forced one, which every carpenter learns exactly once. Crews that work it keep one dependable fool the way other crews keep a whetstone, and its willingness, once cut, lasts about a season — riverwood cannot be hoarded, only used glad and used soon. At a fair it is sold mid-joke, and a riverwood stall's reputation is the quality of its jokes.

- **This could become:** a mood-token material — acquisition and working both gated on a "the room is laughing" condition the player builds (comedy pieces, festival nodes); a mid-tier engine wood with a use-by season, teaching inventory-as-garden.
- **Visual:** green-gold grain like sun through leaves on moving water; the figure of the grain seems to widen when the room laughs, the way a pond widens its rings; freshly split faces so bright they look wet.

### `festival-glass`

Glass blown before a crowd keeps its crowd: flecks of bunting-color drift in it forever, and a pane of good festival-glass warms a room like company — while glass blown alone clouds fog-grey in the annealing, every time, no exceptions, one of the first laws children prove at a windowsill. So the glassworks travel with the Round-Fair, the whole craft is venue-bound, and the scarcity of festival-glass is the scarcity of gathered watching itself: no crowd, no glass. Buying it is buying a preserved audience, and towns glaze their best windows with it for the plainest of reasons — a room the glass keeps company in wakes its work a little deeper.

- **This could become:** the venue-bound material — acquirable only at high-attention nodes (fairs, festivals, red-thread-days); Fairwright-affine; works made of it grant small passive attention, the Moth Fair condition kept in a windowpane.
- **Visual:** clear glass with tiny motionless confetti of crowd-color caught inside; held to light, a pane reads like a fair seen from very far away. Lonely-glass beside it: flat, fogged, the grey of a morning nobody watched.

### `last-red`

When a town pales, the hearth-line red goes last — the hearth is where the living gather nightly, and living regard re-binds that one wash even as the rest of the scene lets go — so in long-pale country the red concentrates: lintels, kettle-rust, dooryard madder, flakes a careful hand can gather like coal from ash. Ground and mulled it becomes last-red, the stubbornest pigment in the world, and color laid in last-red takes *root*: a re-making painted with it holds its wash against the seep noticeably longer, the new red as patient as the old one that taught it. It exists only where the Fade has worked long — the greyest roads carry the richest red, the out-breath's one export — and at a fair a gleaner's jars are read like news: where the jars are full, the road is pale.

- **This could become:** the pale-node harvest resource — gathered on grey routes, richest where askings have compounded largest; spent as a catalyst on re-makings to slow that node's future paling; the dyestuff of red thread; the Gleaners' economy in a jar.
- **Visual:** ember-madder red so saturated it looks lit — it is not lit; it is only *certain* — the single warm note in a grey square; brushstrokes of it staying wet-looking a heartbeat longer than they should.

### `the-red-thread`

Spun from dooryard flax and dyed in last-red at a fireside while tales are told, the red thread is thread made in the middle of regard, and it does what every small made-and-woken thing does: it attends. A red-threaded gate is noticed by every walker, murmured of by every woken kettle, mentioned at every hearth down the road — so the place it marks stays a little in the country's mind, and what is held in mind seeps slower. It is what askings hang by, what a found combing is tied off with, and the most casually magical thing in any pack — and none of it binds anyone to anything: thread marks what is *minded*, never what must be done, and a thread untied is a thought moved on, no more.

- **This could become:** a place-marking consumable — tie thread at a paling node to slow its seep until your return (a bookmark of regard, bought with a scarce material rather than a rule exception); also the literal carrier of contract tokens in the pack UI.
- **Visual:** one red line in any composition — a knot on a grey stile like a single brushstroke on unpainted paper; the asking-lantern's slowly turning threads at dusk; a spool worn smooth at the belt, the game's most recognizable small prop.

### `the-twice-benched`

When a last lighting retires a mill, the wash goes down to the table — but the beams, shafts, and querns keep the ease of having been long worked: fired regard weathers slow as stone, and grain that once held a use takes a new one the way a walked path takes feet. Twice-benched timber and iron wake a shade easier than fresh stock — light-followers have chalked it at nine parts the attention fresh grain wants — so every Evener camp trails a wagon of it, and masters send first-year apprentices to that wagon before any other stall, because a first waking is easiest on grain that has known a bench. It is sold with its stories — a twice-benched beam comes with the telling of the mill it kept, and the telling is half the worth. And be exact, because the register depends on it: nothing collapsed and nothing was scavenged; retirement in Roundelay is chosen, tended, and festival-lit, a standing mill is worth more than its beams will ever be, and the wagon's plenty is downstream of tenderness, never of ruin.

- **This could become:** the retirement-yield material — last lightings and card-exhausts return twice-benched stock with a discounted waking threshold; the Evener sacrifice-economy's fuel; sinks made visibly generative.
- **Visual:** honey-dark grain polished by handling, old tool-marks like handwriting; no gold line — the use went home with the mill — but a warmth the eye reads as late afternoon even at noon; the Eveners' wagon like a shelf of well-read books.

### `the-courting`

The acquisition law of the whole material world, named: nothing is mined, everything is asked, and the asking has two gates the world keeps in strict order — first the *vouching* (gleam's business: will the stuff state its terms to these hands at all), then the *terms* (the material's own, always performable, always legible: the song sung, the laugh genuine, the audience present). The Mannerly notebooks are the economy's true reference-work — which valley's clay likes weather-talk, how long a given hill's silver expects to be sung to — lent and returned like hearts. And courted stock stays willing only while its terms are kept warm, a season at most, so a maker's pack is a garden, not a hoard: everything in it is being tended or going ordinary, and the wealthiest pack on the Rounds is the best-*kept* one, never the fullest.

- **This could become:** resource acquisition as visible two-stage micro-conditions (gleam gate → performable term), with inventory freshness in place of weight limits — stock reverts if untended, making pack management a craft loop rather than a knapsack problem. Standing gates and never spends, per canon.
- **Visual:** the hat-in-hand scene at a shelf of jars whose contents visibly perk; pressed samples and marginalia in a swollen notebook; a maker at dusk singing quietly down into an open pack, keeping the silver warm.

### `the-gleaning`

The pale roads have their own harvest, and it is walked with a soft brush, a scraper, hazel, and chalk: last-red from abandoned lintels, spiral-counts copied forward for the road-marks, seam-readings where the wash has begun to run toward a crossing. Gleaner manners are strict in the one way that matters — you glean the left-behind, never the lived-in; a hearth still lit keeps its red — and the craft's whole feel is taking what the out-breath leaves without taking anything a town still keeps. A gleaner comes in off the greyest road with the reddest jars, yawning happily, rich in exactly proportion to how far into the quiet she was willing to walk.

- **This could become:** the pale-route harvest action — a scouting-plus-gathering move whose yield scales with node paleness (richest exactly where askings have compounded), making grey detours a lawful economic choice rather than a dare; Gleaner-affine, road-mark-fed.
- **Visual:** a grey terrace, one kneeling figure at a doorway with a brush, a jar of impossible red on the step beside her; chalk spirals on every gatepost; the gleaner the single warm-rimmed thing in the frame.

### `the-glad-price` *(anchor — declared: the market stall with wares laid out and prices found is deliberately familiar furniture — every player has stood at one — kept so its cargo reads in a single frame: **worth as wakefulness and price as a reading taken in the palm** — no numbers posted, no tally kept, nothing insisted on; the coin itself is the meter of a fair trade.)*

No stall on the Rounds posts a number, because the coin does the telling: a handsel pressed into a palm brightens exactly as far as the giving is glad, and a grudged trade dims a shade even as it passes, which both parties can see and neither can fake. So a price is *read* — wares set out, handsels laid down one by one until the pile in the keeper's palm sings, and everyone at the stall watching the coins, not each other — and change comes back a shade brighter than the asking strictly wanted, because that is what held light does when giving goes well. Haggling exists and is a delight-craft, a small performance in which each side tries to make the *other's* handsels brighten; a good haggle leaves both purses more awake than either arrived, and the best hagglers on the Rounds are minor celebrities who are never, ever rich.

- **This could become:** pricing as a read, not a rule — vendor costs expressed in wakefulness (dull/warm/singing) rather than a gold count; glad overpaying returns brightened change (a tempo-for-value choice); the anti-hoard handsel physics and the price system as one mechanic.
- **Visual:** close-up: two hands over a stall-board, a short row of tokens in graded glow, the exact moment one brightens under a closing palm; wide: the market that sounds like rain on bells, stall-fronts strung dull-to-singing like lanterns tuning.

### `the-tale-pace`

Tales walk at a hearth's pace — one fireside a night, along the roads, never skipping a hearth and never flying — which is why the light of a good deed travels on foot and why incoming light is *readable*: any maker at any fire may ask what is told of them here, and learn to the evening which tellings walk which road and how many nights out they stand. Lantern-keepers keep the same easy count, and a lantern's reading rises only when a telling *arrives and is seen landing* — never sooner, never by surprise; the ratchet is weather with a walking speed. Makers plan whole seasons around it: an Untold runner outwalks her own tale for weeks on the between-roads, and a Fairwright's good ground goes on brightening him all winter, fireside by fireside, as the Fair's stories reach new towns.

- **This could become:** the I-015 rule made playable — delayed source-stamped Standing shown as tellings walking the map's roads at fixed pace, arrival turns readable in advance; lantern sizings update only on witnessed arrivals, so escalation never fires unpredictably.
- **Visual:** (map-layer diagram, exempt like the naturalist plates) a night map where hearth-points kindle one per night along a road, a telling as a slow bright bead between them; in-scene, only the effect is ever shown — a maker at a strange fire, brightening for work done three towns back.

### `lane-glimmers`

Where work once disappointed, the lane is found brighter, and a later walker can gather a little of that loose color into the day's work — a glimmer, the world's smallest good, a shade more ease at the bench, a shade more welcome in the square. But loose light is the cheapest light there is: unbound, it spreads itself thin across a whole lane's stones, and the light-followers put the finding plainly — *a lane's whole glimmer gathers less than the least of a maker's shine* — because the rare work in all the world is binding, and glimmers are precisely the light that came unbound. Nobody farms disappointment; you would be spending brightness to gather dimness, and every child who has held both knows which is which.

- **This could become:** spill-boons as small ambient node pickups seeded by past failures (this run's NPCs', prior runs' makers') — claimable by later visitors only, never by the spiller in the run that spilled, and priced by the stated law well under any point of Standing.
- **Visual:** watercolor-stained flagstones behind a fair-stage; a traveler's boot-toe pausing at unexpected color; nothing ever seen arriving — the lane only ever *found* brighter.

### `the-stilled-summer`

The walking-songs hold three stillings in living memory — Quill Vale, and two smaller valleys — and most makers walk every Round of a long life without crossing one: rarer than a new-found closed round, the light-followers say, and they note, carefully, the near-closed wonders that sat in each stilled valley, and say no more than that. Inside a stilling nothing pales and nothing returns: waxy over-bright color, staling knacks, thin grey mornings, birds confused at noon — a country with no pressure and no income, holding its breath. And a stilling has never cleared mid-season: the tide turns at an equinox, the whole countryside watches the turning day come, and the first reissued dawn returns the entire withheld season at once — a Quill-Vale Morning, a season of mornings in one, worth exactly what the stilling withheld and not a dram more, because the table mints nothing and loses nothing.

- **This could become:** the I-014 anomaly region — very rare, telegraphed, pressure-free but income-free inside (boons stale, no dawn draw, no paling), with a known release date (the equinox tick) whose one-dawn windfall equals the region's withheld seep: a routing puzzle about *being there when it turns*.
- **Visual:** canon's curdled palette — fruit-colors gone waxy, light like varnish — then the turning: rose-gold coming up off a whole valley at once, and three towns' worth of small figures who walked all night standing still in it.

## The systems map

Six interacting systems. Every arrow below is traceable in the element fictions above or in locked canon; S1 is canon's master loop, restated because all five others drink from it.

**S1 · The Breath (master loop, canon).**
dawn (reissue) → living attention → the bench → { *sets* into works | *overflows* to gleam | returns *loose* to the room } → wash on places → Fade seep (downhill) → gloaming-table → tomorrow's dawn.
*Everything below is a tributary or a tap of this circulation; nothing anywhere exits it.*

**S2 · The Bench Chain (attention → works → Standing).**
morning attention + audience (venues, crowds, woken things) → journey-pieces played → works woken (fired) → delight given → handsels set moving (feeds S3) + overflow gleam → Standing → *gates* `the-courting` (opens S4) and lantern showings (S6).
*Sink:* attention set into works leaves the day's pool; *return:* unspent surplus goes loose to the room → S1.

**S3 · The Handsel Round (money).**
delight (from S2) → handsels pressed hand to glad hand → brightening (held waking) → spent at `the-glad-price` for lodging, stock, and pieces → brighter change → keeps circulating.
*Sink:* a boxed handsel's binding lapses → ordinary seep → gloaming-table → returns as dawn income (S1). The money's only sink is the world's only income; hoarding literally funds other people's mornings.

**S4 · The Courting Web (materials).**
gleam (from S2) opens the vouching → shy stock states terms (`singing-silver`, `laughing-riverwood`, `festival-glass`) → terms performed → stock into the pack → tended warm (or reverts to ordinary) → the bench (S2).
`the-apprentice-stuffs` bypass the vouching gate entirely (the comeback path). A term accepted is an asking like any other and can go stale → disappointment → the Spill (S5).

**S5 · The Compost Loop (failure and retirement made generative).**
disappointment → the spilling → `lane-glimmers` for later walkers + loose seep → gloaming-table (S1);
retirement → last lighting → wash to the table (richer local dawns, S1) + `the-twice-benched` reclaimed → eased wakings at the bench (S2).
*Both of the economy's "losses" are sources one step downstream — for someone else, or for your own next leg.*

**S6 · The Paling Market (pressure as economy).**
unanswered towns pale (S1's seep, compounding by `the-paling-arithmetic`) → bigger askings + richer dawns (fuller local tables, S1) + `last-red` abundance → `the-gleaning` harvests the grey → last-red catalyzes re-makings → re-colored towns hold their wash longer (slows local seep, back into S1) and `the-red-thread` bookmarks what must wait → seven rings unanswered → the combing → a great asking (boss tier), answered before the gathered bench → the largest overflow event in the game (S2, S3).

## Carried obligations, answered

**I-012 — spill-boon values.** Stated in-fiction at `lane-glimmers` as a light-followers' finding: *a lane's whole glimmer gathers less than the least of a maker's shine*, because binding is the rare work and spilled light is exactly the light that came unbound. The principle for game-architect: any spill-boon's total worth sits well below one Standing point's marginal value, and spill-boons are claimable only by later visitors, never the spiller in the run that spilled. Exact numbers are game-architect's.

**I-013 — between-runs ambient forgetting: CLAMPED.** Every re-spark is a fresh maker walking a new Round. Diegetically: the song keeps the *singing*, never the singer — what persists between makers is only ever what was given (fired works, road-marks, porch-elders, woken things), and gleam, being the world currently holding a particular life in mind, is always new light on new hands. Nothing of a past maker sits on a shelf dimming, because nothing of a maker is ever kept — only what they gave. Reason for the clamp: gentle meta-decay would put homework between runs and read punitive against the register; the meta-layer stays gifts-only. (The L2 continuation option — a same maker returning from the Quiet Walk with lanterns' fired readings intact — remains available to game-architect as an explicitly high-stakes opt-in mode; default canon is the fresh voice.)

**I-014 — Stillsummer rarity and release worth.** Answered at `the-stilled-summer`: three stillings in living memory, most makers never cross one — rarer than a new-found closed round. Release is calendar-lawful (a stilling has never cleared mid-season; the tide turns at an equinox) and worth exactly the withheld season's seep returned in one dawn — a Quill-Vale Morning: large, honest, and never minted, because the table keeps nothing and loses nothing.

**I-015 — delayed gleam visibility.** Answered at `the-tale-pace`: tellings walk one fireside a night along roads, never skipping and never flying; any maker may read at any hearth what walks which road and how many nights out; and a lantern's reading rises only when a telling arrives and is seen landing. The ratchet therefore never fires unpredictably — incoming light is weather with a posted walking speed.

**I-016** (visual signatures) is Layer 6's and is not solved here; the visual hooks above are offers to it, not rulings.

## The arc of a run's economy — felt from inside

**Early leg — the shy market.** A thread of gleam, a light pack, dull handsels. The world answers you in apprentice-stuffs: dough, straw, river clay, kettle askings off the lantern's lowest hooks. Your one reliable wealth is the morning — first light is the day's regard at its most gatherable, and you are up for it because you cannot afford not to be. Scarcity here is not want; it is *shyness*: the silver stall goes quiet as you pass, and that silence is the tutorial.

**Mid leg — the engine.** The notebooks start paying: you know what this valley's clay likes, the riverwood crew keeps you for your jokes, and your first last-lighting leaves a wagon-share of twice-benched grain that wakes easy under your hands. Handsels move through you warm and come back warmer; the Fair crosses your circuit and the glassworks know your face; a grey detour with a brush and a jar turns paleness into last-red and last-red into re-makings that *hold*. The pack is a garden in full tending — everything in it either working or being kept warm — and the lanterns have begun reaching toward their rafters when you walk in.

**Late leg — abundance under pressure.** Now everything states its terms to you, and that is exactly the weight of it: the dawns are enormous because you route the pale country on purpose, the stalls sing when you stop at them, the proud stuff leans in from every shelf — and the rafters hold work sized to the brightest the road has heard you be, which is very bright now, and fired, and patient. Your wealth and the world's asking trace to the same shine and rise together; spirals are converging on one valley the way your hazel said they would; and the last leg's question is the economy's whole lesson worn as a season — with this much given to your hands, how much of it will you set, how much will you feed back, and will you be standing in the right valley when the country's missing combs itself into the biggest audience of your life.

## Anchor declaration (Layer 4)

One anchor this layer, declared inline above: **`the-glad-price`** — the familiar market stall, kept so its cargo (worth as wakefulness; price as a reading taken in the palm, never posted, never insisted on; haggling as mutual brightening) reads in one frame. Anchor share: **1 of 13 elements.**

Near-anchors defused in the text rather than declared: `the-twice-benched` is explicitly not a salvage economy (retirement is chosen, tended, festival-lit; a standing mill outvalues its beams; the do-not-resemble ban on salvage-from-collapse is answered in the fiction itself); `the-red-thread` marks what is minded and never binds anyone to anything (no obligation-token drift); `the-courting`'s "terms" are behavioral and read like a cat's, never spoken (I-003 restraint held).

## Register check

Nothing above is owed, filed, counted into a verdict, or enforced. Failure consequences remain ecological and mnemonic (stock goes ordinary, terms go stale, lanes are found brighter); Standing gates and never spends; the Fade touches board and stock, never the skin; woken things attend and do not act; and the only court at any stall is whether the coin brightens.
