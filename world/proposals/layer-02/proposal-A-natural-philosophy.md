# Proposal A — Natural Philosophy: THE ROUND OF LIGHT

**Layer 2 (cosmology) · proposer A-natural-philosophy · pipeline-test-02 · world: ROUNDELAY**

**Lens commitment:** the cosmology as lawful physics of attention — conservation, flow, phase, and one irreversible transformation. Every wonder below is the kind a curious child could test from a windowsill, and children do (see `the-noticing-game`). No origin story is offered anywhere in this proposal, on principle: a round-song has no first verse. You do not ask where a roundelay began; you join it already going. That is not an evasion — it is the cosmology's shape. The world's deepest law is the same shape as its name.

**Thesis in one breath:** Attention — the world's regard, its holding-things-in-mind — is Roundelay's one conserved stuff. Light is what regard looks like from outside. Regard is never created and never destroyed; it pools loose in places, runs live in the living, and sets fast in made things, and it moves between those three holdings through exactly one gate — the work of attentive hands. The Fade is the return stroke of its circulation, dawn is its reissue, gleam is its overflow tracing back to a cause, and the asking ratchet is its one irreversibility. Contracts that escalate and a Standing that depletes are not systems anyone built. They are what conservation, overflow, and no-unfiring feel like from inside a single walking life.

---

## 1. What attention is — the deep law

Matter in Roundelay is ordinary. Stone is stone, water is water, and none of it is dreamed, sung, or thought into being — a natural philosopher of the Rounds would find that suggestion faintly embarrassing, like being told bread is made of hunger. What regard does is subtler and stranger: **regard is what holds a thing's character crisp.** A well without regard is still a hole with water in it; what it loses is the *trick* of itself — the knack of sweet water, the exact welcome of its rope-worn coping. Character is fine-grained, and fine grain needs holding. Regard is the holding.

The first law, the one every child recites skipping: ***nothing is noticed away.*** Regard is conserved. When it leaves a place, it has not perished; it has gone somewhere, and it can be followed — and the whole discipline of the light-followers, the Rounds' patient hedge-philosophers, is exactly that: following it. Light is not a second substance. Light is regard seen from outside, the way heat-shimmer is heat seen from across a field.

### The three settlings — why the channels never cross

Regard takes three holdings, and the holding depends entirely on what is doing the holding. This is the grammar of light, given its floor:

- **In places, regard lies loose.** Ground has no wanting and no use; it can hold regard only the way a meadow holds mist — a wash, beautiful and unbound, free to seep. This loose wash *is* the color and knack of scenery.
- **In the living, regard runs.** Only the living want, and wanting is the lens: a living creature gathers loose regard through itself and *aims* it. Living light is always a current, never a pool. This is also why the Fade cannot touch people — its tide skims still water only. A current shrugs off the skimming; there is nothing settled to take. The Fade does not spare the living out of mercy. It spares them out of physics.
- **In made things, regard sets.** Setting needs a *use* to set into — regard fires into function the way glaze fires into clay, which is why the gold line lives on the working edge and nowhere else: the lip of the bowl, the blade of the plane, the hem that does the coat's actual keeping-warm. And a living body will not take the set, ever, for the plainest reason in the world: *you cannot fire wet clay, and a living thing is never done being made.* No person will ever carry a working-edge. No place will ever gleam — gleam is overflow tracing back to a cause (see §3), and a place is not a cause; a place is a where.

Three holdings, three impossibilities, each one lawful. The channels never cross because they are not three lights. They are three phases of one light, and the phase belongs to the holder.

### Conservation at the bench

When true work is done before watchers, the regard in the room moves in one legible transaction. Some of it **sets** into the work — that is waking. Some of it **overflows** — a made thing, like a cup, holds only so much, and the excess runs back along the line of cause to the maker — that is gleam (§3). The remainder returns **loose** to the room — which is why workshops and market squares are the most colorful places on any Round, and why a town re-made before a crowd re-colors like watercolor blooming: the bench is a fountain that pours in three directions at once, and not one dram is lost. Light-followers have sat through ten thousand makings with tally-chalk and never once caught the world cheating.

## 2. The Fade — the return stroke, and where the taken light goes

The Fade is not decay, not appetite, not an enemy dressed as weather. **The Fade is collection.** Loose regard — and only loose regard; the tide skims the unbound wash of places, never the set gold of things, never the running light of the living — seeps out of unregarded scenery at a slow seasonal rate, strongest in late summer and autumn when the world exhales, near-still in deep winter. It seeps *downhill*. This is measurable and measured: hilltop shrines pale seasons before valley mills; a high pasture goes grey at the fence-line while the low orchard under it holds its reds like a cupped hand. The drained light gathers along the grain of the ground into the **gloaming-table** — the world's low standing reserve of spent regard, pooled under valley floors and deep water the way groundwater pools under fields.

And from the gloaming-table it comes back. **Dawn is the reissue.** Every morning's light is spent regard returned — loose, unclaimed, free to every eye that opens — which is why the oldest folk-saw on the Rounds is also a verified finding: *pale country gets the best mornings.* The greyer the evening, the redder the dawn, because the fuller the local table, the richer the return. Every morning is made of everything the world stopped noticing, given back to be noticed again. That is the round of light entire: dawn to eyes, eyes to hands, hands to work, work to places, places seeping to the gloaming-table, the table rising as dawn. The world is not coming apart at the edges. The world is *breathing*, and the Fade is the out-breath without which there is no in.

The proof is a remembered season. In **the Stillsummer of Quill Vale**, some forty years back, the Fade in that one valley simply stopped — no one knows why to this day, though light-followers note, carefully, that the valley holds a near-closed wonder (§5) and say no more than that. The valley did not bloom. It *curdled*. Colors went waxy and over-bright, like fruit that will not ripen because it will not soften; knacks hoarded in things and staled there; and the mornings — this is what everyone remembers — the mornings came grey and thin all summer, because the light had nowhere to return from. Birds sang at noon, confused. When the Fade resumed at the autumn equinox, the first reissued dawn was so vast and so rose-gold that people walked all night from three towns to stand in it, and *the Quill Vale Morning* is still what makers say when they mean an ending that turns out to be a gift. Nobody who stood in that dawn has ever again called the Fade a thief.

Why knack goes before color: character is fine-grained, and fine grain needs the most holding — so the trick of the well seeps first, and the red of the roof follows, coarse and slow. Why unwoken work pales fastest: it holds only the loose wash, and loose is what the tide takes. Why woken work stands generations: set regard is fired regard, bound into use, and the tide cannot skim what has been fired — it can only weather it, slow as stone.

## 3. The gleam cycle — why people-light fills, why it thins, and how it returns

*(This is Standing. Mechanism first, as world-law demands of its tellers.)*

At the **Moth Fair at Ferrow, sixty-one autumns back**, a fiddler named Immy Ash played *The Long Thaw* three times through on her grandmother's fiddle — an old, well-woken instrument, its gold edge steady along the bass-bar. By the second time through, four hundred people were aiming everything they had at that fiddle, and here is what the whole fairground saw, and what two lantern-keepers with tally-chalk timed: the fiddle's gold edge blazed to a fixed brightness *and then would not brighten further* — a woken thing, like a cup, holds only so much — and the crowd's regard, still pouring, **ran visibly up the strings and into her hands.** Her knuckles took the light first, then her hair, then the rims of her ears, doubling before the reel ended. The light-followers' phrase for the finding is four words long: ***a full cup runs to the pourer.***

That is the whole mechanism of gleam. Regard traces lines of cause — it does not splash at random; it runs *backward along the making*, the way water finds the channel that brought it. When delight overfills what the work can hold, the overflow follows the line of cause to the maker and **clings** — at the edges, knuckles and hair and ear-rims, the way dew clings where surfaces end. And moments overfill exactly as objects do: a joke can hold only so much of a kitchen's laughter, a told story only so much of a hearth's leaning-in, and the excess runs to the one who caused it. This is why gleam is earnable in any coin the world honors, and why the Quiet Walk works with an empty pack: an instant of caused delight is a cup like any other, and it, too, runs over to the pourer.

But gleam is *loose* light clinging to a living surface — it is not fired, cannot be fired (§1: never fire wet clay) — so what holds it on? **The world's continued regard for what you did.** Gleam clings exactly as long as the delight it traces to is still being held in mind somewhere: retold at hearths, murmured between woken things, carried town to town as tale. Gleam is, quite literally, *the world currently holding you in mind, worn as light.* And so it thins the only way it could: **the world forgets.** Talk of the Moth Fair stopped being carried past Whistledown by midwinter, and Immy Ash's shine thinned that same winter at the same measured rate — not taken, not docked, simply no longer held. And when work *disappoints*, the same law runs sudden instead of slow: an unmoved audience is regard withdrawn all at once, the lines of cause go slack in a single evening, and the slackened light slides off where the maker stands — into the flagstones, the doorframes, the lane (see `the-spilling`, §6). It does not vanish. Nothing is noticed away. The prettiest alley in Ferrow is the one behind the fair-stage, colored generation by generation by every performer who ever died out there.

Now derive the mechanic, which no longer needs deriving: a light that is *the world holding you in mind* must deplete — minds move on; that is what the Fade already taught us minds lawfully do — and must be restorable by any fresh caused delight, from a re-made bridge to laughter in a stranger's kitchen, because every overfilled moment runs to its pourer. **Standing that falls when work disappoints or goes stale, rises when delight is given in any coin, gates the shy materials (proud stuff will not state terms to hands the world is not currently holding in mind), and ends the run not in death but in the world going quiet toward you — that is not a designed meter. That is overflow, clinging, and forgetting, worn on the skin.** The register holds too: the consequence of failure is ecological and mnemonic all the way down, because gleam-loss *is* forgetting, and the spilled light makes the world around your failure slightly more beautiful. Roundelay does not punish. It composts.

## 4. The asking ratchet — no unfiring, and why need grows one way

*(Mechanism first, again.)*

**Twenty-two springs ago, Sorrel Vane re-made the flood-broken bridge at Brindle-on-the-Wold** before a crowd of two hundred — a great work, the kind a road talks about for a generation. What happened next is the best-documented case the light-followers hold on lantern-law. Every asking-lantern down the Wold road, towns Vane had never entered, began lowering her rafters-work the day she arrived: granary roofs, a river's silted voice, a fouled spring two valleys wide. At Merrylees, tired, she asked the lantern-keeper for something small — a kettle season, a resting year. The keeper, fond of her, hung a tin whistle at eye height that very evening. By morning Vane could not find it. She stood under the lantern a full hour while the keeper's hand went straight to the whistle again and again; to her own eyes there was nothing at that height but shadow and thread. The lantern was not withholding out of cruelty and could not have done otherwise: **a lantern is a woken thing, and what a woken thing has attended, it holds attended. There is no unfiring.**

Two laws meet in that hour under the lantern, and the ratchet falls out of them like a dropped tool.

**First: setting is the one irreversible transformation in the round of light.** Loose becomes set through attentive making, and set never becomes loose again on any timescale a life contains — you can weather fired glaze, you can never un-fire it. This single one-way gate is why woken things outlast their makers *and* why need only grows: the permanence players love and the escalation players fear are the same law wearing two coats.

**Second: need takes aim, and it aims by reading grain.** Gleam is overflow traced from causes, so gleam has *grain* — bridge-delight lies differently on the knuckles than song-delight; a trained lantern (and every lantern is trained; it is a woken thing whose one fixed attending is *the reading of hands*) reads in your shine the size and kind of the delights that fed it. You wear your history as texture. And an asking, itself a made and woken thing, attends toward the brightest hands its town has heard of — heard of through the murmur-net of woken things and hearth-tales, which is to say through the same regard-lines that hold your gleam on. Rumor does not un-travel *because attending does not un-set.* The lantern that has read a bridge in your grain has fired that reading. It will lower you bridges forever.

Underneath both, the Fade runs its patient arithmetic on anything skipped: a pale town's loose regard keeps seeping at the season's fixed rate, so the re-making a need requires grows by simple steady drainage — pass it by in spring and by harvest it is twice the work, twice the reissue waiting in its dawns, twice the wonder when the road brings you back. Nothing compounds out of spite. It compounds out of weather.

So the conventional shape arrives last, as a consequence, the only way it could: **contracts escalate** because settings don't unset and need aims at the brightest grain it has read; **and they never scale down** because the reading is fired, not filed. Being asked is the honor; the size of the asking is the compliment; and the compliment, like everything else fired in this world, is permanent.

## 5. The unmarked wonders — the I-004 decision

**Decision: the answer exists in canon by exactly half, and the boundary between the knowable half and the unknowable half is itself a law of the world.** Not mystery as fog — mystery as horizon: lawful, located, and impossible to cross from where anyone stands.

**The knowable half — canon, in-world, tested and teachable to children:** an unmarked wonder is a **closed round** — a made thing whose circulation of regard has closed into a self-sustaining loop. An ordinary wonder sheds: it delights, the overflow runs off to its maker, the loose remainder seeps to the gloaming-table, and one day it needs re-making. A closed round *keeps what it pours.* The delight it causes runs its overflow back into its own setting; it wakes what passes near it, and what it wakes attends it in return; it is a whirlpool in the round of light that never unwinds. Light-followers have measured this directly — stand by the thousand-year stair with tally-chalk and you can watch the circulation, a slow visible current under the stone like glass under water. This also dissolves the mark question lawfully rather than answering it with a *who*: a maker's mark persists because runoff keeps tracing back to its cause — but in a closed loop the runoff circles *inside*, and the maker's share, mark, name, and grain were long ago swallowed into the whole and worn smooth. **The wonders may well have had makers — ordinary ones, no elder anything, no one buried, no one hiding — and closure itself ate the attribution.** That is canon.

**The unknowable half — permanently open, and lawfully so:** *how a round closes.* No maker has ever closed one on purpose; the light-followers keep a short, beloved list of near-closures (the stair at Setterby holds nine parts in ten of what it sheds, and pilgrim-makers leave journey-pieces on it the way you'd encourage a student). And here the measurement limit does its quiet work: **you cannot trace causes inside a closed loop from outside it.** The very completeness that makes a wonder permanent makes its history unreadable — attribution has no line left to run along. This is why the cheerful heresy survives untouched and untouchable: if the world's own great round — dawn, eyes, hands, work, places, table, dawn — is itself closed, then the world is a workshop finishing its own small jobs overnight, and *no one inside it could ever confirm that*, for exactly the same reason no one can read the stair. The heresy stays a heresy: never confirmed, never cheapened, protected forever by the same law that makes it thinkable.

**How much the game may reveal, in canon terms:** all of the *what* (closed circulation, measurable in play, discoverable wonder by wonder), all of the *why-unmarked* (closure eats attribution), none of the *how* and none of the *whether-the-world-is-one* — and the endgame aspiration this leaves standing is the best kind: somewhere past the great askings lives the question of whether one maker's masterwork, made attentively enough, before audience enough, might close. The cosmology neither promises this nor forbids it. Nothing in the laws says it is impossible. Nothing in the record says it has ever been done.

## 6. Named elements

#### `the-round-of-light` *(anchor — declared: this cycle deliberately wears the shape of the water cycle — rain, river, sea, rain — the one circulation every player already knows in their bones, kept familiar so that the strange claims it carries — dawn is recycled regard, the Fade is collection not loss, conservation of attention as playable law — are legible in a single glance at a diagram an eight-year-old could chalk on slate.)*

The one circulation: dawn issues loose regard over everything; the living gather and aim it; making sets some, overflows some, returns the rest to the room; places hold the loose wash until the Fade's out-breath seeps it downhill to the gloaming-table; the table rises as tomorrow's dawn. It has no first verse and needs none — you join it already going, which is what *Roundelay* has meant all along. Light-followers have never once caught it losing a dram.

- **This could become:** the run's master economy invariant — attention spent anywhere visibly arrives somewhere (into cards as waking, to the maker as Standing, into the node as re-coloring), and unspent attention is never wasted, only returned: end-of-turn surplus seeds the next dawn's base income. A "where it went" readout replaces any discard-to-void; nothing in the UI ever just disappears.
- **Visual:** the chalk-slate diagram as a living UI motif — a circle of small pictures (sun, eye, hand, bowl, roof, pool, sun) that the game's actual light-flows animate along; in-world, every schoolroom and guild-hall wall carries some folk version, embroidered, inlaid, or scratched.

#### `the-three-settlings`

Regard takes three holdings and the holding belongs to the holder: loose in places (ground cannot want or use, so it holds light as a wash), running in the living (wanting is the lens; living light is always a current), set in things (regard fires into *use*, which is why the gold line lives only on the working edge). The three impossibilities are the grammar of light with its floor put in: the tide can't skim a current, so people never fade; a body is never done being made, so no person takes the set; a place is a where and not a cause, so no place gleams.

- **This could become:** the hard rule that the game's three resource-readings (node color, Standing, card wakefulness) never convert directly — every transfer between them must pass through a played card, making the act of play the world's only exchange gate, mechanically and cosmologically at once.
- **Visual:** one light, three renderings, strictly enforced: wash (watercolor bloom on scenery), rim (key-light on characters), edge (one gold line on objects) — a style-guide law an artist can check any frame against.

#### `the-unclaimed-morning`

Dawn is spent regard reissued from the gloaming-table — loose, unclaimed, equal, free to every eye that opens, beggar and master alike. Every morning is made of everything the world stopped noticing, handed back to be noticed again; the world has no other income and needs none. Makers on the road rise early not from virtue but from craft-sense: first light is the day's regard at its most gatherable, before the towns wake and begin aiming it.

- **This could become:** the per-turn base attention income — flat, universal, ungameable, arriving at turn-start as "the morning" — with pale nodes granting a richer dawn (their table is fuller), so the risk of traveling faded country carries a lawful, legible reward baked into the physics.
- **Visual:** dawns rendered as the emotional weather-report of the map: over kept country, brief and ordinary; over pale country, immense, long, rose-gold, almost embarrassing — the world's generosity most visible exactly where it has been least attended.

#### `the-gloaming-table`

The world's low reserve of spent regard, pooled under valley floors and deep water like groundwater under fields, fed by the Fade's downhill seep, drawn on by every dawn. It gives the Fade its direction (hilltops pale first, valleys hold color like cupped hands) and the world its tenderest hydrology: dowsers exist in Roundelay, but what they dowse is *light*, walking pale country with a forked hazel wand to find where the gloaming runs shallow and a re-making will take root quickest.

- **This could become:** map elevation and terrain as Fade-rate modifiers (high nodes pale faster, valley nodes hold), plus a light-dowsing scout action that reveals which pale node will re-color cheapest — route-planning strategy pulled straight out of the world's plumbing.
- **Visual:** cross-section vignettes like a naturalist's plate: a hill in autumn greying from the crown down while, underground, painted veins of warm gold gather and run valleyward — the world's kindness happening out of sight, in the dark, all the time.

#### `the-stillsummer`

Forty years back, the Fade in Quill Vale stopped, and the valley learned what it was for: colors went waxy and would not ripen, knacks staled and hoarded in things, and all summer the mornings came grey and thin — the light had nowhere to return from. When the out-breath resumed at equinox, the reissued dawn was so vast that people walked all night from three towns to stand in it, and *the Quill Vale Morning* still means an ending that turns out to be a gift. The cause was never found; light-followers note only, carefully, that the valley holds a near-closed wonder, and say no more.

- **This could become:** a rare map anomaly — a "stilled" region where nothing pales but nothing re-colors either, wrong in the way over-sweet fruit is wrong: no pressure, no dawn income, staling boons — teaching the player, through one uncanny detour, why the Fade is the friend; clearing or outlasting it releases a Quill-Vale-Morning burst.
- **Visual:** the uncanny valley of too-much: over-saturated, waxen, shadowless noon-light that never moves, birds singing at the wrong hour — followed, on release, by the game's single most extravagant dawn.

#### `gleam-runoff`

A made thing, like a cup, holds only so much; when delight overfills the work, the overflow runs backward along the line of cause to the maker and clings at the body's edges — knuckles, hair, ear-rims — the way dew clings where surfaces end. Moments overfill exactly as objects do: a joke holds only so much of a kitchen's laughter, and the rest runs to the pourer. Everyone in Roundelay has seen it happen at least once at a fair — the work blazing to its fixed fullness, then the maker beginning, softly, to shine.

- **This could become:** the Standing-gain rule with real texture: attention played *beyond* what a card can hold (overkill delight above the waking threshold) converts to Standing at full rate — so pushing a performance past "enough" is never waste, and the run's biggest Standing swings happen on stage, in public, at the top of the engine, where the fiction says they should.
- **Visual:** the Moth Fair shot — a gold working-edge burning steady at its fixed maximum while surplus light runs visibly *up the strings* into a fiddler's hands; rain falling upward along lines of cause.

#### `the-grain-of-gleam`

Gleam is overflow traced from causes, so it keeps the texture of what fed it: bridge-delight lies in long calm spans across the knuckles, song-gleam ripples like water under wind, hearth-laughter freckles. Lanterns read grain — it is their one fixed attending — and so do proud materials, which is how silver knows whose hands have held an audience and whose have only held tools. On the Rounds your résumé is not told; it is worn, and it is legible at dusk from across a square.

- **This could become:** Standing as a tagged resource, not a bare scalar — the *kinds* of delight that earned your gleam gate which askings lanterns lower and which shy materials will state terms, giving builds identity ("bridge-grained" vs "song-grained" runs) without a single menu of classes.
- **Visual:** close-up character portraits where the rim-light has visible texture — spans, ripples, freckles — readable at a glance like tree-rings, so a veteran player can read a maker's whole history in a loading-screen portrait.

#### `the-spilling`

When work disappoints, the audience's regard withdraws all at once, the slackened gleam slides off where the maker stands, and — nothing being noticed away — it soaks loose into the flagstones, the doorframes, the lane. Failure in Roundelay leaves the world slightly more beautiful exactly where it happened: the prettiest alley in Ferrow is the one behind the fair-stage, colored generation by generation by everyone who ever died out there. Makers know this and are strangely comforted by it; there is an old toast — *to the lanes we've painted.*

- **This could become:** the Standing-loss rule with a gift inside it: every point of gleam lost re-colors the current node (small permanent boons for future visits, or seeds for the next run's map), so failure feeds the same world the next maker walks — loss aversion softened by lawful, visible compost, and the Quiet Walk's meta-progression grounded in physics rather than menu design.
- **Visual:** the slumped fiddler on a dim stage — and through the door behind her, the alley blushing into watercolor as her light finds the stones; grief and gift in a single frame, no caption needed.

#### `no-unfiring`

Setting is the round of light's one irreversible transformation: loose becomes set through attentive making, and set never comes loose again — you can weather fired glaze; you cannot un-fire it. This single one-way gate carries both of the world's permanences: woken things outlast their makers, and what a woken thing has attended, it holds attended — which is why a lantern that has read your brightness can never again show you kettles, however fond its keeper. The permanence players love and the escalation players fear are the same law wearing two coats.

- **This could become:** the game's irreversibility doctrine, stated once and enforced everywhere: card wakings are permanent within the run, contract tiers never scale down, and any "reset" the design ever wants must be diegetically routed through the living (only currents re-aim) — a designer's razor as much as a rule.
- **Visual:** kiln iconography as the UI's language of permanence — tier-ups and wakings marked by a brief glaze-crackle bloom, beautiful and slightly final, like a signature you watch dry.

#### `need-takes-aim`

An asking is a made, woken thing, and its one fixed attending is toward the brightest hands its town has heard of — heard through hearth-tales and the murmur of woken things, the same regard-lines that hold gleam on. Rumor does not un-travel because attending does not un-set: the lantern at Merrylees, twenty-two springs ago, could not show Sorrel Vane a tin whistle its keeper had hung for her at eye height — to her eyes, shadow and thread; to anyone else's, a whistle. Need is not policy and not appetite; it is aim, and aim, once fired, holds.

- **This could become:** the escalating-contract generator, exactly and lawfully: available askings key off peak-Standing-this-run (read from grain, never from a hidden counter), never offer below the peak once read, and preview next-tier silhouettes in the lantern's rafters — so the player always sees the ratchet as the world's regard, aimed and holding, rather than as difficulty curve.
- **Visual:** the Merrylees hour — a tired, brightly-rimmed maker staring up into a lantern's dark rafters while the keeper's hand goes straight to a whistle she cannot see; the game's tenderest statement that being believed in is a weather you cannot stand out of.

#### `the-noticing-game`

The Rounds' empirical method is a children's game older than any guild: paired trials on a windowsill — a pebble carved with full attention beside one cut hasty and alone, chalk tallies beneath, a winter of watching which grays first. Light-followers are simply children who never stopped playing it; their instruments are tally-chalk, hazel wands, pin-lanterns, and patience, and their whole literature fits in walking-songs. Every law in this cosmology entered canon through somebody's windowsill, which is why nobody in Roundelay experiences the world's physics as doctrine: it is the stuff you checked yourself, age seven, against your own chalk marks.

- **This could become:** the tutorial and discovery layer — laws are never stated in menus but demonstrated as folk-experiments the player performs (two pebbles, one winter), with a journeyman's notebook that fills in each law only after play has evidenced it; late-game, the same notebook becomes the riddle-tracker for closed rounds.
- **Visual:** windowsill still-lifes as the tutorial's whole art direction — paired objects, one gold-edged and one matte, chalk tallies, a child's breath fogging the pane; science rendered as a picture-book of small patiences.

#### `the-closed-round`

An unmarked wonder is a made thing whose circulation of regard has closed: it keeps what it pours, wakes what passes, is attended by what it wakes — a whirlpool in the round of light that never unwinds, measurable with tally-chalk as a slow current under the stone. Closure eats attribution lawfully: the maker's runoff circled inside instead of running home, and mark, name, and grain were worn smooth into the whole — the wonders likely had makers, ordinary ones, and the loop swallowed the signature. How a round closes, no one knows; whether the world's own great round is one, no one *can* know from inside it — and the light-followers' short list of near-closures (the stair at Setterby holds nine parts in ten of what it sheds) is the closest thing the Rounds have to a holy text, except that everyone on the list is jointed, weathered, and available for study.

- **This could become:** relic-class discovery nodes whose "circulation" is a measurable stat the notebook tracks; unmarked cards that fit no suit and never leave the pack between runs; and the game's horizon-question, never promised and never forbidden — whether a sufficiently attended masterwork of the player's own might close, the collection screen quietly assembling the evidence either way.
- **Visual:** edge-light along every line at once, softly, like a drawing the light colored before anyone else could — and, on long observation (the player who stops and waits is rewarded), a visible slow current circulating under the surface, glass under water.

## 7. Anchor declaration

One anchor in this layer, declared inline above and summarized here:

1. **`the-round-of-light`** — the circulation deliberately wears the shape of the **water cycle**, the one closed loop every player already carries from childhood. It is kept familiar precisely so the layer's genuinely strange cargo — dawn as recycled regard, the Fade as collection rather than loss, strict conservation as a playable economy invariant, the gloaming-table as light's groundwater — reads in a single chalk diagram with no exposition. ## 8. Why the locked spine is now inevitable — and the bans, checked

**Standing** is not modeled by this cosmology; it *precipitates* from it. Given conservation, overflow-to-cause, and the fact that gleam is the world holding you in mind, a people-light that rises with delight given in any coin, thins as the world's talk moves on, collapses gently when work disappoints, gates the proud materials, and ends a run in quiet rather than death is the only shape people-light could take. **Escalating contracts** likewise: given that setting never unsets, that askings are woken things whose aim is fixed by what they have attended, and that the Fade compounds anything skipped by plain steady drainage, a one-way, never-descending, peak-keyed contract ladder is not a difficulty curve — it is what it feels like to be heard of, in a world that reads your grain and cannot unread it.

Ban compliance, briefly: no creation myth exists here and none is missing — a round has no first verse (the absence is load-bearing, stated in-text). The Fade is the out-breath that makes dawn possible: non-hostile by construction, and Quill Vale shows the world is *worse* without it. Grey is never dark-as-evil; grey is light gone home to be reissued. No elemental planes, no dualism, no debt or obligation anywhere in the physics — regard runs on overflow and forgetting, never on anything held against anyone. Channel ban enforced at the level of law (§1: three impossibilities). Restraint ban kept: lanterns *show and withhold showing*, which is attending; nothing in this layer acts, speaks, or pours itself. Riddle ban kept and armored: the unmarked wonders resolve to mechanism plus a lawful measurement horizon — no elder makers, no precursors, no gods, and the self-making-workshop heresy remains forever thinkable and forever unconfirmable, which is the best thing a heresy can be.
