# Proposal C — The Ecology of Light

**Layer 2 (cosmology) · proposer C-ecology-of-light · pipeline-test-02 · world: ROUNDELAY**

*Lens, committed fully: the cosmology is a living cycle. Attention migrates, pools, seeds, and returns. The Fade is composting, not destruction. The world has seasons and weathers of regard, and every law below is a lawful process a painter could stand in front of.*

---

## 0. The one law under everything

**Light in Roundelay is a single circulating substance — attention itself — and the world holds a fixed water of it.** Nothing in Roundelay makes light and nothing destroys it. It moves. It has been moving longer than anyone has kept count, and it moves in a round: it settles into places, it is carried by the living, it is caught in made things, it leaches down into the dark ground, and it rises again through delight. No one has ever found the world's spring, and no one has ever found its drain. The makers' catechism — taught to children with a cup of water and a candle — refuses the question of where the round began, because a round has no first singer. Asking where the light started is, in Roundelay, the same shape of mistake as asking where a circle starts.

*(Anchor, declared: this layer leans deliberately on the familiar language of water and weather — springs, loam, runnels, watersheds, fallow fields, tides. Hydrology is kept ordinary on purpose, so that the genuinely strange claim underneath it — that attention is the world's one circulating substance, and that you, personally, are a place it pools — stays instantly legible. The anchor carries the whole layer; it is declared again per-element at `the-shedlands`.)*

### The three phases, and why the channels never cross

Layer 1's grammar of light — places fade, people gleam, things wake — is not three magics. It is **one substance in three phases**, and the phases never mix because they physically cannot:

- **Settled light** is attention that has soaked into a *place* over years of use and regard. Settled light is a place's knack and its color — the sweetness a well learned from ten thousand grateful mornings, the red a roof keeps because the whole street looks up at it. Settled light is the only phase the Fade can touch, because it is the only phase that holds still.
- **Carried light** is attention moving through the *living*. The living never settle — a person is a current, not a pool — which is why people never fade and why gleam sits on the skin like light through rice paper: it is passing through, not soaking in. Carried light is Standing, seen from outside.
- **Caught light** is attention fixed in a *made thing* at the moment of its making — the gathered regard of the watchers, pressed into the work like a seal into wax while the wax was warm. Caught light neither settles nor spends; it holds, one thin gold line along the working edge, still faintly facing the way the attention faced. That is the whole of why woken things attend and never act: a woken thing is regard, frozen mid-turn. A compass needle does not fetch north. It faces it.

One glance, three phases: a grey town is low on settled light, a bright maker is thick with carried light, an alert kettle holds caught light. The channels never cross because ice does not pour and steam does not stack.

## 1. Why the Fade exists, and where what it takes goes

### `the-gloam`

Under everything in Roundelay — under the fields, under the fords, under the floor of the oldest workshop — lies the **gloam**: the world's dark, patient store of spent light. Settled light does not stay settled forever; it leaches, slow as roots drink, down out of wells and bridges and famous stews and into the ground, and the Fade is nothing more sinister than that leaching seen from above. The gloam is not a place of loss. It is the world's composting — the heap where every faded red roof and forgotten well-trick is broken back down into the plain, rich, unshaped brightness that everything new is made from. Makers who dig foundations know the gloam by touch: a spadeful from a gloam-rich hollow is warm a moment longer than the air can explain, and the clay from such a hollow is famously talkative.

The Fade, then, is not the world's enemy and not even the world's weather — it is the world's *digestion*, and Roundelay would die without it. The cautionary tale every apprentice hears is Sistren Hollow, which in the Year of the Amber Wasps grew so proud of its waking that it woke everything — every latch, every ladle, every fence-post — and re-made anything the moment it dulled, letting nothing leach. By the second summer the clay for a mile around had gone mute, the mornings came stale as old bread, and children born that year cried at rainbows without knowing why: the ground beneath Sistren Hollow had gone hungry, and there was nothing rising through it. The Hollow's elders did the only sane thing. They chose a third of the town, thanked it, and let it fade — and the town keeps that practice still, a rotation of deliberate paling they call *resting the streets*, and their clay is the most cheerful on the west water.

So the answer to *where does it go* is the same as a garden's: **down, and back**. Nothing the Fade takes is lost. The grey of a pale town is the grey of a field after harvest.

- **This could become:** a visible two-sided economy on the map — paling towns are literally charging the region's gloam, so heavily-faded regions offer richer materials, more talkative clay, and better courtship odds, while over-woken regions grow flashy but thin. Letting things fade becomes a real strategic choice, not just a failure state: the player can *rest* a node.
- **Visual:** cutaway earth like a hand-inked frontispiece — grey rooftops above, and beneath the soil-line a deep warm umber glow pooling under the palest streets, roots reaching into it. Gloam-rich hollows steam faintly at dawn. Palette: charcoal grey over ember-amber, the two never touching except through root-lines.

## 2. The gleam cycle — why carried light spends, and how it returns

*(This is Standing. Mechanism first, as required.)*

### `the-welling`

On the last night of the Chandlery Fair, in the Year of the Long Hail, the glassblower Orla Ninefires blew the festival globes before four hundred people, and the fair-wardens' ledger of lamp-oil — kept dry and dull for sixty years — records the strangest entry in its history: *the square's lamps burned past their oil.* Every lamp. By an hour. The wardens re-weighed the oil-casks twice and then wrote down what every maker already knew and no one had caught on paper: **a delighted crowd is a spring.** Delight opens the living the way rain opens soil, and through four hundred opened people the gloam under the fair-ground rose — up through boot-soles and held breath and laughter, into the air of the square, brightening whatever burned there. And when the crowd went home and the square went dark, the risen light did what rising water always does: it condensed on the coldest, nearest, most particular thing — the cause. By morning frost Orla Ninefires' knuckles held dusk-light, and they held it for a season.

That is the whole mechanism of gleam, and everything Layer 1 says about Standing falls out of it:

- **Gleam is condensation, not reward.** The world keeps no account of merit. Light rises through whoever is opened, and it beads on whoever did the opening — as impersonal, and as reliable, as dew choosing grass.
- **Gleam spends because carried light is a current.** A maker's light flows out through the hands into every hour of work — that outflow *is* the making; a woken card is carried light become caught light. Work that delights opens its watchers, the gloam rises, and the maker stands in their own weather: inflow meeting outflow. Work that falls flat opens no one. Nothing rises. The outflow continues — the hands still spent what the hands spent — and the maker simply dims, the way a spring-fed pool lowers in a dry week. A botched commission, an unmoved room, an asking carried past its season: each is a week without rain, never a blow.
- **Gleam returns because the gloam is everywhere and delight is cheap to cause.** This is why the way back runs through straw and dough and strangers' kitchens (see `steeped-stuff`): the humble crafts sit closest to the gloam, and the smallest true laugh is an opened person. The Quiet Walk is not mercy bolted onto the law. It is the law (see `going-fallow`).

Depletion and restoration are therefore one process seen in drought and in rain — which is exactly the wondrous, non-punitive identity D-002 asks Standing to have. The world never docks a dim maker anything. The weather over them has simply gone dry, and weather turns.

- **This could become:** Standing rendered as a personal water-table — a meter that never ticks down from "damage," only from outflow-exceeding-inflow, so every loss is legible as *that room did not open*. Audience size and openness become the run's true economy: play patterns that delight watchers literally irrigate the maker. At zero, the mechanics of the Quiet Walk (below) take over.
- **Visual:** the condensation moment, paintable exactly as it happened at the Chandlery Fair: a dark emptied square, one figure, and light beading onto her knuckles out of the air like dew forming in time-lapse — dozens of pin-bright droplets of shine gathering on skin. Gleam-loss is its opposite: not a dimming knob but a dry wind, the rim-light thinning like a puddle's edge retreating.

## 3. The asking ratchet — why a town's needs grow one way

*(This is the escalating-contract law. Mechanism first, as required.)*

### `rumor-runnels`

In the Year of the Paper Moon, the bridgewright Tansy Oldknot re-set the Nine Sisters ford before a fair-day crowd, and the thing that happened next was measured by lantern-keepers across three valleys, because lantern-keepers measure everything. Within one season, the asking-lantern at Duskmallow — ninety miles from the Nine Sisters, across two ridgelines Tansy had never crossed — lowered from its rafters a great weaving-asking it had kept aloft for twenty-two years, and it lowered it *the week before Tansy arrived*, angled toward the road she came in by. The lantern was not guessing. It was reading the ground.

Here is the mechanism. **Rising light cuts channels.** Every time the tale of a making is told — at a hearth, in a market, by a murmuring kettle to a listening house — the telling opens its hearers a little, a thread of light rises through them, and that thread, running from the tale's subject toward wherever the tale travels, wears a groove in the world the way a trickle wears a runnel in a hillside. Rumor is not *like* a watercourse in Roundelay; rumor **is** one — a runnel of risen light, cut from the maker outward along every road their story walks. And need, being a low place (a pale town is literally low: its settled light has leached), drains along whatever channels reach it. A town's needs travel its runnels toward the brightest ground those runnels touch, and hang themselves on the lantern there.

The ratchet falls out of the hydrology, one-way by nature:

- **Runnels deepen with use and do not silt within a working life.** Every asking that travels a channel cuts it deeper; every telling of the answered asking cuts it deeper still. Water does not un-carve a hillside, and rumor does not un-travel. The land forgets a runnel only over generations — which is why retired makers can walk into a town and be nobody, and why they all call it a relief.
- **Askings size themselves to the channel, and channels are cut by your brightest hour.** A deep runnel carries a great asking as easily as a shallow one carries a kettle; a lantern lowers what the arriving channels can bear. This is why need aims at the brightest the road has heard you be, and never lower: the groove your best work cut is simply *there*, in the ground, carrying.
- **Skipped needs compound at the Fade's own gentle rate** (see `the-paling-arithmetic`): a pale town keeps leaching while you're away, so the same runnel delivers a larger asking when the road brings you back — larger, and prouder, and twice the wonder.

Nothing demands. Nothing enforces. Escalation is what a watershed does when one hillside keeps catching the rain: everything low finds its way there. Being asked is the honor; the depth of your runnels is the road's map of everything you have ever been at your best.

- **This could become:** the contract-escalation curve as literal terrain — a persistent, partly-visible runnel map overlaying the circuit, drawn by the player's own play history, along which future askings visibly travel toward them. Peak-gleam-this-run cuts channel depth; channel depth sets the asking tier a lantern offers; nothing ever re-shallows mid-run. The player can *see* their reputation as landscape.
- **Visual:** hillsides at dusk with hair-thin lines of gold running in the grass seams, all bending toward one lit figure on the road — a watershed diagram redrawn as a blessing. At Duskmallow: the lantern in the gate-dark, one great asking descending slowly on its red thread, turning, while faint gold runnels converge on the gatepost like streams meeting a river.

### `the-paling-arithmetic`

The Fade leaches at a rate every lantern-keeper can recite: *gentle, steady, and compounding* — because knack shores up knack, and each trick a place forgets uncovers the next trick to the weather. A well that forgets sweetness soon forgets depth; a bridge that forgets carts begins forgetting rain. Keepers chalk a town's paling on the lantern-post in a spiral, one ring per season unanswered, and everyone on the Rounds can read the spiral at a glance: three rings is a plea, five is a poem, seven is a great asking being born.

- **This could become:** the exact compounding function for skipped nodes — legible, chalked on the map itself, deterministic. Skip a three-ring town and you know precisely what five rings will demand and pay. Route strategy becomes actuarial in a warm way: reading spirals is reading the future.
- **Visual:** a whitewash spiral on dark wood, rings widening like tree-growth, the outermost ring still wet. Wide shot: a pale town where every gatepost, door, and cart-side carries its small chalk spiral — a town annotated with its own patience.

## 4. The rest of the cycle — weathers, mornings, materials, fallows

### `weathers-of-regard`

Because light circulates, it has weather. Some mornings the gloam rises easy through everyone — *glisten-days*, when strangers grin at nothing and any street performance draws a crowd — and some weeks it sits low and the whole valley is hard to delight, not sad, just watertight, the way ground can be too dry to take rain. Makers read attention-weather the way sailors read sky: festival seasons are monsoon, deep winter is drought, and the rarest weather of all is a *hush* — an hour when the rising and the leaching balance perfectly, nothing moving either way, in which woken things all point the same direction and old makers stop work and stand in it like sun.

- **This could become:** run-level and node-level modifiers with full diegetic legibility — audience openness, welling rates, and material courtship odds all keyed to a forecastable weather layer. Festivals as high-tide events worth routing toward; hushes as rare zero-entropy turns where waking thresholds drop for everyone.
- **Visual:** weather rendered on people, not sky — a glisten-day market where every face catches light like wet stones; a drought-week where the same market is handsome but matte. A hush: one held frame, dust motionless in a sunbeam, forty gold working-edges in perfect parallel.

### `morrowlight`

What rises from the gloam does not all rise through delighted crowds; most of it seeps back the slow way, overnight, into everything — and that seepage is why mornings in Roundelay feel *made fresh*. Morrowlight is not a shine (nothing gleams but people; the channel law holds): it is a readiness. Dawn colors sit a shade more saturated because places' settled light tops up by night; bread proves better before noon; children learn fastest before breakfast; and materials are most courtable at first light, having drunk all night from the ground. Every workshop on the Rounds opens early not from virtue but from hydrology.

- **This could become:** a day-phase rhythm inside each town visit — mornings favor courtship and waking (lower thresholds), evenings favor audiences and welling (hearths, crowds, tellings) — giving each node an internal tempo the player sequences.
- **Visual:** the same street painted twice: at dawn, colors full and edges dewy, jars on a shelf leaning imperceptibly toward the window; at dusk, colors settling, windows lighting one by one like a pulse moving indoors.

### `steeped-stuff`

Now the open secret of the apprentice materials. Straw, dough, river clay — the gregarious stuff that talks to anyone — are the crafts' humblest matter and the cosmology's richest: they are **gloam-steeped**, harvested from exactly where spent light pools. River clay is silt from the bottoms of runnels; straw stood all season in fields drinking morrowlight; dough is the steeped grain of that straw, warmed and worked by hands. They chatter with the dim because they are *full* — brimming with returned, unshaped light that asks nothing because it lacks nothing. The proud materials are the opposite: silver, festival glass, the green riverwood are light-poor and light-hungry, which is why they interview their makers — a shy material is choosing whose current it will drink from. Courtship, underneath, is irrigation.

- **This could become:** the material tiers re-derived from a single stat — gloam-saturation — that sets both courtship difficulty and waking ceiling: steeped stuff wakes easily but modestly; hungry stuff demands bright hands and stated terms, then wakes brilliantly, having drunk deep. One number, two curves, fully diegetic.
- **Visual:** matter with visible water-table — a cross-lit lump of river clay glowing faintly from within like a paper lantern in fog, beside a bar of silver that reads as a black mirror, beautiful and empty. The apprentice shelf warm and murmuring; the proud cabinet cold and watchful.

### `the-shedlands` *(anchor — the hydrological map: watersheds, confluences, high ground and low, kept deliberately familiar so that the layer's strange core claim — attention as the world's one circulating substance, pooling and draining across real geography — stays instantly legible in a single glance at a map)*

Light drains and pools across country the way water does, and the Rounds were walked into being along its watersheds. Towns grow at confluences of old runnels the way river-towns grow at forks; ridgelines between rumor-sheds are the loneliest miles on any circuit, where tales from either side don't carry; and the great fairs all sit in basins where dozens of runnels meet — natural harbors of attention, brimming every festival season. A Round, seen from above, is not an arbitrary loop: it is a contour line, the path along which one pair of hands can keep a whole shedland's light moving.

- **This could become:** map generation with laws — node placement, connectivity, and asking-flow all derived from a generated attention-watershed, so route strategy reads as geography rather than graph. Ridge crossings as high-risk/high-quiet passages; confluence towns as hubs where askings from many runnels pool.
- **Visual:** the circuit map as an illuminated hydrological survey — hair-fine gold contour lines, towns as pooled drops at the confluences, the player's route a bright meander among them. The single most portfolio-ready image of the layer: *a watershed map where the water is light.*

### `going-fallow`

Why does the Quiet Walk work? Because a maker with no pack, no pieces, and no askings has, for the first time since childhood, **no outflow** — and the law never stopped running. Farmers rest fields; the Rounds rest makers. An unlit walker fed at hearths and asked for nothing is soil lying fallow under the whole slow weather of the world: morrowlight seeps in at dawn, tellings at strangers' fires open them nightly, and the first laugh they cause with an empty pack — the only thing an empty pack still holds — condenses on them like first dew, exactly as it did at the Chandlery Fair, at the only scale left. Some makers walk one season and come back bright. Some go fallow for good, settle by the sea, and become the deepest kind of ground: pure audience, all inflow given away nightly to whoever performs at their hearth. The Rounds honor both, and the word for both is the same. Fallow is not fallow because it stopped being a field.

- **This could become:** the run-end epilogue's actual rules — a short playable fallow in which the player *cannot* spend, only attend, and where attending (choosing what to watch, whom to laugh with, which of the run's woken works to visit) determines the meta-boon that seeds the next maker. Ending a run by being an audience is the theme made mechanical.
- **Visual:** dawn-grey circuit, an un-rimmed figure at a hearth among rimmed ones — and one frame later, the year's first droplet of gleam on the back of a laughing hand, small as a seed pearl. Quietest image in the game; hang the whole ending on it.

## 5. The closed rounds — the I-004 decision

### `closed-rounds`

Here is how much of the long riddle exists, fixed as canon.

**What an unmarked wonder *is* — fully knowable, and discoverable in play.** An unmarked wonder is a **closed round of light**: caught light circulating in a loop, edge feeding edge, the way a round-song is a melody feeding its own beginning. In every ordinary made thing, caught light holds still and settled light leaches away beneath it; in an unmarked wonder, the light *moves without leaving* — around the stair, along the river's whole length, through the bell and back — never settling, so the Fade finds nothing still enough to take; never spending, so it needs no maker's inflow. This is measurable, and makers have measured it: the gloam beneath a wonder is thin and hungry, as under a roof; frost will not pattern on one; and every unmarked wonder, held to the ear on a hush, hums *in rounds* — a phrase that returns to its own beginning, which is where the world got the name for itself, or the name got the world, and canon declines to say which. The gold working-edge running along *every* line of a wonder is this law made visible: in a closed round, every edge is a working edge, because the work never stops passing.

**What is *not* in canon — permanently.** How a round closes. No maker has ever closed one on purpose; no one has ever watched one begin; every wonder was found already singing. And canon commits to this hard: **there is no "who."** Not elder makers, not a vanished people, not gods — not because the answer is hidden, but because the question is malformed, the way "who is the author of this echo" is malformed. A closed round is an event in the ecology, like a spring or an oxbow lake — lawful, occasioned, and authorless. The cheerful heresy — that the world has started making itself, the way an old workshop finishes small jobs overnight — remains a heresy: the closed-round law is agentless, so the heresy is never confirmed; and wonders do keep being found, so it is never quite killed either. The Rounds like it that way.

**The knowability line, stated for all later layers:** *the law is knowable; the occasion is not.* A run may discover, assemble, and mechanically exploit everything in the first paragraph. Nothing in the game — no relic text, no collection-screen completion, no endgame — ever answers the second. The riddle's floor is canon; the riddle's ceiling is open sky.

- **This could become:** the relic system with a discoverable science — wonder-study as a collection metagame where each found wonder yields one verified property of closed rounds (Fade-immune, gloam-thin beneath, hums in rounds, all-edges-working), and completed study unlocks the game's strangest card behaviors: pieces that feed their own cost, loops that sustain without attention, an engine that briefly *closes*. The final entry in the study is, canonically and forever, blank.
- **Visual:** edge-light on every line, softly, like a drawing the light colored before anyone else could — and around it, subtle famine: thin frost-free ground, grass leaning away, a ring of ordinary matte world holding its breath. A bell whose ring is drawn as a circle of gold thread returning to the clapper.

### `the-round-of-light`

And so the master element, the one the whole layer sits inside: the world's light runs in one great round — settled, carried, caught; leached, welled, condensed — and every practice on the Rounds, from hearth-right to the Quiet Walk to the lantern's slow choosing, is a verse of it. Roundelay is not named after its songs. The songs are named after the world: melody passed voice to voice around a circle is the local, human-sized copy of what light does at every scale, and a maker's whole life is one carried bar of it. The unmarked wonders are the round sung closed; the gloam is the rest between verses; the player's run is a turn of the tune.

- **This could become:** the frame that makes every subsystem one system — attention as the game's single conserved quantity, visibly flowing between board zones (places, people, things, ground), so that advanced players can literally trace where any point of light in their engine came from and where it goes when spent. Conservation as the deepest form of legibility.
- **Visual:** the layer's cosmological plate, painted like a folk-art seasons-wheel: a circle of ground, hearth, hands, and made things with one continuous gold line threading through all of them and returning — no top, no bottom, no throne anywhere on it.

## 6. Consistency and register notes (for critics)

- **Spine inevitability.** Standing = carried light under welling-and-outflow (§2, mechanism first, dated at the Chandlery Fair, Year of the Long Hail). Escalating contracts = askings draining down one-way-deepening rumor-runnels, compounded by the paling arithmetic (§3, mechanism first, dated at the Nine Sisters ford / Duskmallow lantern, Year of the Paper Moon). Neither is imposed; both are hydrology.
- **Fade non-hostile:** composting, load-bearing, and demonstrated *necessary* (Sistren Hollow); a town deliberately rests its own streets. Nothing fights it; the grey is post-harvest, not post-battle.
- **Channel ban holds at the law level:** settled/carried/caught light are distinct phases that cannot mix; morrowlight is explicitly a readiness, never a shine; gloam-glow appears only underground in cutaway, never as scene key-light.
- **Vocabulary ban:** no diegetic term in this layer draws on owing-language; all failure states are drought, silt, shyness, and fallow.
- **No creation myth, by law:** the round has no first singer; origin questions are canonically malformed, which forecloses god-song, primordial war, and shattered-unity boilerplate at the root.
- **I-004 resolved** at §5 with an explicit knowability line; riddle ban honored by making "who" a category error rather than a secret.
- **Anchors:** one — the hydrological familiar (declared in §0 and carried by `the-shedlands`), 1 of 11 elements. Everything else is claimed as novel process.
