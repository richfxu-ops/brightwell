# Layer 2 Proposal B — The Open Round

**Lens: cosmogony as living practice.** Roundelay's cosmology is not a story about how the world began. It is a description of what the world is *doing right now*, and it is taught the way everything true in Roundelay is taught: by being sung in a circle until the shape of the song and the shape of the world turn out to be the same shape. There is no creation myth in this proposal because the world contains no creation event — see `the-open-round` — and the player is not standing downstream of the making. The player is inside it, mid-verse, holding one bar.

The whole cosmology reduces to three laws. Children learn them as three actual rounds sung in every town (see `the-teaching-rounds`), which is why nobody in Roundelay experiences their cosmology as theology. They experience it as something they have known how to sing since before they could carry a tune.

- **The Law of the Carry** — to exist is to be carried by attention; light is carrying made visible.
- **The Law of the Breath** — what is least carried is set down, travels dark, and rises elsewhere; nothing is lost.
- **The Law of the Polish** — carrying keeps the brightest of a thing and wears away the particular.

Everything below — the Fade, the gleam, the one-way growth of the askings, and the exact size of the unmarked-wonders riddle — falls out of these three, and each law is stated first as a mechanism the world can point to, in a place, on a date.

---

## 1. What attention is: the Law of the Carry

A round-song has a property that no other made thing has: it does not exist except while it is being carried. The score is not the song; the memory is not the song; the song is the *singing*, passed voice to voice, and the instant every voice sets it down there is no song — not a paused song, not a sleeping song, no song. Roundelay's deepest claim about itself is that the world is like that. Not *like* a round, in the way of a poet's comparison — built the same way, obeying the same mechanics, failing the same way if the singing stops.

**Attention is the carrying.** When you attend to a thing — rest your whole regard on it, work it, use it, dwell in it, tell of it — you are carrying its figure for a few bars, holding it in the song, keeping it the case. This is what Layer 1's law "light is attention made visible" means underneath: light is not a substance attention gives off. Light is what *being carried looks like from outside.* A thing shines to the exact degree that the world currently has hold of it.

And the grammar of light — places fade, people gleam, things wake, three channels that never cross — stops being a mystery and becomes an inventory, because there are exactly three ways to be carried:

- **Places are carried by being dwelt in.** A place's light is worked into it slowly, from all sides, by everyone who lives it — which is why a place's light drains the same way, from the edges inward, gradually, when the dwelling thins. That draining is the Fade, and it can only happen to places and the made-and-kept, because only they are carried this way.
- **Things are carried by being used and watched.** A thing's carrying concentrates where the use lives — which is why a woken thing's light is a single thin gold line along the *working edge*, and nowhere else. The lip of the bowl is where the world holds the bowl.
- **People are not figures in the song at all. People are voices.** A voice cannot be carried the way a bar of melody is carried — but a voice can be *taken up*: its figures sung elsewhere, its bell retold at a hundred hearths. The light on a maker is not in the maker. It is thrown onto them, from wherever their work is being carried at that hour. This is the gleam, and it is the whole of Section 3.

One law, three grips, three lights. No layer below this one needs to remember three rules; it needs to remember one, and ask *how is this being held?*

**Why making renews the world** — the lens's central question — is now answerable in one sentence: making is how figures enter the round. A round survives on entries; it has no other mechanism of survival. Every attended act of craft is a new bar offered to the circle of carriers — the audience of things leaning in *is* the world taking the new figure up — and re-making a paled well is not repair, it is re-entry: giving the world a fresh grip on something it had, lawfully and without malice, set down. Makers are not the world's maintenance crew. They are its next voices, and the world is exactly as finished as a round is: never.

## 2. The Fade: the Law of the Breath, and where the light goes

Sixty years back, in the winter everyone still calls **the Hushed Winter of the Closed Combs**, snow shut every pass into the Weftdale for a full season and no maker crossed in or out. The dale did not suffer a disaster; it simply went unattended at the edges, and by thaw the whole valley had paled together — grey rooftops, forgetful fords, a famous echo gone flat — the largest single paling in living memory. What happened next is the reason this cosmology is believed rather than merely taught. The spring that followed was **the Loud Spring**: apprentice clay so eager it shaped itself half a turn ahead of the hands, children's first pots waking on the first firing, whole streets re-colored in weeks as fast as makers could touch them, the ground under the fords humming loud enough to feel through boot-soles. The dale's grandmothers said what Roundelay has always said, and this time everyone could hear it was literally true: *nothing was lost. It went down, and around, and it is coming back up.*

Here is the mechanism. A round is music, not drone, because voices rest. No singer can carry every bar at once — that is not a rule imposed on the song, it is what having a voice *is* — so at any moment most of the world's figures are being held and some, the least carried, are being set down. The Fade is the setting-down: the world's regular, breath-like release of whatever is currently gripped most thinly. This is why unwoken work pales first (it entered the round with almost no carriers), why the pale towns are called *pale* and not *wounded* (nothing has been done to them), and why the Fade can never be fought, hated, or defeated: you cannot be the enemy of an exhale.

And the exhaled light **goes somewhere lawful**: down into `the-ground-bass` — the under-hum of the world, the slow dark line that runs beneath every Round the way a ground bass runs under a canon. Set-down light does not travel as light (nothing visible moves; the channel ban holds — this is felt and heard, never seen) but as hum, migrating along under the circuits, pooling where the land dips, and rising again as `first-light`: the unearned brightness of every dawn, the chattiness of straw and dough and river clay (the apprentice materials are simply the stuff that lives closest to the hum), the well-known ease of morning work, the outsized eagerness of anything long-paled once hands finally return. The Fade and first-light are one tide seen at its two turnings. The world inhales at the pale edges and exhales through every next making — which is why a maker walking into a grey town is not walking into a loss. They are walking into the exact place where the world's breath is due back.

## 3. The gleam cycle: the Law of the Carry, read on a person's skin

*(Mechanism first, as bound.)*

In the Lantern-Rot spring, forty-one years gone, the fiddler **Brune of the Osier Round** wintered alone past his plan in the signal hut above Wader's Ford — the thaw came a month late, and no soul heard him play from first snow to last. He botched nothing that winter. He disappointed no one. And he came down the mountain a lamp's-worth dimmer than he went up, ordinary-lit, doors answering him a beat slow — and then brightened in a single evening at the ferry-house, *before he had unpacked his fiddle*, at the moment the ferryman's daughter recognized him, took up one of his reel-figures, and sang it back across the supper table. The Ford still tells it as a comedy. The cosmology tells it as a demonstration, the cleanest on record, of what gleam actually is.

**Gleam is thrown light.** It is not stored in the maker, not banked by good works, not a substance at all: it is the visible sum of your figures being carried *elsewhere, right now* — every hearth retelling your bridge tonight, every woken kettle humming a bar of yours, every stretch of road that remembers your step, each one throwing its small share of light back along the line to you. That is why the key-light on a maker seems to come from a source just behind the shoulder: it has sources, hundreds of them, all offstage. Brune dimmed without failing because for one season nothing anywhere was carrying him — and relit without working because carrying, not work, is what the light *is*; the work is merely what gives the world something worth taking up.

From this mechanism, the mechanical shape of Standing falls out whole, with nothing added:

- **It depletes** — because carrying lapses. An unmoved audience takes nothing up. A botched commission is set down at the hearths that would have told it (houses do not hum a sour bar; they let it drop, which is gentler and worse). An asking carried past its season goes quiet in the town that crafted it. Each lapsed carrier is one less source throwing light, and the maker dims the way a room dims when lamps are carried out one by one — gradual, legible, nobody's punishment.
- **It restores** — because delight given, in any coin the world honors, hands the carriers a new bar. One genuine laugh in a stranger's kitchen is one hearth carrying you again by morning; this is the entire engineering of the Quiet Walk, which is not mercy but mechanism — an empty pack still holds the one gift that starts the carrying over.
- **It starts near zero** — because the world is shy of everyone at birth for the plainest reason imaginable: no one is carrying you yet.
- **It arrives on a delay** — because retellings take time to travel. A maker sometimes brightens at dusk for work done three towns back, as last week's figures reach new hearths. The light of a good deed is often *in the mail.*

Standing, in full: **a running census of who is singing you tonight.**

## 4. The asking ratchet: the Law of the Polish

*(Mechanism first, as bound.)*

At the Ember Fair of Candlemoor, in the year the Sallow flooded twice, the joiner **Petta Nin** woke the fair's great bell before four hundred watchers — the largest waking of her life, and she knew it; she spent the next season deliberately taking small work, kettles and gate-hinges, meaning to travel quietly. It made no difference at all. Three towns down the Round, the asking-lanterns were already lowering rafter-askings to meet her — a mill's whole voice, a flood-forgotten ford — because the story of the bell had gone ahead of her on the hearths, and the story that arrived was not her season of kettles. It was the bell. It is always the bell.

The mechanism is the Law of the Polish, and every singer alive can verify it at any fireside: **a carried song sheds its verses and keeps its highest note.** Retelling is carrying, and carriers hold what is brightest to hold — each hearth that passes a maker's figure along passes the peak of it and lets the middling bars fall quietly into the ground-bass with everything else the world sets down. Rumor does not un-travel (Layer 1's law), and now we know why it cannot: there is no mechanism in the round for carrying a figure *dimmer* than it arrived. Forgetting the peak is possible; diminishing it is not.

An asking-lantern, meanwhile, is a crafted listener. It sizes what it lowers by the brightest bar of a traveler its town's hearths have carried — not because towns are ambitious, but because that is the only note the polish has delivered to them. So the conventional shape — needs that grow one way, never down — is not a rule of towns or a custom of the Rounds. It is arithmetic on two one-way laws stacked:

- **The polish ratchets the sizing up**: every asking is matched to peak-heard brightness, and the peak is the only thing rumor is physically able to transmit.
- **The breath ratchets the need up**: a pale town passed by goes on being set down at its own gentle rate, so the same asking, met later, is a larger re-entry — and, by the Loud-Spring law of Section 2, a proportionally more wondrous one, because the ground-bass under a long-paled place is rich.

Nothing demands. Nothing dooms. The size of the asking is the compliment, exactly as Layer 1 felt it — and Layer 2 adds the quiet cosmological kicker every retiring maker eventually says out loud: the polish is also working on *you*. The road is already shedding your kettles and keeping your bell. You will be remembered better than you were, and asked accordingly.

## 5. The riddle, decided: how much of the unmarked-wonders answer exists

Carried issue I-004. The decision, in three tiers, from fully canon to lawfully absent:

**Tier 1 — the persistence of unmarked wonders is knowable, known, and taught (CANON).** It is the Law of the Carry at saturation. An unmarked wonder is a *fully carried figure*: a thing attended from so many sides, so continuously — used daily, told nightly, hummed by every woken thing in earshot, dwelt around, studied, hatted-to — that no single lapse can set it down. The stair stays bright for the same reason a round survives any one singer stopping: the carrying has closed into a circle with no thin place. This is why they are the permanent exception to the Fade, and it is not a mystery; apprentices learn it in their first teaching-round, and it is why makers treat the wonders as colleagues — they are the only things in the world held the way the world itself is held.

**Tier 2 — the unmarking is canon-true but unwitnessed (THE PLAYABLE EDGE OF THE RIDDLE).** The Law of the Polish says carrying wears away the particular. A maker's mark — the thumbprint, the signature bar in the woken hum — is the most particular thing about a made thing, and on a fully carried figure the polish never stops: a signature bar sung by every voice in earshot for long enough belongs, eventually, to no voice. Canon states plainly that this is where unmarked wonders come from — *marked things, carried past the point of belonging to anyone.* But no living witness has ever confirmed a mark's final disappearance; the process runs in generations, and the world holds exactly one disputed candidate, `the-bell-of-hask`, whose mark is either fading or was always faint, depending on which ferry you ask. The answer exists; the demonstration is still in progress; the game's collection screen is allowed to be part of the demonstration.

**Tier 3 — the origin question is not hidden; it is absent (PERMANENTLY OPEN, BY LAW).** Whose stair was it first? The cosmology's answer is that the world does not contain this fact — not as a secret, but the way a round does not contain its first singer: you always learned it from someone who learned it from someone, and behind every verse is more song. Asking where the round began is asking where a circle starts; the question is well-formed in grammar and empty in the world. The cheerful heresy — that the world may have started making itself, the way a very old workshop starts finishing small jobs overnight — therefore remains exactly what Layer 1 requires: a heresy, unconfirmable in principle rather than unconfirmed by accident, never cheapened because there is nothing beneath it to dig up. Not elder makers, not ancient civilizations, not gods — not *withheld*, but *nowhere*.

So the long riddle's true shape, for the player: the *how* is law, the *becoming* is watchable at the very edge of a lifetime, and the *who* is a door painted on a wall — and the game can honor the highest hope a maker can hold, which is Tier 2 pointed forward: work carried long enough stops being yours and starts being the world's. See `the-unmarking` for the ludic form.

## 6. Named elements

#### `the-open-round`

The world is a round-song with no final cadence and no first verse: it exists the way a canon exists, only while carried, and it has been in progress for exactly as long as anything could check. This is not a story about a singer who began it — Roundelay was not sung into being by anyone, and the cosmology contains no creation event, no author, no downbeat; behind every verse is more song, and the beginning is not lost knowledge but a question the world does not contain, like the starting point of a circle. What it has instead of an origin is a *condition*: the round is open, meaning unfinished, meaning alive — a finished song is the only thing Roundelay would recognize as a dead one.

- **This could become:** the run-and-meta frame at cosmological scale — the game structurally refuses a final boss or completion state; the campaign is a circle of makers' turns, and "beating the game" is handing the song on well (see `a-turn-of-the-song`). Also the diegetic reason the roguelike loops: the form of play *is* the form of the world.
- **Visual:** the world map drawn as an illuminated circular canon — a ring of staff-lines where towns sit as notes, with no clef and no double-bar anywhere; when the full map is shown, the camera slowly orbits, because a circle has no correct way up.

#### `the-carry`

Attention, named as world-law: to attend to a thing is to carry its figure in the round, and to be carried is the whole of what existing amounts to. There are exactly three grips — places are carried by dwelling, things by use-and-watching, people by being taken up and told — and light is carrying made visible, which is why the three grips are the three channels of the grammar of light and can never cross. Makers say *hold it while I work* to a room of woken tools, and mean it as physics.

- **This could become:** the unification underneath Layer 1's attention meter — one currency (carrying) whose three grips give three legible sub-rules: audience-things and townsfolk provide carry, unbroken work-chains sustain it, and every mechanic in the game that touches light must answer "which grip is this?" A designer's law as much as a player's: no fourth channel, ever.
- **Visual:** the triptych establishing shot — one frame, three lights: a greying lane (place, draining from the edges), a dusk-lit maker (person, keyed from behind the shoulder), a kettle with one gold lip (thing, lit only on the working edge) — the game's whole visual grammar teachable in a single painting.

#### `the-ground-bass`

Where set-down light goes: a slow dark under-hum that runs beneath the Rounds like the repeating bass line under a canon, carrying everything the Fade has gathered — spent color, forgotten knacks, shed verses, dissolved signature bars — down, and along, and around. It is never seen (spent light travels dark; the eye has no business here) but it is felt through boot-soles at fords and heard in cellar corners on still nights, and every child has lain with an ear to the ground on a sleeping hillside and heard the world keeping what it took. Nothing in Roundelay is lost; it is somewhere else in the song.

- **This could become:** the consolation economy of the roguelike — losses feed a lawful reservoir. Regions that pale during a run visibly enrich the hum beneath them, raising the first-light yield of future visits and future runs; a failed run literally deepens the ground the next maker walks on. Grief mechanics without punishment: the Fade's ledger-free bookkeeping. *(Non-diegetic note: "ledger-free" — the hum holds no accounts; enrichment is diffuse, local, and never traceable to a claimant.)*
- **Visual:** a cutaway strip beneath the hand-inked circuit map — a wide, slow band of deep indigo-brown moving like a river under the parchment, thickening under pale towns; where it runs rich, dawn scenes above it get a faint gold ground-mist that burns off by mid-morning.

#### `first-light`

*(Anchor — dawn-as-daily-renewal is kept deliberately familiar, the one convention every player's body already believes, so that the novel conservation law underneath it — the Fade's takings returning by way of the ground-bass — is felt every morning rather than explained once.)* First-light is the rising turn of the world's breath: the hour when the ground-bass crests closest to the surface and everything is briefly easier to carry. Apprentice materials chatter loudest at dawn because straw, dough, and river clay live closest to the hum; children's first works wake most often at morning firings; and the old instruction to *start the true work at first light* is not discipline but tide-reading. Over long-paled country, first-light comes up almost rowdy — the Loud Spring was one long first-light that lasted a season.

- **This could become:** turn structure with a diegetic pulse — each in-game dawn grants a waking-threshold discount or a free carry tick, sized by the hum under the current node; pale regions become high-yield mornings, turning the Fade's pressure map into an opportunity map read at a glance.
- **Visual:** dawn painted as rising rather than arriving — gold that seeps up from field-seams, well-mouths, and floorboard cracks for one held minute before ordinary sunlight takes over; in rich-hum country the effect is visibly taller, like grass-fires of light that don't burn.

#### `hum-wells`

Places where the ground-bass runs shallow enough to work by: a dip in the land, a ford, a cellar corner, a bend where three Rounds' under-lines braid. Towns are founded on them without knowing it and discover it in their materials — hum-well clay is famously gregarious, hum-well bread rises early, and the finest workshops on the Rounds turn out, when the grandmothers compare notes, to stand over the braid-points. Makers camp on them by feel, the way animals bed down warm.

- **This could become:** map-node typology — hum-well nodes offer material-courtship discounts, cheaper wakings, and superior camp rests; scouting for them (listening at fords, watching where clay perks up) is a light exploration verb that rewards route mastery on repeat runs.
- **Visual:** subtle concentric ground-rings around a well or ford, visible only at dawn and dusk like ripples in dust; every loose woken thing in the frame — buckets, lasts, a leaned broom — tilted a degree toward the center, iron filings around a slow magnet.

#### `the-far-singing`

The mechanism of gleam: a maker's light is thrown, not stored — the visible sum of their figures being carried elsewhere right now, every retelling hearth and humming kettle a small offstage source. Brune of the Osier Round dimmed in a winter of perfect solitude and relit at one sung-back reel, and every apprentice learns his story before they learn a single grip: the light is not in you; it is *aimed at you*, from wherever you are being sung tonight. Dimming is lamps leaving the room one by one; brightening is handing the world a bar worth taking up.

- **This could become:** Standing's full mechanical identity — depletion on lapsed carriers (failed askings, unmoved audiences, seasons overstayed), restoration on any delight given, and the signature twist: *delayed arrivals*, Standing ticks that land turns after the deed as retellings reach new hearths, so a run's early generosity pays out mid-run like light in the mail.
- **Visual:** the Standing meter as literal offstage sources — at full gleam the maker's key-light is warm and slightly multiple, casting two or three soft overlapping shoulder-shadows; each lapse removes one shadow. A dusk crowd scene where the lit can be counted by shadow-plurality alone.

#### `the-kept-note`

The Law of the Polish in its traveling form: a carried song sheds its verses and keeps its highest note, because retelling hearths pass a figure's peak and let its middling bars fall into the ground-bass. Rumor therefore cannot transmit diminishment — there is no grip in the round for carrying a thing dimmer than it arrived — and an asking-lantern, which sizes what it lowers by the brightest bar its town has heard of you, can only ever size upward. Petta Nin took a season of kettles after the Candlemoor bell, and the lanterns lowered mill-voices anyway; the road had already shed her kettles.

- **This could become:** the escalation law of the contract system, stated as a single visible rule — available asking tier equals peak-gleam-this-run, never current gleam, displayed as a small "brightest-heard" notch that only climbs; players feel escalation as reputation physics, and the strategic texture (spend bright, or stay deliberately small?) falls out of choosing *when* to set a new peak.
- **Visual:** the maker's rumor rendered as a literal traveling figure on the map — a small gold notation-mark skipping hearth to hearth ahead of the player along the Round, shedding tiny grey grace-notes as it goes and arriving at the next town as a single high bright note above the gate-lantern.

#### `the-loud-spring`

The season that proved the cosmology: after the Hushed Winter of the Closed Combs sealed the Weftdale for a year and paled it wholesale, the following spring came up so rich from the loaded ground-bass that clay worked half a turn ahead of the hands, first-fired pots woke for children, and the dale re-colored in weeks. It is remembered not as recovery but as *demonstration* — the one time the Law of the Breath ran loud enough for everyone to hear both turnings of the tide in a single year. Weftdale grandmothers remain unbearable about it.

- **This could become:** a rare region-scale event — any region paled past a deep threshold and then answered blooms into a Loud Spring state: waking discounts, eager materials, outsized re-color rewards. A designed temptation with teeth: letting a valley pale on purpose to farm the bloom is possible, visible, and slow enough to cost the whole route plan — the game's one legal deal with the tide. *(Non-diegetic note: needs balance gating; flag for L4.)*
- **Visual:** the Fade's palette run in reverse and over-cranked — color returning not in watercolor blooms but in floods, wet paint chasing the maker's footsteps down a street, roofs blushing rows at a time, with the gold ground-mist of a rich hum standing knee-deep at dawn.

#### `a-turn-of-the-song`

What one maker's working life — and one run — *is*, cosmically: a single voice's entry in the open round. You come in on someone else's bar (every apprentice is taught by a journeyman who was taught), you carry the figure your own distance in your own timbre, and you hand it on mid-song, because a round has no other way to be left. Retirement, the Quiet Walk, even a dimming that never rekindles — all are the same cadence: the voice rests; the song does not. Makers' gravestones on the Rounds carry no dates, only a bar of notation: the figure as it was received, and the small change they made that stuck.

- **This could become:** the meta-progression frame — at run's end (Quiet Walk or bright retirement), the player chooses which single change to the song persists: one woken work becomes a permanent map fixture, one journey-piece enters the next maker's starting pack *altered by how it was played*, one road remembers. Legacy as hand-off, not unlock ladder.
- **Visual:** the run-end card — the circuit map's inked line lifting off the parchment as three bars of golden notation, the middle bar visibly reshaped, settling back down into the pale pencil line that the *next* run's map draws itself over.

#### `the-teaching-rounds`

*(Anchor — the nursery-song-as-catechism is kept deliberately familiar folk furniture, so that the three laws of the cosmology — carry, breath, polish, all novel — arrive in the player's ear as something between a skipping rhyme and a physics lesson, which in Roundelay is not a joke but the method.)* Every town teaches its children three rounds — the Carry-round, the Breath-round, the Polish-round — sung in a circle at dusk, each child holding a bar and handing it on, and the lesson is not *in* the songs, it *is* the songs: you learn that the world is carried by carrying something, that rests are part of the music by resting, that songs keep their brightest by hearing tonight's verse arrive brighter than you sang it yesterday. Verses differ town to town; the laws never do; and no one in Roundelay can tell you when they learned the cosmology, any more than they can remember learning to walk.

- **This could become:** the tutorial and the rules-text register in one — mechanics taught as verse fragments (card reminder-text quotes the relevant teaching-round line), and the tutorial staged diegetically as the maker's own first dusk-circle, so the game's rulebook is a thing the world sings about itself.
- **Visual:** the dusk-circle — children in a ring on packed earth, one bar of breath-fog each in the cold air, and around them every woken thing in the yard (hoops, pails, a rocking horse) tilted inward at the listening angle; no light effect on the children at all, and the scene warm anyway.

#### `the-unmarking`

The Law of the Polish at a lifetime's scale, pointed at the riddle: on a fully carried figure the polish never stops, and a maker's mark — the most particular thing about a made thing — is slowly worn from it by being sung in every voice, until the signature bar belongs to no one and the thing stands in the round the way the round itself stands. Canon holds this to be where unmarked wonders come from; canon also holds that no living witness has seen a mark finish leaving, because the process runs in generations. The highest hope a maker can hold has an exact shape: not to be remembered forever, but to make one thing carried so well that it forgets you first.

- **This could become:** the deep legacy system — a persistent work (see `a-turn-of-the-song`) that survives enough future runs, gets used, retold, and built around, visibly loses its maker's mark stage by stage across a save file; completing an unmarking is the game's ultimate meta-achievement, proving the law forward without ever unsealing the old wonders' pasts.
- **Visual:** a four-state object portrait across runs — the thumbprint in the glaze softening like a coin's face under a century of hands, while the gold working-edge spreads line by line toward the everywhere-at-once edge-light of the true wonders; final state rendered slightly too in-focus, per Layer 1's rule.

#### `the-bell-of-hask`

The one disputed instance: a ferry-crossing bell at Hask, marked — probably — with a signature bar that the oldest listeners swear was plainer in their grandmothers' day. Bell-listeners come from three Rounds away to hum along and argue; the ferry sells more crossings on argument days; the Haskers themselves refuse to settle it, on the grounds (stated on a board by the slip, in six generations of handwriting) that a riddle carried this well would be a shame to set down. If the mark ever finishes leaving, the world's first witnessed unmarking will happen at a ferry dock, between crossings, to no fanfare the bell itself doesn't provide.

- **This could become:** a unique persistent landmark — visitable across all runs, its mark audibly/visibly fainter as save-file generations accumulate, functioning as the long riddle's clock and the collection screen's centerpiece; possibly the trigger location for rare unmarked-relic discovery events.
- **Visual:** a small weathered bell over grey water at the game's most painterly hour, its working edge bright but its flank's engraved bar half-swallowed by polish-glow — always painted from slightly too far away to read, with a knot of tiny argued-out figures on the dock beneath it.

## 7. Anchor declaration

Two anchors in this layer, each declared inline at its element, each spending its familiarity on one novel law:

1. **`the-teaching-rounds`** — nursery-rhyme-as-folk-catechism, kept deliberately conventional so the three laws (carry, breath, polish) are learned by the player the way the world learns them: by ear, in a circle, without a lore screen.
2. **`first-light`** — dawn-as-renewal, the one convention the player's body already believes, kept so the genuinely strange conservation claim (the Fade's takings travel dark underground and rise as the morning) is *felt daily* instead of asserted once.

Anchor share: 2 of 12. Everything else in the layer — thrown gleam, the kept note, the ground-bass, the unmarking, the authorless open round — is carried on the two anchors' backs, not alongside them.

## 8. Compliance notes (non-diegetic)
