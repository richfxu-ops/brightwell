# Layer 3 Proposal A — Craft Traditions

**Lens: factions as schools of making.** Roundelay's peoples do not divide by blood, territory, or creed. They divide — cheerfully, endlessly, at every fair and porch — over the only question the world makes urgent: *how is regard best set into the world?* Fired or held? One great work or a thousand small delights? Court the shy silver or keep faith with the common clay? Walk the bright roads or the grey ones? Build the work, or build the room the work wakes in?

Six answers have walked the Rounds long enough to become traditions. A tradition is not a hall and keeps no rolls; it is **a way of walking** (see `the-walking-schools`), carried in walking-songs, journey-pieces, and the grain of its makers' gleam. You are Kilnfast because Kilnfast work is what your hands do, a Kilnfast master once gave you a first piece, and whoever happened to be on the road that day saw the giving. Nothing is signed because nothing in Roundelay is signed. The traditions disagree the way rivers disagree about the best way down a mountain — completely, and without ever once colliding.

Every tradition below is a candidate player archetype: a distinct way of making, traveling, and spending light.

## The six traditions

### `the-kilnfast`

The Kilnfast are the tradition of the fired waking: they hold that regard is best given to the world *set* — benched under gathered eyes, fired into use, holding its knack for generations after the hands are gone. A Kilnfast maker takes few askings and finishes them utterly; the proudest among them, the one-work folk, aim an entire walking life at a single masterwork, and every Kilnfast child learns the light-followers' short list of near-closures the way other children learn lullabies — the stair at Setterby is their dearest place on any Round, and they leave journey-pieces on it like students encouraging a beloved teacher. They keep no lodge and no secrets; their whole strangeness is patience, and their heresy-adjacent hope is the horizon question itself: that one day a work made attentively enough, before audience enough, might close its round and never weather at all. Of the Fade they speak with respect, as of a worthy rival: the weathering is the only clock they honor. Of the Brightabouts they say, fondly, "writing on water" — and the Brightabouts say back that a fired masterpiece in a locked hall is a boxed coin the size of a house, and both go on saying it at every fair, because the argument is older than either school and neither would part with it.

- **Relations:** askings — few, large, finished to the last degree; lanterns learn to save their rafters-work for Kilnfast hands. Gleam — they let it come slow ("a long dusk is still dusk"). The Fade — honored opponent. Great askings — what a Kilnfast life is *for*.
- **This could become:** the **tall permanence archetype** — a small, dense deck of heavily woken cards and fired artifacts that persist all run; attention hoarded into single enormous plays; resource preference: attention over handsels; risk posture: patient and low-variance, weak openings, the strongest boss-tier scaling in the game, with an aspirational win-more horizon flavored toward the closed round.
- **Visual:** kiln-light palette — umber, glaze-blue, ember-orange; heavy aproned silhouettes that move like ovens cooling; tools gold-edged from decades of waking, carried like relics. Their gleam-grain lies in long calm spans across the knuckles (the bridge-grain of canon). Across a fair you know a Kilnfast because they are the stillest thing in it that isn't for sale.

### `the-brightabouts`

The Brightabouts are the tradition of the held waking: light is a current, and their craft is the current itself. Their bench is the market and the doorstep; their masterworks are hours, not objects — a market day that sounds like rain on bells, a joke folded into a paper bird and given away, a dance that leaves a whole square a shade warmer than the dusk deserves. They give away their best work on principle, because a Brightabout's wealth is exactly what has left their hands brightly, and they can prove it: coats strung with handsels that have crossed a thousand glad palms, every one humming. They find the Kilnfast a little deaf to the present tense — "you can weather glaze," they say, sweetly — and consider a strongbox the saddest object a person can build. The Fade does not trouble them at all: everything comes back at dawn, and they plan to be there when it does.

- **Relations:** askings — the soft strange ones; lanterns show Brightabouts the stitched swallows and the hinges opening onto hope, the needs no bench can answer. Gleam — quick to rise, quick to spend into introductions and doors. The Fade — an old dance partner. Other schools — welcome everywhere, because they arrive with the weather in a good mood.
- **This could become:** the **tempo/velocity archetype** — a handsel-flow deck built on the held waking: cards that brighten when spent promptly and dim if idle, change returned a shade brighter as an engine, exhaust-heavy performance pieces (the paper-bird joke, played to destruction); resource preference: handsels over attention; risk posture: aggressive tempo, fragile when stalled — this is the deck that literally must keep moving.
- **Visual:** ribbon-trimmed coats strung with graded-glow handsels, so a walking Brightabout is a soft chord coming down the road; quick, bird-light motion; palette of festival brass and worn bright thread. Gleam-grain rippling like water under wind (the song-grain of canon). Across a fair, follow the ringing.

### `the-smallwrights`

The Smallwrights hold that the world is kept — actually, literally kept — by buttons, whistles, spoons, latches, and hinges: a thousand small delights against a Fade that also works everywhere, a little, always. They walk with packs like hedgehogs of usefulness and mend as they go, seeding every town with small woken things, each one a watcher — one more eye against the grey, one more attender at some future maker's bench; their doctrine is *a spoon in every drawer*, and their quiet arithmetic is that more of the world's gold edges are Smallwright work than any other school's, and hardly one of them carries a remembered name. They are beloved everywhere and famous nowhere, which is precisely the plan. Toward great askings they are tender skeptics: "a countryside that misses nothing combs nothing" — answer the small needs in season, and the seventh ring never comes. The Morning-Gleaners, who love what the seventh ring becomes, find this position scandalous, and the two schools have been happily scandalized by each other for generations.

- **Relations:** askings — many, small, constant; the kettle-tier is not their apprenticeship but their creed. Gleam — a steady freckling drip, never a blaze. The Fade — met daily, at its own scale. Great askings — prevented, when Smallwrights have their way; attended with the whole pack when they don't. *(Their standing argument with the Gleaners is the slate's core doctrinal rivalry.)*
- **This could become:** the **go-wide swarm archetype** — many cheap cards, token woken-things that grant passive attention (the audience-of-things made into board presence), incremental Standing from many small delights; resource preference: breadth and redundancy of attention; risk posture: the lowest variance in the game, shock-resilient, with a designed ceiling problem — converting width into a great asking is this archetype's signature challenge.
- **Visual:** patchwork-neat clothes with pockets everywhere; a silhouette bristling with tiny tools; motion of constant small industry — hands always finishing something, even mid-conversation. Palette: tin, straw-gold, thread-bright accents. Gleam-grain freckled (the hearth-laughter grain of canon). Across a fair, they're the ones fixing the fair.

### `the-mannerly`

The Mannerly are the tradition of courtship: the finest work, they hold, begins as listening, and a material met on its own terms gives more than any skill can take. They keep the great traveling notebooks of what the world answers to — which valley's clay likes hearing about the weather, what the green riverwood wants to find funny, how long the silver of a given hill expects to be sung to before it will state its terms at all — and everyone borrows those notebooks, and everyone returns them, because being lent one is the closest thing the Rounds have to being trusted with someone's heart. Their one true rudeness is forcing common clay into proud work: to the Mannerly, the crime is not failure but *not asking the stone first*. They need their gleam high and tend it deliberately, since proud stuff will not treat with hands the world is not currently holding in mind — but they insist the shine is a courtesy the world extends, never a wage, and they dim gracefully when they dim. They find the Kilnfast a little deaf ("they ask the kiln, never the clay") and the Brightabouts a little loud, and are received with pleasure by both, because nobody's guest-manners are better.

- **Relations:** askings — matched to introductions; a Mannerly maker sometimes courts a material for a season before touching the asking it was courted for. Gleam — an engine stat, cultivated on purpose, mourned without bitterness. The Fade — the reason introductions must be renewed. Other schools — the slate's diplomats, welcome at every bench.
- **This could become:** the **condition-puzzle control archetype** — a deck of high-power shy-material cards, each behind a legible micro-condition (sing first; keep the chain unbroken; hold an audience token), with Standing as a gating resource that widens the material market; resource preference: Standing itself, held high and spent on access; risk posture: greedy value — dead cards in a dim hand, spectacular payoffs when the courtship lands.
- **Visual:** quiet elegance — dove-grey wools with iridescent accents given, not taken; materials carried in cradles rather than sacks; a stillness so attentive it reads across a square. The fair's Mannerly is the one standing motionless before a shelf of jars, hat in hand, while the jars visibly perk. Gleam-grain fine-threaded, like watered silk.

### `the-morning-gleaners`

The Morning-Gleaners walk toward the grey. Where every other tradition routes for bright country, Gleaners read the gatepost spirals like an almanac and choose the palest roads on purpose — partly for the work (the deep re-makings, the five-ring poems, the compounded wonders nobody else reached in season), and partly, frankly, for the mornings, because pale country gets the best dawns and the Gleaners intend to be standing in them. Their doctrine is that the Fade is the world's generosity wearing its walking clothes: grey country is the world at its most gatherable, and the out-breath is the friend. Half of them never stopped playing the noticing-game; most carry a hazel wand through the pack-strap and will walk a while with any light-follower they meet — though the Gleaners make and the followers only count, and both like it that way. They are the ones who walked all night to stand in the Quill Vale Morning, and the ones who tie the red thread when a great asking is found, and other schools find them slightly uncanny and completely indispensable.

- **Relations:** askings — the compounded ones, twice the work and twice the wonder; they treat a seven-ring spiral the way the Kilnfast treat Setterby. Gleam — banked in great swings, since deep re-makings move whole valleys. The Fade — the friend, said without irony. Great askings — their weather; the combing gathers where they walk. *(Their tender quarrel with the Smallwrights — harvest the great need vs. prevent it — is doctrine, never grievance.)*
- **This could become:** the **adversity-economy archetype** — a route-risk specialist whose engine scales with node paleness: the richest dawn income in the game (fuller local tables), re-coloring as fuel, bonuses keyed to compounded askings; resource preference: the unclaimed morning itself; risk posture: highest route risk, boss-affine — this is the archetype that steers the map toward the combing on purpose.
- **Visual:** dawn worn on the body — rose-grey wools, chalk-dusted hems, hazel wand through the strap; long-strided, earliest awake, asleep at the fair by noon. Gleam-grain lying slantwise, like first light across furrows. Across a fair you spot them at dusk, facing the palest hills, reading spirals nobody else has walked out to see.

### `the-fairwrights`

The Fairwrights hold that the watching is half the work — so they make the watching. Theirs is the craft of rooms delight happens in: workshop-theaters with tools racked in raked rows like an audience that never goes home, market-rings pitched so a crowd pools like water finding a bowl, festival grounds, porch-stages, the whole architecture of gathered regard — and their unending masterpiece is the Round-Fair itself, the fair that walks the circuit and never stops being made. They keep the Moth Fair at Ferrow as their founding telling, and not for the fiddler: for the four hundred, and for whoever raked the ground that held them. No work wakes thin in a room a Fairwright raised — that is the school's whole boast, and the tally-chalk bears it out. The other traditions need them, tease them ("they play to the overflow"), and pack their wagons first when the fair moves on; the Fairwrights answer that a bench without watchers is a boat without water, and go back to turning the chairs a quarter-turn toward the stage.

- **Relations:** askings — they take the town-scale ones (a square, a festival, a paling commons) and quietly serve everyone else's by building the room around it. Gleam — feast and famine: nobody's rises faster before a full ground or slides off harder behind a dead one (the prettiest alley in Ferrow is theirs by inheritance). The Fade — answered by assembly: a town that gathers, holds. Other schools — the connective tissue of the slate.
- **This could become:** the **multiplier-spike archetype** — an audience-engine deck: venue cards, audience-token generators, attention multipliers, all bent toward one enormous public turn where delight overfills the work and the overkill converts to Standing at full rate (the Moth Fair condition as a build-around); resource preference: attention throughput; risk posture: the highest variance in the game — a dead room spills, a full one makes the run.
- **Visual:** festival palette — lantern-red, bunting, banner-cream; tool-rolls that unroll into tiny grandstands; scaffold-poles carried like pilgrims' staves; motion of perpetual arranging — chairs a quarter-turn, lamps steadied, sightlines checked. Gleam-grain dappled, many small overlapping rings, like light through bunting. Across a fair you can't spot the Fairwright, because the fair is what you're spotting.

## How the traditions rub — and never break

No tradition has ever raised a hand against another, for the plainest reason in the world: there is nothing to take. Regard cannot be seized — it runs to causes and settles by law — so every disagreement between schools is a disagreement about *how to give*, and those are conducted in the only registers Roundelay honors:

- **The standing arguments.** Fired versus held (Kilnfast/Brightabouts). One great work versus a thousand small (Kilnfast/Smallwrights). Harvest the great need versus prevent it (Gleaners/Smallwrights). Ask the clay versus ask the kiln (Mannerly/Kilnfast). Bench versus room (everyone/Fairwrights). These are rehearsed at every fair like beloved songs, and a maker who resolves one would be gently resented for a generation.
- **The fair-answer** (see below): when a contested asking could honestly be answered two ways, both ways answer, side by side, before the town — and the only court in Roundelay is the audience of things.
- **Styles of round-walking.** Traditions differ in how they walk before they differ in what they make: Gleaners route pale, Brightabouts route markets, Smallwrights route everything at kettle-pace, Kilnfast route straight to the one work and stay. A road can be read like a signature, and road-memory holds each school's habits — the same Round greets each tradition differently, because it has learned different things from each.
- **Festivals as the diplomacy.** The Round-Fair is where pieces trade, apprentices choose their walking, notebooks are lent, and every rivalry is fed just enough to stay delicious.

## Shared practices, institutions, and events

### `the-walking-schools`

How a tradition exists at all without a hall, a roll, or a rite: it is a way of walking, and its knowledge lives in walking-songs sung along particular stretches of the Rounds — the Kilnfast stretch of a circuit teaches slow songs about heat and patience, the Brightabout stretch teaches rounds you can juggle to. You study by walking with a master, carrying their pack, learning the songs in the order the road gives them; you belong when a master hands you a first journey-piece of the school and the road's witnesses see it done. Dabbling across schools is lawful and common — but gleam keeps the truth, because grain shows what actually fed your shine.

- **This could become:** the diegetic card-pool structure — each tradition is a card school; acquisition is walking with masters (map events on that school's stretches); multi-school decks are legal, while grain bonuses (see `the-grain-of-gleam`, L2) lawfully reward focus. Archetype selection without a single menu.
- **Visual:** two silhouettes on a ridge road at dusk, a song visibly passing between them in the tilt of their heads; each school's stretch of Round drawn in its own marginalia style on the circuit map — kiln-arches, ringing dots, thimbles, jars, dawn-lines, bunting.

### `the-round-fair` *(anchor — the traveling festival is folk-tale furniture, kept deliberately familiar so that three genuinely novel things stay instantly legible through it: commerce priced in wakefulness rather than metal — stalls of handsels in graded glow, dull to warm to singing; contest resolved by which work wakes, never by any judge (`the-fair-answer`); and recruitment-by-walking, as masters of six schools teach by lamplight and take on whoever keeps up when the wagons roll.)*

The fair that walks the Round: a few dozen wagons perpetually a week ahead of autumn, Fairwright-built and everybody-kept, arriving in each town like a season with opinions. For three days a pale square remembers every color it ever had; journey-pieces change hands by teaching, winning, and plain delight; the six gleam-grains mingle at dusk into one shifting shimmer that regulars can read like a crowd-sized portrait. Then the wagons roll, and the town keeps what woke.

- **This could become:** the run's moving hub node — draft, trade, teach, contest — which rotates around the circuit so that reaching the hub is itself a route decision; fair-timing becomes a strategic layer (chase the fair, or let it come to you at compounded cost elsewhere).
- **Visual:** the market that sounds like rain on bells; bunting between wagons; stall-fronts of small tokens in graded glow; and at blue dusk, six kinds of rim-light texture crossing in one square.

### `the-fair-answer`

When a lantern's asking could honestly be answered two ways — fired or held, one great or many small — and two traditions both believe theirs is what the town needs, the custom is old and beloved: both answer, side by side, in the open, before the whole town, and the audience of things settles it. Chairs turn a quarter-turn; the old kettles take up their hum; and whichever work wakes — sometimes both do, and the town keeps both and the argument continues deliciously — that is the answer. The dimmer work goes home with someone anyway, as a gift, because nothing made before a whole town is ever wasted.

- **This could become:** rival-maker events — parallel-objective nodes where the player answers an asking alongside an NPC tradition working the same need in its own style; winning takes the full reward and the telling, losing yields the smaller gift and never touches Standing directly (only the crowd's actual response moves gleam, per the L2 laws — a flat performance spills like any other).
- **Visual:** two benches, one square, one crowd — and between the benches, the town's woken things tilting by slow degrees toward one of them, like compass needles agreeing.

### `the-gathered-bench`

Who tends the boss tier: everyone, the way the combing itself was made — from all sides, with no one presiding. When a great asking warms to some maker's pack, no tradition on the Round lets those hands meet it alone. The schools converge on the site and raise a working-theater around the answering maker, each contributing its signature: Fairwrights rake the ground and pitch the ring; Kilnfast fire the staging; Smallwrights seed the slope with a hundred small woken watchers; the Mannerly arrive with materials already courted and introduced; Brightabouts set the handsel-tide ringing through the camps; Gleaners read the seams with their wands and choose the dawn on which the work should begin. Nobody summons this and nobody could — it assembles the way weather assembles, because a countryside that combed a great asking into being is the same countryside that wants to watch it answered.

- **This could become:** the boss-prep phase — before a great asking, the player chooses which traditions' gifts to accept, each one a legible boss-modifier (a raked ring raises the attention ceiling; fired staging protects board-state; seeded watchers grant passive attention; introductions unlock proud materials for the fight; the ringing tide accelerates the handsel economy; the chosen dawn sets the income of the opening turns) — so every boss is partly authored by the player's relationships with the slate.
- **Visual:** a scaffold amphitheater on a fell at first light, red thread still tied where the asking lay, six kinds of silhouette on the rakes and one small bright-rimmed figure at the center, pack open, looking up.

### `the-quiet-porch`

Every tradition keeps porches along its stretches of Round — deep benches under wide eaves where Quiet Walkers are met as honored audience and fed with nothing asked, because a maker gone dim is not an ex-maker: they are the finest watchers alive, and every school knows that work done before a porch of old hands wakes deep. Some Walkers sit a season, laugh at something in a stranger's kitchen, and feel the first flicker come back; some sit for good and become what the retiring have always become — the audience every future maker plays to. The porches are where the traditions stop being six and become one practice, and no one has ever proposed changing that.

- **This could become:** the meta-progression home — past runs' makers persist on the porches of the traditions they walked with, appearing as audience figures who grant passive attention and grain bonuses to later runs; porches double as rest nodes where the hearth-right telling is flavored to craft (tell the porch a making, and the school's stretch of road wakes a little around it).
- **Visual:** a dawn-grey porch, a row of ordinary, un-rimmed elders with tea — and every woken thing on the street tilted toward them, the world's most attentive bench; the emotional companion-piece to the Quiet Walk's reversed map.

## Who tends the boss tier — summary

No one issues great askings; the combing does, out of a countryside's crossed and living regard, exactly as Layer 2 lays down. What culture adds is *tending*: lantern-keepers chalk the spirals that predict a combing seasons out (a household turn taken like a verse of the song, never an office); light-followers — a practice, forever, no academy, no order — dowse the gathering seams with hazel and tally-chalk; Morning-Gleaners walk the seven-ring country and tie the red thread; and when the asking warms to a pack, `the-gathered-bench` assembles around whoever carries it. Afterward, every school argues about what the answer proved — the Kilnfast that it should have been fired deeper, the Smallwrights that it should never have been needed — and the argument walks on to the next fair, which is how doctrine stays alive on a road with no doctrine-keepers.

## Anchor declaration

One anchor in this layer, declared inline above and summarized here:

1. **`the-round-fair`** — the traveling festival. The fair-come-to-town is folk furniture every player already knows how to want, kept deliberately familiar so its genuinely novel cargo reads at a glance: **wakefulness-priced commerce** (stalls graded dull/warm/singing), **adjudication-by-waking** (`the-fair-answer` — contests settled by which work wakes, before the only court in Roundelay, the audience of things), and **recruitment-by-walking** (`the-walking-schools` teaching by lamplight, no halls, no rolls).

Anchor share this layer: **1 of 11 elements.** One near-anchor is defused in the text: the Kilnfast could drift toward a masons'-lodge cliché, so canon here states they keep no lodge, no secrets, and no hierarchy — their entire strangeness is patience aimed at the closed round, which no lodge cliché contains.

## Compliance notes (register bans, carried forward)

- **No institutions of the banned kind:** no charters, no ranks, no adjudicating bodies; traditions are ways of walking, membership is a witnessed gift, contest is settled by waking. The light-followers appear only as the institution-free practice canon requires — wands, chalk, patience, windowsills — walked with, never enrolled in.
- **Nothing is owed anywhere in the layer:** the gathered bench assembles like weather; porch-welcome is a gift with nothing asked; fair-answer losers keep the telling and give the work away.
- **Grammar of light held:** faction identity rides on gleam-*grain* (texture of the one people-light: spans, ripples, freckles, threads, slants, dapples), clothing, silhouette, and motion — never on working-edges on people, key-lights on places, or Fade-grey on characters.
- **Attend-don't-act held:** woken things in this layer tilt, steady, hum, and turn by degrees — the fair-answer's compass-needle settling, the porch street's tilt — and never act, speak, or emote; materials perk and lean but never speak, and their "stated terms" remain canon's figurative courtship, legible in behavior only.
- **Failure register held:** the dim are met at porches as honored audience; the Quiet Walk threads through the layer as the practice that unites all six schools.
- **Timescale law held:** no faction mechanic introduces within-run Standing decay; Brightabout dimming pressure applies to handsels (the held waking) only, exactly as Layer 2 prices it.
