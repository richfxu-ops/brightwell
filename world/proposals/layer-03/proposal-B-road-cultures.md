# Proposal B — Road Cultures: The Five Ways of Walking

**Layer 3 (factions) · proposer B-road-cultures · pipeline-test-02 · world: ROUNDELAY**

**Divergence lens, committed:** in Roundelay nobody is what they own or whom they answer to; a maker *is the shape of their route and the manner of their arrival.* The peoples of the Rounds sorted themselves long ago not into houses or orders but into **ways of walking** — five of them, old as the roads, each one a complete philosophy of when to arrive, how much of a town to take up, and what to do about the Fade. Ask a stranger at a fair "whose are you?" and you will get a shrug; ask "how do you walk?" and you will get an hour. Every way of walking is a playable archetype: a route shape, a tempo, a relationship to rumor, and therefore a deck.

A note on the light-followers, per canon: they are not on this slate and never could be. Following the light is a *practice*, not a people — there are sunwise light-followers and ferry light-followers and a famous palefarer with chalk in every pocket. Wherever this proposal says someone dowsed or tallied something, understand: a person did it, on a windowsill or a gatepost, out of a pack. No academy. Ever.

## 1 · The five ways

#### `the-sunwise`

The sunwise walk a Round *entire* — every town in its turn, always with the sun, never a verse dropped — and their whole belief fits in their oldest saying: *a Round skipped is a song hummed.* They take whatever the lantern shows them, smallest first, kettles before rafters, because to the sunwise the size of a deed is the least interesting thing about it; what matters is that the circuit closes, that every hearth on the loop got its turn of the light. Their wealth is road-memory, and no road folds for anyone the way it folds for a sunwise walker on her fourth turn of the same great circle — hedges hold out fruit for her, fords go shallow, dusk waits. They regard the Fade with the easy respect of a gardener for winter: it is the colleague who lays out tomorrow's round, and a sunwise walker overtaken by grief at a pale town has, by their own lights, simply not walked far enough yet to see the town re-made. Toward gleam they are famously unhurried — broad, steady, slow to blaze — and their fond needle for the fair-folk is *bright fast, dim fast.*

Their relations run through patience. They are the road's baseline, the walkers every lantern-keeper knows by stride; the palefarers call them *the metronome* (affectionately, mostly); their one standing quarrel is with the untold, over whether a deed left small is a deed left unfinished — the whole-song argument, fought yearly at the waymeets in proverbs and footraces and never once settled.

- **This could become:** the *completionist engine* archetype — a consistency deck of many small, cheap, reliably-woken cards that compound; resource preference: **road-memory** (travel buffs, folded miles, per-node revisit bonuses stacked across a full circuit); risk posture: **low** — the sunwise win by never skipping, converting the Fade's compounding into a schedule instead of a threat, and their failure mode is tempo (the metronome cannot sprint when a great asking ripens off-beat).
- **Visual:** ochre and umber travel-cloth, boots re-soled in visible rings, the tall pack built around a **bedroll hoop** — a full circle of blanket-wound willow rising above the shoulders, the silhouette of a walking "O" you can read across any fairground. Their motion is the giveaway: an unhurried, absolutely even gait, and before entering any town square they walk it once around, sunwise, hat off — like a needle finding true before it sews.

#### `the-green-wains` *(anchor — the traveling fair is folk-tale furniture, kept deliberately familiar so that Roundelay's genuinely novel claim — attention as a gatherable, spendable force (`audience-of-things`), overflow running to the pourer (`gleam-runoff`) — becomes something a player can see coming down a road: a fair is a portable audience, and the wains are the attention economy on wheels.)*

The green-wains raise fairs. A dozen tall painted wagons come over the hill at noon and by dusk there is a fairground where a field was: stages, bunting, the glass-blower's bench set frankly in the open because festival glass clouds without a crowd. Their belief is the loud corollary of the world's quietest law — *delight shared is delight multiplied* — and they have organized an entire way of life around the Moth Fair condition: work done at the top of a gathered crowd's regard wakes deepest, overflows hardest, and runs brightest back to its makers. So the wains carry their audience *with* them, and every woken bench, lamp, and rocking-horse in the train is another watcher racked and ready; a wain-stage is a small theater that has been paying attention for six generations. They answer askings in glad bundles during fair-week — a town's whole lantern taken down in three nights of public work — and they know better than anyone alive what a flat night costs, because a spilling in front of four hundred is the biggest spilling there is. The prettiest lanes in Roundelay run behind fairground stages, and the wains, who painted most of them, still make the old toast.

They are welcome everywhere and rivals to no one, exactly — but the untold fondly flee them, because a fair *retells*, louder, with staging, and a quiet deed that meets the wains becomes a legend with a costume by Friday. The sunwise tease their tempo; the ferry-round loves them (a fair on the far bank doubles a season's crossings); and when the seventh ring closes somewhere, it is the wains who get the first quiet word, because a great asking will need what only they can raise.

- **This could become:** the *audience combo* archetype — chain-and-burst deck play that builds an attention pool high above the waking threshold and spends it in one enormous public work; resource preference: **attention** (crowd tokens, woken audience-things as persistent multiplier artifacts); risk posture: **high** — the biggest Standing swings in the game, both directions: overkill delight converts to gleam at full rate on stage, and a botched headline act is the run's hardest spill.
- **Visual:** deep bottle-green wagons with madder-red wheels and gilt scrollwork, pennant-tails snapping; palette of carnival lacquer against evening blue. Silhouette: the wain-train cresting a hill reads as a row of bright beads on a thread. Motion signature: *everything sways and rings* — the whole train is hung with woken handsels and small bells, so a fair arrives audibly, like rain on bells getting closer.

#### `the-ferry-round`

The ferry-round keep the water roads, and their circuit is not a loop but a **crossing, repeated** — the same slow reach of river or lake or sound, back and forth, a lifetime deep. They live where the world's kindness pools: light seeps downhill, valleys hold color like cupped hands, and the deep water sits over the fullest tables in Roundelay, so ferry country is the least faded country there is, and the ferry-folk mistrust their own luck with cheerful superstition — *the low road is lent its color*, they say (and a light-follower will tell you, patiently, that it is not lent, it is physics, and the ferry-folk will nod and go on saying it). Their craft-belief is circulation: a handsel that rides a ferry crosses more glad palms in a week than in a village year, and ferry-folk keep money *singing* the way smiths keep a fire fed — it would be rude to let a coin go quiet on your boat. They are also, without ever quite claiming it, the tempo-keepers of rumor. Tales cross water only when a ferry carries them, so the murmur of the Rounds runs at the ferrymen's pace: a deed done on the far bank can be famous in three valleys or still perfectly quiet a season later, depending on what the boatman felt like telling, and every way of walking courts them for it. Their disputed treasure is the bell at Hask, and they like it disputed; six generations of handwriting on the slip-board say so.

With the Fade they have the strangest relation of the five: they barely meet it at home, so they apprentice their young *upslope* — a ferry child walks one high pale circuit with the sunwise or the palefarers before taking the pole, so that the low road's luck is never mistaken for the world.

- **This could become:** the *circulation control* archetype — a flow deck about card-movement and economy: hand-smoothing, tutoring, cycling, and a handsel engine that profits from money changing hands (spend-into-delight returning change a shade brighter, at scale); resource preference: **handsels** (wakefulness-graded currency as both purse and engine); risk posture: **low-to-medium, tempo-controlling** — the ferry player also modulates *rumor speed*, choosing when tales cross (delaying or hastening the lantern ratchet's reach), the game's one lawful lever on how fast being-heard-of travels.
- **Visual:** wet-slate blues and tarred black, one amber lantern at the stern of everything; wide flat hats against silver water. Silhouette: the pole-and-lean — a standing figure inclined against a punt-pole reads as ferry-round at any distance. Motion: **glide** — the only way of walking that doesn't walk; even ashore they move like people who expect the ground to carry them, and their woken bells ring once, low, at each arrival.

#### `the-palefarers`

The palefarers tend pale country *on purpose*, and they are — this surprises every stranger — the cheerfullest walkers on the Rounds. Their founding observation is the folk-saw every child knows, taken with total seriousness as a life: *pale country gets the best mornings*, and where the grey is deepest, the reissue is richest, the askings have ripened largest, and the wonder of the re-making is at its full size. So they walk the roads no one else chooses, camp in grey valleys the way other folk claim festival seats, and practice **ripe mending**: reading the gatepost spirals like an almanac and arriving not as early as possible but *exactly when a need has grown into its best self* — five rings is a poem, and a palefarer answering a five-ring town before two hundred gathered dawn-watchers is the closest thing their way has to a wedding. They dress in undyed wool — nothing on a palefarer's back was ever dyed, so the Fade has nothing to take from it — with one exception, worn at the throat: a woken ember-red scarf, the one stubborn hearth-line of color they carry into the grey. More palefarers keep tally-chalk and hazel wands than any other way of walking, but say "the palefarer academy" at their fire and they will laugh at you for a season: it is a windowsill practice, carried in packs, same as anywhere.

Their standing craft-quarrel is with the sunwise and it is the deepest disagreement on the Rounds: *early mending or ripe mending* — is it kinder to answer a town at three rings, or to let the need ripen to five and give the town a poem and a dawn people walk all night for? (The towns themselves are asked, town by town, and enjoy being fought over in proverbs.) With the combing they have an almost proprietary tenderness: palefarers dowse the gathering seams weeks ahead, tie the first red thread, and send the untold running with the word.

- **This could become:** the *boss-hunter* archetype — high-risk high-reward routing straight into the greyest nodes: biggest askings, biggest rewards, and the richest dawn income in the game (pale nodes grant immense mornings under `the-unclaimed-morning`); resource preference: **dawn income + ripened contracts** (deliberately letting spirals compound, then harvesting); risk posture: **high** — the palefarer plays against the paling-arithmetic like a farmer against frost, and one mistimed season means standing in deep grey with an asking too big for tonight's hands.
- **Visual:** undyed greys, oatmeals, and bone-whites — a figure who *almost* belongs to pale scenery, except that people gleam and places fade, so a palefarer in grey country is the single warm-lit thing in the frame, red scarf and rim-light and nothing else — the grammar of light doing character design by itself. Silhouette: long coat, hazel wand at the belt like a slender sword that isn't one. Signature shot: palefarers sitting in a row on a grey hillside at the end of night, waiting, and then the rose-gold comes up off the whole valley at once.

#### `the-untold`

The untold walk ahead of their own tellings. They take the between-roads — stile-and-hedgerow lines that pass no hearth, so no hearth passes the tale on — arrive at dawn, mend the small true thing, eat standing up, and are two towns gone before the story of them sits down by anyone's fire. It is not secrecy and it is nothing so dour as humility; it is a *craft position*, the miniaturist's creed: a deed left small stays exactly the size the need was, and a maker who lingers lets the hearths braid her kettle into a legend and her next lantern will lower rafters at her for it. So the untold keep their gleam **fine-grained** on purpose — a thousand kitchen-laughs, a hundred sweetened wells, no bridges, thank you — bright as anyone at dusk, but freckled, never spanned: *a shine like sand, not like beams*, and a trained lantern reads that grain and shows them what they love best, which is a rafters' worth of kettles. Every other way of walking finds them slightly maddening and privately indispensable, and when something urgent must cross the country faster than tales can walk, it is an untold runner who carries it.

Their fond feud with the green-wains is the comedy of the Rounds — a fair retells everything at twice the size, so the untold flee the wains like cats before a parade, and the wains, who adore them, have a recurring stage-turn called *The Kettle-Mender* about an untold walker trapped at a fair while her deeds inflate around her. With the sunwise they keep the whole-song argument alive; with the Fade their relation is intimate and light-footed — many small mendings, constantly, everywhere, the drizzle to the palefarers' harvest rain.

- **This could become:** the *ratchet-management tempo* archetype — the one playstyle built on the escalation law itself: a thin, fast deck of cheap fine-grained plays; many small contracts cleared quickly; heavy use of exhaust effects (journey-pieces performed to destruction and given away — the paper-bird joke as a play pattern); resource preference: **speed and route-shape** (between-road map edges, arrive-before-rumor timing); risk posture: **cool and controlled** — the untold deliberately keep peak-gleam grain small to keep the lantern ladder low and legible, winning long runs by never being offered a rafters-work they didn't choose; their failure mode is the combing, which reads a *country's* need, not a town's talk, and will aim at bright hands no matter how finely the brightness is grained.
- **Visual:** dove-grey and dust-colored traveling clothes with one hidden bright lining (a flash of kingfisher blue at the cuff when the hands work — the only vanity of the way); a pack so small it looks like an insult to the work it does. Their rim-light is *freckled* — gleam in scattered points along knuckles and hairline rather than long spans, readable in any portrait. Motion: quick, level, economical; the walker you notice only after they have gone, which is how they'd want it painted.

## 2 · How the ways meet (no thrones, no blades)

Nothing on this slate holds territory, issues commands, or keeps a roll of members — you are sunwise because you walk sunwise, and a maker who changes her way of walking has simply changed it (this happens; it is called *turning your coat to the weather* and it is grounds for a party, not a grudge). Friction between the ways is real, permanent, and entirely made of **craft disagreement, festival, and timing**:

- **The whole-song argument** (sunwise vs. untold): is a deed left small a deed left unfinished? Fought in proverbs, footraces, and paired demonstrations at every waymeet for nine generations. Current standing: unsettled, lovingly.
- **Early mending vs. ripe mending** (sunwise vs. palefarers): answer at three rings or let it ripen to five? The only quarrel on the Rounds with real stakes — a town's whole season hangs on it — and it is settled the only way Roundelay settles anything: the town is asked, and the way it answers becomes part of that town's character, chalked so the whole road knows which kind of town it is.
- **The retelling problem** (green-wains vs. untold): a fair inflates every tale it touches. Played for comedy on stage and in life; structurally important, because it means *the wains can raise any maker's lantern ladder just by singing about them* — the world's one way to escalate someone else's contracts, done only ever as a compliment, received with mixed feelings.
- **The boatman's pace** (ferry-round vs. everyone): tales cross water when ferry-folk carry them, so every way of walking courts the ferries — the wains want their fairs cried up ahead, the untold want silence, the palefarers want spiral-counts carried accurately. A ferry-round boatman deciding what to mention over the water is the closest thing Roundelay has to power, and the ferry-folk's iron custom is to be *slow*, not selective: everything crosses eventually, at the water's pace, which everyone can plan around and no one can bribe.
- **Contested askings** are resolved by the lantern's own attending: when two makers reach for one object, both stand beneath the lantern at dusk and the asking — a woken thing, attending as woken things do — turns on its red thread toward the hands it was sized for, the way a compass needle finds north. No judging, no speeches; the object turns, and the custom is that the passed-over maker eats that night at the other's fire and gets first pick of the tales. Some of the Rounds' great friendships started as a whistle turning the other way.

## 3 · The boss tier: who tends the great askings

No one owns the great askings, and canon forbids anyone to: a combing is made by a countryside's crossed regard, and it aims itself at the brightest grain the country has heard of — the weather picks the hands, and the weather cannot be lobbied. What the road cultures own is *everything around that moment*, and they have built the Rounds' greatest institution on it (see `red-thread-days`): the palefarers dowse the gathering seams and tie the first thread; the untold run the word across country faster than tales can walk; the ferry-round moves proud materials, crowds, and the chosen maker's old teachers to the site; the wains raise a fair around the work — because a world-scale re-making needs a world-scale audience, and the wains are the only people alive who can raise four hundred gathered watchers in a field three valleys from anywhere; and the sunwise, who know the region's roads by body, walk the perimeter circuits so that every small asking around the great one still gets answered while the country holds its breath. The saying that covers it is carved over no door and known to everyone: ***the weather picks the hands; the road makes sure they don't stand there alone.***

## 4 · Shared ground: institutions of the road (named)

#### `the-waymeet`

Where the great circuits cross there is waymeet ground — held by no town, tended by whoever is camped on it, marked by a signpost with as many arms as the roads have moods. Waymeets are where the ways of walking actually mix: journey-pieces change hands, route-tales are traded like the treasure they are, the whole-song argument gets its annual airing, and young makers come to watch the five gaits go by and choose one. Nothing is bought at a waymeet that could be gifted, and the ground's one custom is the oldest on the Rounds: whatever you learned on the road in, you tell before you take the road out.

- **This could become:** the cross-archetype hub node — trade, card-pool access outside your way's suits, route re-planning, and rumor-intel exchange, all framed as gift-and-tale rather than shop; the place a deck deliberately splashes.
- **Visual:** a many-armed signpost worn silver, strings of graded-glow handsels swagged between trees, five distinct silhouettes around one fire; the ground itself worn in a circle, because everything in Roundelay is.

#### `red-thread-days`

When a seventh ring closes and a combing is found, the country around it enters red-thread-days: the found arrangement is tied where it lies, the word goes out by runner and ferry, and for a season the five ways converge on one valley to do the one thing none of them can do alone — palefarer timing, untold speed, ferry carriage, wain-raised audience, sunwise perimeter — while the weather's chosen maker builds toward the work. It ends in the largest gathered regard the Rounds ever assemble, a re-making at the scale of a river's voice or a season's smell, and a dawn afterward that people describe to their grandchildren. Then everyone goes back to arguing about mending schedules, satisfied.

- **This could become:** the boss-encounter structure — a telegraphed convergence event where the player (as the chosen hands) recruits each culture's gift as an encounter modifier: audience scale, material access, timing windows, perimeter relief — the boss fight as barn-raising.
- **Visual:** a grey valley slowly filling with color from the *edges inward* for once — wagons, boats on the river, threads of walkers on every high path, all converging; red thread everywhere like the first strokes of paint; and at the center, one small bright-rimmed figure and a bench.

#### `road-marks`

The gift-graffiti of the Rounds: knotted thread on stiles, chalk birds on gateposts, notches on the fingerboards of waymeet signs — the five ways' shared shorthand for *what the next walker needs to know*. Which town's clay likes weather-talk; where the water went sweet again; a spiral copied forward from a lantern-post two valleys back so the road can read a town's patience before topping the last hill. No one keeps the road-marks and everyone keeps them up; leaving a false one is the nearest thing the Rounds have to a sin, and it is so rare that the stories about it have names.

- **This could become:** the map-intel layer — scouting information as found gifts from previous makers, and the meta-hook: *your own past runs' marks persist*, so the country is annotated by everyone you have ever been.
- **Visual:** close-up folk-signs in chalk and knot against weathered wood — a bird, a wave, a spiral — each drawn in the distinct hand-style of the way that left it (a sunwise ring, an untold dash), so the marks themselves tell you who passed.

#### `the-setting-out`

The spring festival when the year's walking begins. At every waymeet, makers declare their way for the year by the oldest possible method — they walk out of the camp in its manner, and everyone cheers — and each of the five ways gives every departing maker one journey-piece in its own style, whatever way they chose, because the road ahead is long and nobody walks it in only one weather. Coats are turned at the setting-out; first-year makers take their first way; retired makers watch from the good log and make the same joke every year, and it is a good joke, which is why.

- **This could become:** the run-start ceremony — archetype selection rendered diegetically (choose your way of walking), plus the starting-boon draft: one off-archetype journey-piece gifted by each other culture, seeding every deck with lawful splash options.
- **Visual:** dawn at a waymeet, mist to the knee, five roads out of one clearing, and figures peeling off road by road — a wain-train's bells starting up, a punt pushing off, one walker vanishing into a hedgerow — painted like the first page of a picture-book, because it is one.

#### `the-between-roads`

The hedgerow lines, drove-paths, and high stiles that pass no hearth — the untold's home water, though every way uses them at need. Tales walk hearth to hearth, so a between-road is a corridor rumor cannot take; the miles are honest (no road-memory folds a path that barely remembers itself), the fords are cold, and the dawns out there belong to nobody, which is a way of saying they belong to you. Between-road walking is the Rounds' solitude — chosen, temporary, and respected: a maker met on a between-road is greeted with a nod and no questions — the one ground on the Rounds where the gift of a tale is not even hoped for, and a shared fire stays quiet as the hedges.

- **This could become:** the alternate map-edge layer — slower, rumor-silent routes that skip the lantern network entirely: no askings, no tales carried, no ratchet movement — the game's lawful breathing room, bought with tempo.
- **Visual:** England-in-a-dream lanes — high hedges, stiles, dew-lines, a kingfisher flash of cuff-lining as a small-packed figure climbs over and is gone. The palette drops to green-greys and the sound drops to birds: the one place the game goes quiet on purpose.

## 5 · Anchor declaration

One anchor on this slate, declared inline at its element and summarized here:

1. **`the-green-wains`** — the traveling fair is deliberately familiar folk furniture (painted wagons over the hill, bunting by dusk), chosen because it makes Roundelay's strangest economic claims instantly legible: **attention as a gatherable force** (`audience-of-things`) becomes "a fair is a portable audience"; **overflow running to the pourer** (`gleam-runoff`, the Moth Fair condition) becomes "the headline act on the wain-stage"; and the biggest Standing swings in the game arrive pre-explained by every county fair the player has ever attended.

Anchor share this layer: **1 of 10 named elements.** No thieves' guild, no college, no church, no cult, no empire, no houses, no chosen order — the weather picks the hands, and the ways of walking are exactly what they say they are: ways of walking.

## 6 · Register compliance notes (for critics)

- **Nothing is owed anywhere on this slate.** Ferry passage is gift-and-handsel in the ordinary Roundelay way; waymeet telling is custom, not condition (and the between-roads explicitly release even that); red-thread-days are convergence, not conscription.
- **Attend-don't-act holds:** the contested-asking resolution uses only canonical attending (an asking turning on its thread, compass-needle motion); no woken thing acts, speaks, or emotes anywhere above; no material speaks — courtship conditions stay behavioral (clay that likes weather-talk).
- **Grammar of light holds:** palefarers' undyed wool is a *things-fade* detail (nothing dyed, nothing to take), while the walker herself carries key-light only; the untold's freckled rim is gleam-grain on a person, never an edge.
- **Folk-scale guard holds:** light-following appears only as pack-scale practice distributed across all five ways.
- **Failure register holds:** dimming, spilling, and the Quiet Walk are untouched; the wains' stage-risk lands on the canonical spilling (lanes painted, toast made), never on anything punitive.
- **Timescale law holds:** the untold's fine-grain strategy shapes *what grain* gleam takes, never a within-run decay; rumor-pace levers (ferry-round, between-roads) act on the lantern network's information travel, not on Standing.
