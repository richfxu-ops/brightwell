# Layer 3 Proposal C — Relations to Light: The Six Ways of the Round

**Proposer:** C-relations-to-light
**Lens:** factions by their stance toward the Round of Light itself — chasers of overflow, feeders of the gloaming-table, courters of closed rounds, keepers of the noticing-game. Play differences = which lever of the one economy each culture pulls hardest.

---

## Thesis: hours, not nations

Roundelay has no peoples-at-odds. Everyone on the Rounds is roundfolk, and the map has no borders for a faction to defend, because the deep law leaves nothing to fight over: regard is conserved, value is created by moving, and nothing is anyone's to keep. What Roundelay has instead — and what this slate proposes as its factions — are **ways**: whole traveling cultures built on where a maker chooses to stand in the one circulation. The round of light runs dawn → eyes → hands → work → places → table → dawn, and every arc of that circle is somebody's calling. Ask a maker on the road *what are you?* and they will not name a house or an order. They will name an hour of the day.

A way is not joined and cannot be left behind on a shelf; it is *walked*. Nobody keeps a list of who walks which way, because the ways are worn — in cloth, in the hour a camp rises, in what the hands are doing when their owner thinks no one is watching — and worn things can be read across a fair at dusk, which is all the membership Roundelay has ever needed. Children sample all six ways the way children sample everything. Most makers settle into one by their second Round, the way a voice settles into its part of the song.

For the game: **each way is a player archetype** — a starting deck shape, a resource preference, a risk posture — and every way's strategy is a true belief about the world's physics, pushed as far as one pack can carry it.

## 1. The Morrow-Gleaners — the dawn-side way

**`morrow-gleaners`**

*Pale country gets the best mornings.* Every child on the Rounds knows the folk-saw; the morrow-gleaners built a whole traveling life on it. They walk the greyest roads on purpose — three rings chalked on the gatepost, five — sleeping at sundown, rising in the dark, and doing the day's true work inside the reissue, while the dawn is still coming down and the light is at its most gatherable. Where another maker reads a spiral and thinks *large work*, a gleaner reads it and thinks *rich mornings*: the fuller the local table, the redder the return, and nobody in Roundelay stands under more red dawns than they do. They love the Fade the way farmers love winter — not despite what it takes but because of what it turns under — and pale towns love them back with the particular warmth owed to the friend who visits precisely when you are least presentable. It is a gleaner custom never to describe a town as *pale*; among themselves they say a town is *saving up*.

**Relations.** To the Fade: gratitude, and a working partnership — they follow the out-breath the way gulls follow a plough. To askings: they take down the compounded ones other hands pass by, and they take down many, working in wide small strokes across many towns. To gleam: earned broadly and spent promptly; a gleaner bright enough for rafters-work has usually been too busy with wells to notice. To the other ways: provisioners of every crossing-fair, and famously fond of the eveners — the two camps call each other *the other end of the breath*.

- **This could become:** the **economy-rush archetype**. Resource preference: raw attention income — the morrow-gleaner run maximizes `the-unclaimed-morning`, routing deliberately through pale nodes for their richer dawns and their doubled askings. Deck strategy: cheap, wide, early — cards that reward the first plays of a turn ("first-hour" effects) and many small wakings over one great one. Risk posture: highest map exposure in the game, backed by income; the gleaner wins by making the Fade's arithmetic pay for itself and loses when a compounded asking outgrows a small-stroke engine.
- **Visual:** frost-grey wool that takes dawn rose — gleaner cloth is woven loose so the reissue gets into it, and by mid-morning a camp of them still carries a faint pink hem-glow, like people lit slightly from an earlier hour. Dew-beaded shoulders, wide hats slung back on cords, long staffs. Signature motion: the pre-dawn file — a line of silhouettes already on the ridge while the town's lamps are still on, arriving as everyone else's day is being put away. Across a fair you know them because they are the ones yawning happily at noon.

## 2. The Brimfolk — the way of the running-over

**`brimfolk`** *(anchor — declared below)*

*A full cup runs to the pourer* — and the brimfolk intend to be pouring. They are the Rounds' traveling fair-troupes: fanned wagon-trains that raise a fair the way other folk raise a barn, and who hold, as craft-creed, that light is most itself in the instant it overfills — that a work is not finished until it has been filled past its brim before as many eyes as one valley can hold. Every generation gets perhaps a handful of hours when overflow goes visible — the Moth Fair condition, light running up the strings in front of everyone — and the brimfolk arrange their whole wandering lives around being present for all of them, or better, around *building* them: the cup, the crowd, and the reel, timed for the one night a whole countryside can watch. They fail bigger than anyone, in public, from a height, and they hold the patent on the old toast — *to the lanes we've painted* — because more of Roundelay's prettiest alleys are theirs than anyone's.

**Relations.** To gleam: theirs arrives in spikes and leaves in spills; a brimfolk elder's rim-light is all ripples and freckles, not a calm span anywhere on it. To askings: they favor the performance-shaped ones — a summer, a festival, a river's voice — and will walk a week out of their way for an asking with an audience folded inside it. To the Fade: the reason there is always, somewhere, a valley that needs a fair. To the other ways: they quarrel beautifully with the eveners (see the Brim Question, below) and live in open symbiosis with the tale-walkers — fairs make tales, and tales make fairs.

**ANCHOR, declared:** the traveling festival troupe is folk-tale furniture — wagons, ribbons, the fair arriving at the edge of town — and it is kept deliberately familiar so that the genuinely novel cargo riding it stays legible at a glance: **overflow-to-cause as the Standing-gain law** (`gleam-runoff`), **the Moth Fair condition** (light visible in motion only at peak overflow before a crowd), and **attention as a spendable multiplier** (`audience-of-things`). The player who has seen a circus knows the brimfolk in one frame; everything they teach about the physics is new.

- **This could become:** the **combo/burst archetype**. Deck strategy: chained plays that hold a room — attention sustained without stalling, multiplied, then spent in one overfilled masterwork; overkill delight above the waking threshold converts to Standing at full rate, so the brimfolk turn is the biggest single Standing swing in the game. Resource preference: audience and venue above all; a brimfolk run routes by crowd, not by need. Risk posture: feast-or-spill — the highest variance archetype, whose failures visibly re-color the node for future runs (`the-spilling`) and whose successes are the game's fireworks.
- **Visual:** poppy red, brass, and moth-wing white; cuffs sewn with tiny handsels that ring when the hands move, so a working brimfolk sounds like faint rain on bells. Silhouette: always mid-flourish — instrument on the back, one arm describing something. They travel as a fan of wagons and arrive as a fairground unfolding; across a fair you don't spot the brimfolk, you spot everyone else turning toward them.

## 3. The Eveners — the dusk-side way

**`eveners`**

The eveners walk with the out-breath. Their circuits run widdershins, against the sun; their working hour is dusk; and their calling is the tenderest work on the Rounds: they are the ones a town sends for when it has decided, gently and together, that a thing will not be asked-for again. Then the eveners keep a **last lighting** — one full evening of the whole town's gathered regard on the retiring mill, the bridge past its knack, the kiln that has fired its last true glaze: its held light brightened hand to hand one final time, its stories told at its foot, its colors glad — and then it is left to the tide, attended to the end, and the valley's dawns run richer for a season on what it returns. Nothing is taken and nothing is hurried; the tide is simply not met alone. They are also the Rounds' great un-hoarders — around an evener, strongboxes empty not by argument but by example, because no one on the roads spends a handsel more beautifully — and they name themselves for the potter's motion: they even things, the way a thumb evens a rim, and they keep the evening.

**Relations.** To the Fade: colleagues; an evener will tell you, comfortably, that they and the tide do the same work at two speeds. To the gloaming-table: their garden — they plant evenings and the country harvests mornings. To askings: they take down few, and finish every one; a stale asking distresses an evener more than anyone on the Rounds. To the other ways: the morrow-gleaners are the other end of their breath and their oldest fondness — evener evenings feed gleaner dawns, and both camps know it — while the brimfolk are their beloved lifelong argument.

- **This could become:** the **sacrifice-and-velocity archetype**. Deck strategy: exhaust-heavy and self-thinning — pieces performed to destruction for full value, inert and Fade-prone cards converted, by last lighting, into local-table income (richer dawns downstream on this very route). Resource preference: handsel velocity — the evener economy keeps money bright by keeping it moving, and profits from the held-light law more than any other way. Risk posture: low and steady; the control archetype that wins long runs by composting what other decks hoard, and by making even its losses arrive somewhere.
- **Visual:** plum, slate-blue, and one line of ember-orange at the trim; shawls; lanterns carried low, at knee height, so an evener's light pools on the road rather than the face. One small bell, rung once, is the whole liturgy of a last lighting. Motion: dusk-paced — the only people at a fair moving at the speed of the hour after it ends. Across a fair you know an evener because things look somehow *finished* near them.

## 4. The Whorlwise — the way of the closed round

**`whorlwise`**

The whorlwise court the one thing in the world the weathering never reaches. They walk wonder to wonder — the thousand-year stair, the river that never once forgot its knack — and they treat the near-closures the way you treat a student on the verge of something: the journey-pieces heaped on the Setterby stair are mostly theirs, left the way you leave encouragement. Their craft-creed runs against the whole grain of a traveling life and they hold it cheerfully: make *few* things; make them before many eyes; make them to outlast the maker's name — for the whorlwise have taken the closed rounds' one lesson personally, and hold that the mark swallowed is the mark perfected. And at the bottom of the way, the horizon-hope the laws neither offer nor forbid: that someday, somewhere, a masterwork made attentively enough, before audience enough, will close — and some walking life will have been the one that made it. There is no whorlwise anything to join. There is a direction to walk, and some walk it a season, and some walk it a life, and the ones who walk it a life can be found at the Bell of Hask between crossings, standing the way other people stand at the sea.

**Relations.** To the unmarked wonders: colleagues, exactly as all makers hold — but the whorlwise visit. To gleam: almost indifferent as wealth, entirely instrumental as key — they bank shine to spend on the proudest materials, the stuff that must be sung to, because a work meant for centuries is not made of river clay. To askings: they take only the ones that could stand a hundred years — a bridge, a bell, a stair — and lantern-keepers save such askings for pale cloth on the road. To the light-followers: a beloved, generations-old argument conducted on the same step (see below).

- **This could become:** the **permanence-engine archetype**. Deck strategy: the smallest deck in the game, everything bench-fired — few cards, every one woken, compounding artifacts that never leave play; the relic-and-discovery build, whose route bends toward unmarked-wonder nodes and whose notebook fills with circulation measurements. Resource preference: fired waking over all else; proud materials; peak-gleam as a gate to them. Risk posture: the slow-start snowball — weakest opening hours in the game, unmatched endgame, and the archetype whose collection screen quietly assembles the long riddle.
- **Visual:** undyed, bone-pale cloth — chosen because it takes edge-light the way paper takes ink, so a whorlwise standing near a wonder borrows a faint tracing of its glow along every fold. Packs worn open, spread flat like display trays: their pieces are gifts-in-waiting. Motion: stillness — the whorlwise are the long-lookers, the figures who have plainly been standing there an hour. Across a fair, find the one person facing away from the stage, studying the joinery of the stage.

## 5. The Light-Followers — the noticing-game as a calling

**`light-followers`** *(carried from Layer 2; faction-facing treatment here — a practice, never an institution, per canon guard)*

The light-followers are children who never stopped playing the noticing-game, and this layer adds nothing to what they are — only where they stand in a fair. Canon holds and is restated as this slate's hardest wall: there is no academy of them and never will be, no order, no roll of names, no instrument that will not fit in a pack — tally-chalk, hazel wands, pin-lanterns, patience; the windowsill, not the laboratory. You are a light-follower while you are counting, and when you put the chalk down you have left, and you may pick it up again at any age, and most people do at least twice in a life. What makes them a *way* is simply that some folk never put the chalk down: they walk the Rounds reading seams with hazel, sitting through ten thousand makings with tally-chalk, and their counts are trusted in every valley for the plainest reason in the world — the chalk doesn't care. They are how the gatepost spirals came to mean the same thing everywhere: not decreed, just checked, hearth by hearth, until it was so.

**Relations.** To the combing: its readers — they dowse the seams weeks ahead, and the seventh ring's warning travels on their word. To askings: they take few and small; their working attention is spent on watching other people's. To gleam: modest by trade — counting delights no crowds — and they are cheerfully unbothered, having measured exactly what they're forgoing. To every other way: the standing witness; matched benches want a follower with chalk the way a fair wants a fiddler, and the whorlwise argument (gifts versus tallies, on the same stair) is the happiest quarrel either camp has.

- **This could become:** the **information archetype**. Deck strategy: scry-and-select — reveal tomorrow's dawns, preview what waits in a lantern's rafters, learn a material's terms before courting it, read exactly which pale node will re-color cheapest; a deck that spends plays on *knowing* and wins by never wasting one. Resource preference: revealed information as literal resource — chalk marks persist on the map as player-made annotations with mechanical weight. Risk posture: the lowest-variance way in the game; the archetype for the player who wants Roundelay as an almanac, deterministic and deep.
- **Visual:** chalk-dusted fingers and cuffs, a hazel wand through the belt, and at night the pin-lantern — a tin can of pinpricks throwing a small constellation on the ground wherever they crouch. Silhouette: crouched, marking; always slightly out of the crowd's current, at the edge where the counting is easier. And always, among the grown walkers, some actual children — because the way and the game are the same thing, and everyone in Roundelay can tell.

## 6. The Tale-Walkers — the way of the lines of cause

**`tale-walkers`**

Tales walk at a hearth's pace, and the tale-walkers walk with them. Their calling is the tending of the lines of cause themselves: no good deed's light lost on the road. When a well comes back sweet in a town of forty souls, it is a tale-walker who carries the telling over the hill to the firesides that would never otherwise hear it — and three towns away, at dusk, a maker brightens for work she has half-forgotten, because the delight finally reached a new hearth and ran home along the line. They cannot improve a tale and would not — a tale walks how it wants, and every walker learns young that a telling pushed is a telling dropped — but they can keep it from sitting down, and that is the whole craft: which fires to carry it to, in which order, told plain and let go. They own almost nothing, pack lighter than anyone on the Rounds, and are the most-welcomed guests in the world, since a tale-walker at your fire means your house will hear the road's best year in one night — and hearth-right, they like to say, was probably invented by a house that hoped one of them was coming.

**Relations.** To gleam: they cause other people's — the runoff they set moving lands on distant knuckles, and they take their pay in doorways opening. To hearth-right: their home ground and their workshop; the murmur-net of hearths and woken things is the water they swim in. To askings: they carry word of the big ones outward, so that bright hands hear of a need before the road brings them past it. To the brimfolk: open symbiosis. To the light-followers: an old fond friction — a tale improves in the walking, a tally must not, and both camps keep the Moth Fair story, only one version of which has four hundred people in it. Both numbers get told at the same fires, on purpose.

- **This could become:** the **support and delayed-value archetype**. Deck strategy: the run's own history as an instrument — bank events as tellings, spend them at hearths for compounding boons, and harvest late-arriving, source-stamped Standing ("Wader's Ford still tells of the reel — +2") on a several-turn delay. Resource preference: road-memory and relationships — the tale-walker build makes the map itself warmer, and gets stronger the more history a run accumulates. Risk posture: middle — weak burst, no armor, but the only archetype whose past turns keep paying, and the natural second-archetype teacher of the game's deepest law: light travels on foot.
- **Visual:** the patchwork coat — every patch a square of some house's cloth, given at a hearth, so an old walker wears forty years of welcomes and the coat is the résumé the world is allowed to have (a thing of cloth, never of paper). Nearly empty-handed: smallest pack on the Rounds, since their piece is the telling. Motion: quick between towns, slow inside them — they cross country like weather and cross a kitchen like honey. Across a fair, theirs is the coat you can read.

## How the ways meet: rivalry without a single raised hand

Roundelay's factions cannot fight over territory (there is none to hold), wealth (it dims when held), or power (a lantern shows a town's settled belief, and no one can decree a belief). What is left to contest is exactly what a craft culture would contest: **whose way of standing in the light is truest** — and the world, conveniently, is measurable.

**`matched-benches`** — the Rounds' whole grammar of rivalry, in one custom. When two makers want the same asking, or two ways disagree about how a thing should be made, they set two benches under one lantern and both build, before one crowd — and the room decides, not by any vote, but by where its regard visibly pools; a light-follower with chalk keeps the count, because the chalk doesn't care. The loser loses nothing but the evening — the asking goes to the bench the room warmed to, the crowd got a fair, and half the friendships on the Rounds were sealed across matched benches by makers who could not stop laughing at each other's solutions.
- **This could become:** the game's rival-encounter system — never combat: a parallel-build node where an NPC maker of another archetype works the same asking, attention is the contested pool, and you win by moving the room, not by touching the rival. Losses cost tempo and the asking, never Standing directly — the quiet register, mechanized.
- **Visual:** two benches, one lantern, one crowd leaning like grass in a crosswind; between the benches, the pooling of warm light on the flagstones visibly deeper on one side — the game's cleanest single image of contest without harm.

**The Brim Question.** The brimfolk hold that light is most alive in the instant it overfills; the eveners, that it is most alive coming home. This is the Rounds' great craft-quarrel — two true readings of one conservation law — and it is prosecuted with performances, spent handsels, and matched benches, never settled, and never meant to be: elders of both camps have grown old refining the other side's arguments for them.

**Route courtesy.** Morrow-gleaners walk sunwise to meet the dawns; eveners walk widdershins to meet the dusks; and where the two circuits cross at a town, custom makes a small festival of it — a **double-lit day**, dawn-work and a dusk-lighting in one turn of the sun, held to be lucky for weddings and first apprenticings.

**The Setterby disagreement.** The whorlwise leave journey-pieces on the near-closed stair the way you encourage a student; the light-followers' chalk shows the stair's ratio unchanged in forty years of gifts. The whorlwise answer that the encouragement was never for the stair — it was for the givers — and the followers chalk *that* claim on the step as untestable, and both camps consider the exchange one of the Rounds' treasures. Gifts and tallies sit on the same stone, and neither would move the other's.

**Contested tales.** Tale-walkers and light-followers keep rival custodies of every famous hour — the walking version and the chalked version — and the Rounds' settled wisdom is to tell both at the same fire and let the listeners keep whichever they need.

## Who tends the boss tier: the crossing-fair

No one owns the great askings — the land's needs have no gatekeeper, only a weather-report and a welcoming committee — and the answering maker is never chosen, because a great asking, like every held and woken thing, aims itself at the brightest grain the country has heard of. What culture provides is everything *around* that aim, and every way has its part:

**`the-crossing-fair`** — the festival that assembles, over a season, around a forming or found great asking. The light-followers foretell it: seams read with hazel, the seventh ring called on the gateposts, warning walking ahead of the weather. The finder ties the red thread, as everyone has always done. The tale-walkers carry the word outward along the hearth-lines, so the country's brightest hands hear of the need before the aim finds them. And then the country converges: brimfolk raise the ring of watchers — a great work wants an audience the size of its asking, and the fair *is* the venue; morrow-gleaners provision the pale country around the site, working its rich dawns to feed the crowd; eveners keep last lightings for whatever the great re-making will retire, so nothing meets the tide unattended in the middle of a festival; and the whorlwise come and stand at the edge with their packs open, because if a round is ever going to close in front of witnesses, it will close here. When the work is done and the season re-made, the fair walks home in every direction at once, and the site keeps a little of everyone.
- **This could become:** the boss-encounter frame — every great asking arrives wrapped in a crossing-fair, so the boss node is also the run's richest festival economy: maximum available attention (the crowd is the multiplier), every archetype's support practices present as services and events, and the whole encounter readable seasons ahead on the map as the spirals converge. The boss is never a monster; it is the biggest audience of your life, gathered around the hardest work of it.
- **Visual:** a valley at dusk from above — the red-threaded site dark and waiting at the center, and around it, rings: whorlwise pale cloth innermost and stone-still, then the fairground's poppy-and-brass, then wagon-fires and lantern-strings dissolving outward into the hills, the whole country arranged like a woken thing attending. One gleam-lit figure crossing the empty middle, pack on.

## Shared practices that bind the ways

**`the-even-morrow`** — the equinox festival, held wherever a gleaner circuit and an evener circuit cross nearest the turning day. For one day the two ends of the breath trade hours as a courtesy-duel: eveners rise in the dark and work a dawn, gleaners stay up and keep a dusk, each camp performing the other's way with ostentatious, loving incompetence, and the matched benches at even-morrow are the funniest of the year. Underneath the comedy sits the world's calendar: the equinox is when the Fade's seasonal rate turns, and the festival is how the whole Rounds feels the turn together.
- **This could become:** the calendar node — a twice-per-run fixed event where the Fade-rate visibly shifts for the season, with a double-income, double-seep festival day and archetype-swap challenges (play a borrowed off-way card to a bonus) that teach cross-archetype literacy inside the fiction.
- **Visual:** one long table set at a crossroads at first grey light, plum shawls and frost-grey wool interleaved down its whole length, everyone slightly wrong-footed by the hour and delighted about it; two lanterns at the table's ends, one held high, one held low.

**`season-shadowing`** — how craft crosses between the ways: you walk a season behind a master of another way, carrying their pack, keeping their hours, asking nothing. There is no arrangement and nothing changes hands at the start — a shadow simply starts walking, and a master who minds says so, and one who doesn't, doesn't. At season's end the shadow walks away with one journey-piece of the master's way, given, as everything in Roundelay is given, because giving is how it moves. Half the elders on the Rounds can name every shadow they ever kept, and do, at fairs, at length.
- **This could become:** the cross-archetype acquisition system — a run event where the player shadows an NPC master of another way for a map-segment (adopting one of their constraints: keep dusk hours, spend every handsel, play only fired cards) and exits with an off-archetype card or practice, making hybrid builds diegetic: every splash card in a deck has a master's name on it.
- **Visual:** the road at evening, two silhouettes: one broad under a master's pack, one narrow and empty-handed, half a step behind and one stride out of the wheel-rut — the apprenticeship the whole world recognizes at a hundred yards. At season's end, the same road, the same two figures, one small tin token passing between them.

## Anchor declaration (Layer 3)

One anchor in this slate, declared inline above and summarized here:

1. **`brimfolk`** — the traveling festival troupe is folk-tale furniture (wagons, ribbons, the fair at the edge of town), kept deliberately familiar so that the layer's strangest physics stays legible through it: overflow-to-cause as the Standing-gain law, the Moth Fair condition (light visible in motion only at peak overflow before a crowd), and attention as a spendable multiplier. A player who has seen a circus reads the brimfolk in one frame and learns conservation physics from them by turn three.

Anchor share this layer: 1 of 10 named elements. No thieves' anything, no college (the light-followers remain a windowsill practice, restated as a hard wall), no church of the light (the whorlwise have no rites, no roll, and no doctrine beyond a direction to walk and an argument they cherish losing), no houses playing thrones (there is nothing to sit on), and the only contest mechanism in the slate is two benches under one lantern with a crowd deciding by warmth.

## Register compliance

All Layer 1–2 bans honored throughout: nothing here is owed, filed, ranked, or adjudicated — ways are walked, not joined; the tale-walker's coat is cloth, never paper; matched benches cost the loser nothing but the evening; and every failure register above (spills, lost benches, stale askings) resolves as the world growing quiet or the light landing somewhere else, never as penalty. Woken things throughout only attend (the lantern holds, the stage-side crowd leans, the country arranges itself *like* a woken thing attending). The gloaming-table stays a pantry (the eveners garden it; nothing living enters it). The light-followers stay institution-free. No within-run decay clock is introduced on Standing anywhere in the slate — the eveners' economy touches handsels and board-state only, per the timescale law.
