# Proposal B — THE LIVING MAP

**Layer 5 · geography_run_fiction · proposer B**

The lens, committed to fully: a run is not a thing the maker does *to* the countryside. It is one conversation between a maker and a region — and the region talks back the whole way, in color and greyness, in spirals and chalk, in roads that fold their miles for the well-loved and dawns that spend themselves hardest over the country everyone else walked past. This proposal builds the run from the land side. Everything the player needs to know about pressure, income, danger, and the finale is written *on the land*, in the three channels canon already enforces: places fade, people gleam, things wake. The map is not an interface laid over Roundelay. The map is Roundelay, holding still long enough to be read.

Danger, per the locked register, is never combat: the road's perils are weather, staleness, dimming, mistimed seasons, and the land's own strangeness — a ford in spring flood, an asking gone cold in the pack, a stilled valley where nothing breathes, a grey crown at the wrong end of a long day. Nothing on any road in this proposal carries a weapon.

---

## 1 · THE RUN — one walked verse

**A run is a verse** — that is its name on the Rounds, and nobody had to coin it, because canon already sings it: the song keeps the verse and forgets the singer, and every re-spark is a fresh maker, walking a fresh verse. Ask a porch elder how long they traveled and they will answer in verses, not years: *"Nineteen verses, and the Wold Round for eleven of them."* One verse is one maker's turn of one Round: out through the gate in spring, when the tide is near-still and the roads come open; working the circuit through summer; and ending in autumn — every verse ends in autumn, because in Roundelay a run's end *is* autumn — in one of exactly two ways, both of them canon and both of them kind.

**How it begins:** see `the-setting-out` below. A fresh maker is sparked onto the road the morning their home lantern first turns something toward their hands — a spoon, a whistle, something kettle-sized — and the town walks them to the gate. Because every verse is a fresh maker (the fresh-verse ruling, I-013), every verse begins unread: no lantern anywhere holds a fired sizing of these hands yet, and the whole ladder is still ahead.

**A leg is a stretch.** Canon already gave the word — stretches are the song-habits the road learned — and the run adopts it: a stretch is the road from one lantern to the next, walked as one piece, with its own weather, its own marks, its own manners. The verse is a necklace of stretches strung around the circuit.

**A turn is a morning.** The unclaimed morning is the day's whole income of working attention, and a morning can only be stood in once — so the run's atomic beat is dawn-to-dusk: stand in the morning, spend the day's regard on the road or at the bench, tell or rest at the fire. A stretch is one to a few mornings; a verse is one spring-to-autumn of them.

**How it ends, dimming:** exactly as locked canon holds. Standing exhausted, the world gone shy, the pack set down — the Quiet Walk, the circuit walked once more in dawn-grey with nothing asked, everything the maker woke still carrying its gold edge behind them. Autumn, the quiet kind.

**How it ends, triumphant:** see `the-coming-round` below. The bright ending is not escaping the circuit — a Round is a loop with a home note, and you never finish one; you hand it on mid-song. A triumphant verse *comes round*: the maker answers the great asking their own skipped springs combed into being, walks the last stretches home with the whole circuit re-colored behind them, and hands the Round on at a waymeet — one journey-piece taught to a season-shadow, one woken work chosen to stand, and then the porch, and the tea, and the watching. Autumn, the harvest kind. Both endings feed the same meta-law: nothing of a maker is ever kept, only what they gave.

## 2 · THE MAP — what a route strings together

A Round is drawn the way makers actually draw them (see `the-inked-round`): a hand-inked loop with a home note, threaded through **lightsheds** — the basins of the world's light, this layer's master texture (see `the-lightshed`) — and strung with nodes whose silhouettes a player learns the way they learn weather:

- **Bright towns** — colored roofs stacked to the hearth-line, and at the gate the asking-lantern, dozens of small objects turning on red thread. Work here is sized and witnessed; dawns are ordinary, because a kept town's table runs thin.
- **Pale towns** — grey at the rooftops, one stubborn ember of color at the hearth-line, and every gatepost ringed with chalk spirals. The paling-arithmetic makes the pressure legible from the last hill: three rings is a plea, five is a poem, seven is a great asking being born. Richer work, richer glad-loads, and — by the lightshed law — the richest mornings on the Round.
- **Hearth-hamlets** — a single thread of smoke between towns; a hearth-right night, a telling given, a doorstep asking or two on the door-nails (see `the-doorstep-askings`). The small change of the road.
- **Quiet porches** — wide eaves, a row of un-rimmed elders, every woken thing on the street tilted toward them. Rest, audience, and forecasting: a porch can read the tellings walking toward you two dusks out.
- **Waymeets** — the many-fingered signpost, the plank table, the crossing of circuits. Swap ground, shadow ground, the place the six gaits mix. Held by no one, fed like a fire.
- **The Round-Fair** — the one node that moves: bottle-green wagons perpetually a week ahead of autumn, sweeping the circuit like a second hand on a clock face. Chase it, intercept it, or let it come to you at compounded cost elsewhere.
- **Crown-country** — shrines, beacon-stones, high pastures on the basin rims: first to pale, thin of dawn, rich in last-red, and the map's weather-glass (see `crown-country`).
- **Crossings and fords** — ferry landings, laughing-riverwood water, the Bell of Hask on its slow public riddle. Where roads meet water the land is at its most opinionated: floods close stretches, and the glad places grow the glad materials.
- **Between-roads** — the stile-and-hedgerow edges that pass no hearth: no askings, no tales carried, honest miles. The map's lawful breathing room, bought with tempo.
- **Combing-grounds** — where a countryside's missing is converging (see `the-combing-grounds`): seams a dowser can read, spirals summing on the inked round, and at last the red thread on a high stone. The finale, telegraphed for seasons.
- **The long grey roads** — left-behind country, hearths gone out kindly, the gleaning's home water. The greyest miles on the Round and, at dawn, from the basin floor, the most beautiful.
- **Stilled sheds** — three in living memory (see the carried issues): a whole lightshed holding its breath, waxy and over-bright, no pressure and no income, with a release date the whole country watches for.
- **Unmarked wonders** — a stair, a bell, a river that never once forgot: edge-lit along every line, discovery nodes, colleagues to tip your hat to.

**What choosing a route feels like:** less like reading a chart than like choosing whom to travel with and which patience to answer. The player reads five weathers at once, all of them painted on the land: the *spirals* (what is saving up, and how far along); the *crowns* (which basins are shedding hardest — grey up top means a full table below); the *dawn-forecast* (where the mornings will be immense); the *fair's position* (a week ahead of autumn, always); and the *year itself* (how loud the tide is this season — see `the-breathing-year`). Under all five runs the sixth weather, the personal one: which roads know you (see `the-remembering-roads`), and where your own tellings are tonight, walking fireside to fireside at their posted pace. Route choice in Roundelay is almanac-reading in a warm way — and every one of those readings is diegetic paint, not overlay.

**How pressure reads on the land:** the gatepost spirals are the countryside's own notation, copied forward along the roads as road-marks so a maker reads a town's patience two valleys before topping the last hill; crown-country greys first, so the rims of the map are its early-warning line; and when patience runs long enough, the seams themselves become legible — hazel-readable currents in the wash, converging on the place the country's missing will comb. Nothing about the run's difficulty lives in a menu. It lives on gateposts, hilltops, and high stones, in chalk and grey and red thread.

## 3 · WHO HANGS THE NEED — issuers and witnesses

Canon permits exactly three sources of asking, and this layer adds one lawful smallest tier below them:

1. **Towns, through their lanterns** — the ordinary contracts. A lantern is a woken thing whose one fixed attending is the reading of hands: it *sizes* to the brightest the road has heard you be (fired, one-way, forever) and *shows* by tonight's gleam. A town's asking is crafted, woken, and aimed; it ratchets, per locked canon.
2. **The countryside, through the combing** — the great askings, boss tier. No one issues them and canon forbids anyone to: a whole country's crossed and living regard combs the loose wash into shape at the heart of its palest shed, and the asking aims itself at the brightest grain the country has heard of. The weather picks the hands.
3. **Materials, through their terms** — canon's quiet third issuer: a term accepted is an asking like any other and can go stale. The courting is contract-shaped all the way down — which is why a pack is a garden, and why a Mannerly's risks are real.
4. **Households, through the door-nail** — this layer's addition, smallest tier (see `the-doorstep-askings`): one kitchen's need, hung hastily on its own doorstep, unwoken and unaimed, first-come and ratchet-free. Below the lantern's notice, and the road's small daily bread.

**Who witnesses and receives the finished work:** never an official, because there are none. The work is received by the *place* — it stays where it was made, and the square re-colors around it like watercolor blooming — and witnessed by the only court in Roundelay, the audience of things: chairs a quarter-turn, kettles humming, the flagstones found richer in the morning. The town answers with the glad-load at the gate, scaled to the spiral's rings; the hearths receive the *telling*, and carry it down the roads at a fireside a night, which is how the next lantern learns; the porches receive the maker; and the light-followers sit through all of it with tally-chalk, counting for the joy of counting, deciding nothing. A great asking is witnessed by the gathered bench — a countryside that combed a need into being is the same countryside that wants to watch it answered.

## 4 · CARRIED ISSUES, ANSWERED IN-FICTION

**I-017 — the elevation law, in one line.** ***Every lightshed dawns on what it paled: the seep leaves by the crown and returns by the floor of one and the same basin.*** The Fade's seep runs downhill *within* a basin, into that basin's own gloaming-table under its own valley floor; dawn is reissued from the local table over the country that fed it. So the two canon facts are one fact seen from two elevations: **crowns pale first and dawn thin** (their wash runs off downhill, and what they shed is no longer beneath them to come back), while **floors pale slow and dawn rich** (they sit on their own cupped catchment, holding color like cupped hands — and when a whole countryside *has* paled, everything it stopped holding is pooled directly under its floors). "Pale country gets the best mornings" is a basin-scale saying and exactly true at basin scale: the greyer a lightshed's evenings, the fuller its table, the redder the mornings over its floor. A pale hilltop gets a modest dawn; the pale valley below it gets the immense one. Both follow from the same downhill seep, and neither edits a word of Layers 2–4. (Full geography at `the-lightshed`.)

**I-030 — the speed of regard-movement, in one law.** ***Light is quick in the room and on foot on the road.*** Overflow runs backward along its line of cause — and where cause and delight stand in one place, the line is *live*, and the overflow lands in the instant: the Moth Fair condition, four hundred people and one fiddler in one field, the light visibly up the strings and into her hands before the reel ended. But the moment the maker walks on, no live line remains — a line of cause beyond the room exists only as *tellings*, and tellings walk at a hearth's pace, one fireside a night, landing only when seen landing. So all present delight is instant, and all distant rise is on foot: the square is the fast place and the road is the slow one, and both speeds are the same law — regard moves at the speed of its line of cause, which is the speed of light in a room and the speed of a story on a dark road. This adds no courier and touches no clock canon has set; it only names why the fair pays tonight and the lantern pays in a fortnight. (Full fiction at `the-two-paces-of-light`.)

**I-043 — stilled-summer rarity, in geography terms.** A stilling is *shed-bound*: it fills exactly one lightshed, rim to rim — the held breath stops at the crown-line, and no stilling in any walking-song has ever crossed one — and the songs hold **three stillings in living memory across all the Rounds together**, each in a shed that holds a near-closed wonder, out of the many thousands of lightsheds the circuits thread. Set that against a traveling life: one verse crosses a few dozen sheds; a long life is forty-odd verses, a couple of thousand basin-crossings — and still most makers walk every verse of that life and never once cross a stilled one. Rarer than a new-found closed round, exactly as Layer 4 holds — the light-followers' short list of near-closures is *longer* than the list of stillings — and when a maker does cross one, it is the story their porch tells for a generation.

## 5 · NAMED ELEMENTS

#### `the-walked-verse`

A run's true name on the Rounds, and nobody coined it: the song keeps the verse and forgets the singer, so one maker's turn of one Round is **a verse**, and traveling lives are counted in them. A verse has the shape of the year it rides — out the home gate in spring when the tide is near-still, working the circuit sunwise or widdershins as your way walks, and ending, both ways, in autumn. Its beats are canon's own: the **morning** is the turn, because a morning can only be stood in once; the **stretch** is the leg, one lantern to the next, walked as one piece with its own weather and marks and manners. And a verse belongs to the land as much as the maker: the Round is only a Round because it goes on being walked, and what the region keeps of a verse — the fired works, the re-colorings, the marks, the tellings still walking after the walker has gone — is the land's half of the conversation, spoken slowly, in color, for years. Retiring makers all say the same thing, and mean it as geography: you never finish a Round; you hand it on mid-song.

- **This could become:** the run's formal structure — a loop-with-home-note circuit of stretches, played in mornings (turn = one dawn income, spent once), with the season as act structure; run summary screens count "the verse" in stretches walked, towns re-colored, tellings still on the road at autumn.
- **Visual:** the verse as a painted year: one continuous hand-inked line that leaves a spring-green gate, threads summer-gold basins, and comes home (or goes quiet) under autumn amber — the whole run readable as a single calligraphic stroke around a circle.

#### `the-setting-out` *(anchor — declared: the young journeyman leaving home at first light with a pack is folk-tale furniture, kept deliberately familiar so that three strange things read through it at a glance: the fresh-verse law — every run a new maker whom no lantern has read; the first reading — contract sizing witnessed at its smallest, so the ratchet is legible from minute one; and the town-walks-you-out gift economy, a glad-load in miniature with nothing asked anywhere in it.)*

A verse begins the morning a lantern first turns something toward a new pair of hands. It is always spring — the roads come open when the tide goes near-still — and it is always small: a tin spoon, a cracked whistle, something kettle-sized off the lowest hooks, because this lantern has never read these hands and a first reading is taken close. The town does the rest without being asked, because it cannot be helped: someone fills the pack's side-loops with mostly-dull handsels for the whittling of them, someone's grandmother teaches one journey-piece at the gate, and a child runs ahead to chalk the first road-mark of the maker's life — a bird, pointing outward — so the first stretch already knows someone is coming. No rite, no roll, no witnessed enrollment; the lantern's first reading is the only beginning the world requires, and by the time the gate is out of sight the verse is simply *going*, the way the song is always simply going when you join it.

- **This could become:** the run-start node and tutorial — a first contract sized at kettle-tier by law (fresh maker, unread hands), a starter glad-load in miniature, and the first road-mark as the map's first revealed intel; meta-variation lives in *which* home town, whose lantern, and what the child chalks.
- **Visual:** the gate at first light from inside the town looking out — pack silhouette against a spring-pale road, lantern objects turning overhead, one fresh chalk bird on the gatepost still wet, and the whole scene's color leaning toward the doorway like the first note of something.

#### `the-lightshed`

The map's master texture, and the one elevation law worn as country: as rain has watersheds, light has **lightsheds** — the basin within which all of a country's seep runs downhill to one gloaming-table, and out of which all of that table's dawns rise. Every lightshed dawns on what it paled: the seep leaves by the crown and returns by the floor. So a shed's rim greys first and wakes to thin ordinary mornings, its floor holds color longest like a cupped hand — and when a floor *does* pale, it pales sitting directly on top of everything it ever shed, which is why the immense dawns belong to the palest floors, and why Gleaners walking the grey descend at dusk on purpose: glean the crowns by day, sleep at the floor, stand in the return. Makers speak of sheds the way sailors speak of waters — *"that's Quill Vale's shed; her mornings run rich this year"* — and the shed-lines, walked and checked hearth by hearth for generations, are inked on every circuit map as faint crown-ridges, because to know where the line runs is to know where tonight's greyness will surface as tomorrow's gold.

- **This could become:** terrain as a two-dial system — elevation sets Fade-rate (crowns pale fast, floors slow) and dawn-income geography (turn-start draw keyed to the local shed's table, richest over pale floors), with shed borders as strategic map regions: paling anywhere in a shed feeds dawn income everywhere on its floor, making "where to sleep" a real decision every night.
- **Visual:** the naturalist's-plate cross-section scaled to a whole map: basins shaded in paired gradients — grey gathering at the rims, gold veins running under the floors — so a single glance reads every shed's state as weather: grey crown over warm floor means mornings are coming.

#### `crown-country`

The rims of the lightsheds — hilltop shrines, beacon-stones, high pastures, the fence-line country where the world greys first. Crown-country is poor by every measure the valleys use and rich by three of its own. It is the map's **weather-glass**: the crown pales seasons before the floor, so the road reads a shed's state from its rim — *when the crown goes grey, the floor is saving up* — and makers top a ridge and read the next basin the way farmers read a sky. It is **gleaning country**: the left-behind heights hold the last-red longest, and the greyest crowns fill the reddest jars. And it is the **honest high road**: crown paths are the loneliest stretches on any Round — no hearths, thin dawns, weather with opinions — walked for the reading, the red, and the long sight of three sheds at once. Crown shrines are kept the way porches are kept, by whoever is sitting there; leaving a shrine brighter than you found it is old, uncommanded custom, and a re-made crown shrine is the one work whose re-coloring the whole shed can see from below.

- **This could become:** high-route nodes trading income for intelligence — thin dawns and no lanterns, but a free read of adjacent sheds' states (spiral-sums, paling forecasts, combing seams) plus the game's best gleaning yields; the shrine re-making as a low-reward, whole-shed-visible act that seeds road-memory and tellings across every town below.
- **Visual:** a bare hill's silhouette against a colored valley — grey cairn, one tied red thread snapping in wind, and below and beyond, three basins in three different states of grey-and-gold; the maker small on the ridge line, coat pressed flat by weather.

#### `the-remembering-roads`

Roads in Roundelay have names, manners, and memories, and a route is therefore a *relationship*: the Wold Road is broad and sociable and passes a hearth every mile; the Setterby stair-road is high and patient and expects you to stop and study the joinery; the between-roads are quiet on purpose and greet you with a nod and no questions. Road-memory — canon's own law — accrues to *how you travel*, not who you are: keep a stretch's marks up, feed its waymeet table, sing its stretch-song, answer its doorsteps, and the miles begin to fold politely, the fords shallow, the hedge holds out blackberries on the return leg. Neglect is never punished; an unwalked road simply stays a stranger, full length and full weather. So choosing a route is choosing which conversations to continue, and every veteran of a Round has roads that are old friends and roads that are nodding acquaintances — and one, usually, that they are still courting, the way you court proud silver: patiently, and on its terms.

- **This could become:** edge-based (not node-based) relationship tracks — travel buffs that live on specific road segments and deepen with tended re-travel: folded miles (reduced travel cost), shallow fords (weather resistance), return-leg gifts; the system that makes a loop structure strategically rich, since the circuit's second half walks roads the first half befriended.
- **Visual:** the same stretch painted twice — first crossing: pencil-pale, hedgerow stiff, ford wide; return crossing: the lane's colors deepened, the hedge leaning out with fruit, the water stepping-stone shallow — the road visibly *pleased*, without one thing in it moving.

#### `the-inked-round` *(anchor — declared: the hand-drawn overworld map that fills in node by node is genre furniture — every walker of roguelikes knows it at a glance — kept deliberately familiar so its strange cargo reads instantly: the map as a diegetic woken thing that attends and never acts; pressure, income, and finale painted on it in the world's own notation — spirals, shed-shading, red thread — rather than iconography; and route-light, the walked line brightening behind you, as the run's own emotional record.)*

A maker's map is not bought finished; it is *kept* — a strip of oiled canvas inked by the maker's own hand at each night's fire, the day's stretch drawn in, the towns answered gaining their small marginalia: a bird, a kettle, a face. A well-kept round is usually woken — inked under the hearth's attention, night after night — and like every woken thing it attends and no more: unrolled at dusk it lies with a listening tilt, and makers swear a woken round curls very slightly toward the road where their tellings are walking, which the porch elders call *the map wanting news*. What it shows, it shows in the world's own notation: shed-lines as faint crown-ridges, paling as true grey wash, spirals copied forward from gateposts, the fair's line of travel a week ahead of autumn, and behind the maker the **route-light** — the walked line illuminated-manuscript bright, the road ahead a pale pencil stroke. Nothing on it is an icon. Everything on it is paint.

- **This could become:** the run's map UI made fully diegetic — fog-of-war as unwalked pencil, intel as copied road-marks, pressure as actual grey wash and chalk spirals, the boss telegraph as converging seam-lines and a red thread; the map screen doubles as the run's autobiography and the Quiet Walk's reversed-light epilogue replays it backwards.
- **Visual:** worn canvas across two knees at firelight: an incomplete circle, its finished arc jeweled with tiny marginalia and warm color, its remainder ghost-pale; one corner of the canvas lifting, very slightly, toward the dark road east — the map attending, never acting.

#### `the-doorstep-askings`

Below the lantern's notice lives the road's smallest tier of need: a household's asking, hung on its own door-nail — a kettle with a split seam, a boot with a sprung sole, a doll with one button eye, out on the doorstep where any passing maker can lift it. Doorstep askings are made the way suppers are made, quickly and by one kitchen, and so they are almost always *hasty things* — unwoken, unaimed, warming for nobody in particular — which is exactly their mercy: no reading is taken, nothing is sized, nothing ratchets, and rumor gains nothing from a mended boot it wouldn't gain from the telling anyway. First hands to lift it, mend it, and knock. What comes back is supper, a bed by the fire, a story's worth of welcome, and a handsel or two rung bright by the household's glad palms — the small change of the road, and the reason no maker with an empty pack is ever truly poor between towns. The Untold, naturally, adore them; there is an old joke that an Untold's whole verse is one long doorstep.

- **This could become:** micro-contracts on hamlet and roadside nodes — unsized, first-come, ratchet-free filler work paying small immediate bundles (rest, handsels, a telling opportunity) without ever touching lantern sizing or peak-gleam; the lawful low-pressure income valve, and the Untold archetype's home water.
- **Visual:** a low doorway at midday, one small mended thing hung back on its nail catching the sun, and chalked beside the door in a child's hand the road's oldest thank-you: a tiny bird, facing the way the maker went.

#### `the-two-paces-of-light`

Every maker learns the road's first arithmetic young: *light is quick in the room and on foot on the road.* Overflow runs backward along its line of cause — and where cause and delight stand in one place, the line is live and the landing is instant: the square, the fair-ring, the kitchen, the gathered bench, any room where the work and its watchers share one air. That is the Moth Fair condition, and it is why the run's great Standing swings happen on stage, tonight, in public. But walk on, and the live line walks with you no further than the door: beyond the room, your cause exists only as *tellings*, and tellings go one fireside a night, never skipping a hearth and never flying, landing only when seen landing. So the map has two speeds stitched together at every threshold — squares are quick, roads are slow — and makers plan seasons around the seam: a Fairwright's full ground pays him all winter, fireside by fireside; an Untold runner outwalks her own tale for weeks; and every maker knows the particular dusk-time sweetness of brightening on a lonely stretch for work done three towns back, the good deed arriving on foot, right on time.

- **This could become:** the two Standing-gain channels formalized — in-node overflow resolves same-turn (public plays, fair stages, gathered benches), while distant rises travel the map's road-graph at fixed hearth-pace as visible walking tellings with readable arrival turns; one law covering both the instant fireworks and the delayed, source-stamped ticks.
- **Visual:** a split dusk: left, a lit square mid-performance, light landing on knuckles *now*; right, the same maker three nights later on an empty road, and far behind on the inked round a small warm dot one hearth closer — the two paces of one light, quick and walking.

#### `the-combing-grounds`

Where a great asking will lie is written on the land seasons before it lies there. A combing gathers at the heart of its shed — some feature the whole country's regard already passes through: the spring source, the high stone, the ford everyone's wanting crosses on its way to the lack — and it announces itself in the world's own slow notation: the shed's spirals summing ring by ring on gateposts and stiles, Gleaner hazel reading the wash running in long seams weeks out, the seams converging on the inked round like streams seen from a crown. When the arrangement is found — antler and cloudmoss, a morning that smells wrong to everyone at once — red thread is tied where it lies, and the ground becomes what the Rounds have no other word for than *hallowed by attendance*: red-thread-days converge, and around the answering maker the gathered bench rises from all sides — raked staging on a fell, wagons on every road, threads of walkers on every high path — a basin turned amphitheater, because a countryside that combed a need into being is the same countryside that will watch it answered. The boss arena is never a lair. It is the biggest audience of your life, standing in the palest, fullest-tabled, richest-dawned basin you routed into being.

- **This could become:** the finale's geography — a boss-site generator keyed to the palest shed's heart-feature, telegraphed on the map for seasons (spiral-sums, seam-lines, then the red thread), with the gathered-bench modifiers of red-thread-days staged as the basin's own terrain: raked rings on the slopes, staging at the stone, the chosen dawn's immense income drawn from the very table the player's skipped springs filled.
- **Visual:** a grey basin filling with color from the rim inward for once — every road a line of wagons and walkers, every high path threaded, red everywhere in single strokes — and at the dead center, at first light, one small bright-rimmed figure at a high stone, pack open, looking up into the biggest morning the shed has ever returned.

#### `the-breathing-year`

The verse rides the world's breath, and the breath has a calendar every lantern-keeper can recite: the seep runs near-still in deep winter, wakes gently through spring, and blows strongest in late summer and autumn, when the world exhales. So the year is the run's pressure curve, worn as weather and palette: **spring** is the setting-out season — wet greens, chalk skies, spirals ringing slowly, the tide barely breathing, the roads open and forgiving; **midsummer** is the working season — festival gold, the Fair in full swing, crowds thick, glass bright, everything stating its terms; **late summer into autumn** is the reckoning — amber light, long shadows, the seep at full voice, skipped towns ringing fast now, dawns growing immense over everything going grey, the combing's seams quickening; and at the **equinox**, the even-morrow, the tide's turning day, when Gleaner and Evener tables meet at first light and any stilled shed in the world releases its withheld season in one morning. Deep winter belongs to no verse: the roads rest, the porches fill, and the between-verses forgetting does its slow, kind work. Every run is one breath, walked.

- **This could become:** the global difficulty curve as a visible calendar — Fade-rate, spiral speed, dawn richness, and crowd availability all keyed to season; the equinox as a fixed, watchable map-wide event tick (stilling releases, even-morrow festival nodes); act structure with zero abstract acts.
- **Visual:** four palettes for one valley, painted as a year-wheel: spring's wet green and chalk, midsummer's bunting gold, autumn's amber-and-ember with grey climbing the rims, winter's blue porch-light — the run's whole arc readable as a color wheel with a gate at the top.

#### `the-coming-round`

The bright ending of a verse, and the land's own shape for triumph: the circuit closed. A maker who has answered their great asking — or simply sung their circuit whole — walks the last stretches *home*: familiar roads folding their miles with something like affection, return-leg hedges heavy, and behind them on the inked round a full circle of route-light, every answered town's marginalia glowing in the finished arc. At the last waymeet before the home gate, the handing-on: a season-shadow falls in behind them for the final stretch — begun, as always, without arrangement — and at the gate the maker teaches one journey-piece, chooses one woken work of the verse to stand for it, and stops walking. Then the porch, and the tea, and the audience-life of the honored: because you never finish a Round, and the triumphant do not escape the song — they hand it on mid-song, on purpose, at home, in autumn, with the whole circuit still bright behind them and someone new already walking it. The Quiet Walk and the coming-round are the same autumn at two brightnesses, and the world keeps exactly the same thing from each: what was given.

- **This could become:** the victory condition and epilogue — completing the circuit after the great asking triggers the handing-on: a playable last stretch with a shadow in tow, a taught card and one woken work chosen to persist into the meta-layer (the same persistence law as the Quiet Walk, at full brightness), and a run-summary screen that is literally the finished inked round.
- **Visual:** the emotional counterweight to the Quiet Walk's reversed map: the home gate at harvest dusk, lantern hung with nothing — the rafters empty, every need answered — the maker's rim-light at its warmest going *in* through the gate for once, and half a step behind, one narrow silhouette pausing to read the chalk bird a child drew there in spring.

## 6 · ANCHOR DECLARATION

Two anchors this layer, each declared inline at its element:

1. **`the-setting-out`** — the young journeyman leaving home at first light is folk-tale furniture, kept familiar so that the fresh-verse law (every run an unread maker), the first lantern reading (the ratchet witnessed at kettle-scale), and the town-walks-you-out gift all read in one doorway-shaped scene with no exposition.

Anchor share: **2 of 11 elements.** Near-anchors defused in the text rather than declared: `the-combing-grounds` is never a lair or dungeon — the boss site is an audience and a dawn, telegraphed by the player's own routing; `crown-country` is never a "danger zone" — its perils are weather, thin income, and loneliness, and its riches are readings and red; `the-doorstep-askings` are explicitly hasty, unwoken, and unaimed, so the smallest contract tier cannot drift toward quest-board furniture — no board, no posting, one nail, one kitchen.

## 7 · REGISTER CHECK

Nothing in this layer is owed, filed, posted, or adjudicated: doorstep askings are lifted, never assigned; the handing-on is a gift and a tea, not a ceremony of record; witnesses are the audience of things, glad hands, and porch elders, never officials. No combat anywhere: the road's dangers are flood, staleness, grey, and strangeness. All three light-channels hold (places grey, the maker's rim-light, gold edges on woken maps and mended kettles; the inked round attends and never acts). The courier law holds: tellings walk at hearth-pace and only tellings move readings; road-marks and chalk birds aim eyes and move nothing. The mint ban holds: shed dawns return exactly what the shed shed; the coming-round creates nothing, and persists only what was given. Harvest never keys to ruin: gleaning stays in the left-behind crowns; nothing in the finale sources plenty from misfortune.
