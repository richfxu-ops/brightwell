Way Rebuild — Feasibility & Mechanics Review

Phase 0 of way-rebuild (D-024) · 2026-07-16 · what the card grammar can say, what each locked identity needs, and the menu of mechanics we could add — so cards are only ever designed against things that exist.
TL;DR. The card grammar is small but has real unused headroom (three triggers never touched, two verbs nearly unused). Four of the six identities are fully expressible today — Eveners and Fairwrights need nothing but better cards. Two have hard engine gaps: Untold's exhausting one-shots don't exist (the tag is decorative) and Mannerly's courtship never delivers the piece (courted stock lands in an inventory no card can play — the "bench" pipeline was deferred). The best news: most "broader mechanics" candidates aren't new inventions — seven canon-sanctioned systems are designed, named, and stubbed, waiting to be built; the ones worth building first are exactly the ones the rebuild needs (the bench, Untold's count-pressure, the combing boss for difficulty).

1 · What a card can say today (the grammar)

Verbs (14, locked): gather rest steady fill brim* mark-grain draw retire whittle court soothe* read warm keep  (* brim is Fairwright-only, soothe Gleaner-only — canon law)

Triggers (8): on-play · on-chain · on-wake · on-fulfil · on-overkill · on-court · on-stall · on-dawn (fires from woken pieces at dawn) · at-dusk. Targets (6): self · held:capstone · hand:offgrain · hand:cheapest · inert:hand · inert:pack. Reads (10): room · chain · woken:<suit> (accumulated audience) · grain:<suit> (this morning's wakes/stamps) · fill · season · spiral · handsels · over-ceiling.

Amounts are a flat number or one bare read — no caps, coefficients, or arithmetic. The single conditional in the grammar is court's term ({if: read, at_least: n}).

Bucket B — engine support for locked identities (new capability, no new canon)

  • Exhaust / one-shot pieces — a piece flag: played once, then leaves the run (or the morning). Canon demands it for Untold ("pieces given away or performed to destruction… never repeat"); the deck-thinning laws already assume it. Recommend: build with the Untold batch.
  • Generalized terms — court's {if, at_least} conditional allowed on other verbs ("fill 3 if chain ≥ 4"). One small resolver change; huge decision-texture win; grammar stays closed. Recommend: yes, with restraint rules in the rubric (a card's headline shouldn't hide behind a condition — R10).

Bucket C — new grammar (locked-canon change; each needs a logged decision)

  • Amount caps / coefficients — {do:"read", source, cap} or per-style scaling. I-022's "per-leg count coefficients" already implies the tuning layer wants this. Without it, every scaling read is unbounded and nerfs are all-or-nothing (we hit exactly this with the-long-tally). Recommend: adopt cap only — smallest possible extension, solves the real recurring problem.
  • Modal choices ("choose one"), multi-read arithmetic, cost modifiers — defer all; nothing in the rebuild needs them, and each one grows the space the lint/rubric must police.

4 · Doc contradictions to rule before Phase 1 briefs

  • Tier table vs GDD signature marks — tiers.json capstones are mark 7–8; the GDD's own example capstones sit at 4–6. Proposed ruling: the tier table governs costs, the GDD governs shapes — rebuilt capstones get canon shapes at tier-table costs (what Part 1 did to the-long-tally). One decision, nine R4 flags resolve.
  • R3's grain-read ban vs Untold (A3) vs the vocabulary's own fill note — three docs, three positions. Proposed ruling: R3 amended — bare grain:<suit> fills are Untold-only (the exhaust Way re-wakes every morning, so the read doesn't dry up for them); fill(room) stays banned pool-wide.
  • Mannerly's payoff read: §7 "song count" vs A5 "chain depth" — proposed ruling: A5 (the GDD, the deeper doc) wins: chain-depth is the courtship identity; card-design §7 gets corrected. The woken:song fillers that currently work become support pieces, not the payoff.
