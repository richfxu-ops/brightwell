Design Brief — The Eveners

way-rebuild Phase 1 · the purse, moving · first Way in the rebuild · sources: GDD A2 (locked identity), D-025 rulings, post-Part-1 telemetry. You design; I compile, validate, and measure.

The fantasy & the lever

Identity (locked): the composting Way. Exhaust-heavy, always for conversion — last-light the deck's dead weight into room and table income while a handsel engine keeps money bright by keeping it moving. Lowest drama, highest consistency; wins long runs on a prepared board, never on a spike. Grains: dance and thread.

The win-lever: retired pieces fatten the room → the chain multiplies it → the capstone clears its mark on the fattened room and the Way fills by chain. The core loop: retire → room/table · whittle → handsels · steady → chain · capstone rest(room).

The decision texture that makes it interesting (design toward these forks):

  • Room now vs money later — the fattened room can be whittled into handsels (deck growth) or poured into the capstone (the win). Same fuel, two spouts: the Way's central choice.
  • Retire-to-room vs retire-to-table — room helps this morning; the table pays tomorrow's dawn, only if you camp. Ties the Way to the camp/travel decision — and at-dusk is an unused trigger begging for exactly this.
  • What to feed the fire — every retire thins the deck permanently. Composting chaff is free; composting a good card for tempo is a real cost.

Hard canon laws (a design that breaks these bounces)

  • Room is fuelled by retires, never the purse — no read(handsels) feeding gather/room; handsels are never spent inside a turn.
  • No brim (Fairwright-only), no soothe (Gleaner-only).
  • fill(room) banned pool-wide; bare grain: fills are Untold-only (D-025). Evener fills read chain.
  • Twice-benched grade keys only to chosen last-lightings, never to failure.

Costs & the open canon question

Costs come from the tier table (D-025 ruling): apprentice m1–2/wd1 · mid m3–5/wd2 · proud m6–7/wd3 · capstone m7–8/wd3–5. Shapes come from the GDD; where a canon shape needs an off-band number, we log it.

One honest gap you get to resolve by design: the GDD's Evener chain (C1 last-lighting → C2 bright-hand → C3 even-the-rim) shows the engine but no explicit filler — canon C3 wakes big on the fattened room and draws on-fulfil, but nothing in the signature chain pours fill. The pool bridged this with chain-fill pieces (that's consistent with §7's "fill by chain"). Your call in the design: does the capstone itself fill (rest(room) + fill(chain) on one card), or is the capstone the engine-peak and 1–2 mid pieces carry the fill? Both are canon-legal.

New tools available (D-025)

  • Conditions on any verb — {if: read, at_least: n}, e.g. "retire→room; if chain ≥ 3, also draw 1". Restraint: the headline must work without the condition (R10).
  • Read caps — fill(chain, cap 4) etc. Use to make scaling safe, not to complicate.
  • Engine support for either lands with the first card that uses it.
