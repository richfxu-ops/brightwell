Way Design Review

2026-07-16 · groundwork for the card-fix-pass Part 3 conversation · sources: GDD §A1–A6 (locked), card-design.md §7, post-Part-1 telemetry (50 seeds/cohort)
TL;DR — the Ways converged, and that's the disease. The GDD gives each Way a distinct win-lever: Kilnfast compounds a permanent audience, Untold counts this morning's tempo, Fairwrights spike overkill into Standing, Mannerly pays by courtship depth, Eveners convert trash to velocity, Gleaners farm the grey. But the current pool has flattened most payoffs into two generic reads — fill(chain) and fill(woken:suit). Untold's count-read (grain:thread) appears nowhere; Fairwrights' capstone gained a fill it never had in canon (making it the new exploit engine); Mannerly's payoff swapped chain-depth for a woken-count. When every Way's payoff is the same verb reading the same two sources, "the cards all play the same" isn't a feeling — it's literally true, and fill-spam approximates every identity. Part 3's real job is restoring the six distinct levers, not buffing numbers.

Kilnfast — the fired, held ✅ mostly canon-true

Canon: tiny dense deck; fired pieces seat as permanent audience; gather(woken:joinery) compounds the room every turn; lowest variance; wins by out-scaling. Pool reality: the signature loop survives intact (setterby-trestle → calipers → fired-beam) and the data shows it working: the compounder cards carry positive win-deltas, gap only +4. Diagnosis: the healthiest reference implementation — this is what "identity that works" looks like in the data.

Q1. Kilnfast dropped 28→19 wins after Part 1 (cross-Way Fair effects). Is ~40% bot win rate the power level to tune the others toward, or is the GDD's .70–.99 crown band the bar (which would mean everyone, Kilnfast included, tunes up once bots improve)?

Eveners — the purse, moving ❌ structurally incomplete

Canon: retire dead weight into room/table income, whittle the fattened room into bright handsels, capstone rest(room)×chain clears the mark — convert, never hoard. Pool reality: the kit is 11 cyclers with no payoff role — a conversion engine with nothing to spend into. even-the-rim lost its canon rest(room) (it's now a bare fill(chain) at capstone cost) and is dead at <3% of plays; every top-played Evener card carries a negative win-delta. The bot dutifully cycles and loses. Diagnosis: the loop's last link is missing — canon's "fattened room × chain → clear the mark" doesn't exist in data.

Q2. Restore even-the-rim to canon (rest(room) + the chain glue via bright-hand-to-hand) and add one true payoff/builder — or redesign the Evener win-path from scratch? (Canon is explicit here; I'd restore first, measure, then judge.)

Untold — the pace, ratcheted ❌ identity absent from data

Canon: thin fast deck of cheap exhausting one-shots; pressure = count-and-pace; capstone reads grain:thread — pieces woken/stamped this morning, the per-morning tempo count — plus chain, converting tempo into one crown burst. Pool reality: no Untold card reads grain:thread; paper-bird and the-standing-count both read chain — a generic pace-read every Way can use. The one-shot exhaust identity ("its pieces never seat") also appears unmodeled — Untold cards have wake marks and delight like everyone else's. Part 1 removed its off-canon crutch (long-tally's woken-count fill) and wins fell 27→10: without the borrowed Kilnfast lever there's no Untold lever at all. Diagnosis: the Way with the least of its canon in the data.

Q3. Restore the count-lever: the-standing-count reads grain:thread per canon. But card-design R3 currently bans bare grain-reads on fills ("dries up once the deck is woken") — for the exhaust-and-rewake Untold, the ban is wrong. Amend R3 with an Untold exception, or keep the ban and find another count mechanism? Also: does the one-shot exhaust need engine/data support (a real fidelity question), or does the thin-deck feel survive without it?

Fairwrights — the room, spiked ⚠ strong, but for the wrong reason

Canon: assemble a huge transient crowd, dump it past a ceiling in one enormous turn, overkill runs to Standing via brim — the only brim Way; wins by spike, not by fill. Pool reality: the-whole-fair-turn is mark 6 (canon: 8) and gained a fill(woken:dance) canon never gave it — so the "spike" Way actually wins by generic woken-count filling, and that fill is now the exploit cohort's #1 engine (12% of exploit fill; feed-the-crown adds 11%). The gap grew to +16 after Part 1 because exploit decks lean harder on these. Diagnosis: strong win rate, weak identity — it wins the way every other Way does, not the way canon says.

Q4. Restore the-whole-fair-turn to canon (mark 8, brim payoff, no fill)? That likely drops Fairwrights' win rate meaningfully — brim pays Standing (survival), not fill (the win), so canon Fairwrights need crown-filling from somewhere. Canon answer: fill(woken:dance) belongs on support pieces (feed-the-crown), the capstone is the gleam spike. Is that split the intent to enforce?

Mannerly — the proud, courted ❌ the loop never fires

Canon: build chain depth (the performed term) → court proud stock with gleam as a pure gate → the proud piece pays by courtship depth (fill(chain)). Dead cards in a dim hand until the courtship lands. Pool reality: the payoff (silver-refrain) has the biggest play share in the kit and a −27% win-delta — the bot plays the proud piece without the term-state, exactly the "dead card in a dim hand" canon warns about. Meanwhile the kit's only positive cards (refrain-repeated +49, hold-the-note +40) read woken:song — which contradicts GDD A5's explicit "pays by chain, not read(grain:<suit>)" (card-design §7's "fill by the song count" drifted from the GDD). Diagnosis: two documented win-paths, and the sim says the non-canon one is the only one working; the courtship chain likely never assembles under a greedy bot.

Q5. Which Mannerly is real — the GDD's courtship-depth engine, or §7's song-count engine the data favors? If courtship: the bot needs to be able to execute a 3-step unlock (a Part 3 bot-policy item), and we should check courtings_landed in the records to see if the court ever even fires. This is a locked-canon interpretation call → DECISIONS.md.

Gleaners — the grey, harvested ✅ quietly the healthiest

Canon: walk toward the grey — read(spiral/season) scale gather/whittle on pale nodes, soothe as the signature payoff, mend what you farmed. Pool reality: the only Way whose identity line now beats its exploit line (−4 gap). walk-the-pale turned positive (+15) after Part 1. Two pale-route cards are still dead (mend-the-verge, pale-harvest) — rings are ~0 early, so early-game they read nothing. Diagnosis: the identity works when the weather cooperates; the dead cards are an early-game liveness problem, not an identity problem.

Q6. Fix the two dead pale cards with early floors (flat base + spiral upside), or lean the whole Way harder into rings-camping as a strategy (bigger payoff for standing in the grey)? Part 2 territory, but the direction call shapes it.

Cross-cutting: canon contradictions the audit surfaced

  • GDD signature cards vs the tier table. The GDD's own capstones violate tiers.json's locked bands: the-standing-count (canon mark 4, capstone band is 7–8), even-the-rim (mark 6). Nine cards now carry R4 flags. Which canon wins — the tier table or the signature chains? Needs a logged decision; the audit can't score R4 fairly until it's made.
  • R3's grain-read ban vs Untold's identity. card-design.md R3 bans bare grain:<suit> fills pool-wide; GDD A3 builds Untold's entire identity on one. (Engine fact: grain: counts this morning's wakes/stamps — for an exhaust-heavy Way it doesn't dry up.) R3 needs an exception clause or A3 needs a re-read.
  • card-design §7 vs GDD A5 on Mannerly's payoff read (song-count vs chain-depth) — see Q5.
  • The shared base pack is 100% dead — all 7 shared cards flag R14 even in the apprentice cohort where they're the whole starting deck. The apprentice wins 0/50 playing them and 16/50 ignoring them for drafted fill. They need jobs or cuts (Part 2, group C).
  • The bot-competence confound. Mannerly's courtship and Fairwrights' one-enormous-turn need multi-step setup the greedy bots don't attempt. Before judging those kits weak, Part 3 should teach the bots the 3-step line (or hand-verify it) — the sim is a floor, not a verdict (D-016).
