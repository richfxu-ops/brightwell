Six Walking Ways

One game, six completely different ways to play it.

You never pick a class from a menu. You become a Way by how you play — the country teaches you into it — and mixing Ways is allowed. Three are gentle on-ramps you can play with just the basics; three ride a deeper subsystem. Every Way can win, each by a different route to the same winter crown.
🔥

The Kilnfast

“Fire it once, keep it forever.”

The patient collector. You keep a small, tight deck and slowly turn pieces permanent, so the crowd you can call on grows every single morning. No showy turns — just a quiet curve that climbs until it towers over the winter finale.

You dowake sturdy pieces and keep them — each one makes tomorrow's crowd bigger
You winby out-growing the boss: by winter your ordinary morning beats everyone's best

“Many small, very fast.”

The quick one who outruns the tale. A thin, fast deck of cheap little acts. You don't do one big job — you do lots of tiny ones, faster than trouble can catch you. Your pressure isn't how big a need is, it's how many.

“Court the proud stuff.”

The one with taste. Your best pieces are shy — proud materials that sit dead in your hand until you perform exactly the right courtship to win them over. Set it up right and one piece lands like a wedding. It has the single highest ceiling in the game.
