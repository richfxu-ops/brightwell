Choices nobody has made yet. Most are parked until later build stages, but each is a real fork with a recommendation on the table.

OpenC4 How big is the map?

How many towns per leg of the journey, and how many card offers appear each dusk?

Leaning toward: ~3 offers a night from a pool of 5–6 towns, with "camp here" always available (other rules depend on camping existing). Decided in the Phase-5 build.

OpenF2 · F3 Two build-process calls

F2: what platform the first real playable prototype ships on (web is the likely default). F3: whether the balancing loop is hand-run or handed to an automated skill.

Leaning toward: decide each once the current stage teaches us what we need — not yet urgent.

OpenG1–G5 What carries between runs?

The whole meta-progression layer — what you keep after a run ends. Five linked questions:

G1 — what a "bead" is (leaning: one keepsake card carried into your next run, inert until re-woken).
G2 — what a lost run hands on (leaning: a bead too, chosen for you).
G3 — how you unlock the other three classes (leaning: by accidentally playing like them once).
G4 — how harder difficulty works (leaning: "deeper Rounds" — towns start more overdue).
G5 — the guiding principle (leaning: the meta carries meaning, never raw power).
All parked until after the core game is balanced.

Settled questions

These were open once. Each is now closed — the code points to the decision it became (see the Decisions tab).

Settled§A Code vs. canon mismatches → all fixed

Six places where the code didn't match the design (a mis-capped resource, a wrong healing formula, a hard ceiling where there should be a gentle taper, etc.).

Outcome: all six corrected on 2026-07-14; one number (the overkill decay rate) was then deliberately tightened by D-018 to give the lose-condition real teeth.

Settled§B → D-010 The five rules of a turn

How a single morning plays out, decided one rule at a time:

B1 unspent resources seep into the local town's pool at dusk (recover ⅔ by camping).
B2 any non-card action "stalls" and halves your momentum unless you spent a "brace."
B3 triggered effects fire in the order you played the cards.
B4 unplayed cards go to a discard pile that reshuffles when the deck runs dry.
B5 only "woken" cards act on later turns; unwoken ones are inert until woken once.

Settled§C → D-012 · D-014 Contracts, the year, the ending

How quests fail and cost you (C1–C3), whether delayed rewards exist (C6), and how you win (C5).

Outcome: a flopped quest costs gleam equal to its size; a run ends at winter with a single demand-20 crown — stand it to win, or take the Quiet Walk. (Only C4, map size, stayed open.)

Settled§D → D-015 How the deck grows

Whether new cards are bought or earned, and how.

Outcome: a tiered system — cheap cards are bought with money; the best cards are courted (won by performing, never bought). Your reputation unlocks which tiers appear.

Settled§H → D-022 The card-quality rulebook

Four calls that finalised the standard every card is judged against.

Outcome: quality stays a judgement call for now (no false-precision numbers); every class's full winning path is seeded from the start; "dead" and "overpowered" cards get hard usage cutoffs; and six weak filler cards were given real abilities.
