Why the other tabs read strangely. Brightwell deliberately dresses its mechanics in picture-book words — your score is your gleam, a quest is an asking, the final level is the Wintering. This tab gives the plain meaning of each, so the Questions and Decisions tabs make sense.

The words the notes lean on most
Maker
You — the player. A wandering craftsperson.
Gleam / Standing
Your reputation meter. It's the lose-condition: if it empties, the run ends.
The Paling
The world slowly fading. The background pressure that forces you to keep working.
Asking
A quest / contract — a town's request for something made.
The crown / great asking
The big final contract. Fulfil it to win the run.
The Wintering
The final act of a run, where the crown is waiting.
Dawn / morning
The start of a turn. You draw a hand and receive income.
Attention
Your main spendable resource — the "energy" you play cards with.
Room
The pool of built-up momentum in a performance that you pour into a contract.
Piece / journey-piece
A card in your deck.
Waking / fired / woken
Permanently activating a card so it keeps working every turn — like upgrading it.
The Fair
The card draft / shop where you acquire new cards.
Handsels
Money.
The six Ways
The six playable classes: Kilnfast, Mannerly, Gleaners, Eveners, Untold, Fairwrights.
Grain
A card's suit / colour (thread, joinery, dough, song, glaze, dance).
Rings
How overdue a town is — more rings means bigger reward and bigger demand.
The dusk sweep
End-of-turn cleanup of anything you didn't spend.
Camping
Staying at a town to recover unspent resources.
The Quiet Walk
The "you lost, but survived" ending.
Bead
A keepsake carried from one run into the next (meta-progression).
The harness / the sim
A robot that auto-plays thousands of games to test whether the balance is fair.
