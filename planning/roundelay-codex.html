Roundelay

~ a round-song of a game, still being sung ~
a roguelike economy deckbuilder

Attention is a
physical force

You are a maker walking one wander-year through a countryside that is quietly fading — and everything you truly attend to wakes.

places palepeople gleamthings wake

Each turn is one worked morning: you gather the room (a pool of attention), play journey-pieces from your deck in an unbroken chain, and — when a piece has rested enough attention past its waking-mark — it wakes: it takes a thin gold working-edge, fires permanently into your deck, and starts drawing a crowd of its own. Pour attention past a card's ceiling and the overflow runs to Standing — your gleam, the light the world holds you in.

The pressure is the weather. The land's fading (the Paling) grows stronger through the year, raising the size of the askings — contracts you take off town lanterns — toward a calendar-floored crown at winter. You never lose Standing by sitting still; you lose it only by reaching and falling short (the spilling). To win you must stand the crown — which means you must have grown, which means you must have risked.

Six playstyles (the Walking Ways), one gorgeous economy of light, and a deck that is an autobiography of everyone who ever taught you a piece.

Attention as physics

Every number a card pays is a read of shared state an earlier card wrote. The room you build with card 1 is the number card 3 dumps.

Failure is a reach, not a bar

No ambient decay. Standing falls only when a performance falls short — so growth and risk are the same act.

Escalation is weather

The season's fading raises the stakes on its own. A turtle who skips nothing still walks into autumn.

Wonder, not grimdark

No debt, no fees, no paperwork. Every mechanic is the world's own craft-physics — a lifting room, a gold edge taking, grey ground held back.

The Worked Morning

Play

First — five words
The whole game in five ideas
🔦

Attention

Your energy for the morning — like a spotlight you shine on your cards to bring them to life. You get a fresh pool every dawn.

🎴

A piece

A card — one little act you perform. Your deck is your troupe. It starts tiny and grows as pieces "wake."

👥

The room

How big a crowd you've gathered right now. A bigger room lets you wake bigger, better pieces this turn.

🌡️

Waking-mark

Each piece needs a certain amount of attention to wake up. Give it more than its ceiling and the extra spills over.

✦

Standing

Your reputation — and it's both your score and your life bar. Hits zero, the run ends. You earn it by over-delivering.

Now — watch one morning
A single turn, with real numbers
1

The town posts a need

You arrive at a pale little town. Its lantern shows an asking — a job with a size. Deliver that much delight and the town is re-made (and pays you). Early in the year the needs are small; they grow as winter nears.

Today's asking: a NEED of size 3.  Your job this morning is to pay 3 delight into it.
2

Gather a crowd (build the room)

You play a card whose job is to draw a crowd. That raises the room — your pool of attention for the pieces you play next. The bigger the room, the bigger the piece you can wake.

You play a room-builder. The room is now 6.  You have 6 attention to pour onto your next piece.
3

Wake your star piece — the core move

Now you pour the room onto a piece. Every piece has a waking-mark (how much it needs to come alive) and a ceiling (how much it can hold). Reach the mark and it WAKES: it fires into your deck permanently and joins your crowd on future mornings. Pour past the ceiling and the leftover spills into Standing.

Your star piece has waking-mark 4, ceiling 5. You pour all 6 onto it: 6 ≥ 4, so it WAKES ✦ — and 6 is 1 past its ceiling of 5, so +1 Standing.
4

Deliver — and get paid

The piece you just woke pays its delight into the town's need. Fill the need and the town is re-made: the square blooms back into colour, and you're paid a bundle — a new taught card, bright money, map hints. Your deck just grew, and your show is a little bigger tomorrow.

Your woken piece pays 3 delight → the need of 3 is filled → town re-made, you're paid. And the piece you woke is now in your deck for good.
Then dawn comes again — and the piece you woke is back, bringing its crowd.
Each morning your show gets bigger. That snowball is the whole game.
The key rule
Every drop of attention goes somewhere

Nothing is ever wasted — when you pour attention onto a piece, it takes one of these four paths. Learning to steer it is the skill.

✦

It wakes

Reached the mark → fired into your deck forever and joins your crowd. This is how you grow.

⤴

It overflows

Poured past the ceiling → the extra becomes Standing (your score + life bar). Showing off pays.

↩

It returns

Attention you didn't spend isn't lost — it waits on the town's table and gives you a richer dawn tomorrow.

○

It stays cold

Not enough to reach the mark → the piece does its bit once, then stays asleep and slowly fades.

The big picture
One run is one year — and it keeps getting harder

You get about 27 mornings, spring to winter. The land's fading rises on its own — the needs grow whether you engage or not — building to a final job you must be strong enough to answer. Grow, or get left behind by the weather.

The one idea that makes it clever:
a card's payoff isn't a fixed number — it reads what your earlier cards built.
The crowd you gather with card 1 is the amount card 3 pours. Right cards, wrong order → your reach falls short.
attention — your spotlight waking — your growing deck Standing — score & life the need — the town's job the Paling — the rising difficulty

The Design, Layer by Layer

Codex

The locked GDD, condensed — tap a layer to unfold it.
WorldRoundelay & the Palingtheme, tone, the six Ways›

Roundelay is a made world — not made once, long ago, but made continuously, by hands. It is named for a round-song passed voice to voice around a circle, that only stays wonderful as long as someone keeps singing their bars.

The Paling is the world exhaling: a tide, not a villain, that takes knack from made things first and color after. It never touches people or the light itself — it is the reason makers exist at all. Answering a town's need re-makes it: the square re-colors in watercolor strokes, and the maker is paid in the town's own overflow of gladness (the glad-load).

The board is painted in one light and three brushes — places pale, people gleam, things wake — three channels that never cross, plus attention as the medium that carries between them.

The six Walking Ways

Makers travel in loose traditions, each a different answer to the same fading. They are the game's six archetypes: the Kilnfast (fire it, keep it), the Eveners (keep the evening moving), the Untold (outrun the tale), the Fairwrights (make the watching), the Mannerly (mind your courtships), and the Morning-Gleaners (glean the grey left behind).

Aesthetic target: wonder, fantasy, whimsy, elegance — closer to Miyazaki or Le Guin or a beautifully illustrated picture-book than to grimdark or bureaucratic satire. Every layer must generate imagery an artist could paint.
Layer 1The Core Loopthe worked morning & the win›

A run is one wander-year of fixed length — ~27 worked mornings across five legs (Green Going → Long Light → Deep Gold → Red Walk → the Wintering finale). The calendar advances by worked mornings only, never by travel — so a camp-in-place player evades nothing.

One play at a time

  • Dawn (income). The morning issues attention: a flat base + a draw from the local table (a paler node gives a richer dawn) + yesterday's returned surplus. The base always wakes at least the cheapest piece — no morning is ever decision-free. Draw a hand of five.
  • Gather the room. Your pool = the morning draw + the gathered room (woken audience-things you own, townsfolk, a venue). A thin room caps how bright anything can wake.
  • Play a chain. Each play spends attention as a multiplier; back-to-back plays hold the room high, a stall lets it cool. Attention is a budget and a momentum at once.
  • The waking line. Rest a piece's attention at or above its fixed waking-mark and it wakes — fired to your deck permanently, paying delight into the need's fill and joining the room as a new audience-thing. Below its mark it still resolves once but stays inert and paling-prone.
  • The full cup. Attention poured past a card's ceiling converts to Standing at full rate — a full cup runs to the pourer. The run's biggest gleam swings happen here, on stage, in public.
  • Fulfil. When the need's fill completes, the node is re-made and the glad-load pays out.

Standing, and how you lose

Standing is gleam — the world's trust worn as a key-light behind the shoulder. It rises on overflow, glad palms, and delayed tellings. It falls only by the spilling: an unmoved room, or an asking carried past its season. There is no ambient decay. At Standing-zero the run ends into the Quiet Walk — not death, the world going shy.

The win: reach the first still dawn of winter still lit and having stood your crown — a boss-tier performance the whole engine you built must answer. The turtle-dodge is closed not by a decay meter but by the crown being un-standable without a grown deck. Opting out of risk means opting out of winning.
Layer 2Resourcesfour interacting systems›

A lean set of resources, each on a different clock so none can substitute for another:

ResourceWhat it isIts clock
Attention — the roomThe per-turn pool you spend to wake pieces. Never banked; unspent attention returns to the local table.the dawn
HandselsA decaying currency. Bright only while it keeps moving; idle money dims and seeps back to tomorrow's dawn.the rest
Gleam — StandingThe never-spent gate + fail-resource. Gates which askings and materials answer you; ends the run at zero.the run
The PalingBoard pressure. Accretes as spiral rings on skipped nodes; raises askings and richens dawns.the season

Plus journey-pieces (your deck) and courted stock (shy materials). Everything rides one conservation substrate — nothing is noticed away; nothing is minted. Four subsystems (the Bench, the Handsel Round, the Courting Web, the Paling Market) tap and feed that single circulation. The full square is browsable under Cards → Resources.

The firewall: Standing never interacts with the Paling. Board pressure can never touch the gleam meter — so players always know which losses are the world's weather and which are their own.
Layer 3Effect Vocabulary14 primitives; cards are data›

A card is declarative data: a craft-suit, a fixed waking-mark, a ceiling, a printed delight, and an ordered list of effects. Each effect is { when, if?, do, params }. The depth comes from one idea: amounts are references, not constants — the single primitive read turns any live quantity into the amount of another effect. All fourteen verbs are browsable under Cards → Effects.

The grammar of light is compiler-enforced. A gleam-write (brim) can only read the room / chain / genuine overflow — never a board surface. Paleness may scale attention, handsels, and harvest — never Standing. And no waking-mark can ever be lowered; the only lever is bringing more attention.
Layer 4Acquisitionhow the deck grows — no card-shop›

Only three things ever newly enter a run — journey-pieces, courted stock, handsels — and never through a shop. Acquisition is a bundle paid across the existing economies, gated by two independent things at once:

  • The glad-load — the passive spine. Answering a town's asking loads your pack across everything at once: one pressed taught piece (arriving with that town's name on it), one introduction-jar of local material, bright handsels, and map-intel chalked a day ahead. Its richness scales off the node's spiral rings — rings-in equals load-out, so the same number raises both the difficulty and the reward.
  • The Round-Fair — the active hub, and where choice lives: teach / win / delight lanes, priced in wakefulness, that you must route to intercept.
  • The gleaning — walk the pale, left-behind country for last-red and salvage. Highest route risk; the Gleaner's home.
  • Courting — proud materials are never bought. You spend gleam as a gate (which stalls will deal with you) then perform a term to draw the stuff in.

Every acquired piece arrives inert — the room still has to wake it. That is why a fast draft can never trivialize the run: you cannot draft your way past the requirement to perform. Every faucet pairs with a canon drain (retire, gifting, exhaust), so the deck is a garden, not a hoard.

The intersection is the strategy: your shine decides who will deal with you; your route + season decides what a place even has. You need both gates open at once — the mending question worn as a supply chain.
Layer 5The Six Archetypessix engines, not palette-swaps›

Each Walking Way pulls one lever of the resource square hardest, wins the same crown by a different route, and sits on a different risk axis. You are never handed a class menu — the country teaches you into the maker you are being, by the grain your gleam shows.

WayLeverFeelHow it wins
Kilnfastthe fired, heldlowest variance; a curve that out-scales the bossa compounding fired-audience floor (read(woken) → bigger room, no spike)

Untoldthe pace, ratchetedthin, fast, coolclears many small needs; pressure is count-and-pace, not tonnage
Fairwrightsthe room, spikedhighest variance; feast or famineone enormous crowd dumped past a ceiling — the biggest gleam swing (brim)
Mannerlythe proud, courtedgreedy, swingy; highest ceilingcourt a term-state, then one proud piece pays by the courtship's depth
Gleanersthe grey, harvestedhighest route risk; boss-affinethe fading itself is fuel — richest dawns, then soothe the ground you farmed
A late design pass gave the Gleaner and the Mannerly their own winning verbs so no two builds win the same way. The Gleaner's marquee is now mending the ripened grey it just harvested (soothe); the Mannerly's is the courtship landing (court, with gleam a pure gate). Only two builds still win by a raw grain-count — and those two are set apart by other levers entirely.
Layer 6Numbers & Curvesthe economy, made concrete›

Concrete numbers fitted to the design and proven by an executable economy model. Every worked morning offers a median of four meaningful decisions.

DialValue
Deck size over the run~7 → ~20 (grown enough to stand the crown, thinned along the way)
Hand size5, refilled each morning
The rising need (per morning, by leg)1 → 3 → 5 → 7, climbing to a crown demand of 10
Card tiers (fixed marks)apprentice 1–2 · mid 3–5 · proud 6–7 · capstone 7–8
Spiral rings3 = a plea · 5 = a poem · 7 = a great asking born
Overflow → gleamfull rate for the first 6 points past a ceiling, then diminishing (closes bank-then-dump)
Handselsdull 1 · warm 2 · singing 3; brightened only by circulation
Archetype balancesix power totals inside a 4-point band; each best on exactly one axis; zero dominated pairs

The crown's 8.5-of-10 gap is deliberately closed by the whole engine — chain multiplier, overflow, and count-stacking — never by a single card. A static apprentice deck simply cannot reach it: growth is forced by the weather, not optional.

The commerce ban holds even in the numbers: the shy-material stalls render diegetically as the-vouching / the-stalls-that-lean-in — never an in-world "market".
Layer 7The Starter Pool49 cards, ready to play›

The concrete opening cards, as data: a shared 7-card apprentice deck every run begins with, plus ~7 cards for each of the six Ways so its signature chain opens from turn one. 84% of the pool participates in a combo — the standalones are the deliberate legible floor. Every card is browsable under Cards.

A signature chain, as the engine sees it — the Kilnfast's compounding hearth

// C1 — Setterby Trestle: the room grows by every joinery piece already fired (cross-turn)
{ "grain":"joinery", "mark":2, "ceiling":4, effects:[
    gather( read woken:joinery ),   // +room per fired joinery
    steady( links:1, brace )  ]}                 // hold the room across a pause

// C2 — Calipers at the Bench: pre-rest the capstone toward its FIXED mark by the held room
{ "grain":"joinery", "mark":3, "ceiling":5, effects:[
    rest( target: capstone, read room ),
    mark-grain( a hand piece → joinery )  ]}     // grows read(grain:joinery)

// C3 — The Fired Beam: clear it, pay by joinery-count, then wake and seat as one more piece
{ "grain":"joinery", "mark":7, "ceiling":8, tags:[capstone], effects:[
    rest( self, read room ),
    fill( read grain:joinery ),        // every number here was written by C1–C2
    steady( brace )  ]}

Break the order and the capstone under-fills — a reach that falls short, the spilling. The combo is the growth; the growth is the Standing at stake.

NextWhat the design leaves openfor the balance stage›

This document is the finished design. Its balance is asserted over hand-authored economy checks — proven to hold its shape, not yet its exact values. The next stage (the game-loop) derives real balance from bot-played simulations and tunes the exact coefficients within these locked shapes:

  • the six archetypes' true win-rates and the crown-stand spread;
  • whether the glad-load (the passive spine) flattens build diversity;
  • the exact coefficients — the Untold's per-leg counts, the last-red and Fair rates, the skip-can't-out-scale margin;
  • the Gleaner's flagged boss-affinity.

The simulation is a thermometer, not an oracle: it proves the game is balanced and deep, not that it is fun. That last judgment stays human.
