Roundelay · how it works

You put on a morning show
to mend a fading town

Spend attention → wake your pieces → deliver the town's need → grow strong enough for winter.

First — five words
The whole game in five ideas
🔦

Attention

Your energy for the morning — like a spotlight you shine on your cards to bring them to life. You get a fresh pool every dawn.

🎴

A piece

A card — one little act you perform. Your deck is your troupe. It starts tiny and grows as pieces "wake."

👥

The room

How big a crowd you've gathered right now. A bigger room lets you wake bigger, better pieces this turn.

🌡️

Waking-mark

Each piece needs a certain amount of attention to wake up. Give it more than its ceiling and the extra spills over.

✦

Standing

Your reputation — and it's both your score and your life bar. Hits zero, the run ends. You earn it by over-delivering.

Now — watch one morning
A single turn, with real numbers
1

The town posts a need

You arrive at a pale little town. Its lantern shows an asking — a job with a size. Deliver that much delight and the town is re-made (and pays you). Early in the year the needs are small; they grow as winter nears.

Today's asking: a NEED of size 3.  Your job this morning is to pay 3 delight into it.
2

Gather a crowd (build the room)

You play a card whose job is to draw a crowd. That raises the room — your pool of attention for the pieces you play next. The bigger the room, the bigger the piece you can wake.

You play a room-builder. The room is now 6.  You have 6 attention to pour onto your next piece.
3

Wake your star piece — the core move

Now you pour the room onto a piece. Every piece has a waking-mark (how much it needs to come alive) and a ceiling (how much it can hold). Reach the mark and it WAKES: it fires into your deck permanently and joins your crowd on future mornings. Pour past the ceiling and the leftover spills into Standing.

Your star piece has waking-mark 4, ceiling 5. You pour all 6 onto it: 6 ≥ 4, so it WAKES ✦ — and 6 is 1 past its ceiling of 5, so +1 Standing.
4

Deliver — and get paid

The piece you just woke pays its delight into the town's need. Fill the need and the town is re-made: the square blooms back into colour, and you're paid a bundle — a new taught card, bright money, map hints. Your deck just grew, and your show is a little bigger tomorrow.

Your woken piece pays 3 delight → the need of 3 is filled → town re-made, you're paid. And the piece you woke is now in your deck for good.
Then dawn comes again — and the piece you woke is back, bringing its crowd.
Each morning your show gets bigger. That snowball is the whole game.
The key rule
Every drop of attention goes somewhere

Nothing is ever wasted — when you pour attention onto a piece, it takes one of these four paths. Learning to steer it is the skill.

✦

It wakes

Reached the mark → fired into your deck forever and joins your crowd. This is how you grow.

⤴

It overflows

Poured past the ceiling → the extra becomes Standing (your score + life bar). Showing off pays.

↩

It returns

Attention you didn't spend isn't lost — it waits on the town's table and gives you a richer dawn tomorrow.

○

It stays cold

Not enough to reach the mark → the piece does its bit once, then stays asleep and slowly fades.

The big picture
One run is one year — and it keeps getting harder

You get about 27 mornings, spring to winter. The land's fading rises on its own — the needs grow whether you engage or not — building to a final job you must be strong enough to answer. Grow, or get left behind by the weather.

The one idea that makes it clever:
a card's payoff isn't a fixed number — it reads what your earlier cards built.
The crowd you gather with card 1 is the amount card 3 pours. Right cards, wrong order → your reach falls short.
