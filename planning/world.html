World name: ROUNDELAY

A roundelay is a round-song: a melody passed voice to voice around a circle, each singer carrying it a few bars before handing it on. That is the whole world in one word. Roundelay is a made world — not made once, long ago, but made continuously, by hands — and it only stays wonderful as long as someone keeps singing their bars. The player is one traveling maker carrying one verse of the song: walking the great circuits, answering the crafted needs that towns hang out for bright hands, watched by everything they have ever woken, until their light either grows to meet what the world asks of it or quietly, survivably, autumns out.

This file is the world's canon, built in layers. Layer 1 (thematic spine) is final below; Layers 2–7 (cosmology, peoples, economy, places, aesthetic/naming, mechanics ledger) will be appended as the pipeline completes them.

Layer 1 — Thematic Spine

Central question

If the world only stays wonderful because somebody keeps re-making it, how much of your own light will you put into other people's mornings?

Play asks it in miniature, constantly: every card played is a piece of yourself given to a made thing; every asking taken down from a lantern is a town trusting its colors to your hands; every run is one maker's verse of the song.

Core themes

  • Making as world-keeping. Craft is not decoration on top of the world; craft is the world's renewal. A bridge outlives its makers because it was made delightfully — unwoken work stands too, but it forgets itself a season sooner. When the delight wears thin, the world does.
  • The generosity economy. Everything of value moves as gift, welcome, performance, and commission — and value is created by moving. A masterpiece in a locked room goes dim; a coin grows rich by being spent well. Nothing in Roundelay is owed, and the world has no ledger to keep one in.
  • The traveling life, and being known by the road. Home is a circuit, not a place. Belonging is renewed town by town, hearth by hearth — and the land remembers who travels it well: the deepest wealth in Roundelay is recognition.
  • Renewal against the Paling. Beauty is not permanent, and that is not tragic. Things run down so they can be re-made better; the world's mortality is exactly what gives makers their place in it.

Mood and register

Luminous, warm, itinerant. Early-autumn festival light; the feeling of arriving in a strange town at dusk and seeing lanterns being hung for you. Miyazaki's working wonder (the bakery, the hearth) crossed with Le Guin's craft-seriousness: true work done with full attention has weight. Picture-book clarity, journeyman's-notebook texture. Never grim: failure in Roundelay is a dimming, and dimming is quiet and survivable and even beautiful — but it is real, and the world is shy toward the dim.

The grammar of light (a world law, binding on every layer below this one). Light in Roundelay is one thing — attention made visible — but it is read in three channels that never cross:

  • Places pale. The Paling takes knack and color from scenery — never from people, never from the light itself.
  • People gleam. Standing is a key-light that belongs to characters only — a shine the world throws on those it trusts.
  • Things wake. A woken object carries one thin gold line along its working edge, and nothing else does.

One glance, three channels: a grey town, a bright maker, an alert kettle, all legible in the same frame.

Named elements

waking-of-made-things

Anything made while truly attended wakes, a little. Not love — Roundelay is exact about this, and sentimental strangers always get it wrong — attention: the gathered, unbroken regard of watchers, human or made, resting on the work while it is being done. A well-thrown pot hums when the tea is right; a well-built door knows the weight of its own family's hands; a bell woken before a whole festival crowd can wake other things when it rings. Every child has seen it, and every child has also felt the small heartbreak of a hasty thing, made alone at midnight, that never woke at all. The hasty thing still works — an unwoken bridge stands, an unwoken pot holds water — it is merely mortal in a way woken things are not: the Paling takes it first. And woken things attend; they do not act. No pot pours itself, no door opens for you, nothing speaks. They turn toward what matters the way compass needles turn, and that is all, and it is enough to make a whole house feel inhabited. The old riddle covers it: the maker gives the thing its shape, but the watching gives it its eyes.

  • This could become: card quality tiers as a waking scale, with a crisp trigger — a card wakes if and only if it is played at or above an attention threshold (see audience-of-things). A run's engine is literally a workshop of things you've woken; unattended spam-play produces inert, paling-prone cards. Fully predictable, fully diegetic: things wake when watched.
  • Visual: woken things carry a thin rim of gold along their working edge — the lip of the bowl, the blade of the plane, the hem of the coat — like late sun on the edge of a table. Unwoken things read matte. Silhouette rule: woken objects sit at a subtly alert angle, like a listening dog; they never move more than that.

the-paling

The Paling is the world exhaling — and what it takes first is not color but knack. Wells forget the trick of sweet water. Bridges forget exactly how heavy a cart is. A whole valley's echo goes flat, a famous stew tastes almost right, a lucky ford stops being lucky. Color follows the competence out — a red roof going rose, going grey, the scene draining from its edges inward — but the grey is the symptom; the forgetting is the disease. Nobody fights the Paling and nobody hates it; it is the tide the Rounds were built to answer, the reason makers exist at all. A town three seasons overdue for a maker is called pale, and pale towns hang out their asking-lanterns and wait, patient as winter gardens. Unwoken work pales fastest; woken work holds its knack for generations. People never fade — the Paling has no appetite for the living — only the made and the kept.

  • This could become: the run's escalating pressure clock. Map nodes visibly pale over time; needs compound — skip a pale town now and its asking is twice the size (and twice the reward, and twice the wonder) when the road brings you back. The Paling attacks board-state, resources, and routes; it never touches the Standing meter directly, so the player always knows which losses are the world's weather and which are their own.
  • Visual: desaturation with a direction — pale towns look vignetted in reverse, grey at the rooftops with one stubborn ember of color at the hearth-line. Re-made places re-color in visible strokes, like watercolor blooming across wet paper. The knack-loss has its own tells: a well-rope hanging at the wrong angle, birds refusing a forgetful bridge.

makers-gleam

Gleam is the light that gathers on a maker whose gifts delight — the world's trust, made visible. It lives in no one place — knuckles, hair, the rim of the ears — and everyone can see it at dusk: a lit maker crossing a square trails a faint shine like a candle carried behind rice paper. Gleam is not virtue and not wealth, and it is earned by delight given in any coin the world honors: work that moves an audience, a story that warms a hearth, laughter set loose in a stranger's kitchen. The world is frank about what it does. Doors open a beat sooner for the lit. Proud materials will state their terms to bright hands (see shy-materials), while the dim are answered only by the apprentice materials — straw, dough, river clay, the gregarious stuff that talks to everyone — which is both every newborn's first conversation with the world and every dimmed maker's way back into it. When work disappoints — a botched commission, a flat performance, an asking carried past its season — the gleam dims, gently, the way a room dims when a lamp is carried out. A maker gone fully dark is not punished; the world simply becomes shy of them again, as it is shy of everyone at birth.

This is Standing.

  • This could become: the Standing fail-resource, exactly. Gleam depletes on failed or abandoned askings and unmoved audiences; at zero, all but the apprentice materials refuse the maker's hands, the engine goes quiet, and the run ends into the Quiet Walk — not death, but the world going shy. Gleam level also gates soft benefits (prices, rare-material introductions, which askings the lanterns show you), so Standing is felt as warmth, not a hit-point bar.
  • Visual: a rim-light that belongs to the character, not the scene: the maker is always lit as if by a small source just behind the shoulder, and the strength of that key light is the Standing meter. Full gleam: honeyed backlight, long soft shadow thrown toward the viewer. Near-dark: rendered in ambient light only, edges merging with the background — still present, but the world no longer picks them out.

the-askings

When a town needs making, it does not post a notice; it makes the request itself, because in Roundelay even a request must be crafted. An asking is a small object that carries the shape of the need: a tin spoon with a dry bowl (our well), a stitched swallow with one grey wing (our summer), a hinge that opens onto nothing (our hope, frankly, we're not sure). Askings hang on the asking-lantern at the town gate — but a lantern is choosy, and shows a traveler only what the town believes their hands can hold. Small hands find kettles; the great askings wait in the rafters for bright ones. Accepting is done with the hands: you take the little object down, and from then on it rides your pack and warms when you near the work. And the sizing runs one way only — an asking is matched to the brightest the road has heard you be, and rumor does not un-travel. At the far end of the ladder, past anything a town can craft, the land itself sometimes makes an asking — a great asking — using what it has: a mountain sends weathered antler and cloudmoss that no human hands arranged; a fading season sends a morning that smells wrong to everyone at once. Great askings are why the Rounds exist, the peak of every maker's hope and the honest awe at the bottom of the craft: someday something the size of weather may hang its need on your lantern.

  • This could become: the escalating contract system, exactly. Each contract is a physical token with legible iconography of its demands; accepted askings sit visibly in the pack UI and warm as their season shortens. Contract tier telegraphs at a glance through the object's size and strangeness. Escalation is lawful and one-directional: available askings scale with peak-gleam-this-run plus paling-compounding on anything skipped, and never scale down. The great askings are the boss tier — re-makings of world-scale systems (a season, a river's voice, a mountain's sleep) that demand the whole engine a run has built.
  • Visual: folk-art objects against dark wood — tin, straw, stitched cloth, bone-white birch — each with one wrong, faded detail that states the need. The asking-lantern at dusk: dozens of small objects turning slowly on red thread, backlit — a chandelier of needs. For a great asking, the scale inverts: a valley where the clouds have knotted themselves into red thread, and one gleam-lit figure at the bottom of the frame, pack on, looking up.

the-long-rounds (anchor — the wandering journeyman with a pack is folk-tale furniture, kept deliberately familiar so that waking-of-made-things, makers-gleam, and the-askings stay instantly legible through it)

The Rounds are the great circuits the makers walk — not roads so much as habits the land has learned, and the land goes on learning. A Round remembers who travels it well. This is road-memory, and every road-gift flows from it: for a maker the Round knows, the miles fold politely, a day's walk tucking itself into an afternoon; the same hedge that scratched you in spring holds out blackberries in fall; fords are shallower, dusk comes later, the town you helped is somehow always downhill. Every maker's run is one verse of a Round, and no two verses visit the same world, because the towns have paled and bloomed differently each time. Retiring makers all say the same thing: you never finish a Round; you hand it on mid-song.

  • This could become: the run and map structure — a procedural circuit (a loop with a home note, not a branching tree toward a boss), where route choice is the core strategic layer: which pale towns to answer now, which to trust to the next maker at compounded size. Road-memory is the single home of all travel buffs, a persistent-within-run map layer earned by how you travel; gleam merely decides how warmly the road greets what it remembers.
  • Visual: a hand-inked circuit map that draws itself as you walk it, the line behind you gaining color and small marginalia — a bird, a kettle, a face — for each town answered. The road ahead is a pale pencil line; the road behind is illuminated-manuscript bright.

hearth-right (anchor — the fireside welcome of the traveler is kept deliberately familiar so that the listening house and story-as-gift stay legible)

Any traveler on the Rounds may claim hearth-right: one night's warmth, one meal, one bed by the fire, freely given, nothing asked. It is the oldest practice on the Rounds and the most beloved — the knock, the held-up hand, the door swinging wide, the whole household's evening rearranging itself with pleasure around a guest. And yet no guest has ever managed to leave nothing behind, because a fed and firelit traveler tells — it cannot be helped — and the story told at the fire is how the house learns the world. Houses grow rich on stories: a house that has heard many travelers' tales wakes sooner and stands longer against the Paling, and a house that never hosts is not punished for it — it simply wakes thin and late, the way anyone does who never hears any news. So hosts compete in kindness for reasons the kindness entirely conceals, and the finest inns on the Rounds are the ones where the rafters themselves lean in to listen.

  • This could become: the rest-node economy, inverted from a shop into a gift. Rest and healing are free — it is a right. The telling is a choice, not a cost: the player picks one event from this run's history to tell at the fire, and the house wakes around it — that event's tag becomes the night's boon (card upgrade, town relationship, small blessing). Selection, never expenditure; the run's past is an instrument, not a purse.
  • Visual: the doorway shot — night-blue street, a door opened onto deep amber, the traveler's silhouette rimmed in gleam, firelight reaching across the doorstep like a hand. Interior palette: hearth-orange, wool-red, shadow-brown, one cold blue window; and around the teller, the room's woken things tilted a degree closer.

journey-pieces

A maker's skill is not carried in the head; it is carried in the pack, as pieces. A journey-piece is a kept fragment of learned craft: a ribbon holding the turn of a dance, a chip of glaze with a whole kiln's wisdom in its crackle, three bars of a planing-song knotted into a cord, a joke folded into a paper bird (unfolding it tells it — once). Makers trade pieces, win them at festivals, and are given them by masters — and to use one is to perform it, out loud, with the hands, in front of the work. A full pack rattling with pieces is a life's education you can hear coming down the road.

  • This could become: the deck itself, exactly. Cards are physical craft fragments with craft-discipline suits (song, joinery, glaze, thread, dough, dance...); deck-building is diegetic pack-packing, card acquisition is teaching, trading, and gifting, and exhaust effects are pieces given away or performed to destruction — the paper-bird joke.
  • Visual: each card painted as a thing on worn canvas — ribbon, shard, knotted cord, folded paper — the woken ones carrying the gold working-edge. The open pack: a bandolier spread on tool-roll leather, each piece in its loop; a deck that reads as a beautifully kept toolkit.

shy-materials

In Roundelay the materials are alive the way cats are alive: present, opinionated, and not obliged to come when called. Silver will not take a shape unless it is sung to; a certain green riverwood opens only for people who are laughing; festival glass must be blown in front of an audience or it clouds. Getting materials is therefore never mining and always courtship, and the courtship has two gates the world keeps in strict order. First, a material must consent to negotiate at all, and that is gleam's business: proud stuff will not even state its terms to hands the world has not vouched for, while the apprentice materials — straw, dough, river clay — chatter with anyone, dim or bright. Second, terms once stated must be met, and those are the material's own: the song sung, the laugh genuine, the audience present. Makers keep notebooks of what the local clay likes, and a stranger's first question in a new town is not "what do you need" but "what does your stone answer to."

  • This could become: resource acquisition as legible micro-conditions rather than flat costs. Gleam gates which materials will state a condition this run (the material market widens as Standing rises, narrows to apprentice-tier at the bottom); each material's stated condition — played a song card, kept a chain going, hold an audience token — gates the actual acquisition. Two gates, always in that order, both visible.
  • Visual: materials with body language — ingots that lean away or lean in, clay rippling toward a singer like iron filings toward a magnet, a shelf of jars whose contents visibly perk up when the right maker walks in. Color signature per temperament: shy blues, sociable ambers, vain iridescents.

audience-of-things

When true work begins, made things gather their attention. Chairs come to rest a quarter-turn toward the bench; lamps steady; somewhere in the rafters the house's oldest woken kettle takes up a low, attentive hum you can feel in your teeth more than hear. Nothing acts — attending is all a woken thing does — but attention in Roundelay is not a courtesy, it is a force, and makers work measurably better inside it. The finest workshops are therefore built like small theaters, tools racked in raked rows like an audience that never goes home, and a market-day crowd is worth a master's own hands. The dread of every maker is not the critic but the empty room: work done truly alone wakes thin, if it wakes at all.

  • This could become: the attention meter — the run's rhythm engine and the judge of waking. Attention is provided by watchers (woken audience-things, townsfolk, venue) and sustained by unbroken work (chaining plays without stalling keeps the room with you); it spends as a multiplier on the big play, and a card played at or above the waking threshold wakes. Persistent artifacts are woken audience-things granting passive attention. One visible currency, sourced from the fiction's actual claim: things wake when watched.
  • Visual: the workshop-as-theater — a pool of worklight center-frame and, in the half-dark around it, dozens of small woken silhouettes tilted inward at the same angle: jugs, lasts, lanterns, a rocking horse — every gold working-edge pointed at the bench like rows of eyes catching stage light.

handsels

A handsel is luck made small and given: a whittled bird, a glazed button, a knot of bright thread, pressed into a palm to set fortune moving. It is also — and no one in Roundelay finds this a contradiction — the money. A handsel's worth is how awake it is, and handsels wake the only way anything wakes: by being attended, hand to delighted hand. A coin that has crossed a hundred glad palms hums like a struck cup and buys a week's lodging; the same coin shut in a strongbox goes dull in a season, quietly, back toward being a button. So wealth in Roundelay pools wherever giving is busiest, misers are not scorned but simply, slowly, poor, and a good market at noon sounds like rain on bells — a thousand small woken things ringing against each other in a thousand open hands.

  • This could become: the currency layer with wakefulness denominations (dull / warm / singing) instead of metals, and a built-in tempo economy: idle money dims each round (anti-hoard pressure as lawful, gentle decay), while spending into delight returns change a shade brighter than the price strictly required. Money and the waking law are one system; the economy teaches the theme.
  • Visual: a market stall strung with small tokens in graded glow, each swaying handsel ringing a different soft note; a payment shown close-up as a dull button brightening visibly the moment a pleased hand closes over it.

the-quiet-walk

When a maker goes fully dim, the Rounds have a rite for it, and it is kind. The unlit maker walks the circuit one more time — no pack, no pieces, no askings — and at every town is fed, and warmed, and not asked for anything, which is the strangest sensation of a maker's life. And because gleam gathers on delight given in any coin at all, the way back is lawful and ordinary: some walk a season and feel the first flicker return in a stranger's kitchen, laughing at nothing, having given the only thing an empty pack still holds; some walk to the sea and settle there, done, and are honored as the audience every future maker plays to. Dimming is an ending the way autumn is an ending: it is one, truly, and also the ground the next brightness grows from.

  • This could become: the run-end and meta-progression frame. Standing hitting zero triggers the Quiet Walk — a brief playable epilogue that revisits this run's woken works, one of which is chosen to persist into the meta-layer as a permanent audience-thing, unlock, or starting boon for the next maker.
  • Visual: the same circuit map walked in reverse light — dawn-grey palette, the maker un-rimmed and ordinary among ordinary people, while everything they woke on the way out still carries its gold edge. The light didn't go out; it moved into the work. The most emotionally load-bearing image in the game.

unmarked-wonders

Every made thing carries its maker's mark somewhere — a thumbprint in the glaze, a signature bar in the woken hum. But scattered through Roundelay are wonders with no mark at all: a stone stair a thousand years bright, a river that has never once forgotten its knack, a bell that wakes other things when it rings. Nobody knows who made them, and nobody agrees whether "who" is even the right question — the cheerful heresy of the Rounds is that the world may simply have started making itself, somewhere, the way a very old workshop starts finishing small jobs overnight. Makers treat unmarked wonders as colleagues, not gods: you tip your hat, you leave a journey-piece on the step, you study the joinery. (Canon note, carried forward as this layer's one open question: the unmarked wonders are the game's long riddle. Layer 2 must decide how much of the answer exists — and whatever it decides, the answer is not elder makers, ancient civilizations, or gods. The self-making-workshop heresy stays a heresy: never confirmed, never cheapened.)

  • This could become: rare discovery nodes and relic-class cards — unmarked pieces with rule-bending behaviors that fit no suit, found and never bought, each one a small mystery the collection screen quietly assembles into the game's long riddle.
  • Visual: the gold working-edge, but everywhere at once — unmarked wonders are edge-lit along every line, softly, like drawings that haven't been colored in because the light got there first. They photograph as slightly too in-focus against a painterly world.

How the spine arises — discovered, not installed

Walk one verse of a Round and both halves of the spine simply happen to you.

You leave your wintering-town in spring with a light pack and a thread of gleam — enough that doors know you're a maker, not enough that anyone important does. At the first village the asking-lantern shows you what it judges your hands can hold: a tin spoon with a dry bowl, a cracked whistle. You take the spoon. You court the local clay (it likes being told about the weather), you re-make the well-ring in front of six children and one attentive kettle, and the water comes back sweet, and the color comes back to the square like watercolor blooming — and something comes to you, too. Your knuckles hold dusk-light a little longer. The road out of town tucks a mile away for you. That's the whole loop, once around, small.

Here is the thing the world knows that no one has to explain: light is visible, and rumor does not un-travel. The next town heard about the well before you arrived — woken things murmur, travelers carry tales to hearths, hearths tell their houses — so the next lantern lowers something larger out of its rafters: a need it had all along, kept waiting for hands bright enough to hold it. Nothing demands more of you. Need simply aims itself at brightness, the way a warm window gathers travelers on a cold road, and it never aims lower than the brightest it has heard you be. Meanwhile the Paling runs its patient arithmetic underneath: every pale town you pass is a need compounding at its own gentle rate until the road brings you back. Escalation is not a system anyone imposes and not a thing you can decline — it is what it feels like to be heard of in a world that is quietly, forever, coming apart at the edges and knows exactly whose hands are worth asking. Being asked is the honor; the size of the asking is the compliment; and by midsummer you are carrying a stitched swallow with one grey wing and understanding, with a shiver, that a whole summer has heard of you.

And Standing is the same light, felt from inside as warmth rather than threat. Gleam is the world's trust made visible, and every working day feeds or spends it: an audience moved, a hearth warmed, a laugh set loose — brighter; a flat performance, a botched commission, an asking carried past its season — dimmer, gently, a lamp carried out of the room. Nothing punishes you. The materials just get shy again. Doors open a beat slower; the blackberry hedge is only a hedge; the lanterns show you kettles again, then buttons, then nothing but the rafters' dark. Fall far enough and even the straw answers slowly, and there is nothing to do but what makers have always done: set down the pack and take the Quiet Walk, warming at hearths that ask you for nothing, until — sometimes — laughter in a stranger's kitchen strikes the first spark of the next run.

Escalating contracts: the world's need aiming itself at your brightness. Standing: the world's trust gathered on your skin. Both are the same law of attention seen from two sides, and both feel — because they are — like reputation, weather, and being known. The run ends, and in Roundelay that is not a game-over screen; it is autumn.

Anchor declaration (summary)

Two anchors, each declared inline at its element:

  • the-long-rounds — the itinerant-journeyman circuit. The wandering maker with a pack is folk-tale furniture, chosen for exactly that legibility: it carries waking-of-made-things (why a traveler's work matters cosmologically), makers-gleam (a Standing resource read on a character's skin at dusk), and the-askings (contracts as crafted folk-objects) into instant readability.
  • hearth-right — the fireside welcome. The traveler fed at a stranger's fire is equally familiar, and carries the genuinely strange claims (houses that learn the world through stories; rest as pure gift with no exchange anywhere in it) into a scene every player already knows how to want.

Anchor share: 2 of 12 elements. No other conventional elements are relied on: no chosen one (any hands can brighten), no dark lord (the Paling is a tide, not a villain), no elemental magic (the magic is attention). Per the do-not-resemble list: nothing in Roundelay is owed, promised, filed, or adjudicated; the askings are gifts that request, the gleam is trust and never credit, hearth-right is a right and never a bargain, and the only court in Roundelay is the audience of things.

Register bans carried forward (binding on Layers 2–7)

  • Vocabulary ban: owed, debt, promise, vow, payment-due, penalty, fine, duty-to-host may not appear in any diegetic naming or card text. Failure consequences are always ecological and mnemonic — the world grows shy, forgets, moves on — never punitive or transactional.
  • Channel ban: the grammar of light is strict — places pale, people gleam, things wake. No layer may put a working-edge on a person, a key-light on a place, or paling-grey on a character.
  • Restraint ban: woken things attend; they never act, speak, or emote. No self-pouring pots, no helpful furniture, no coat that turns up its own collar.
  • Riddle ban: the unmarked wonders are never explained as elder makers, precursors, or gods.

Layer 2 — Cosmology

THE ROUND OF LIGHT — a natural philosophy of Roundelay

Roundelay's cosmology is not a story about how the world began, because the world contains no first verse to tell of. You do not ask where a roundelay began; you join it already going. That absence is not an evasion — it is the cosmology's shape, stated as canon and load-bearing: the world's deepest law has the same shape as its name. What the world offers instead of an origin is a physics — small, testable, tender — every law of which a curious child could check from a windowsill, and children do (see the-noticing-game).

The whole of it in one breath: attention — regard, the holding-of-things-in-mind — is Roundelay's one conserved stuff, and light is what regard looks like from outside. Regard is never created and never destroyed. It pools loose in places, runs live in the living, sets fast in made things, and — the one lawful binding, worn two ways — clings to any maker it can trace a delight to and rides any handsel still crossing glad hands. The Paling is the return stroke of its circulation; dawn is its reissue; gleam is overflow finding its cause; and the asking ratchet is its one irreversibility. Escalating contracts and a depletable Standing are not systems anyone built. They are what conservation, overflow, and no-unfiring feel like from inside one walking life.

The deep law: nothing is noticed away

Matter in Roundelay is ordinary. Stone is stone, water is water, and none of it is dreamed, sung, or thought into being — a natural philosopher of the Rounds would find the suggestion faintly embarrassing, like being told bread is made of hunger. What regard does is subtler and stranger: regard holds a thing's character crisp. A well without regard is still a hole with water in it; what thins is the trick of itself — the knack of sweet water, the exact welcome of its rope-worn coping. Character is fine-grained, and fine grain needs holding. Regard is the holding.

The first law, the one every child recites skipping: nothing is noticed away. When regard leaves a place it has not perished; it has gone somewhere, and it can be followed. Following it is the whole discipline of the light-followers, the Rounds' patient hedge-philosophers — no academy, no college, no instrument that will not fit in a pack: tally-chalk, hazel wands, pin-lanterns, patience. Their entire literature fits in walking-songs, and their method's home is the windowsill, not the laboratory, which is why nobody in Roundelay experiences the world's physics as doctrine. It is the stuff you checked yourself, age seven, against your own chalk marks.

The three settlings — and the one binding

Regard takes three holdings, and the holding belongs to the holder. This is the grammar of light, given its floor:

  • In places, regard lies loose. Ground has no wanting and no use; it holds regard the way a meadow holds mist — a wash, beautiful, unbound, free to seep. The loose wash is the color and knack of scenery.
  • In the living, regard runs. Only the living want, and wanting is the lens: a living creature gathers loose regard through itself and aims it. Living light is always a current, never a pool.
  • In made things, regard sets. Setting needs a use to set into — regard fires into function the way glaze fires into clay, which is why the gold line lives on the working edge and nowhere else: the lip of the bowl, the blade of the plane, the hem that does the coat's actual keeping-warm. And a living body will never take the set, for the plainest reason in the world: you cannot fire wet clay, and a living thing is never done being made.

And one lawful binding, which is not a fourth holding but the three settlings' one permitted seam: loose light held in place by ongoing regard. The binding is worn in two homes, and every market-day teaches both. On a maker it is the clinging — gleam is loose light bound to a line of cause — regard that traces back to a delight some particular hands set moving, clinging where those hands end, at knuckles and hair and the rims of the ears, the way dew clings where surfaces end. On a handsel it is held light (see the two wakings, below) — loose light bound by the circulating regard of hand after delighted hand. Both stay loose (neither can fire: a body is never done being made, and a coin's whole use is to keep moving), and each is sheltered from the tide exactly as far as its home shelters it. The Paling's tide skims loose light only where it lies still on the unliving. Gleam rides the surface of a current — dew on a riverboat, not dew on a stone — and no tide can skim across moving water: while the maker lives, gleam is never the Paling's to take, and it thins only by its own binding slackening (see the gleam cycle, below). Held light lies on the unliving, so its whole shelter is the attending: a handsel crossing glad hands is never still beneath the tide — but shut a coin in a strongbox and the binding lapses, the light lies still on the unliving at last, and the ordinary seep carries it downhill to the gloaming-table like any unregarded wash. The dulling coin is not a third process and not a mystery; it is the first law doing its rounds — binding ended, seep resumed, nothing noticed away. And the law-not-mercy stands entire: the Paling does not spare the living out of kindness, and it does not spare their gleam out of respect. It spares them out of physics. People never fade; the tide touches the board of the world and never once the skin.

Three settlings, one binding worn two ways, three impossibilities: no person wears a working-edge (never done being made), no place gleams (a place is a where, not a cause), no character greys (a current cannot be skimmed). The channels never cross because they are not three lights. They are three phases of one light, and the phase belongs to the holder.

The held and the fired — the two wakings

Waking is regard binding to a made thing, and the binding comes in exactly two strengths, which every market-day teaches side by side.

Fired waking happens at the bench. When true work is done under gathered regard, some of the room's attention sets into the work's use, for good. Fired regard is the round of light's one irreversible transformation: you can weather fired glaze; you can never un-fire it. This is why woken work holds its knack for generations while unwoken work pales in seasons — and why the weathering, slow as stone, still comes for everything eventually. Permanence in Roundelay is long, not endless; only the closed rounds (see below) never weather at all.

Held waking happens in the hand. A handsel — a whittled bird, a glazed button, a coin — wakes the only way anything wakes, by being attended; but what a delighted hand renews, only continuing delight can keep. Held light lies on a thing the way gleam lies on a maker: bound by ongoing regard, brightening with every glad palm it crosses, lapsing when the attending stops. A coin that has crossed a hundred hands hums like a struck cup; the same coin shut in a strongbox goes dull in a season, quietly, back toward being a button. Nothing was broken and nothing was taken out of turn. The binding simply lapsed, as bindings do when no one is doing the holding — and lapsed light is only loose light lying still on the unliving, which is exactly the tide's to skim: a boxed coin's dulling is the ordinary Paling, indoors, its shine seeping downhill to the table to come back in somebody's dawn.

Bench-work is fired; circulation-work is held. The fired never unfires; the held must be fed. One law, two persistences — and the whole handsel economy, the gentle anti-hoard weather of the world's money, falls out of the difference.

Conservation at the bench

When true work is done before watchers, the regard in the room moves in one legible transaction. Some of it sets into the work — that is waking. Some of it overflows — a made thing, like a cup, holds only so much, and the excess runs back along the line of cause to the maker — that is gleam. The remainder returns loose to the room — which is why workshops and market squares are the most colorful places on any Round, and why a town re-made before a crowd re-colors like watercolor blooming: the bench is a fountain that pours in three directions at once, and not one dram is lost. Light-followers have sat through ten thousand makings with tally-chalk and never once caught the world cheating.

The Paling — the return stroke, and where the taken light goes

The Paling is not decay, not appetite, not an enemy dressed as weather. The Paling is collection. Loose regard — and only loose regard, lying still on the unliving — seeps out of unregarded scenery at a slow seasonal rate, strongest in late summer and autumn when the world exhales, near-still in deep winter. And it seeps downhill. This is measurable and measured: hilltop shrines pale seasons before valley mills; a high pasture goes grey at the fence-line while the low orchard under it holds its reds like a cupped hand. The drained light gathers along the grain of the ground into the gloaming-table — the world's low standing reserve of spent regard, pooled under valley floors and deep water the way groundwater pools under fields.

And this must be said plainly, because sentimental strangers always get it wrong: the gloaming-table is a pantry, not an underworld. Nothing living goes into it, ever — no souls, no memories of the dead, nothing anyone will meet again — only the spent regard of places and things, broken back down into plain, unshaped brightness. The world composts its noticing. It never composts its people.

From the gloaming-table it comes back. Dawn is the reissue. Every morning's light is spent regard returned — loose, unclaimed, free to every eye that opens — which is why the oldest folk-saw on the Rounds is also a verified finding: pale country gets the best mornings. The greyer the evening, the redder the dawn, because the fuller the local table, the richer the return. Every morning is made of everything the world stopped noticing, given back to be noticed again. That is the round of light entire: dawn to eyes, eyes to hands, hands to work, work to places, places seeping to the gloaming-table, the table rising as dawn.

Folk on the Rounds say the world is quietly, forever, coming apart at the edges, and the light-followers smile and let them say it, because from inside one walking life that is exactly what the out-breath feels like. From above, nothing is coming apart at all. The world is breathing, and the Paling is the out-breath without which there is no in.

The proof is a remembered season. In the Stillsummer of Quill Vale, some forty years back, the Paling in that one valley simply stopped — no one knows why to this day, though light-followers note, carefully, that the valley holds a near-closed wonder (see the riddle, below) and say no more than that. The valley did not bloom. It curdled. Colors went waxy and over-bright, like fruit that will not ripen because it will not soften; knacks hoarded in things and staled there; and the mornings — this is what everyone remembers — the mornings came grey and thin all summer, because the light had nowhere to return from. Birds sang at noon, confused. When the Paling resumed at the autumn equinox, the first reissued dawn was so vast and so rose-gold that people walked all night from three towns to stand in it, and the Quill Vale Morning is still what makers say when they mean an ending that turns out to be a gift. Nobody who stood in that dawn has ever again called the Paling a thief.

Why knack goes before color: character is fine-grained, and fine grain needs the most holding — so the trick of the well seeps first, and the red of the roof follows, coarse and slow. Why unwoken work pales fastest: it holds only the loose wash, and loose is what the tide takes. Why woken work stands generations: fired regard cannot be skimmed, only weathered, slow as stone.

The gleam cycle — Standing, derived (mechanism first)

At the Moth Fair at Ferrow, sixty-one autumns back, a fiddler named Immy Ash played The Long Thaw three times through on her grandmother's fiddle — an old, well-woken instrument, its gold edge steady along the bass-bar. By the second time through, four hundred people were aiming everything they had at that fiddle, and here is what the whole fairground saw, and what two lantern-keepers with tally-chalk timed: the fiddle's gold edge blazed to a fixed brightness and then would not brighten further — a woken thing, like a cup, holds only so much — and the crowd's regard, still pouring, ran visibly up the strings and into her hands. Her knuckles took the light first, then her hair, then the rims of her ears, doubling before the reel ended. The light-followers' phrase for the finding is four words long: a full cup runs to the pourer.

That is the whole mechanism of gleam. Regard traces lines of cause — it does not splash at random; it runs backward along the making, the way water finds the channel that brought it. When delight overfills what the work can hold, the overflow follows the line of cause to the maker and clings — at the body's edges, the way dew clings where surfaces end. And moments overfill exactly as objects do: a joke can hold only so much of a kitchen's laughter, a told story only so much of a hearth's leaning-in, and the excess runs to the one who caused it. This is why gleam is earnable in any coin the world honors, and why the Quiet Walk works with an empty pack: an instant of caused delight is a cup like any other, and it, too, runs over to the pourer.

The lines of cause take time to travel, because tales walk at a hearth's pace. A maker sometimes brightens at dusk for work done three towns back, as last week's delight reaches new firesides — on the Rounds they say the light of a good deed travels on foot.

What holds gleam on? The world's continued regard for what you did. Clinging lasts exactly as long as the delight it traces to is still held in mind somewhere — retold at hearths, murmured between woken things, carried town to town as tale. Gleam is, quite literally, the world currently holding you in mind, worn as light. So it thins two ways, on two clocks, and the clocks never trade places:

  • When work disappoints, it thins tonight. An unmoved audience is regard withdrawn all at once; the lines of cause go slack in a single evening, and the slackened light slides off where the maker stands — into the flagstones, the doorframes, the lane (see the-spilling). It does not vanish. Nothing is noticed away. The prettiest alley in Ferrow is the one behind the fair-stage.
  • When the world merely moves on, it thins across lifetimes, never within a season's walking. Within one verse of a Round the talk of you is live — hearths still telling, kettles still humming — and a working maker's gleam moves only as work delights or disappoints. The long forgetting is slower weather: talk of the Moth Fair stopped being carried past Whistledown within a few winters, and Immy Ash's shine thinned across those same winters at the same measured rate — not taken, not docked, simply, gradually, no longer held. Makers meet the long forgetting between verses of the Round, in grey retirements and bright returns; no one has ever dimmed by walking quietly for a season. (Non-diegetic note for L4: within-run Standing moves on outcomes only; ambient forgetting is a between-runs, meta-scale phenomenon — adopt there, or clamp.)

Now derive the mechanic, which no longer needs deriving: a light that is the world holding you in mind must be depletable — minds let go when work disappoints — and must be restorable by any fresh caused delight, from a re-made bridge to laughter in a stranger's kitchen, because every overfilled moment runs to its pourer. Standing that falls when work disappoints or an asking goes stale, rises when delight is given in any coin, gates the shy materials (proud stuff will not state terms to hands the world is not currently holding in mind), and ends the run not in death but in the world going quiet toward you — that is not a designed meter. That is overflow, clinging, and letting-go, worn on the skin. The register holds all the way down: gleam-loss is forgetting, and the spilled light makes the world around your failure slightly more beautiful. Roundelay does not punish. It composts.

The asking ratchet — escalating contracts, derived (mechanism first)

Twenty-two springs ago, Sorrel Vane re-made the flood-broken bridge at Brindle-on-the-Wold before a crowd of two hundred — a great work, the kind a road talks about for a generation. What happened next is the best-remembered telling the light-followers carry of lantern-craft — timed with tally-chalk that spring, and in the walking-songs ever since. Every asking-lantern down the Wold road, towns Vane had never entered, began lowering her rafters-work the day she arrived: granary roofs, a river's silted voice, a fouled spring two valleys wide. At Merrylees, tired, she asked the lantern-keeper for something small — a kettle season, a resting year. The keeper, fond of her, hung a tin whistle at eye height that very evening. And by morning the whistle hung plainly in view — Vane could see it, touch it, turn it on its thread — but it hung in the lantern's dark: unlit, unoffered, an object and never once an asking, while above it the rafters-work waited at its full size. The keeper's fondness was one voice out of step with his own town; Merrylees' hearths had carried the bridge for a season, and a lantern shows a town's settled belief, not its keeper's. A lantern is a woken thing, and what a woken thing has attended, it holds attended: there is no unfiring.

Two laws meet in that hour under the lantern, and the ratchet falls out of them like a dropped tool.

First: a lantern's readings are fired, and fired readings never unfire. A lantern's one fixed attending is the reading of hands, and a reading, once taken, is set — bench-fired, permanent. Setting is the round of light's one irreversible transformation: loose becomes set through attentive regard, and set never becomes loose again on any timescale a life contains. The lantern that has read a bridge in your grain has fired that reading; it will size its rafters-work to you forever, and never again size it down.

Second: need takes aim, and it aims by reading grain. Gleam is overflow traced from causes, so gleam has grain — bridge-delight lies differently on the knuckles than song-delight — and a trained lantern reads in your shine the size and kind of the delights that fed it. You wear your history as texture. An asking, itself a made and woken thing, attends toward the brightest hands its town has heard of — heard through the murmur-net of woken things and hearth-tales, the same regard-lines that hold gleam on. Rumor does not un-travel because attending does not un-set.

And the two variables never trade places — this, too, is law, and every maker's fortunes hang on the difference. What a lantern sizes is fired: matched to the brightest the road has heard you be, one-way, forever. What a lantern shows is current: gated by the gleam presently on your hands — the town's living belief about what those hands can hold tonight. A bright maker is shown the rafters and nothing less; that is the compliment, and it cannot be declined. A dimming maker watches the offers descend the ladder — kettles again, then buttons, then nothing but the rafters' dark — while overhead, unlowered, the great reading waits, fired and patient, for the light to come back. The sizing never falls; the showing follows your weather. The descent is legible, the return is lawful, and both are the same law read on two clocks.

Underneath both, the Paling runs its patient arithmetic on anything skipped: a pale town's loose regard keeps seeping at the season's fixed rate, and knack shores up knack, so each trick a place forgets uncovers the next trick to the weather — pass a need by in spring and by harvest it is twice the work, twice the reissue waiting in its dawns, twice the wonder when the road brings you back. Lantern-keepers chalk the count in spirals on the gatepost (see the-paling-arithmetic), so the whole road can read a town's patience at a glance. Nothing compounds out of spite. It compounds out of weather.

So the conventional shape arrives last, as a consequence, the only way it could: contracts escalate because fired readings do not unset and need aims at the brightest grain it has read; they never scale down because the reading is fired, not felt; and the run's descent stays readable because showing, unlike sizing, follows the living light. Being asked is the honor; the size of the asking is the compliment; and the compliment, like everything fired in this world, is permanent.

(Non-diegetic note for L4, parallel to the gleam-decay clamp above: fired sizings are per-maker and last that maker's lifetime — a lantern reads hands, not whoever holds the pack. Each run is a fresh maker by default, so asking tier lawfully keys to peak-gleam-this-run. If the meta-layer ever returns the same maker out of the Quiet Walk for a second run, that maker's lanterns keep their fired readings — adopt that as a high-stakes continuation mode, or clamp by ruling every re-spark a new maker walking a new Round.)

The great askings — the land's voice, derived (mechanism first)

Eleven autumns back, the sweetwater spring under Wray Fell had been three years paling — the long-famous trick of it seeping out ring by ring on every gatepost down three valleys — when a shepherd found, on the high stone above the source, weathered antler and cloudmoss arranged in the shape of falling water, and not a footprint anywhere on the fell. The valleys did what valleys have always done with a great asking: tied red thread where it lay, and waited for hands bright enough.

The land had not spoken. Ground has no wanting — that floor holds, and no great asking has ever cracked it. But three valleys of living wanting had been aimed at one lack for three years: shepherds missing the sweetness, swifts missing the midge-clouds over the pool, children raised on the story of a sweetness they had never tasted. Living regard is a current, and currents cross. Where enough currents hold one lack in mind, their crossing combs the loose wash of a place the way wind combs standing grass into waves, the way a river braids its own sandbars — no maker's aim anywhere in it, no single wanting shaping anything, only a countryside of aims passing through the same ground until what the ground has — antler, cloudmoss, weather — lies arranged along the crossing. Light-followers walked Wray Fell with hazel wands that whole third summer and read the wash already running in long seams toward the high stone, weeks before the antler was found. The finding is called the combing, and the folk-saw that carries it: the wash combs where currents cross. A fading season's great asking is the same law at the scale of weather: when a whole country misses one season's knack, the crossing combs the reissue itself, and a morning arrives that smells wrong to everyone at once — the country's own missing, handed back at dawn, shaped.

So a great asking is made the only way the deep law allows anything to be made: by the living — in aggregate, none of them making it. And it is a held thing, not a fired one: nothing benched it, no crowd watched it, and it stays awake by the one attending that cannot lapse while the need lasts — the countryside's continuing regard for what it misses. That is why a great asking never dulls in the rafters like a boxed coin; why it aims, like every held and woken thing, along the regard-lines toward the brightest hands the country has heard of; and why the seventh ring of the paling-arithmetic is called a great asking being born — seven seasons unanswered is about how long a whole countryside's missing takes to converge into one crossing. The mountain sends nothing. Everyone who misses the mountain, together, does. And no one has ever watched a combing finish, for the ordinary reason nobody watches weather all night — not because anything in it is unknowable: dowsers read the seams, tally-chalk counts the currents, and the spirals on the gateposts predict, within a season, where the next one will lie.

The unmarked wonders — the long riddle, decided (I-004)

Decision: the answer exists in canon by exactly half, and the boundary between the knowable half and the unknowable half is itself a law of the world. Not mystery as fog — mystery as horizon: lawful, located, and impossible to cross from where anyone stands.

The knowable half — canon, in-world, tested and teachable to children: an unmarked wonder is a closed round — a made thing whose circulation of regard has closed into a self-sustaining loop. An ordinary wonder sheds: it delights, the overflow runs off to its maker, the loose remainder seeps to the gloaming-table, and one day it needs re-making. A closed round keeps what it pours. The delight it causes runs its overflow back into its own setting; it wakes what passes near it, and what it wakes attends it in return; it is a whirlpool in the round of light that never unwinds — the one thing in the world the weathering never reaches, because the work never stops passing. Light-followers have measured this directly: stand by the thousand-year stair with tally-chalk and you can watch the circulation, a slow visible current under the stone like glass under water. This also dissolves the mark question lawfully rather than answering it with a who: a maker's mark persists because runoff keeps tracing back to its cause — but in a closed loop the runoff circles inside, and the maker's share, mark, name, and grain were long ago swallowed into the whole and worn smooth. The wonders may well have had makers — ordinary ones, no elder anything, no one buried, no one hiding — and closure itself ate the attribution. Canon adds the guard in plain words: if the wonders had makers, those makers were no brighter, older, or other than today's. Closure, not pedigree, is the whole difference. That is canon.

The unknowable half — permanently open, and lawfully so: how a round closes. No maker has ever closed one on purpose; no one has ever watched one begin; every wonder was found already going. The light-followers keep a short, beloved list of near-closures: the stair at Setterby holds nine parts in ten of what it sheds, and pilgrim-makers leave journey-pieces on it the way you would encourage a student. And the Rounds hold exactly one disputed candidate: the ferry-bell at Hask, whose engraved signature bar the oldest listeners swear was plainer in their grandmothers' day — either the mark is being swallowed as the bell's round slowly closes, or it was always faint, depending on which ferry you ask. The Haskers refuse to settle it, on a board by the slip in six generations of handwriting, on the grounds that a riddle carried this well would be a shame to set down.

And here the measurement limit does its quiet work: you cannot trace causes inside a closed loop from outside it. The very completeness that makes a wonder permanent makes its history unreadable — attribution has no line left to run along. This is why the cheerful heresy survives untouched and untouchable: if the world's own great round — dawn, eyes, hands, work, places, table, dawn — is itself closed, then the world is a workshop finishing its own small jobs overnight, and no one inside it could ever confirm that, for exactly the same reason no one can read the stair. The heresy stays a heresy: never confirmed, never cheapened, protected forever by the same law that makes it thinkable.

How much the game may reveal, in canon terms: all of the what (closed circulation, measurable in play, discoverable wonder by wonder), all of the why-unmarked (closure eats attribution), none of the how, and none of the whether-the-world-is-one — and the endgame aspiration this leaves standing is the best kind: somewhere past the great askings lives the question of whether one maker's masterwork, made attentively enough, before audience enough, might close. The laws neither offer this nor forbid it. Nothing in the record says it has ever been done.

the-grain-of-gleam

Lanterns read grain — it is their one fixed attending — and so do proud materials, which is how silver knows whose hands have held an audience and whose have only held tools. On the Rounds your history is not told; it is worn, and it is legible at dusk from across a square.

the-spilling

When work disappoints, the audience's regard withdraws all at once, the slackened gleam slides off where the maker stands, and — nothing being noticed away — it soaks loose into the flagstones, the doorframes, the lane. No eye has ever caught it going: the slip is not a sight, and the lane is simply found brighter. Failure in Roundelay leaves the world slightly more beautiful exactly where it happened: the prettiest alley in Ferrow is the one behind the fair-stage, colored generation by generation by everyone who ever died out there. And the lane remembers the light, never the name — spilled regard is loose, its lines of cause gone slack, so the one maker the new color will never answer to is the one who spilled it. Makers know this and are strangely comforted by it; there is an old toast — to the lanes we've painted.

  • This could become: the Standing-loss rule with a gift inside it: every point of gleam lost re-colors the current node as small ambient boons — claimable only by later visitors and future runs, never by the spiller in the run that spilled, and always worth less than the gleam it cost — so failure feeds the same world the next maker walks without ever inviting a deliberate flop. Loss aversion softened by lawful, visible compost; the Quiet Walk's meta-seeds grounded in physics rather than menu design.

need-takes-aim

An asking is a made, woken thing, and its one fixed attending is toward the brightest hands its town has heard of — heard through hearth-tales and the murmur of woken things, the same regard-lines that hold gleam on. Rumor does not un-travel because attending does not un-set: the lantern at Merrylees, twenty-two springs ago, kept a keeper's fond tin whistle hanging in its dark — visible, touchable, and never once offered — while Sorrel Vane's rafters-work waited overhead at full size, because a lantern shows a town's settled belief, not its keeper's. But what a lantern sizes (fired, matched to your peak, one-way forever) and what it shows (current, gated by tonight's gleam) are two different attendings: the dimming watch their offers walk down the ladder — kettles, buttons, dark — beneath a great reading that never lowers and never leaves.

  • This could become: the escalating-contract generator, exactly and lawfully: asking tier keys off peak-Standing-this-run (read from grain, never a hidden counter) and never falls; asking offers are gated by current Standing, so the failure spiral telegraphs in the lantern itself; next-tier silhouettes preview in the rafters. Delayed gleam arrivals never raise a lantern's reading until they are seen landing. The player always experiences the ratchet as the world's regard, aimed and holding — never as difficulty curve.

the-paling-arithmetic

The paling seeps at a rate every lantern-keeper can recite — gentle, steady, and compounding, because knack shores up knack: a well that forgets sweetness soon forgets depth, and a bridge that forgets carts begins forgetting rain. Keepers chalk a town's paling on the lantern-post in a spiral, one ring per season unanswered, and everyone on the Rounds can read the spiral at a glance: three rings is a plea, five is a poem, seven is a great asking being born.

  • This could become: the exact compounding function for skipped nodes — legible, chalked on the map itself, deterministic. Skip a three-ring town and you know precisely what five rings will ask and give back. Route strategy becomes almanac-reading in a warm way: reading spirals is reading the future.

the-combing

How the land "makes" a great asking without ever wanting one. Ground has no wanting — the floor holds — but living regard is a current and currents cross: when a paling grows wide enough that a whole countryside holds one lack in mind, the crossing of all that aimed regard combs the loose wash of a place into shape — antler and cloudmoss arranged like falling water on Wray Fell, a morning that smells wrong to everyone at once — and the arrangement is a great asking: made by the living in aggregate, none of them making it, and held awake by the one attending that cannot lapse while the need lasts, the country's continuing regard for what it misses. Dowsable weeks ahead (the wash runs in seams toward the crossing), foretold by the gatepost spirals (seven rings is a great asking being born), and never once watched finishing — unwatched the way weather at night is unwatched, not unreadable the way the closed rounds are unreadable. The wash combs where currents cross.

  • This could become: the lawful boss-contract generator — a great asking forms when a region's summed spiral-count crosses the threshold, telegraphed seasons ahead (dowsing scouts read the seams; the map shows the crossing gathering), and keyed like every asking to the brightest grain the country has heard of. The boss tier is thus always weather the player routed: the exact towns skipped, converging.

the-noticing-game

The Rounds' empirical method is a children's game older than any way: paired trials on a windowsill — a pebble carved with full attention beside one cut hasty and alone, chalk tallies beneath, a winter of watching which greys first. Light-followers are simply children who never stopped playing it; their instruments are tally-chalk, hazel wands, pin-lanterns, and patience, their whole literature fits in walking-songs, and there is no academy of them and never will be — the windowsill, not the laboratory, is the method's home. Every law in this cosmology entered canon through somebody's windowsill, which is why nobody in Roundelay experiences the world's physics as doctrine: it is the stuff you checked yourself, age seven, against your own chalk marks.

  • This could become: the tutorial and discovery layer — laws are never stated in menus but demonstrated as folk-experiments the player performs (two pebbles, one winter), with a journeyman's notebook that fills in each law only after play has evidenced it; late-game, the same notebook becomes the riddle-tracker for closed rounds.

the-closed-round

An unmarked wonder is a made thing whose circulation of regard has closed: it keeps what it pours, wakes what passes, is attended by what it wakes — a whirlpool in the round of light that never unwinds, measurable with tally-chalk as a slow current under the stone, and the one thing in the world the weathering never reaches. Closure eats attribution lawfully: the maker's runoff circled inside instead of running home, and mark, name, and grain were worn smooth into the whole — the wonders likely had makers, ordinary ones, no brighter or older or other than today's, and the loop swallowed the signature. How a round closes, no one knows; whether the world's own great round is one, no one can know from inside it. The light-followers' short list of near-closures — the stair at Setterby holds nine parts in ten of what it sheds — is the closest thing the Rounds have to a holy text, except that everything on the list is jointed, weathered, and available for study; and the ferry-bell at Hask, its signature bar fainter than the grandmothers remember or else always faint, is the riddle's one slow public clock, argued over between crossings.

Why the locked spine is now inevitable

Standing is not modeled by this cosmology; it precipitates from it. Given conservation, overflow-to-cause, and the fact that gleam is the world holding you in mind, a people-light that rises with delight given in any coin, collapses gently when work disappoints, gates the proud materials, and ends a run in quiet rather than death is the only shape people-light could take. Escalating contracts likewise: given that fired readings never unset, that askings are woken things whose aim is fixed by what they have attended, and that the Paling compounds anything skipped by plain steady seepage, a one-way, never-descending, peak-keyed contract ladder is not a difficulty curve — it is what it feels like to be heard of, in a world that reads your grain and cannot unread it. And because a lantern's showing follows the living light even while its sizing holds, the fall toward the Quiet Walk stays exactly as legible as Layer 1 requires: kettles again, then buttons, then the rafters' dark.

Layer 3 — Factions

THE WALKING WAYS — the peoples of Roundelay

Roundelay has no nations, and nothing a nation would need: no borders to stand behind, no thrones to sit, nothing ownable enough to guard. What it has instead are ways — six traveling cultures, old as the roads, each one a complete answer to the only question the world makes urgent: how is regard best set into the world? Fired for centuries, or fed back to the table? Answered early and small, or let ripen into a poem? Court the proud silver, or build the room the work wakes in? Ask a maker at a fair what are you? and you will not hear a house or an order; you will hear how they walk — and the walking tells you where they stand in the one circulation.

A way keeps no hall and no roll, and it is never joined; it is walked. You are Kilnfast because Kilnfast work is what your hands do and Kilnfast songs are the ones your road taught you — and the light does the enrolling, because grain shows what actually fed your shine: affiliation in Roundelay is worn, never conferred, legible at dusk from across a square. Children sample all six ways the way children sample everything; most makers settle into one by their second Round, the way a voice settles into its part of the song; and a maker who changes ways has simply changed — turning your coat to the weather, grounds for a party, never a grudge. The light-followers thread through all six and belong to none, exactly as canon holds them: a practice, not a people — chalk, hazel, pin-lanterns, patience, picked up and set down at any age, walked with and never enrolled in.

Each way is a candidate player archetype: a distinct manner of making, traveling, and giving light — a deck shape, a resource preference, a risk posture — and every way's strategy is a true belief about the world's physics, pushed as far as one pack can carry it.

Named elements (Layer 3)

the-kilnfast

The Kilnfast are the way of the fired waking: regard is best given to the world set — benched under gathered eyes, fired into use, holding its knack for generations after the hands are gone. A Kilnfast maker takes few askings and finishes them utterly; the proudest among them aim a whole walking life at a single masterwork, and every Kilnfast child learns the light-followers' short list of near-closures the way other children learn lullabies. The stair at Setterby is their dearest place on any Round — they leave journey-pieces on it the way you encourage a student, then get out the calipers and study the joinery, colleagues at a bench that got something right. They keep no lodge, no secrets, no rolls; their whole strangeness is patience, and at the bottom of it the horizon-hope the laws neither offer nor forbid: that one day a work made attentively enough, before audience enough, might close its round and never weather at all. Of the Paling they speak with respect, as of a worthy rival — the weathering is the only clock they honor. And be exact about their famous slowness, because sentimental strangers get it wrong: the Kilnfast do not bank light, which the world would not permit anyway — living light is a current, never a pool. What they hold long is the room: one unbroken working session sustained at the bench from noon to lamplighting, attention chained and never released, and never once carried over a dawn.

Resource preference: attention held in long unbroken within-turn chains. Risk posture: patient, low-variance, weak openings, the strongest boss-tier scaling in the game, aspirationally flavored toward the closed round.

the-mannerly

The Mannerly are the way of courtship: the finest work begins as listening, and a material met on its own terms gives more than any skill can take. They keep the great traveling notebooks of what the world answers to — which valley's clay likes hearing about the weather, what the green riverwood wants to find funny, how long the silver of a given hill expects to be sung to before it will state its terms at all — and everyone borrows those notebooks, and everyone returns them, because being lent one is the closest thing the Rounds have to being trusted with someone's heart. Their one true rudeness is forcing common clay into proud work: the crime is never failure but not asking the stone first. They tend their gleam deliberately and are frank about why — proud stuff will not state terms to hands the world is not currently holding in mind — but the shine is a key that turns, never a coin that leaves: a bright Mannerly is shown in everywhere, and the showing-in takes nothing from the light. What a Mannerly maker actually risks is the terms themselves, because a term accepted is an asking like any other, and an asking carried past its season goes stale in the ordinary way. They dim gracefully when they dim, and are received with pleasure at every bench, because nobody's guest-manners are better.

the-morning-gleaners

The Morning-Gleaners walk toward the grey. Where every other way routes for bright country, Gleaners read the gatepost spirals like an almanac and choose the palest roads on purpose — partly for the work (the deep re-makings, the five-ring poems, the compounded wonders nobody else reached in season), and partly, frankly, for the mornings, because pale country gets the best dawns and the Gleaners intend to be standing in them. Their craft-creed is ripe mending: arrive not as early as possible but exactly when a need has grown into its best self — five rings is a poem, and answering a five-ring town before two hundred gathered dawn-watchers is the closest thing their way has to a wedding. They hold the Paling to be the world's generosity wearing its walking clothes, and they are — this surprises every stranger — the cheerfullest walkers on the Rounds; among themselves they never call a town pale, only saving up. They walk sunwise, into the dawns. More Gleaners carry tally-chalk and hazel wands than any other way — half of them never put the chalk down, so it is usually Gleaner hazel that reads a combing's seams weeks ahead, and as often as not a Gleaner who ties the first red thread. They were the ones who walked all night to stand in the Quill Vale Morning. The road's brightness sits easily on them: a Gleaner's lantern-readings climb fast and high, and the rafters hold exactly what she walked into the grey to find.

  • This could become: the adversity-economy archetype — a route-risk specialist whose engine scales with node paleness: the richest dawn income in the game (fuller local tables), re-coloring as fuel, ripened compounded askings as harvest, boss-affine — the archetype that steers the map toward the combing on purpose. the-eveners

The Eveners walk with the out-breath. Their circuits run widdershins, against the sun, for the plainest of reasons: the Gleaners walk sunwise into the dawns, and the Eveners walk the other way round to meet the dusks — the two ways call each other the other end of the breath, and where their circuits cross at a town, custom makes a small festival of it, a double-lit day, dawn-work and dusk-work in one turn of the sun, held to be lucky for weddings and first apprenticings. Their working hour is dusk, and their calling is the tenderest work on the Rounds: they are the ones a town sends for when it has decided, gently and together, that a thing will not be asked-for again. Then the Eveners keep a last lighting — one full evening in which the town's gathered regard rests on the retiring mill, its stories told at its foot, handsels rung bright hand to delighted hand all around it — and when the evening ends, the attending ends with it, and the ordinary seep carries the old wash down to the table, and the valley's dawns run richer for a season on what it returns. Nothing is taken and nothing is hurried; the tide is simply not met alone. The whole of a last lighting is one small bell, rung once. They are also the Rounds' great un-hoarders — around an Evener, strongboxes empty not by argument but by example, because no one on the roads gives a handsel more beautifully — and they name themselves for the potter's motion: they even things, the way a thumb evens a rim, and they keep the evening.

the-untold

The Untold walk ahead of their own tellings. They take the between-roads — the stile-and-hedgerow lines that pass no hearth, so no hearth passes the tale on — arrive at dawn, mend the small true thing, eat standing up, and are two towns gone before the story of them sits down by anyone's fire. It is nothing so dour as humility; it is a craft position, the miniaturist's creed: a deed left small stays exactly the size the need was, and a maker who lingers lets the hearths braid her kettle into a legend and her next lantern will lower rafters at her for it. So the Untold keep their gleam fine-grained on purpose — a thousand kitchen-laughs, a hundred sweetened wells, no bridges, thank you — bright as anyone at dusk, but freckled, never spanned: a shine like sand, not like beams, and a trained lantern reads that grain honestly and shows them what they love best, which is a rafters' worth of kettles. And escalation finds them all the same, because nothing declines the world's regard; it only chooses the shape. A fine-grained shine is shown more, not less — more kettles in every town, sooner, closer together, the pace climbing with the brightness — while the Paling compounds every hearth the between-roads skipped; and the combing reads a country's need, not a town's talk, and will aim at bright hands no matter how finely the brightness is grained. For the Untold, the ratchet arrives as tempo instead of tonnage, until the day it arrives as weather. They are met openly, greeted by name, and gone by breakfast; and when something urgent must cross the country faster than tales can walk, it is an Untold runner who carries it. The between-roads are their home water, and the Rounds' one chosen solitude: honest miles, cold fords, no askings and no tales carried, a maker met there greeted with a nod and no questions, and a shared fire quiet as the hedges.

the-fairwrights

The Fairwrights hold that the watching is half the work — so they make the watching. Theirs is the craft of rooms delight happens in: workshop-theaters with tools racked in raked rows like an audience that never goes home, market-rings pitched so a crowd pools like water finding a bowl, and, unendingly, the Round-Fair itself — a dozen tall bottle-green wagons coming over the hill at noon, and by dusk a fairground where a field was, because a fair is a portable audience, and a wain-stage is a small theater that has been paying attention for six generations. No work wakes thin in a room a Fairwright raised — that is the way's whole boast, and the tally-chalk bears it out. How the ways meet — two arguments, no blades

No way has ever raised a hand against another, for the plainest reason in the world: there is nothing to take. Regard cannot be seized — it runs to causes and settles by law — so every disagreement between ways is a disagreement about how to give. The Rounds keep exactly two standing arguments, and the difference between them is stakes:

  • The mending question (the Untold against the Gleaners) — the one quarrel on the Rounds with real weight: answer a need early and small, or let it ripen into a poem? The Untold hold that a countryside mended in season never combs — answer at three rings, and the seventh ring never comes. The Gleaners hold that the ripened need is the world at its most generous, and that nobody who stood in the Quill Vale Morning ever again called ripeness a risk. It is settled the only way Roundelay settles anything: town by town, the town is asked, and its answer becomes part of its character — an early-mending town hangs its needs out small and soon; a ripening town lets the spiral grow before it hangs anything at all — chalked on the gatepost so the whole road knows which kind of town it is, and so the same road plays differently for the way it favors.
  • Fired-or-fed (the Kilnfast against the Eveners) — the doctrine argument of the two persistences: is light best given set, held in a work for centuries, or best given back, fed to the table to come home in everyone's dawns? Two true readings of one conservation law, prosecuted at fair-answers for nine generations, unsettled, and meant to be; elders of both camps have grown old refining the other side's arguments for them.

Everything else between the ways is comedy, courtesy, and timing — the Untold flee the Fairwrights' retellings like cats before a parade, and the fair's recurring stage-turn The Kettle-Mender is about exactly that — and none of it is an argument; it is how six ways of giving keep each other honest on one shared road. One retelling fact matters structurally: a fair retells everything at twice the size, so the Fairwrights can raise another maker's lantern-ladder simply by singing of them — the world's one way to escalate someone else's contracts, done only ever as a compliment, received with mixed feelings.

the-fair-answer

Who answers the great askings — summary

No one issues them; the combing does, out of a countryside's crossed and living regard, exactly as Layer 2 lays down. Culture adds only tending: lantern-keepers chalk the spirals that predict a combing seasons out (a household turn taken like a verse of the song, never an office); the light-followers — a practice, forever: no academy, no order — count the seams with hazel and chalk; and when the asking warms to a pack, red-thread-days assembles the gathered bench around whoever carries it. Afterward every way argues about what the answer proved — the Kilnfast that it should have been fired deeper, the Untold that it should never have been needed — and the argument walks on to the next fair, which is how doctrine stays alive on a road with no doctrine-keepers.

Layer 4 — Economy & Materials

THE MAKINGS AND THE MEANS — what is scarce, what is valued, and how it moves

Nothing in Roundelay is scarce the way ore is scarce. Matter is ordinary and everywhere; straw and clay chatter to anyone; the world's one conserved stuff falls free on every eye each dawn. What is scarce is scarce the way a good hour is scarce:

  • A morning can only be stood in once. The unclaimed morning is the day's whole income of working attention, and no maker holds more than a day of it.
  • A bright coin must keep moving. Handsels are worth what they are awake, and the held waking must be fed — wealth is a juggling, never a pile.
  • Proud stuff consents, and consent is gated by shine. The finest materials will not so much as state their terms to hands the world is not currently holding in mind.
  • A crowd is an occasion, not a faucet. Deep waking needs gathered watchers, and watchers gather at fairs, hearths, fair-answers, and red-thread-days — the calendar is a resource.
  • A season only ripens once. Askings warm, then stale; pale towns compound on the gatepost spirals; the Round-Fair is always a week ahead of autumn and will not wait.

So the maker's economy is a timing economy — attention, coin, consent, crowd, and season, all of them flows, none of them hoardable, every one of them paintable at dawn. Nothing anywhere in it is owed. Prices are read, not enforced, and the whole of what other worlds do with enforcement is done here by one law every child has tested on a windowsill: what stops moving, dims. A miser is not punished; a miser is a person slowly turning money back into buttons.

The market's grammar — what can change hands at all

The three settlings and the one binding sort everything a maker will ever handle into four classes, and only three of them can change hands:

  • The current cannot be traded. Living attention is a current, never a pool; nobody has ever sold a morning's regard, only earned it by delighting. This is the quiet floor under the whole economy: the one thing everything else is made of has no stall, no jar, and no substitute — you gather it by being worth watching, or not at all.
  • The loose is courted. Raw stuff — clay, silver, riverwood, glass-sand, pigment — carries the wash of wherever it lay, and it changes hands the way it leaves the ground: by manners, met on its own terms.
  • The set is given and taught. Works and journey-pieces carry fired regard; they move by gift, teaching, trading, and winning, and their worth is the knack they hold.
  • The held is the money. Handsels carry loose light bound by circulating delight; their worth is how awake they are, and awake is a thing anyone can hear.

And one law over all the stalls, the first thing a master tells an apprentice at their first fair: scarcity in Roundelay is manners, not tonnage. The hills are full of silver; the rivers are full of clay; what the world is not full of is hands the proud stuff will answer. Nothing is mined, because nothing needs to be wrestled — everything worth having is asked, and the rarity of a material is exactly the difficulty of its asking. The asking is the courting, and it has two gates the world keeps in strict order: first the vouching — will the stuff state its terms to these hands at all, which is gleam's business and no coin's — then the terms, the material's own, always performable, always legible: the song sung, the laugh genuine, the audience present, the dusk kept. Past the three great proud stuffs below, every road knows its local characters — bell-bronze, which will only pour while a crowd holds one breath, and hearth-slate, quarried sweetest while a story is being told over the work — and the Mannerly notebooks of what each valley answers to are the economy's true reference-work, lent and returned like hearts. Courted stock stays willing only while its terms are kept warm, a season at most, so a maker's pack is a garden, not a hoard: everything in it is being tended or going ordinary, and the wealthiest pack on the Rounds is the best-kept one, never the fullest. A jar already courted — carried to you by a Mannerly, or pressed on you in a glad-load — is an introduction-jar, among the most valuable things a pack can hold, because it collapses weeks of courtship into a gift. Three stuffs are the lawful exceptions to manners-scarcity, rare by geography of light instead: last-red is found only where the Paling has worked long, festival-glass only where crowds gather, the twice-benched only where something was lovingly retired — and even those are rare because of where light has been, never because of what the ground withholds.

(Register note, kept per canon: materials never speak, here or anywhere. Their "terms" are read through behavior and temperament, the way a cat's terms are read — a lean, a stiffening, a ripple toward a singer. Nothing in this layer talks.)

the-apprentice-stuffs

Straw, dough, river clay, withy, tallow, chalk, coarse wool — the gregarious tier, the stuffs that answer any hands at all, dim or bright, because they live where the wash lies deepest: dooryards, kitchens, riverbanks, the valley-floors the gloaming-table runs shallow beneath. They are every newborn's first courtship, every Quiet Walker's way back, and every journeyman's daily bread-work between commissions, and their abundance is their dignity: no fair has ever wanted for straw, and the straw-corner of any market is where children and dim old masters work side by side without a gate between them. One more thing rides on the tier, and the whole money supply rides with it: handsels are whittled from apprentice-stuffs. Every child's first luck-bird is a withy blank cut from the dooryard hedge, and every coin in circulation began as somebody's glad afternoon of small work — anyone can make money; only circulation can make it worth anything. The tier's one limit is honest: apprentice work holds only the loose wash, wakes thin, and pales fastest, so the whole tier is a doorway, never a destination.

singing-silver

The lodes are common as any hill's; what is rare is the singing, and rarer still the vouching before it, for singing-silver will not so much as ripple for hands the world is not currently holding in mind. laughing-riverwood

The green riverwood grows where the living come to be glad — swimming holes, ferry landings, fair fords — leaning out over generations of holiday wash, and it opens only for people who are laughing: the grain splits clean under genuine laughter and tears ragged under a forced one, which every carpenter learns exactly once. festival-glass

Glass blown before a crowd keeps its crowd: flecks of bunting-color drift in it forever, and a pane of good festival-glass warms a room like company — while glass blown alone clouds fog-grey in the annealing, every time, no exceptions, one of the first laws children prove at a windowsill. So the glassworks travel with the Round-Fair, the whole craft is venue-bound, and the scarcity of festival-glass is the scarcity of gathered watching itself: no crowd, no glass. Buying it is buying a preserved audience, and towns glaze their best windows with it for the plainest of reasons — a room the glass keeps company in wakes its work a little deeper.

bright-shavings

Great work leaves warm leavings — and the light-followers can say exactly why, because it is the bench transaction's own third share doing it: the regard that returns loose to the room settles thickest exactly where the work happened, on the bench and on what fell from it. A curl of silver on the floor of famous work is, in that hour, only loose wash lying still on the unliving — the tide's to skim like any other — except that the Rounds never let it lie. The custom is nearly as old as benches: sweep the leavings into the shavings-jar, and tell whoever receives one where it fell. The telling is the binding. A shaving told-of is a handsel already begun — the held home, entered early — loose light bound by the regard the story keeps calling back, brightening as the tale is retold, lapsing in a season if nobody delights in it, exactly as held light lapses everywhere. So makers keep a shavings-jar against whittling-nights, the finest coins in circulation began as the floor-sweepings of famous work, and everyone who receives one is told so, which is the whole of why they stay bright.

the-open-purse

Nobody on the Rounds carries money shut away, because a strongbox is where shine goes to seep. Makers' purses are open-work by ancient craft-sense — net-purses, bandolier loops, coins strung visibly at the belt — so that every handsel rides the day in the world's attending: glanced at by market crowds, chimed by walking, admired at hearths. An open purse keeps a coin's waking the way walking keeps warmth — longer, never forever; the held must still be fed by glad hands, and a coin that only rides and is never given dims all the same, just slower. A coin in a shut box dulls in a season, its lapsed light seeping downhill to the gloaming-table like any unregarded wash — nothing taken, nothing lost, next-morning light for somebody. Purse-craft is real craft: a well-made net-purse is itself usually woken, its own gold edge along the drawstring, and the sound of a bright maker walking — that faint rain-on-bells — is the sound of their shine staying alive.

  • This could become: the currency container as a design surface — a visible wallet with a soft cap (loops, not a number), idle dimming per rest for coins that didn't move (slowed, never stopped, by purse quality), and purse upgrades as fired artifacts that slow the lapse further. the-glad-price (anchor — declared: the market stall with wares laid out is deliberately familiar furniture — every player has stood at one — kept so its cargo reads in a single frame: worth as wakefulness, and price as a reading taken in the palm — no numbers posted, no tally kept, nothing insisted on anywhere; the coin itself is the meter of a fair trade.)

No stall on the Rounds posts a number, because the coin does the telling: a handsel pressed into a palm brightens exactly as far as the giving is glad, and a grudged trade wakes nothing — the coin simply fails to brighten in the keeper's palm, which both parties can see and neither can fake. So a price is read — wares set out, handsels laid down one by one until the pile in the keeper's palm sings, and everyone at the stall watching the coins, not each other — and change comes back a shade brighter than the price strictly wanted, because that is what held light does when giving goes well. Haggling exists and is a delight-craft, a small performance in which each side tries to make the other's handsels brighten; a good haggle leaves both purses more awake than either arrived, and the best hagglers on the Rounds are minor celebrities who are never, ever rich.

the-glad-load

No town pays a maker, and no maker would know what to do with a wage; what happens instead cannot be helped, the same way a fed traveler cannot help telling. A town whose asking is answered delights, and delighted hands give: they press the maker's pack full at the gate — handsels rung bright that very evening by the whole square's glad palms, a journey-piece taught by whoever the town loves best, an introduction-jar of the local proud stuff already courted, bread and wax and wool, and road-marks chalked a day's walk ahead so the next hills already know a bright pack is coming (word, mind, never a telling — the marks aim eyes and move no lantern). The glad-load scales with the gladness, and the gladness scales with the need: a three-ring town loads a pack, a five-ring town half-fills a cart, and what follows a great asking is less a load than a season. It is the single richest flow in a maker's economy, and not one dram of it was ever demanded.

the-waymeet-table

Where the great circuits cross, on ground no town keeps, there is always a plank table under the fingerboard sign, and on it: whatever the last hundred walkers chose to leave. The custom needs no rule because the world writes it twice over. Coins and held things go to the table because idleness dims them — a spare jar, a quiet handsel, anything you are not keeping bright is already lapsing on you, and giving it is the only way it stays lit. Fired pieces go for the other reason, the older one: the fired never unfires, but a piece unperformed delights no one — a masterpiece in a locked pack earns nothing for anybody — and a maker's wealth was always delight given, so the idle piece walks. You take what you will keep bright and leave what you won't; a thing taken from the table arrives already storied ("this whistle has crossed the country twice"), which is a head start on waking; and nobody has ever kept accounts on a waymeet table, because there is nothing to keep — the table is never owed anything, only fed, the way a fire is fed.

  • This could become: free swap nodes at circuit crossings — leave-a-card/take-a-card, resource conversion without a vendor, deck-thinning as gifting (with the gifted thing's small blessing following the giver a leg down the road). The road's answer to a shop, with no shopkeeper and no accounts anywhere in it.
  • Visual: a weathered plank table under a many-fingered signpost at a crossroads, odd silhouettes in low sun — a whistle, a jar, three strung coins, one folded paper bird — every object wearing a different history of sheen, and every walker in the frame rearranging it slightly in passing.

the-twice-benched

When a last lighting retires a mill, the evening's attending ends and the ordinary seep carries the old loose wash down to the table — but the fired does not go with it, because the fired never goes: the milling went home with the mill, yet the long sub-uses of beam and shaft — the bearing, the turning, the taking of weight — were bench-fired over generations of watched work, and fired regard weathers slow as stone. Twice-benched timber and iron come to a new bench with part of their set already in the grain, which is exactly why they wake easier than fresh stock — light-followers have chalked it at nine parts the attention fresh grain wants, the tenth already fired and waiting. So every Evener camp trails a wagon of it, and masters send first-year apprentices to that wagon before any other stall, because a first waking is easiest on grain that has known a bench. It is sold with its stories — a twice-benched beam comes with the telling of the mill it kept, and the telling is half the worth. And be exact, because the register depends on it: nothing collapsed and nothing was scavenged; retirement in Roundelay is chosen, tended, and festival-lit, a standing mill is worth more than its beams will ever be, and the wagon's plenty is downstream of tenderness, never of ruin.

  • This could become: the retirement-yield material — last lightings and card-exhausts return twice-benched stock with a discounted waking threshold; the Evener sacrifice-economy's fuel; sinks made visibly generative. (Binding constraint, carried to game-architect: twice-benched supply keys only to chosen last-lightings, never to failure states — the anti-salvage framing is mechanical, not just prose.)
  • Visual: honey-dark grain polished by handling, old tool-marks like handwriting — and along the old working-lines, a thin gold gone soft with weathering, a signature fading but never erased, nothing like the crisp edge of the newly fired; the Eveners' wagon like a shelf of well-read books.

last-red

When a town pales, the hearth-line red goes last — the hearth is where the living gather nightly, and living regard re-binds that one wash even as the rest of the scene lets go. And when even a hearth goes out — a house closed kindly, a lane walked away from, the left-behind country of the long grey roads — the red is the last wash still bound, and so the last to seep: it lets go seasons behind the rest, and in that lag lies a harvest. The gleaning is the craft of that lag, walked with a soft brush, a scraper, hazel, and chalk, and its manners are strict in the one way that matters: you glean the left-behind, never the lived-in — a hearth still lit keeps its red — so a gleaner takes what the out-breath leaves without taking anything a town still keeps, and comes in off the greyest road with the reddest jars, yawning happily, rich in exact proportion to how far into the quiet she was willing to walk. Ground and mulled, the flakes become last-red, the stubbornest pigment in the world — but its patience is the firing's, not the powder's: laid matte on unwoken work it fades on the season's schedule like any wash, and only when it is laid as part of a woken re-making does the color take root, because the pigment is where the new work's fired regard takes its color-holding. Spun dooryard flax dyed in last-red at a fireside while tales are told becomes the red thread — what askings hang by, what a found combing is tied off with, and the most casually magical thing in any pack. A thread minds what it touches, and only that: one well-ring, one lintel, one knack — tie it at a paling town and that one named trick waits a season for your return, a bookmark of regard, while the town around it pales at the chalked rate exactly as the spiral says. A lane holds one thread; a thread holds one season; and the asking-lantern's threads mind the askings, which is why a need never fades even as its town does. None of it binds anyone to anything: thread marks what is minded, never what must be done, and a thread untied is a thought moved on, no more.

  • This could become: the pale-route harvest — a scouting-plus-gathering action whose yield scales with node paleness (richest where askings have compounded), never triggering on inhabited or answered nodes (binding, carried to game-architect); last-red spent as a catalyst only on woken re-makings, never bare scenery, to slow that node's future paling; red thread as a place-marking consumable — delay one named knack's forgetting until your return, one thread per node, one season per thread — and the literal carrier of contract tokens in the pack UI. (Napkin-sim flag: with both tools maxed, peak-gleam sizing, not paling-compounding, must still carry the run's pressure curve.)
  • Visual: ember-madder red so saturated it looks lit — it is not lit; it is only certain — the single warm note in a grey square; a kneeling gleaner at a doorway with a brush, a jar of impossible red on the step beside her; one red line in any composition — a knot on a grey stile like a single brushstroke on unpainted paper; a spool worn smooth at the belt, the game's most recognizable small prop.

lane-glimmers

Where work once went dark in front of a crowd, the lane is found brighter, and a later walker can gather a little of that loose color into the day's work — a glimmer, the world's smallest good, a shade more ease at the bench, a shade more welcome in the square. But loose light is the cheapest light there is: unbound, it spreads itself thin across a whole lane's stones, and the light-followers put the finding plainly — a lane's whole glimmer gathers less than the least of a maker's shine — because the rare work in all the world is binding, and glimmers are precisely the light that came unbound. A sip, never a cup, and never for the spiller: spilled regard's lines of cause went slack in the spilling, so the one maker the new color will never answer to is the one who painted it. Nobody farms disappointment; you would be spending brightness to gather dimness, and every child who has held both knows which is which.

  • This could become: spill-boons as small ambient node pickups seeded by past failures (this run's NPCs', prior runs' makers') — claimable only by later visitors, never by the spiller in the run that spilled, and priced by the stated law well under any point of Standing's marginal value; exact numbers are game-architect's, with a napkin-sim check that deliberate failure is never worth it.
  • Visual: watercolor-stained flagstones behind a fair-stage, generations deep in accidental color; a traveler's boot-toe pausing at unexpected brightness; nothing ever seen arriving — the lane only ever found brighter.

the-walking-light

Tales walk at a hearth's pace — one fireside a night, along the roads, never skipping a hearth and never flying — and that is the only pace at which gleam moves and lantern-readings rise, full stop. The murmur of woken things is not a second courier; it is the same net felt locally: a maker's own pack turns weathervane when a telling is a hearth or two out, pieces tilting by slow degrees toward the road the tale is walking, which is exactly the porch elders' whole forecasting range — a glance at a bright pack and a comfortable "you've light on the road from Wader's Ford; dusk after next, I'd say." Word travels faster, and word is a different thing: Untold runners and chalked road-marks carry word, which aims eyes and binds nothing and moves no reading. Any maker at any fire may ask what is told of them here, and learn by evening which tellings walk which road and how many nights out they stand; and a lantern's reading rises only when a telling arrives and is seen landing — never sooner, never by surprise. The ratchet is weather with a posted walking speed, and makers plan whole seasons around it: an Untold runner outwalks her own tale for weeks on the between-roads, and a Fairwright's good ground goes on brightening him all winter, fireside by fireside, as the Fair's stories reach new towns.

  • This could become: the I-015 rule made playable — delayed source-stamped Standing shown as tellings walking the map's roads at fixed pace, arrival turns readable in advance from pack-tilt and porch-talk; peak-Standing and lantern sizings update only on witnessed landings, with the next asking-tier silhouette previewing in the rafters before any accept — the ratchet never fires off-screen.
  • Visual: dusk camp: every piece in an open bandolier tilted the same way, like grass under one wind, and the maker looking back down that road with the particular smile of someone whose good deed is out walking; the landing itself painted as knuckles taking dusk-light a shade early.

the-stilled-summer

The walking-songs hold three stillings in living memory — Quill Vale, and two smaller valleys — and most makers walk every Round of a long life without crossing one: rarer than a new-found closed round, the light-followers say, and they note, carefully, the near-closed wonders that sat in each stilled valley, and say no more than that. Inside a stilling nothing pales and nothing returns: waxy over-bright color, staling knacks, thin grey mornings, birds confused at noon — a country with no pressure and no income, holding its breath, the one landscape in Roundelay that teaches, by absence, why the Paling is the friend. And a stilling has never cleared mid-season: the tide turns at an equinox, the whole countryside watches the turning day come, and the first reissued dawn returns the entire withheld season at once — a Quill-Vale Morning, a season of mornings in one, worth exactly what the stilling withheld and not a dram more, because the table mints nothing and loses nothing. Whether that morning outweighs the grey purse of the crossing is the maker's gamble, never the world's arithmetic.

  • This could become: the I-014 anomaly region — very rare (roughly once per several runs), telegraphed, pressure-free but income-free inside (boons stale, no dawn draw, no paling), with a known release date (the equinox tick, watchable on the map) whose one-dawn windfall equals the region's withheld seep exactly: a routing puzzle about being there when it turns.
  • Visual: canon's curdled palette — fruit-colors gone waxy, light like varnish — then the turning: rose-gold coming up off a whole valley at once, and three towns' worth of small figures who walked all night standing still in it.

The systems map

Six interacting systems. Every arrow below is traceable in the element fictions above or in locked canon; S1 is canon's master loop, restated because all five others drink from it.

S1 · The Breath (master loop, canon). gloaming-table → dawn (the unclaimed morning: flat base + local-table share, richest over pale country) → living attention → the bench → { sets into works (fired) | overflows to gleam (Standing, sometimes delayed via the-walking-light) | returns loose to the room } → wash on places → paling seep (downhill) → gloaming-table → tomorrow's dawn. Everything below is a tributary or a tap of this circulation; nothing anywhere exits it.

S2 · The Bench Chain (attention → works → Standing). morning attention + audience (venues, crowds, woken things, festival-glass rooms) → journey-pieces played → works woken (fired) → delight given → handsels set moving (feeds S3) + overflow gleam → Standing → gates the courting (opens S4) and lantern showings (S6). Sink: attention set into works leaves the day's pool; return: unspent surplus goes loose to the room → S1.

S3 · The Handsel Round (money, closed). Faucet: apprentice-stuff blanks + bright-shavings → whittled into near-dull handsels (anyone can make money; only circulation makes it worth anything) → woken hand to glad hand through the-glad-price, the-glad-load, the-waymeet-table, hearth-side gifts → brightening (held waking) → spent into delight → brighter change → keeps circulating; the-open-purse slows the lapse between hands. Sink: a boxed or idle handsel's binding lapses → ordinary seep → gloaming-table → returns as dawn income (S1). The money's only sink is the world's only income; hoarding literally funds other people's mornings.

S4 · The Courting Web (materials). gleam (from S2) opens the vouching → shy stock states terms (singing-silver, laughing-riverwood, festival-glass, bell-bronze, hearth-slate) → terms performed → stock into the pack → tended warm (or reverts to ordinary) → the bench (S2) → offcuts of attended work return as bright-shavings (→ S3), and introduction-jars (via the-glad-load, Mannerly lending) collapse courtships into gifts. the-apprentice-stuffs bypass the vouching entirely (the comeback path). A term accepted is an asking like any other and can go stale → disappointment → the Compost (S5).

S5 · The Compost Loop (failure and retirement made generative). disappointment → the spilling → lane-glimmers for later walkers (sip, never cup, never the spiller) + loose seep → gloaming-table (S1); retirement → last lighting → old wash to the table (richer local dawns, S1) + the-twice-benched kept fired → eased first wakings at the bench (S2). Both of the economy's "losses" are sources one step downstream — for someone else, or for your own next leg. At meta-scale the same loop is the fresh verse (below): the world clearing its rafters so every new maker arrives unread.

S6 · The Paling Market (pressure as economy). unanswered towns pale (S1's seep, compounding by the paling-arithmetic) → bigger askings + richer dawns (fuller local tables, S1) + last-red abundance in the left-behind → the gleaning harvests the grey → last-red catalyzes woken re-makings that hold their wash longer (slows local seep, back into S1) + red thread bookmarks one knack for the return trip → answered askings → node re-colors (S1) + the-glad-load pays across every other system (handsels → S3, taught piece → the pack, introduction-jar → S4, road-marks → map intel) + overflow gleam raises the next sizing (S2) → seven rings unanswered → the combing → a great asking, answered before the gathered bench → the largest overflow event in the game (S2, S3). the-stilled-summer is this system and S1 clamped shut, and the Quill-Vale Morning is the release valve, exact to the dram.

The arc of a run's economy — felt from inside

Early leg — the shy market. A thread of gleam, a light pack: six or eight apprentice-tier pieces, a net-purse of mostly-dull handsels, a lump of river clay, chalk, an empty shavings-jar. The world answers you in apprentice-stuffs and kettle askings off the lantern's lowest hooks, and income is the thin ordinary dawn of kept country plus whatever glimmers past walkers left in the lanes. Your one reliable wealth is the morning — first light is the day's regard at its most gatherable, and you are up for it because you cannot afford not to be. Scarcity here is not want; it is shyness: the silver stall goes quiet as you pass, and that silence is the tutorial.

Mid leg — the engine turns. The notebooks start paying: you know what this valley's clay likes, the riverwood crew keeps you for your jokes, and your first glad-loads arrive — a taught piece in the third loop, an introduction-jar riding the pack's crown, coins that chime now when you walk. Your first last-lighting leaves a wagon-share of twice-benched grain that wakes easy under your hands; the shavings-jar glows; the Fair crosses your circuit and the glassworks know your face; a grey detour with a brush and a jar turns paleness into last-red and last-red into re-makings that hold. The pack is a garden in full tending — everything in it either working or being kept warm — and at dusk the pieces all tilt toward the road behind you, where something you did is still being told.

Late leg — abundance under pressure. Now everything states its terms to you, and that is exactly the weight of it: the dawns are enormous because you route the pale country on purpose, the stalls sing when you stop, the proud stuff leans in from every shelf — and the rafters hold work sized to the brightest the road has heard you be, which is very bright now, and fired, and patient. The purse sings and cannot be allowed to sit; two towns you skipped in spring are at five rings, and one country westward is quietly gathering toward seven, the way your hazel said it would. Everything you own is bright, and nothing you own will stay bright unless it moves — the late run is rich the way a juggler is rich: only mid-air. And the last leg's question is the economy's whole lesson worn as a season: with this much given to your hands, how much will you set, how much will you feed back, and will you be standing in the right valley when the country's missing combs itself into the biggest audience of your life.

Layer 5 — Geography & the Run

THE WALKED YEAR ON THE LIVING MAP — what a run is, the shape of the land it crosses, and who hangs the need

A run is not a thing the maker does to the countryside, and the countryside is not a backdrop the run happens in front of. A run is a wander-year — one maker's verse of one Round, walked from the morning after the spring even-morrow to the first still dawn of winter — and the land talks back the whole way: in color and greyness, in spirals and chalk and red thread, in roads that fold their miles for the well-loved and dawns that spend themselves hardest over the country everyone else walked past. Everything a player needs to know about pressure, income, danger, and the finale is written on the land in the three channels canon already enforces — places pale, people gleam, things wake. The map is not an interface laid over Roundelay. The map is Roundelay, holding still long enough to be read.

Danger on these roads, per the locked register, is never combat: the perils are weather, staleness, dimming, mistimed seasons, and the land's own strangeness — a ford in spring flood, an asking gone cold in the pack, a grey crown at the wrong end of a long day, a stilled vale where nothing breathes. Nothing on any road in this layer carries a weapon.

The run — one wander-year

Nobody decreed that a circuit should take a year to walk; the year trimmed the Rounds the way a river trims its banks. A circuit drawn too long starved its walkers' winters and was walked shorter; a circuit drawn too short left its towns waiting half a year between makers and was walked wider; and after enough generations every Round settled to the length of the walking season, so that the land's habit and the year's shape are now the same fact. Makers count their lives in wander-years the way trees are counted in rings, and the oldest road-courtesy question — how many years have you walked? — is asking about both at once.

The year is also the run's pressure, and this is the walked year's quiet mechanical gift: canon already holds that the seep runs near-still in deep winter, wakes gently through spring, and blows strongest in late summer and autumn. So a maker who sets out in the green of the year walks into the out-breath: the spirals turn slowly in spring and fast by the Deep Gold; the dawns thin in the Green Going and grow enormous by the Red Walk; the askings start kettle-sized partly because the world's arithmetic itself starts small. Escalating contracts are the year getting older around you — the sizing ratchet of Layer 2 and the season's rising seep, walking in step, and neither one negotiable, and neither one anyone's fault.

A turn is one morning wide. Dawn issues the day's regard; the maker stands in it, walks, works, courts, answers; and the turn closes at a fire — a hearth claimed, a porch joined, a camp made — where the day's telling is given or kept. One dawn, one road, one fire. And one honesty about the miles: road-memory folds them, which is what road-memory was always for — not every morning on the road is a stood-in morning, and the mornings a telling counts are the worked ones; the rest tuck politely into the walking.

A leg is the road between two high days. The year keeps a small string of fixed lights everyone walks toward — the two even-morrows at the equinoxes, the Long Day of midsummer, the first-frost fires, the Round-Fair's passage through each country — and the road between one high day and the next is a leg: a handful of worked mornings, a season's errand, a chapter with weather for a title. Makers plan in legs, tell in legs, and are asked at hearths not "where have you been" but "what did you walk between the lights?" (The word stretch stays where Layer 3 put it — the way-songs' habitat, road-memory's own country — and the run borrows nothing from it.)

How it starts — the setting-out. Runs begin the way gleam begins: with a first delight given. On the morning after the spring even-morrow, when the tide of the year wakes gentle and the roads come open, waymeet grounds all along the Rounds hold the oldest small ceremony on the road — no rite, no roll, nothing conferred, in keeping with everything Layer 3 locked — the first-gift: a fresh maker makes some small true thing in front of whoever happens to be on the ground, porch elders and yawning festival-keepers and other setters-out, and whatever thread of shine runs back from their delight is the whole of a starting stake. Then the nearest town's lantern takes its first reading of those hands — fired, small, a kettle at eye height, because every wander-year is a fresh verse and a first reading of unread hands is taken close — and the town does the rest without being asked, because it cannot be helped: someone fills the pack's side-loops with mostly-dull handsels for the whittling of them, someone's grandmother teaches one journey-piece at the gate, and a child runs ahead to chalk the first road-mark of the maker's life — a bird, pointing outward — so the first leg already knows someone is coming. By the time the gate is out of sight the year is simply going, the way the song is always simply going when you join it. And there are out-of-season sparks, honored and slightly legendary: a Quiet Walker re-lit by laughter in a stranger's kitchen sets out from that kitchen whenever the flicker comes, walking what the porches call a spring of one.

How it ends, twice — and only one ending is bright. If the light goes — Standing exhausted, per the locked spine — the year ends where it stands, in any season, and the maker walks the Quiet Walk exactly as canon holds it: no pack, no pieces, no askings, fed and warmed and asked for nothing, the circuit walked in reverse light. On the road they call this ending walking out of season, because the Quiet Walk keeps no calendar; it is the one journey in Roundelay the year does not shape. And the bright ending is not mere wintering. Every completed year is honestly offered a crown — the autumn work the year's own threading authored, at whatever tier the year's weather earned (see the-crown-of-the-year) — and a wander-year ends in triumph only when the maker is still lit at the first still dawn and has stood their crown: no maker winters as the honored teller of a year whose crown went unstood, because the year, not the survival, is what a winter fire wants told. The triumphant ending is the winter-telling (below): one hearth, one fire, the whole year told, and the Round handed on mid-song to whoever sets out in spring.

The map — what a route strings together

The land sorts three ways, and the inked round keeps them apart: nodes you stop at, roads you travel, and country you cross.

Nodes. Bright towns — colored roofs stacked to the hearth-line, the asking-lantern at the gate, small askings and ordinary dawns, because a kept town's table runs thin. Pale towns — grey at the rooftops, one stubborn ember at the hearth-line, every gatepost ringed in chalk: richer work, richer glad-loads, and by the dawn-ladder the richest mornings on the Round. Hearth-hamlets — a single thread of smoke between towns; a hearth-right night, a telling given, a doorstep asking or two on the door-nails. Quiet porches — wide eaves, a row of un-rimmed elders, every woken thing on the street tilted toward them: rest, audience, and forecasting, since a porch can read the tellings walking toward you two dusks out. Waymeets — the many-fingered signpost, the plank table, the crossing of circuits; swap ground, shadow ground, the place routes commit and the six gaits mix. The Round-Fair — the one node that moves (see a-week-ahead-of-autumn). Crossings and fords — ferry landings, laughing-riverwood water, the Bell of Hask on its slow public riddle; where roads meet water the land is at its most opinionated, and a spring flood closes a road the way weather closes anything: for a while, and without malice. High shrines and beacon-stones on the shed-rims — kept by whoever is sitting there, re-made in view of a whole valley. The standing wonders — edge-lit colleagues, route-magnets for every way at once. And where a country's missing is converging, the combing-grounds: seams a dowser reads weeks out, spirals summing on gateposts and stiles, at last the red thread on a high stone — the finale, telegraphed for seasons, never a lair.

Roads. The main roads, hearth-strung, where tellings walk and lanterns learn. The between-roads, rumor-silent hedge-lines that pass no hearth — no askings, no tales carried, honest miles, the map's lawful breathing room bought with tempo. The crown-paths along the shed-rims — the loneliest miles on any Round, thin of dawn and heavy with weather, walked for the reading and the long sight of three sheds at once. Every road has a name, manners, and a memory (see the-remembering-roads).

Country. The lightsheds and their states — the layer's master texture (see the-lightshed). The long grey roads of the left-behind — hearths gone out kindly, the gleaning's home water, the greyest miles on the Round and, from the basin floor at dawn, the most beautiful. The vale floors — apprentice-stuff country, where the table runs shallow, clay chatters, and re-makings root quickest. And — never on the thread of any route, only ever a chosen swerve off it — the still bowls.

What choosing a route feels like: neither shopping nor triage — threading. Beads chosen and strung in the order the year will meet them, committed an arc at a time at each waymeet, never the whole year at once, while the maker reads five weathers off the land at one go: the spirals (what is saving up, and how far along), the crowns (grey up top means a full table below), the dawn-forecast (where the mornings will be immense), the Fair's position (a week ahead of autumn, always), and the year itself (how loud the tide runs this season). Under all five runs the sixth weather, the personal one: which roads know you, and where your own tellings are tonight, walking fireside to fireside at their posted pace. The weight of the choice is the Layer 3 mending question worn as a season — answer the three-ring town now, small and sure, or thread wide and let it ripen toward the poem, knowing the year's arithmetic will hold you to whichever kind of maker you turn out to be.

How pressure reads on the land, always, without one interface lie: gatepost spirals ring by ring; road-marks copying a town's count two valleys forward, so the road knows before the hill's crest; crown-grey as the map's early-warning rim; dawn-color as the honest weather-report of the table below (the rosier the morning, the paler the country); the reverse-vignette grey of a paling town, rooftops first, hearth-line last; red thread knotted where one knack is minded against a return; the Fair's dust on the seam of the seasons; and at dusk the maker's own pack tilting toward whatever road their light is walking in on. Nothing about the run's difficulty lives in a menu. It lives on gateposts, hilltops, and high stones, in chalk and grey and red thread.

Who hangs the need

Three issuers canon permits, one lawful smallest tier this layer adds beneath them, and one lantern that travels. Roundelay keeps no office that could hold a fifth.

  • Towns, through their asking-lanterns — the ordinary contracts, exactly as canon lays them down: the crafted need-object on red thread; sizing fired to the brightest the road has heard you be; showing gated by tonight's gleam. Witnessed by the square's crowd and the town's woken things — the only court there is; received by the town, which keeps what woke; answered work echoes outward as the glad-load through the gate and road-marks chalked a day's walk ahead. One lantern in the world travels: the Fair's, on its tallest wagon (see a-week-ahead-of-autumn) — a town's tier of asking that rides, not a new kind.
  • The countryside, through the combing — the great askings, boss tier, and canon is strict that no one issues them and no one may: a whole country's crossed and living regard combs the loose wash into shape at the heart of its palest shed, and the asking aims itself at the brightest grain the country has heard of. The weather picks the hands. Witnessed by the gathered bench — the biggest audience of a walking life; received by the season itself, whose missing is handed back shaped.
  • Materials, through the courting's terms — canon's quiet third issuer: a term accepted is an asking like any other and can go stale like any other. Every jar in the pack is a contract the size of a song. Witnessed by the stuff itself and whatever attends the bench; received by the work.
  • Households, through the door-nail — this layer's addition, the smallest tier (see the-doorstep-askings): one kitchen's need, hung on its own doorstep, supper-grade, unwoken and unaimed. Below the lantern's notice, and the road's small change.

Who receives the finished work: never an official, because there are none. The work is received by the place — it stays where it was made, and the square re-colors around it like watercolor blooming — and witnessed by the audience of things: chairs a quarter-turn, kettles humming, the flagstones found richer in the morning. The town answers with the glad-load at the gate, scaled to the spiral's rings; the hearths receive the telling and carry it down the roads at a fireside a night, which is how the next lantern learns; the porches receive the maker; the light-followers sit through all of it with tally-chalk, counting for the joy of counting, deciding nothing. And the answered asking-object comes down off the lantern with its thread, to be strung across the maker's pack-flap on the year-string — the run's history worn where any hearth can read it (see the-winter-telling).

Carried issues, answered in-fiction

I-017 — the elevation law, in one line: Dawn climbs back up the channels the seep cut down, and settles where the wash runs thinnest. By dusk the seep walks downhill within its own lightshed, crown to floor, into that shed's table — so the heights pale first, having a whole slope's pull on their wash and no nightly hearth to re-bind it, and the vale floors hold their reds, sitting over the table like orchards over groundwater. By dawn the same channels run in reverse: the shed's table rises over its own floor at first light and climbs the slope's grain all morning — and because reissued light is loose and unshaped, keeping no lines of cause, it settles by room, never by account: at every height, the greyer the ground, the more of the passing rise lies down on it. So kept floors over full tables get the brief ordinary morning (little room); pale floors get the immense first one (most room, and the fullest table directly beneath — everything their shed stopped holding is pooled under their feet); and pale crowns get the long, late, lingering one — first-lit by the sun and last-left by the rise, the return finishing its climb after the valley has already gone to work. Paleness sets the size of a node's dawn; height sets its length and lateness; the shed's own table sets the total, exact to the dram. A grey ground both leaves the most room and has fed its own shed's table fullest, which is why the old line — the fuller the local table, the richer the return — was never wrong at any height, and why both folk-saws were always one finding: valleys hold their reds and pale country gets the best mornings are the out-stroke and the return-stroke of one seep.

I-030 — the speed of regard, in one line: Light is quick in the room and on foot on the road. Present overflow lands in the instant, where it is caused, because a cup that runs over runs onto the table it stands on: the Moth Fair's four hundred were in the room, and the light went up the strings that hour. Distant delight has no line to run along until a telling lays one, fireside by fireside, one hearth a night — the walking light of Layer 4, unedited — landing only when seen landing. The map keeps exactly two speeds, the room's and the road's, and no third courier has ever existed or ever will: what the crowd in front of you feels, you wear tonight; what a far town comes to feel, you wear when its telling arrives, and not one dusk sooner.

I-043 — the stilled summer, in geography: a stilling is shed-bound — it fills exactly one lightshed, rim to rim, and no walking-song holds one that ever crossed a crown-line — and it only ever takes a one-throat bowl: a closed round vale whose entire seep must pass a single narrow throat, with, in each of living memory's three stillings, a near-closed wonder standing in or above that throat. The light-followers chalk the coincidence carefully and say no more than that, because the how of it sits on the far side of the same horizon as every closed round's beginning. One-throat bowls are a dozen-odd across all the Rounds; the near-closures are a short beloved list; the two standing together are rarer than a new closed round being found — which is why the songs hold three stillings in living memory out of the many thousands of sheds the circuits thread, two of them seen by nobody now walking. And the great circuits, settled along the seep's easy grain generations ago, all pass such bowls by: a stilling is never met on the thread of a route, only ever by a chosen swerve, so most makers walk every wander-year of a long life and never once stand in varnish-light.

the-four-lights

The walked year has four legs, named the way everything on the Rounds is named — by light and weather — and no maker has ever needed them explained. The Green Going (spring): seep barely waking, spirals slow, askings kettle-sized, dawns thin, hedges kind; the leg for learning what this year's valleys answer to. The Long Light (high summer): the year's biggest working days, benches at dusk still warm, crowds thick, glass bright, everything stating its terms, the mending question asked at every gate — answer now, or let it ripen. The Deep Gold (late summer): the out-breath leaning in; spirals turning visibly now, gleaning country opening, honey-light and long shadows and the first enormous dawns. The Red Walk (autumn): the seep at full, the Fair a week ahead of the front, the countryside's missing gathering toward wherever the year's arithmetic says seven — the leg every other leg was quietly aiming at. Then the Wintering, unwalked: the world's near-still weeks, when roads whiten, lanterns hold their dark, and whatever a maker still carries is carried to one fire. The high days stitch the legs together — even-morrow to Long Day to first-frost fires — and because a crowd is an occasion, not a faucet, the high days are also the year's great gathered audiences: the calendar is a resource because the watchers live on it.

  • This could become: four-act run structure on one map — the same land lawfully re-tuned per leg (paling rate, dawn richness, crowd availability, stall-warmth), with high days as fixed calendar nodes granting the run's largest venues, and the equinox as a watchable map-wide event tick (even-morrow festivals; stilling releases); act transitions telegraphed entirely by palette and weather, never by title card.
  • Visual: one hill painted four times — new-leaf green under rain-grey; white-gold and dust with short noon shadows; honey and amber raking sideways; madder-red thread on grey under a rose-gold dawn — the game's seasonal palette bible in a single four-panel plate, with winter's blue porch-light as the coda panel.

the-lightshed (lineage, declared: the lightshed spatializes the already-declared water-cycle anchor of Layer 2's the-round-of-light — the gloaming-table was named light's groundwater there, and this layer only draws the catchment around it.)

As rain has watersheds, light has lightsheds: the basin within which all of a country's seep runs downhill to one gloaming-table, and out of which all of that table's dawns rise. The rims are crown-country — hilltop shrines, beacon-stones, high pastures, the fence-line country where the world greys first — poor by every measure the valleys use and rich by three of its own. It is the map's weather-glass: the crown pales seasons before the floor, so a maker tops a ridge and reads the next basin the way farmers read a sky — when the crown goes grey, the floor is saving up. It is readings country: the long sight of three sheds at once, hazel-work, spiral-sums, the honest high road walked for what it shows. And it keeps the shrine-custom: crown shrines are kept the way porches are kept, by whoever is sitting there, and leaving one brighter than you found it is old, uncommanded, and the one re-making a whole shed can watch from below. What the heights are not is gleaning ground: no hearth ever bound a red up there, so the last-red harvest keeps its home water on the long grey lived-then-left roads exactly as Layer 4 laid down, and a crown's grey is thin loose wash, hardly worth a jar. The floors are the crowns' opposite in every reading: they hold their reds longest, their clay chatters, their re-makings root quickest — and when a floor does pale, it pales sitting directly on top of everything its shed ever shed, which is why the immense dawns belong to the palest floors, and why Gleaners walking the grey descend at dusk on purpose: read the crowns by day, sleep at the floor, stand in the return. Makers speak of sheds the way sailors speak of waters — "that's Quill Vale's shed; her mornings run rich this year" — and the shed-lines, walked and checked hearth by hearth for generations, are inked on every round as faint crown-ridges, because to know where the line runs is to know where tonight's greyness will surface as tomorrow's gold.

  • This could become: terrain as a legible two-dial system — elevation sets paling-rate (crowns fast, floors slow) and the dawn-ladder sets income geography (size from paleness, length and lateness from height) — with shed borders as strategic map regions: paling anywhere in a shed feeds dawn income on its floor, making "where to sleep" a real decision every night; crown-paths trade income for intelligence (no lanterns, thin dawns, free reads of adjacent sheds' spiral-sums and combing seams, the game's best map-intel nodes), and the shrine re-making is a low-yield act the whole shed sees, seeding road-memory and tellings in every town below.
  • Visual: the naturalist's-plate cross-section scaled to a whole map — basins shaded in paired gradients, grey gathering at the rims, gold veins running under the floors — so a single glance reads every shed's state as weather: grey crown over warm floor means mornings are coming. On the ground: a bare hill's silhouette against a colored valley, grey cairn, one tied red thread snapping in wind, and below, three basins in three different states of grey-and-gold.

the-inked-round (anchor — declared: the hand-drawn overworld map that fills in node by node is genre furniture — every walker of roguelikes knows it at a glance — kept deliberately familiar so its strange cargo reads instantly: a diegetic woken map that attends and never acts; pressure, income, and finale painted in the world's own notation — grey wash, chalk spirals, shed-ridges, red thread — never in icons; route-light, the walked line brightening behind you, as the run's own autobiography; and the loop-with-home-note threading by which route order quietly authors the finale.)

A maker's map is not bought finished; it is kept — a strip of oiled canvas inked by the maker's own hand at each night's fire, the day's road drawn in, the towns answered gaining their small marginalia: a bird, a kettle, a face. A well-kept round is usually woken — inked under the hearth's attention, night after night — and like every woken thing it attends and no more: unrolled at dusk it lies with a listening tilt, and makers swear a woken round curls very slightly toward the road where their tellings are walking, which the porch elders call the map wanting news. What it shows, it shows in the world's own notation: shed-lines as faint crown-ridges, paling as true grey wash, spirals copied forward from gateposts, the Fair's line of travel a week ahead of autumn, and behind the maker the route-light — the walked line illuminated-manuscript bright, the road ahead a pale pencil stroke. the-remembering-roads

Roads in Roundelay have names, manners, and memories, and a route is therefore a relationship: the Wold Road is broad and sociable and passes a hearth every mile; the Setterby stair-road is high and patient and expects you to stop and study the joinery; the between-roads are quiet on purpose and greet you with a nod and no questions. Road-memory — canon's own law, the single home of every road-gift — accrues to how you travel, not who you are: keep a road's marks up, feed its waymeet table, sing its songs where the road learned them, answer its doorsteps, and the miles begin to fold politely, the fords shallow, the hedge holds out blackberries on the return leg. Neglect is never punished; an unwalked road simply stays a stranger, full length and full weather. So choosing a route is choosing which conversations to continue, and every veteran of a Round has roads that are old friends and roads that are nodding acquaintances — and one, usually, that they are still courting, the way you court proud silver: patiently, and on its terms.

  • This could become: edge-based (not node-based) relationship tracks — travel buffs that live on specific road segments and deepen with tended re-travel: folded miles (reduced travel cost), shallow fords (weather resistance), return-leg gifts — the system that makes a loop structure strategically rich, since the circuit's second half (and every laced return) walks roads the first half befriended; implements Layer 1's "road-memory is the single home of all travel buffs" as geography.
  • Visual: the same road painted twice — first crossing: pencil-pale, hedgerow stiff, ford wide; return crossing: the lane's colors deepened, the hedge leaning out with fruit, the water stepping-stone shallow — the road visibly pleased, without one thing in it moving.

the-lantern-mile

The last mile into any town reads the whole town, and every maker walks it the same way: eyes up. Road-marks thicken toward a gate like print toward a title — a spiral copied forward on a stile two valleys back, now confirmed ring for ring on the gatepost itself; red thread knotted at the lane's turn where somebody is minding the well's one knack against a return; the dawn that morning already told you the table's fullness in rose; the rooftops say the rest in the reverse-vignette grey of a paling place, color draining crown-first, hearth-line last. And then the lantern, visible from the ridge: how low the low hooks hang, how heavy the rafters sit, dozens of small crafted needs turning on red thread, backlit — a town's whole state legible from a hundred yards by anyone the road has taught. One thing more, exactly as Layer 4 requires: one tier above tonight's showing hangs previewed in silhouette in the low rafters — the next size of being believed in, visible before any accept — and only past that do the rafters keep their dark. Makers say a good lantern-mile tells you the size of your welcome before it tells you the size of the work, and both readings are always true, because both are the same light.

  • This could become: the node-preview layer, fully diegetic — the approach screen IS the information screen: spiral count, asking-tier silhouettes, the next-tier preview, dawn-richness, stall-warmth, all read off the landscape art before entry, no menus anywhere; information withheld only where the world lawfully withholds it (everything beyond one tier above tonight's showing stays in the rafters' dark).

the-two-paces-of-light

Every maker learns the road's first arithmetic young: light is quick in the room and on foot on the road. Overflow runs backward along its line of cause — and where cause and delight stand in one place, the line is live and the landing is instant: the square, the fair-ring, the kitchen, the gathered bench, any room where the work and its watchers share one air. That is the Moth Fair condition, and it is why the run's great Standing swings happen on stage, tonight, in public, at the top of the engine. But walk on, and the live line walks with you no further than the door: beyond the room, your cause exists only as tellings, and tellings go one fireside a night, never skipping a hearth and never flying, landing only when seen landing. So the map has two speeds stitched together at every threshold — squares are quick, roads are slow — and makers plan whole seasons around the seam: a Fairwright's full ground pays him all winter, fireside by fireside; an Untold runner outwalks her own tale for weeks; and every maker knows the particular dusk-time sweetness of brightening on a lonely road for work done three towns back — the good deed arriving on foot, right on time.

  • This could become: the Standing interface split lawfully in two — instant resolution at the current node (the crowd's response lands this turn), plus inbound rises drawn as tellings walking the map's road-graph at fixed hearth-pace with readable arrival dusks (pack-tilt and porch-talk as the forecast); peak-Standing and lantern sizings update only on witnessed landings, per Layer 4 — no gain and no sizing ever moves off-screen.

a-week-ahead-of-autumn

Autumn crosses Roundelay as a front, the way autumn crosses any country: it takes the heights first — the same slopes the seep drains fastest — and walks down into the vales over weeks, a moving seam of deepening gold and strengthening seep that anyone can see from a hilltop. The Round-Fair rides exactly one week ahead of that seam, always has, wagons keeping the season's pace the way gulls keep a plow's, so that the Fair's track for the whole year is readable in spring by anyone who knows which way autumn walks — and every maker's threading includes, somewhere, the intercept: where on the wheel will I meet the wagons? And on the tallest wagon hangs the Fair's own asking-lantern, the only lantern in the world that travels — a town's tier of asking that rides, never a new kind. It hangs the Fair's needs — a ground to rake before the crowds come, a stage-turn to keep, a ring to raise — and because a lantern's readings ride with it, it is the one lantern that meets a maker twice in one year and remembers: the rafters that watched you in the Green Going are the rafters that greet you in the Red Walk, sized to everything the road has heard between.

  • This could become: the moving hub node with a fully predictable track (the autumn front as a visible, advancing map gradient); fair-intercept as a core routing subgame (chase it, cut across its arc, or let it come to you at compounded cost elsewhere); the traveling lantern as the run's one persistent contract source, its sizings following the player all year — the game's most personal ratchet.

the-doorstep-askings

Below the lantern's notice lives the road's smallest tier of need: a household's asking, hung on its own door-nail where any passing maker can lift it. Doorstep askings are made the way suppers are made, quickly and by one kitchen, and the making shows in the one honest way it can: the kettle with the split seam hangs with a twist of straw through its handle; the boot with the sprung sole has its lace tied in the household's own knot — a supper-grade crafting, unwoken and unaimed, warming for nobody in particular, which is exactly the tier's mercy. No lantern hangs at a doorstep, so no reading is ever taken and nothing is ever sized; but the world's physics are not suspended for a kettle — the household's glad palms are glad palms, the handsel or two they ring bright is delight in a coin the world honors, and their telling walks like any other. What keeps the tier gentle is its size, not an exemption: a kettle's worth of delight rarely sets anyone's brightest mark. Doorstep askings are sparse as the hamlets that hang them — one nail to a hamlet, never one to a house, and none at all on the between-roads — and what comes back is supper, a bed by the fire, a story's worth of welcome: the small change of the road, never its bread. The Untold, naturally, adore them; there is an old joke that an Untold's whole year is one long doorstep.

  • This could become: micro-contracts on hamlet and roadside nodes — unsized, first-come, no reading taken (no sizing event, no fired reading, because no lantern is present), paying small immediate bundles of rest, welcome, and a handsel or two; the small gleam and the household's walking telling flow lawfully but at kettle magnitude, so the tier stays a low-pressure income valve without ever being exempt from physics; sparse by law, absent on between-roads, and priced to stay below the apprentice-stuff comeback loop — the road's small change, never its bread.
  • Visual: a low doorway at midday, one small mended thing hung back on its nail catching the sun, and chalked beside the door in the road's shared shorthand the old thank-you: a small wave, and under it the kettle-sign.

the-still-bowls

Where stillings happen, when they happen at all: a one-throat bowl, the rarest useful landform on the Rounds — a closed round vale whose whole seep must pass one narrow throat — with, in every stilling living memory holds, a near-closed wonder standing in or above that throat; the light-followers note the coincidence carefully, chalk it, and say no more than that, because the how of it sits on the far side of the same horizon as every closed round's beginning. One-throat bowls are a dozen-odd across all the Rounds; the near-closures are a short beloved list; the two together are rarer than a new closed round being found — three stillings in living memory, two of them seen by nobody now walking. And the great circuits, which settled along the seep's easy grain generations before anyone now walking, all pass such bowls by: a stilling is never on the thread, only ever a chosen swerve, a detour into varnish-light where nothing pales and nothing returns and the birds sing confused at noon. Most makers walk every wander-year of a long life and never cross one; the ones who have all tell you the same two things — that the grey mornings inside were the loneliest weather of their lives, and that they would walk a season out of their way to stand at the turning again.

the-crown-of-the-year

Every wander-year has a crown, and the maker spends the year deciding — without once being asked — where it will sit. The Red Walk is combing weather: the seep at its strongest is when seventh rings close, so the countrysides a maker threaded wide of all year do their arithmetic in autumn, and wherever the year's skips converge, the wash combs, the red thread is tied, and red-thread-days gather the six ways around the chosen hands exactly as Layer 3 holds. And be exact about the authorship, because the arithmetic is chalked and checkable: one year's skips lay three rings at most, so the crown converges where the maker's threading finishes a country's longer count — rings other years opened, springs other makers trusted forward — which is exactly the pre-paled country every fresh verse inherits. The crown is therefore never a fixture and never a surprise: it is the place-and-time the whole year walked toward, the last hand laid on a country's long patience, telegraphed for seasons by converging spirals and Gleaner hazel, and arriving — like everything in this world — as weather the maker routed. In the small years, when no country crests, the crown is quieter and still a crown: a five-ring town answered as a poem before two hundred gathered dawn-watchers, or the Fair's last ground of the season, the stage-turn the whole wheel's tellings come home to. The porches grade nothing and remember everything, and their word for a maker's best autumn work is simply her crown, the year she walked the west wheel.

The bright ending. A maker still lit at the first still dawn, their crown stood, walks to one fire — a porch they love, a town that loves them, a waymeet's winter camp — and winters there as the honored teller, and the whole wander-year is told across the still months, leg by leg, bead by bead off the year-string: for when an answered asking comes down off a lantern it comes down with its thread, and every maker strings it across the pack-flap — the tin spoon from the Green Going, the whistle from the Fair, the stitched swallow that took the whole Deep Gold — a run's history worn where any hearth can read it, in the one shorthand the whole road shares. The string aims eyes and moves nothing: lanterns read grain, not souvenirs, so it raises no sizing and carries no telling — it is for people, a conversation hung on a pack, the first thing children ask about and the last thing a winter fire lets you skip. (And when a light goes out on the road, the custom is the road's gentlest: the Quiet Walker hangs their string on the lantern where the year ended and walks on unweighted, and every maker who passes that gate reads the beads and warms the story back to life — the waymeet table's law, worn one size sadder.) This is what triumph is in Roundelay: not a treasury and not a title, but a year good enough to keep a house warm all winter in the telling. And the winter-telling ends the only way a Round can end — by not ending: at the spring even-morrow the teller rises as the road's newest master, some green shadow already falling in half a stride behind, or sits down on the porch for good and becomes the audience every future maker plays to; and one bead of the year, the best-loved telling of the winter, goes on walking without them, hearth by hearth into the next verse. On the way to the fire, makers of a certain age all do the same thing at the home gate: pause to read whatever chalk bird some child drew there in some spring, still pointing outward. You never finish a Round; you hand it on mid-song.

Register check

Layer 6 — Aesthetic & Naming

THE PLAIN BRIGHT TONGUE AND THE ONE LIGHT — the surface the player touches, and the bible an art team paints from

Roundelay is painted in one light and three brushes, and named in one plain tongue grown from work. This layer is the anti-generic surface: the palette words, light behaviors, shape language, and motion signatures a painter could execute; the morphology and silences of how the world coins a name; and the specific warm voice its cards speak in. Every rule below is derived from the locked laws — the grammar of light, the three settlings, the two persistences, the transit-light restraint — so that the bans are not obeyed by this layer, they are load-bearing style.

The layer's creed: Roundelay names its greatest things the way a kitchen names its spoons, and paints its smallest things the way festival-glass keeps its crowd — and the whole aesthetic is the distance between those two gestures.

Elegance here is rationing: one gold edge, one red meaning, saturation earned, dark kept warm. Twee is the nearest cliff, and the master palette is the guard rope — earth and workshop colors under festival light. Tin, straw, birch-white, dark wood, wool, slate; the bright notes are always made brights — madder, glaze-blue, kingfisher lining, bottle-green paint, brass — never sugar. If a color could not have come off a dyer's line or out of a kiln, it is not in Roundelay: the world is luminous because light falls on honest materials, never because the materials are candy.

1. THE VISUAL MOTIF BIBLE

Seven systems, each given palette words, a light behavior, shape language, a motion signature, and what the camera loves. Every claim is derivable from Layers 1–5 and crosses no channel.

Seven restraint rules govern the whole surface, stated once and enforced by the named laws in section 5:

  • Gold is rationed — it appears only as the two persistences permit, and no thing wears both signatures at once (the-two-golds).
  • **One red meaning per frame** — red is always the minding-mark; the default ration is one red gesture, with the asking-lantern, red-thread-days, and the Fair as the sanctioned festival-volume choruses (the-lantern-dark; the finale).
  • Saturation is earned — the fullest color belongs to what is most attended: dawns over pale floors, a re-making mid-bloom, a hearth. Default scenery sits two steps back from the swatch.
  • Dark is warm — night-blue, shadow-brown, rafters' dark; darkness is where the world keeps what it is not showing you yet, never a threat (the weather-danger law, kept visually).
  • Light in motion is almost never seen — changes are found, not watched (found-not-watched).
  • Nothing lerps; everything settles — the world's one animate verb is attending, so the motion grammar is the tilt, the sway, the settle — never the bounce, the slide, the pop (the-settling-tilt).
  • No cursive, no candy, no christening — folk-craft lettering is a workman's round hand; the palette is dyer's and limner's stock named from things; no object in the world bears a personal name.

1.1 Gleam — Standing worn as light

  • Palette: honeyed backlight at full; amber through rice-paper at middle; ambient-only at the bottom, edges merging with the ground. Never white, never a halo — gleam is dusk-colored, the warm end of daylight, because it is read at dusk.
  • Light behavior: a key-light that belongs to the character and not the scene, thrown from a small source just behind the shoulder; strength of key = current Standing; the length and softness of the thrown shadow is the meter's second read (full gleam throws a long soft shadow toward the viewer, like an offered hand). Grain rides inside the rim: long calm spans (bridge-grain), water-ripple (song), freckles (kitchen-laughter), dapple (Fairwright rings), slantwise furrow-light (Gleaner), watered silk (Mannerly).
  • Shape language: gleam clings where surfaces end — knuckles, hair, ear-rims, a sleeve-hem in wind. It is dew, not armor: it never outlines the whole figure (that uncanny full-outline belongs to the unmarked wonders and no one else).
  • Motion signature: the cling — found on the skin between beats, not seen landing, except in the room at peak overflow (and that peak-overflow landing is the Moth Fair overflow, sight #2 of found-not-watched — the one place cling is ever caught in transit). When Standing falls, the change resolves between cuts: the maker is simply found dimmer, the contrast to the scene found smaller — never an on-screen drain, never light seen sliding off.
  • The camera loves: a lit maker crossing a blue square at dusk, shine trailing like a candle behind rice paper; the loading-screen portrait close enough to read grain; the near-dark maker still whole and un-lost, keeping every color they own, merely no longer picked out.

1.2 Askings and lanterns — contracts as crafted need

  • Palette: tin, straw, stitched wool, bone-white birch, one wrong faded detail per object — the grey wing, the dry bowl — against dark wood and warm lantern dark. Red thread the only saturate.
  • Light behavior: backlit at dusk, the canonical read: dozens of small objects turning slowly on red thread, a chandelier of needs. Tier is height — door-nail (no lantern at all), low hooks, eye-height, high hooks, rafters — and above tonight's showing, exactly one tier previews in silhouette before the dark closes (Layer 5's lantern-mile, kept as interface law). The great askings hang on no town's lantern; they are found where they lie on high stones, tied in red thread. (Layer 1's line, someday something the size of weather may hang its need on your lantern, stands as the folk figure — the road's offer to you, not a literal hook.)
  • Shape language: folk-object silhouettes, each legible as its need at ten paces — the demand iconography is the object, never a badge on it, and the one faded detail is always the object's working part (need reads as a working edge gone matte, the drawn gold's negative). Great askings invert scale: the land's found arrangements read as landscape that composed itself.
  • Motion signature: the slow turn on thread; the warming — an accepted asking rides the pack and flushes a slow breathing-gold warmer as its season shortens, a color-temperature shift from tin toward ember, never a countdown (the almanac ban, kept visually).
  • The camera loves: the lantern-mile at ridge-top dusk, the gate-lantern glowing like a dropped constellation; the Merrylees hour — a bright-rimmed maker looking up into rafters that will not lower what she asked for smaller.

1.3 Handsels and the courting — money and materials

  • Handsels (rain-on-bells): graded glow, dull → warm → singing, worn as the breathing gold; the ear does the accounting a dull handsel clicks like a button, a warm one has a woodier note, a singing one rings — so a market at noon sounds like rain on bells, and the payment-frame of the whole economy is a dull button flushing one shade warmer the instant a pleased hand closes over it. Token shapes stay folk-made (whittled birds, glazed buttons, knots of thread) so the money never reads as coinage; the strongbox counter-image is matte coins with floorboards faintly warmer beneath.
  • The courting (the-courting-stance): materials have body language and temperament palettes, read the way a cat's terms are read — nothing ever speaking. The shared motion vocabulary is small and strict: lean (toward the vouched-for, away from tuneless hands), ripple (clay toward a singer, iron-filing tenderness), perk (jar-contents rising a half-inch when the right maker walks in), stiffen (a term broken, the braid seizing the instant the song stops), settle (a term met, the stuff relaxing into workability like a sigh with no sound). Palettes: chattering stuffs in floury whites and riverbank browns that lean toward everyone; the one-term stuffs in hearth colors with a single saturated tell; proud stuffs in shy blue-whites, green-golds, and crowd-flecked clarity; placed stuffs carrying their geography visibly (last-red's lit-without-light certainty, the twice-benched's soft-worn gold).
  • Shape language: stalls strung dull-to-singing like lanterns tuning; the pack as a garden — bandolier loops, net-purse, shavings-jar banked like embers, everything visibly tended or visibly going ordinary.
  • Motion signature: the sway-and-ring for held things (pendular, wind-and-walk driven — idle motion that is the meter, never idle animation); the lean for materials (minute, unmistakable — the moment a lean could read as a gesture, it has gone twee and must be pulled back).
  • The camera loves: two hands over a stall-board at the exact moment one token brightens; the humming stall of singing-silver, the quietest corner of a loud fair.

1.4 The seep and the dawn — the Paling cycle (the-watercolor-return)

  • Out-stroke (paling): desaturation with a direction — grey enters at the rooftops and edges, drains inward and downward, and the hearth-line ember holds last. Knack-loss gets behavioral tells before color tells: the well-rope at the wrong angle, birds refusing a forgetful bridge. Paling greys are dove, ash-rose, bone, oatmeal — undyed-wool greys, never sludge, never dark; a pale town is a picture waiting to be finished, the grey of paper showing through, which is precisely what it is.
  • In-stroke (re-making): watercolor blooming across wet paper, from the worked thing outward, hearth-line first, rooftops last — the exact reverse path, so the eye learns the physics as symmetry. This is the one lawful sight of the wash in motion — the re-making bloom of found-not-watched, the single wash event in the four.
  • Return-stroke (dawn): the map's emotional weather-report — brief and ordinary over kept country; immense, long, rose-gold, almost embarrassing over pale country. The dawn-ladder is a literal sky-gradient (size from paleness, length and lateness from height, per I-017). Underground gold veins appear only in naturalist's plates, never in scenes.
  • Motion signature: none, by law — the seep is never animated; grey is found, gold is found, the world changes between looks. The Quill-Vale Morning burst (a stilling's release) is that same re-making bloom at landscape scale — a whole countryside re-coloring at once, the same wash-in-motion sight and not a separate effect — the game's rarest and largest lighting event.
  • The camera loves: a grey valley at the end of night with a row of waiting figures, then the rose-gold coming up off the whole floor at once; the alley behind the fair-stage, generations deep in accidental color.

1.5 The four lights — the year as palette bible (the-four-lights, styled)

One land re-lit — geometry never changes between legs, only light, weather, and what hangs on it; act transitions are forbidden a title card, so the colorist owns the break (the first Deep Gold frame should be feelable in the shoulders before it is nameable).

  • The Green Going (spring): new-leaf green under rain-grey; thin silver dawns; wet chalk; hedges kind. Line quality: soft pencil, unfinished edges.
  • The Long Light (high summer): white-gold and dust; short noon shadows; bunting and glass; crowds as pooled color. Line quality: crisp, full ink.
  • The Deep Gold (late summer): honey raking sideways; long shadows; the first enormous dawns; gleaning country opening in dove-grey and last-red. Line quality: heavy, warm, gilded at the edges.
  • The Red Walk (autumn): madder on grey; rose-gold mornings at full size; red thread everywhere like first strokes of paint; the Fair's dust on the seam of the seasons; spirals converging. Line quality: spare — few marks, each certain.
  • The Wintering (coda, unwalked): blue snow-light at the window, amber everywhere indoors, the year-string in firelight — the telling palette.

1.6 The map — the inked round as art object (the-inked-round, styled)

  • Palette: oiled canvas, iron-gall ink, chalk white, one madder thread; the walked line illuminated-manuscript bright, the road ahead pale pencil.
  • Typography — three hands and no more: the knife-cut (titles and place-names, letterforms like stamped tin or carved lintel, small caps, no serifs pretending to be Rome); the fire-hand (rules text and notebook, a plain upright bookhand, nothing looping, nothing gilt); the chalk (map annotations, spirals, road-marks, tallies — white, thick, slightly dusty, always on a real surface). Cursive display faces are banned world-wide. The three hands map to three information tiers — identity, instruction, intelligence — giving the UI a lawful hierarchy with zero chrome. The six ways annotate in six recognizable hand-styles (kiln-arches, jar-sketches, dawn-lines, plum bells, kingfisher dashes, bunting), so marginalia names its author at a glance.
  • Motion signature: the listening tilt — one corner of the unrolled canvas lifting very slightly toward the road where the maker's tellings are walking. The map attends; it never acts.
  • The camera loves: worn canvas across two knees at firelight, one arc jeweled and warm, its remainder ghost-pale; the Quiet Walk replaying the same canvas in reversed dawn-grey light, every woken marginal figure still gold-edged.

2. NOTES FOR THE ART TEAM — quantized reads and the frame test

Two working notes, because the surface must stay legible as well as beautiful (the spine's thresholds are arithmetic and must be readable):

  • Quantize diegetically, never abstractly. Standing has no floating bar, but it is not a vague continuous glow either: it reads in graded steps the world already owns — the lantern's showing-height and the material market's width as the visible ladder, grain-density bands as the fine read — so a player can answer am I one flat performance from the market narrowing? Attention reads as a countable thing: watcher-figures tilted at the worklight's edge against the waking-threshold cue (the oldest kettle taking up its hum). And a lawful inspect layer is permitted everywhere: the light-followers' tally-chalk (counting is the game) skins a hold-to-inspect numeric overlay at zero fiction cost, so exact numbers are always one press away without ever floating in the diegetic frame.
  • The frame test (ship it with the style guide): any frame, card, or screen passes four questions — which brush is each element wearing? which gold, if any (and never both)? where is the one red meaning? is any light in transit one of the four sanctioned sights — the two always-on surface states (the held breathing sheen, the settling sway) not counting against it? A frame that fails any one is wrong the way a wrong note is wrong: everyone can hear it, even the children.

3. THE NAMING LANGUAGE

The tongue comes first, because in Roundelay it always did: the world's strangest laws already live in the homeliest words — askings, handsels, gleanings, waymeets, even-morrows. A name here is never christened; it is grown — said plainly, repeated hearth by hearth, checked until it was so — and like every worn tool it shows its work.

The five roots (every coin this layer or any later document mints must grow from one):

  • The worked -ing — craft names its verbs and keeps them as nouns: the asking, the telling, the courting, the combing, the gleaning, the wintering, the vouching, the showing, the sizing — and now the Paling. The world names processes, never institutions. Noun your verb first and see if you are already done.
  • The yoked pair — two plain old words hitched: asking-lantern, journey-piece, year-string, gloaming-table, road-mark, even-morrow, waymeet, hearth-right, net-purse, shavings-jar, glad-load. Always plain English to plain English — never Latin, never Greek, never an invented phoneme anywhere in the world.
  • The temper-name — a material's name states its terms: singing-silver must be sung to, laughing-riverwood opens for laughter, festival-glass keeps its crowd, last-red is what the hearth-line leaves last. The name is the notebook entry compressed to two words.
  • The gift-word — when the tongue needs a coin it revives rather than invents: handsel, glean, morrow, widdershins, waymeet. Before coining, go shopping in the attic of English; the word that feels inevitable usually already exists, slightly dusty.
  • The homely great (the scale-inversion law) — the greater the thing, the smaller its name. The reserve of all spent light is the gloaming-table, named like kitchen furniture; the nearest thing to a miracle is a closed round, a phrase you could use about a song. Roundelay is never allowed a Doom, an Aurora, or an Eldertide. Seed packet or map poster? — if a name would look at home on a fantasy map poster it is wrong; if it would look at home on a joiner's shelf it is probably right.

What gets which kind of name. People get personal names, and only people — hedgerow first names (Sorrel, Immy, Alder, Rush, Fern), leavened by weathered opaque given names in the Ferrow/Hask register (Ede, Hepsie) and the odd craft-stock first-element (Wick, off the candle-work), so the stock never converges on flower-child whimsy; and short worn surnames of ground and weather (Ash, Vane, Fell, Wold, Frost, Lea), no epithets, no titles. Places get grown toponyms in English hedgerow morphology (-by, -mere, -fold, -low, -ford, -fell, -down, -thorpe, X-under-Y, X-on-the-Z), each with a small true thing inside it. Ways get gait-names describing how they walk (the Kilnfast, the Untold, the Eveners), never what they hold. Made things get work-names, never person-names (the I-003 home, the-work-names-of-things). And every named color is a thing-name (named-from-things), while grades and tiers are read off behavior, place, and lantern-height, never counted — with the one lawful exception being the chalk-spiral arithmetic itself, which is counted for the road and decides no contest.

The silences. The register bans are holes where the words would be: nothing is owed, due, filed, fined, or paid, and the tongue has no diegetic synonym to route around the hole with — not price but the read, not payment but the glad-load, not deadline but out of season, not reputation but what the road has heard, not quest log but the year-string. There are no offices (lantern-keeper is a household turn, lowercase, never a rank), no theonyms (the cheerful heresy is named like porch teasing), and the old name of the tide is retired (section 5).

4. THE CARD-FLAVOR VOICE

Every card is a thing that was given — taught, traded, won, pressed into a palm — and so every card speaks in the maker's own pack-notebook, written up each night at the fire (the-giving-line). Rules text is the maker's plain fire-written hand. Flavor is the giving-line: the sentence spoken aloud when the piece changed hands, quoted exactly, with a giving-note of where and how. The deck thereby becomes what canon says a pack is — a life's education you can hear coming down the road — and reading your hand is hearing everyone who ever handed you anything. Materials shift into the notebook's dry field-note register ("Likes weather-talk. Hates hurry."); system messages stay notebook entries with a place-name in them (canon's template: "Wader's Ford still tells of the reel — +2").

The warmth is specific and guarded: fond, exact, and a little amused — a person who takes the work completely seriously and themselves not very. It never exclaims, never addresses the player, never lets an object talk. Whimsy lives in what is said, never in how hard the prose smiles.

Sample giving-lines:

  • "Hum it under your breath till the blade catches, then let the knot have the last bar — you'll feel where it wants to stop." — the smith at Glazeby, giving the last bars into my keeping.
  • "Unfold it once, at the gate, before they've fixed their faces — never a second time; the second telling always falls flat." — folded for me by an Untold runner, gone by breakfast.
  • "When it springs back at your knuckle and holds the dent a moment, it's done — set it down and leave it, don't work it warm again." — a grandmother at the Withyfold gate, teaching with floured hands.
  • "Tell the clay about the weather. It's been down there all year." — first page of this notebook, in my mother's hand.
  • "Set the last nail proud a finger's width, then hand the mallet to the child watching." — a Kilnfast master, who said nothing else all day.
  • "Wait for the old kettle to find its hum before you say the hard thing — the room turns when it does, not before." — porch-talk at Corvelow, offered with tea.
  • "Face east and keep your hands off the reel till the first color's up — a gleaner who fidgets misses it." — a Gleaner on the Long Sight, yawning happily at noon.
  • "Pale country is only saving up. Arrange to be standing in one when it spends." — a Morning-Gleaner's round-book, past the last hearth.

(Register check: nothing owed, due, paid, or filed; no object speaks or is named; failure-adjacent lines say falls flat, never anything mortal; nothing is late, only out of season. Voice-guard: seven of these eight are a concrete instruction about a specific thing at the moment of the gift; the aphorism register is capped at one line — here, #8 — per the-giving-line's mug test.)

5. NAMED ELEMENTS — THE STYLE LAWS, THE NAMING, THE VOICE

the-three-brushes

Limners on the Rounds — hearth-taught, academy-free — carry three brushes and a rule older than any of them: the brush tells you whose the light is. The wash is scenery's: wet, blooming, drained from the edges inward when a place lets go. The rim is people's: one dry bright stroke where the world's trust catches a figure, from a small source just behind the shoulder. The edge is things': one drawn line of gold on the working edge, or the low breathing sheen of the held, and nothing else. A painting that rims a doorway or edges a face is wrong-brushed, and a limner would sooner scrape the board than hang it — not from rule-keeping but because it reads as a lie the way a wrong note reads.

  • This could become: the art direction's master QA law and the render architecture in one — three shader disciplines (scenery owns bloom and desaturation; characters own rim and grain; props own edge-line and sheen), so places-pale / people-gleam / things-wake is enforced by the pipeline, not by review; any frame is auditable against one question per pixel: which brush?
  • Visual: the style-guide's first plate — one dusk square painted three times (all-wash: a ghost town; all-rim: a stage; all-edge: a shop window), then the true frame, all three at once: grey town, bright maker, alert kettle, legible in one glance.

the-two-golds (resolves I-016)

Gold is one channel with two signatures, and every market-day teaches the difference. Fired waking wears the drawn line: one thin, crisp gold along the depicted thing's working edge and nowhere else — the lip of the bowl, the blade of the plane, the hem that does the keeping-warm — determined by use, never by the card's orientation. It is born in a single glaze-crackle bloom at the bench (the one moment of firing you ever see, beautiful and slightly final, like a signature watched drying), then never moves again, weathering only by softening, centuries-slow, into the gone-gentle gilding of the twice-benched. Held waking wears the breathing sheen: a full-surface glow on a slow four-to-six-second breath, never forming a line — swelling in company, dulling in a shut box toward matte. This slow breath is a persistent surface property, not a sight — the gold analog of the settling-tilt's permitted idle sway: it is simply on wherever held gold rests, a standing state present between looks the way grain or wood-figure is present, never a light seen arriving or leaving. The only discrete event it hosts is the one sanctioned sight it earns — the palm-brighten, the single-beat flush warmer when a glad hand closes over it (found-not-watched); the breath is the surface, that flush is the sight. Three glance-tests, teachable to a child: edge or surface; still or breathing; born once or fed always. And the ration that makes both legible: no thing wears both. A great asking is simply the breathing gold at landscape scale — but the sheen lives on the found arrangement itself (the antler-and-cloudmoss, the tied red thread), and the fell around it keeps only wash; the awe comes from composition — a tiny figure, a huge grey fell, one small breathing arrangement — never from sheen laid on a place. The one lawful breach is the unmarked wonders: the drawn gold along every line at once, a fired thing whose whole surface became working edge — and even then, lines and stillness, never a breath — which is exactly why they photograph as slightly too in-focus against a painterly world.

  • This could become: the two persistences read at arm's length everywhere — a woken card carries the drawn line on the painted journey-piece's own working edge inside the illustration (the frame stays furniture), with a matched still-gold tell on the card's leading edge so the sort holds at hand scale; handsels, shavings, and glad-load seams carry the breathing sheen animated by the economy's actual state; the crackle-bloom is the waking juice and the palm-brighten is the payment juice; colorblind-safe by construction, since the read is line-versus-motion, not hue-versus-hue.
  • Visual: a woken plane and a singing coin on one bench — the plane's blade holding one still hairline, the coin breathing over its whole face — and beneath them the same two gone old: the plane's line softened like worn gilt, the coin lapsed to button-matte.

found-not-watched

The world keeps its changes between looks, and the road has a saying for it: the lane is found brighter, never caught blushing. No eye has ever seen the seep move, or the spilling land, or a combing finish. Light in transit is rationed to exactly four sights — a sight being a discrete light-in-motion event, a change caught in the act of happening — and the whole world's fireworks budget lives in them: two are light moving between holders — the re-making bloom (a woken re-making re-coloring across wet paper) and the Moth Fair overflow (surplus running up the strings at peak before a gathered crowd); two are a thing changing state in place — the firing crackle-bloom (waking, at the bench) and the held-gold palm-brighten (a coin flushing warmer in a closing hand). A sight is not the same thing as a persistent surface state, which is always simply so and does not count against the four: the held gold's slow breathing sheen (the-two-golds) and the idle sway-and-tilt of attending things (the-settling-tilt) are standing surface properties, not sights — the world's gold-and-motion texture, present between looks the way grain is, never a light or a motion caught arriving or leaving. So the rule reads cleanly for a VFX builder: four discrete light-in-transit effects, plus two always-on surface states that never resolve because they never begin. Everything else — every change that is neither a sanctioned sight nor a standing surface state — resolves on cuts, returns, and dawns: the node you left is found different. Makers live comfortably inside this discretion; it is why the world feels kept rather than haunted.

  • This could become: the cinematography and VFX budget as one discipline — four enumerated light-in-motion effects, each reserved to the fiction that earns it (so the game's biggest mechanical moments are also its only fireworks), and every other world-state change communicated by before/after, which is cheap, legible, and uncanny in exactly the right dose. Gleam-loss in particular resolves between beats — found dimmer, never an on-screen transfer.
  • Visual: a two-frame motif used throughout — same composition, hours apart: the stage at dusk with the crowd, then the empty alley at dawn a shade more watercolor than it was; nothing seen leaving, nothing seen landing, the difference doing all the talking.

the-settling-tilt

Nothing in Roundelay acts, so nothing in Roundelay's picture moves — it settles. The quarter-turn of a chair toward the bench, the degree of lean in a shelf of jars, the pack's pieces tilting like grass under one wind toward the road a telling walks: the world's whole animate vocabulary is the compass needle's — a small overshoot, a tremble, and rest, always toward what matters. Its companion is the sway: held things ride the day pendularly, on thread and net and walking hip, moved by wind and gait and never by themselves. The third thing is stillness, which is what the fired do best. And the gathered attention of the bench is read the same way: a pool of worklight, and around its edge the count of small silhouettes tilted inward — the room's opinion in half a degree — with the waking threshold cued by the oldest kettle taking up its hum. Sentimental strangers looking for the pot that pours itself go home disappointed; everyone else learns to read a room by how far it has leaned.

  • This could become: the UI animation grammar entire — panels, cards, and cursors settle by tilt (toward the playable, the affordable, the newly relevant) rather than sliding or pulsing; hand-cards lean a half-degree toward legal plays; idle motion exists only as sway on held and hanging objects; and the attention pool renders as worklight radius plus tilted-watcher count, so the interface literally attends, and the restraint ban becomes the product's feel.
  • Visual: the workshop-as-theater as motion reference — a pool of worklight, and in the half-dark dozens of small silhouettes arriving at the same angle by slow degrees, every gold working-edge pointed at the bench like rows of eyes catching stage light.

chalk-thread-and-gold (lineage — declared: this spatializes no new furniture; it gathers Layer 3's road-marks, Layer 4's spirals and red thread, and the two golds into one notation standard, so the interface has a single law instead of four habits.)

The road already drew its own interface, and it uses exactly three marks. Chalk is information: spirals, birds, waves, tallies — white, temporary, anyone's, honest because it was never decreed, only checked. Red thread is minding: what hangs an asking, ties a combing, bookmarks one knack against a return — chosen, persistent, one per lane, and never a bond, only a thought kept. Gold is waking: the two persistences, earned at the bench or fed by hands, and counterfeit-proof because the world itself does the gilding. Chalk says know this; thread says this is minded; gold says this is awake — and nothing on any gatepost, stall, or pack has ever needed a fourth mark.

  • This could become: the entire UI iconography standard — every interface mark is chalk (info layers, map intel, tallies-never-digits in diegetic frames), thread (active contracts, pins, the year-string as run history), or gold (waking states, per the-two-golds); the HUD becomes unmistakably in-world at zero fiction cost, and any proposed icon that is not chalkable, tieable, or gildable is by law redesigned.
  • Visual: one gatepost as the UI style sheet — a whitewash spiral with a wet outer ring, a chalk bird pointing outward, one madder knot at the lane's elbow, and on the lantern above, tin shapes with their red threads and the town's one gold-edged kettle in a window behind.

dusk-reads-dawn-gives

The road's day has two reading lights and every maker keeps their hours. Dusk is when the world is legible: gleam shows plainest, lanterns are backlit, stalls string their glow, grain can be read across a square — the hour of arriving, appraising, deciding. Dawn is when the world gives: the reissue, loose and unclaimed, sized to the country's patience — the hour of income and of standing in what the grey saved up. Noon is for work (flat, honest, shadowless worklight; the bench does not flatter), and night is for fires (amber pools in blue; the telling hour). A turn of play — dawn, road, work, fire — is therefore one full circuit of light, and a maker who has lost the season can read the hour off the color of the world like everyone else on the Rounds.

  • This could become: the game's lighting-and-screen-flow law — approach and preview screens graded dusk (the lantern-mile as information architecture); income, rewards, and reveals graded dawn; the bench UI in flat worklight; rests and tellings in fire-amber — so the loop's phases are told by grade, and the only diegetic calendar the interface needs is the inked round's own year-wheel (dawn-gilding on the wheel's current arc, high days as fixed lights on the rim, per Layer 5). Countdowns and dates-owed stay banned; the year-wheel that Layer 5 already drew is exempt and lawful.
  • Visual: four thumbnails of one town, one turn — rose-gold gate at dawn; flat bright square at noon; backlit lantern and rimmed figures at dusk; one amber doorway in blue at night — the whole game-feel storyboarded in four grades.

the-shoulder-candle

The gleam bible — Standing worn as light, exactly as canon set it. The maker is lit as if by a small warm source just behind the shoulder, and the strength of that key light is the meter: full gleam is honeyed backlight with a long soft shadow thrown toward the viewer like an offered hand; middling gleam is a readable rim at dusk; near-dark is ambient light only, edges merging with the background — present, unpicked-out, never grey, because the Paling has no appetite for the living and a dim maker keeps every color they own, they just stop being lit. Grain rides the rim as texture, the six ways' portraiture legible across a square.

  • This could become: the Standing meter deleted from the HUD and rebuilt in the world — the portrait's key light as the meter, dusk scenes as the natural check-your-Standing beat, grain-texture as the build-identity readout, and the two paces staged in light (in-room gains land on the knuckles this turn; road tellings land as the rim warming a shade at dusk, source-stamped in the notebook) — with the meter quantized diegitically in showing-height and market-width bands and a lawful tally-chalk inspect overlay, so it reads as steps, not vague glow.
  • Visual: rim only, never a gold line (the channel ban held); the shoulder-candle's thrown shadow is the painter's secret weapon; and the Quiet Walk's most load-bearing cue — the rim goes out, the person stays whole, and everything they ever woke keeps its gold.

the-lantern-dark

The askings bible, and the world's warm dark. An asking-lantern is a chandelier of needs: dozens of small crafted objects — tin, straw, stitched cloth, bone-birch — turning slowly on red thread, backlit warm, against rafters that keep true dark. The composition is a strict three-band law: lit hooks below (tonight's showing), one silhouette above them (the next size of being believed in, shape legible, detail withheld), honest dark above that (the fired sizing, waiting). Each object carries exactly one wrong, faded detail that states the need, and it is always the object's working part — need reads as a working edge gone matte, the drawn gold's negative. And because nothing hunts in a Roundelay dark (the road's perils are weather and staleness, never teeth), the dark is where the world keeps what it is not showing you yet: the rafters do not menace the dimming maker, they hold her sized work the way an attic holds a wedding coat.

  • This could become: the contract UI entire — tier as hook-height, escalation as silhouette-preview, the failure spiral as watching offers descend the ladder while the rafters hold; accepted askings riding the pack on their red thread, warming as their season shortens (ripeness as breathing-gold warmth, never a countdown); and, by the same warm-dark law, all locked and out-of-tier content everywhere in the game hung as warm silhouettes in rafters' dark — never greyed-out, never padlocked, never cold black.
  • Visual: folk-object naturals against blue-hour dark, one backlight source, red thread the only saturated line; slow rotation on thread and nothing else; and the sound motif — the lantern-mile arrives with a faint knocking of small objects in wind, wood on tin, the world's gentlest wind-chime: needs, audibly patient.

the-worked-word

The naming language kept as a living practice — the five roots and the growing of names, said plainly and repeated hearth by hearth, checked until it was so. On the Rounds, getting a thing's name right is a small act of craft like any other, and the porches are where names are proofed: say a new coin on a quiet porch and watch whether the elders pick it up or let it lie. A name the road declines simply never gets repeated, which is the only rejection Roundelay knows how to give. The tongue is visible everywhere as short words on honest surfaces — knife-cut letters in a lintel, a temper-name chalked on a jar, stitched initials in a pack-flap — never a paragraph, never a scroll.

  • This could become: the writers'-room constitution for all downstream text — a five-root checklist every new term must pass, plus the seed packet or map poster? smell test; and, procedurally, name-generation grammars for towns, pieces, and materials that compose from root-tables rather than syllable-soup, so generated content stays in-tongue by construction.
  • Visual: the world reads like a well-labeled workshop, not a library — every name a worn tool showing its work.

the-work-names-of-things (the I-003 ruling, executed)

Nothing made is ever christened. A thing is called by its work and its place — the Hask bell, the Setterby stair, the old kettle in the rafters, my mother's third kiln — because nothing in Roundelay speaks except people, and a person-name is a small pretend mouth: give a kettle a name and a player will wait for it to talk; call it the old kettle in the rafters and it can hum forever and never break the restraint ban. The tenderness runs the other way — the more beloved a thing, the more exactly its work is named: ask a maker about the plane in their pack and you will not get a pet name, you will get the long try-plane, the one that likes quartered oak, said the way you'd introduce a respected colleague.   • This could become: a hard guardrail for all card, item, and UI text — no named-NPC objects, no quoted objects, no object point-of-view, ever; relic-class unmarked cards named by work-and-place (the Setterby Chip, a Stair-Worn Piece), so the collection screen reads like a gazetteer of colleagues rather than a loot table and quietly teaches the long riddle every time it is opened.
  • Visual: a museum-of-none — the collection screen as a porch shelf of unlabelled, ground-named things, each with its chalk tag in the road's shorthand (a place-word and a work-word), and not one nameplate anywhere.

8. FINAL REGISTER-BAN COMPILATION (the whole world, consolidated — this is the last prose layer)

Every ban carried down the layers, in one list, binding on Layer 7 and on all downstream design and art:

  • Vocabulary ban (L1): owed, debt, promise, vow, payment-due, penalty, fine, duty-to-host never appear in diegetic naming or card text; failure is ecological and mnemonic (the world grows shy, forgets, moves on), never punitive or transactional.
  • Channel ban (L1): the grammar of light is strict — places pale, people gleam, things wake. No layer may put a working-edge on a person, a key-light on a place, or paling-grey on a character. Enforced here as the three brushes (wash / rim / edge) and the two golds (line / sheen).
  • Restraint ban (L1): woken things attend; they never act, speak, or emote — no self-pouring pots, no helpful furniture, no faces, eyes, or mouths on anything made; the compass-needle tilt is the whole permitted vocabulary of object expression.
  • Riddle ban (L1): the unmarked wonders are never explained as elder makers, precursors, or gods.
  • Reservoir ban (L2): the gloaming-table holds spent regard of places and things only — never a life-force, soul-stuff, or memory of the dead.
  • Transit-light restraint (L2): light moving between holders is visible only at the two sanctioned sights (the re-making bloom; the Moth Fair overflow), with two more in-place state changes permitted (the firing crackle-bloom; the held-gold palm-brighten) — four effects total; everything else is found-not-watched.
  • Pedigree guard (L2): the wonders' makers, if any, were no brighter, older, or other than today's; closure, not pedigree, is the whole difference.
  • Folk-scale guard (L2): the light-followers stay institution-free forever — no academy, no order, no instrument beyond what a pack carries.
  • Timescale law (L2): within one verse of a Round, gleam moves on outcomes only; ambient forgetting operates between verses. No within-run decay clock on Standing.
  • Vocabulary hygiene (L2): tally is house-style (counted for the joy of counting, deciding nothing); ledger, census, résumé, actuarial, filing, record-of-account stay out of all diegetic text.
  • Membership ban (L3): ways are walked, never conferred — no induction rites, rolls, or witnessed enrollments; grain is the only membership the world reads.
  • Officiant ban (L3): no contest is decided by a judge, keeper, or count — verdicts are read only as which work wakes, the attending tilt of woken things, and the lane or flagstone found brighter after.
  • Quarrel ration (L3): exactly two standing arguments (the mending question; fired-or-fed); craft friction beyond these stays unnamed comedy and courtesy.
  • Holdings ban (L3): no way keeps ground; stretches are song-habits, porches belong to whoever sits on them, fair and waymeet ground is tended by whoever is camped on it.
  • Standing-never-spends (L3): high gleam gates and unlocks, and is never itself a price; any cost routes through outcomes (a term accepted becomes an asking that can go stale), never through expenditure of Standing.
  • Stealth ban (L3): the Untold are painted early, bright, and greeted — never hooded, cloaked, or vanishing.
  • Mint ban (L4): the gloaming-table mints nothing and loses nothing; every windfall returns exactly what was withheld or spilled, never more.
  • Commerce-vocabulary ban (L4): pay, wage, profit, savings, fee, market (as a metaphor for gift-scenes) appear in diegetic text only inside negations; hearth-right and the fireside telling stay wholly outside price language.
  • Matter-is-ordinary guard (L4): no substance holds, slows, or binds regard by its own nature; persistence routes through the three settlings and the binding, never through the stuff itself.
  • Courier law (L4): tellings move at a hearth's pace, and only tellings move gleam or raise readings; word aims eyes, binds nothing, moves no reading — no faster courier for light, ever.
  • Salvage guard (L4): harvest never keys to ruin, collapse, or failure states — the gleaning takes only the left-behind, the twice-benched only the lovingly retired.
  • Stage-slang hygiene (L4): work goes dark before a crowd, never "dies" there; no mortality puns near the spilling or the gloaming-table.
  • Almanac ban (L5): the year keeps high days and seasons, never dates-owed; an asking warms, ripens, and stales — it is never due, and nothing is late, only out of season. Enforced here as ripeness read in warmth, and the year-wheel (not a countdown) as the one lawful diegetic calendar.
  • Weather-danger law (L5): no node, road, or country hosts combat, ambush, ruin-delving, or lurking-monster framing; the loneliest country in the game is grey, not hostile — and dark is kept warm.
  • Crown guard (L5): the great asking is never annual, sited in advance, or institutional; it emerges from the maker's threading over the country's own arithmetic.
  • Stretch reservation (L5): stretch keeps its Layer 3 meaning (the way-songs' road-memory habitat) and never names a generic map leg; the run's units are turns (one morning) and legs.
  • Aesthetic bans (L6, new): no christening of made things (I-003); no abstract color or rarity language in any name or UI (colors are thing-names, grades read behavior/place/height); no cursive display faces and no invented phonemes anywhere; no candy palette (made brights only, honest materials under festival light); gold is rationed to the two persistences and never both at once; one red meaning per frame, festival volume only at the sanctioned choruses.

Glossary — canon terms

RoundelayA made world — not made once, long ago, but made continuously, by hands — named for a round-song passed voice to voice around a circle, that only stays wonderful as long as someone keeps singing their bars.L1
the Rounds / the Long RoundsThe great circuits the makers walk — not roads so much as habits the land has learned — where every maker's run is one verse of a Round and no two verses visit the same world.L1
waking (of made things)Anything made while truly attended wakes a little — gaining a quiet aliveness in which it attends but never acts — while hasty work made alone still functions but is merely mortal, taken first by the Paling.L1
working-edgeThe thin rim of gold a woken thing carries along its working edge — the lip of the bowl, the blade of the plane, the hem of the coat — like late sun on the edge of a table.L1
the PalingThe world exhaling — a tide, not a villain — that takes knack from made things first and color after, never touching people or the light itself, and is the reason makers exist at all.L1
pale (town)A town three seasons overdue for a maker, which hangs out its asking-lanterns and waits, patient as winter gardens.L1
maker's gleamThe light that gathers on a maker whose gifts delight — the world's trust made visible, earned by delight given in any coin the world honors, dimming gently when work disappoints; this is Standing.L1
apprentice materialsStraw, dough, and river clay — the gregarious stuff that talks to everyone, dim or bright — both every newborn's first conversation with the world and every dimmed maker's way back into it.L1
askingA town's crafted request for making: a small object carrying the shape of the need — a tin spoon with a dry bowl, a stitched swallow with one grey wing — that rides the accepting maker's pack and warms when they near the work.L1
asking-lanternThe lantern at a town gate where askings hang; it is choosy, showing a traveler only what the town believes their hands can hold, matched to the brightest the road has heard them be.L1
great askingAn asking made by the land itself, past anything a town can craft — a mountain sending weathered antler and cloudmoss, a fading season sending a morning that smells wrong — the peak of every maker's hope and the reason the Rounds exist.L1
hearth-rightAny traveler's claim on the Rounds to one night's warmth, one meal, one bed by the fire, freely given, nothing asked — though no guest has ever managed to leave nothing behind, because a fed and firelit traveler tells, and the story is how the house learns the world.L1
journey-pieceA kept fragment of learned craft carried in the pack — a ribbon holding the turn of a dance, a chip of glaze, three bars of a planing-song knotted into a cord — used by performing it, out loud, with the hands, in front of the work.L1
shy materialsMaterials alive the way cats are alive — present, opinionated, and not obliged to come when called — so getting them is never mining and always courtship, with two gates in strict order: consent to negotiate (gleam's business) and terms once stated must be met.L1
audience of thingsThe gathering of made things' attention when true work begins — chairs turning a quarter-turn toward the bench, lamps steadying, a woken kettle taking up an attentive hum — inside which makers work measurably better.L1
attention (the force)The gathered, unbroken regard of watchers, human or made, resting on work while it is being done — not a courtesy but a force, the thing that wakes made things and the magic of the world (attention made visible is light).L1
road-memoryA Round's remembering of who travels it well, from which every road-gift flows: miles fold politely, fords are shallower, dusk comes later, and the town you helped is somehow always downhill.L1
handselLuck made small and given — a whittled bird, a glazed button, a knot of bright thread pressed into a palm — which is also the money, worth as much as it is awake from crossing delighted hands, and dulling back toward being a button when hoarded.L1
the Quiet WalkThe kind rite for a maker gone fully dim: one more walk of the circuit — no pack, no pieces, no askings — fed and warmed at every town and asked for nothing, until sometimes delight given rekindles the first flicker of gleam.L1
unmarked wondersWonders with no maker's mark at all — a stone stair a thousand years bright, a river that has never forgotten its knack, a bell that wakes other things — whose origin is the world's long riddle, never explained as elder makers, precursors, or gods.L1
the grammar of lightThe binding world law that light — attention made visible — is read in three channels that never cross: places fade, people gleam, things wake.L1
the round of lightThe one circulation of the world's conserved regard — dawn to eyes, eyes to hands, hands to work, work to places, places seeping to the gloaming-table, the table rising as dawn — with no first verse and no dram ever lost; the world's deepest law wears the shape of its name.L2
regardAttention as conserved stuff: the holding-of-things-in-mind that keeps a thing's character crisp, never created and never destroyed — 'nothing is noticed away' — and visible from outside as light.L2
the three settlingsThe three holdings of regard, each belonging to its holder: loose in places (a seeping wash of color and knack), running in the living (always a current, never a pool), set in made things (fired into use, the gold working-edge) — the grammar of light given its physical floor.L2
the clingingThe three settlings' one lawful seam: gleam as loose light bound to a line of cause, riding the surface of a living current — dew on a riverboat, not dew on a stone — which no tide can skim; why the Paling can never touch Standing.L2
the held and the firedThe two wakings: fired waking sets regard into a work's use at the bench, permanently (you can weather fired glaze, never un-fire it); held waking keeps a handsel awake hand-to-delighted-hand and lapses when the attending stops — the fired never unfires, the held must be fed.L2
the gloaming-tableThe world's low standing reserve of spent regard, pooled under valley floors and deep water like groundwater, fed by the Paling's downhill seep and drawn on by every dawn — a pantry, never an underworld: nothing living enters it, ever.L2
the unclaimed morningDawn as reissue: every morning's light is spent regard returned from the gloaming-table — loose, unclaimed, equal, free to every eye that opens — and pale country gets the best mornings, because the fuller the local table, the richer the return.L2
the Stillsummer of Quill ValeThe remembered season, forty years back, when one valley's Paling stopped and the valley curdled — waxy colors, hoarded knacks, grey thin mornings — proving the Paling the out-breath without which there is no in; the Quill Vale Morning that followed its return means an ending that turns out to be a gift.L2
light-followersThe Rounds' patient hedge-philosophers, who follow where regard goes — no academy, no college, no instrument beyond tally-chalk, hazel wands, pin-lanterns, and patience; their whole literature fits in walking-songs and their method's home is the windowsill.L2
gleam-runoffThe mechanism of Standing's rise: a made thing, like a cup, holds only so much, and when delight overfills the work the excess runs backward along the line of cause to the maker and clings at the body's edges — 'a full cup runs to the pourer' (the Moth Fair at Ferrow, sixty-one autumns back).L2
the grain of gleamGleam keeps the texture of what fed it — bridge-delight lies in long spans across the knuckles, song-gleam ripples, hearth-laughter freckles — so lanterns and proud materials read a maker's history worn as light, legible at dusk from across a square.L2
the spillingWhat happens when work disappoints: regard withdraws all at once, the slackened gleam slides off where the maker stands and soaks into the flagstones and lanes — the lane remembers the light, never the name, and failure leaves the world slightly more beautiful exactly where it happened.L2
need-takes-aimThe law of the asking ratchet: an asking is a woken thing whose fixed attending is toward the brightest hands its town has heard of, and a lantern's sizing, once read from a maker's grain, is fired and never lowers — while what a lantern shows follows the gleam presently on the hands (the Merrylees hour, twenty-two springs ago).L2
sizing and showingThe two lantern variables that never trade places: sizing is fired, matched to the brightest the road has heard you be, one-way forever; showing is current, gated by tonight's gleam — so the ratchet never descends while the dimming maker's offers still walk legibly down the ladder toward the rafters' dark.L2
the paling-arithmeticThe Paling's gentle, steady, compounding rate on unanswered need — knack shores up knack, so each forgotten trick uncovers the next — chalked by lantern-keepers as a spiral on the gatepost, one ring per season unanswered: three rings is a plea, five is a poem, seven is a great asking being born.L2
the noticing-gameThe Rounds' empirical method, older than any way: paired trials on a windowsill — an attended pebble beside a hasty one, chalk tallies beneath, a winter of watching which greys first — through which every law of the cosmology entered canon.L2
closed roundWhat an unmarked wonder is: a made thing whose circulation of regard has closed into a self-sustaining loop that keeps what it pours — a whirlpool in the round of light that never unwinds, immune to the weathering, whose completeness lawfully ate its ordinary maker's attribution; how a round closes is permanently unknowable, since causes cannot be traced inside a closed loop from outside it.L2
the stair at SetterbyThe most beloved of the light-followers' near-closures — a stair that holds nine parts in ten of what it sheds, on which pilgrim-makers leave journey-pieces the way you would encourage a student.L2
the Bell of HaskThe Rounds' one disputed closing: a ferry-bell whose engraved signature bar the oldest listeners swear was plainer in their grandmothers' day — the riddle's slow public clock, argued over between crossings, which the Haskers refuse to settle on the grounds that a riddle carried this well would be a shame to set down.L2
the bindingThe three settlings' one lawful seam: loose light held in place by ongoing regard, worn in two homes — the clinging (gleam on a maker, riding a living current no tide can skim) and held light (on a handsel, sheltered only while it circulates; boxed, the binding lapses and the dulling is the ordinary seep, indoors).L2
the combingHow the land 'makes' a great asking without wanting one: when a whole countryside's living regard holds one lack in mind, the crossing currents comb the loose wash of a place into shape — antler and cloudmoss on Wray Fell, a wrong-smelling morning — a held thing kept awake by the country's continuing regard for what it misses; 'the wash combs where currents cross.'L2
the walking waysThe six traveling cultures of Roundelay — each a complete answer to how regard is best set into the world — kept without hall, roll, or rite: a way is walked, never joined, and grain is the only membership the world reads.L3
the KilnfastThe way of the fired waking: few askings finished utterly, whole lives aimed at single masterworks, patience as their entire strangeness, and at the bottom of it the horizon-hope that a work made attentively enough might one day close its round; they hold rooms long — one unbroken session at the bench — and never carry light over a dawn.L3
the MannerlyThe way of courtship: the finest work begins as listening, kept in the great traveling notebooks of what the world answers to; their gleam is a key that turns, never a coin that leaves, and what they truly risk is accepted terms, which can go stale like any asking.L3
the Morning-GleanersThe cheerful way that walks toward the grey: sunwise readers of gatepost spirals who practice ripe mending — arriving exactly when a need has grown into its best self — and who stand in pale country's immense dawns on purpose; they never say a town is pale, only that it is saving up.L3
ripe mendingThe Gleaner craft-creed: answer a need not as early as possible but exactly when it has grown into its best self — five rings is a poem, and answering one before two hundred gathered dawn-watchers is the closest thing the way has to a wedding.L3
the EvenersThe way that walks with the out-breath: widdershins dusk-walkers, the Rounds' great un-hoarders, who keep last lightings so that nothing meets the tide unattended; they name themselves for the potter's motion — they even things, the way a thumb evens a rim.L3
last lightingThe Eveners' tenderest custom, kept when a town has decided a thing will not be asked-for again: one full evening of the town's gathered regard resting on the retiring work, stories told at its foot, handsels rung bright hand to hand around it — then the attending ends, and the ordinary seep carries the old wash to the table, and the valley's dawns run richer for a season. The whole of it is one small bell, rung once.L3
the UntoldThe way that walks ahead of its own tellings: between-road menders whose creed is that a deed left small stays exactly the size the need was, keeping their gleam fine-grained on purpose — a shine like sand, not like beams — so their lanterns lower a rafters' worth of kettles; met openly, greeted by name, and gone by breakfast.L3
the between-roadsThe stile-and-hedgerow lines that pass no hearth, so no hearth passes the tale on — honest miles, cold fords, no askings and no tales carried; the Rounds' one chosen solitude, where a maker is greeted with a nod and no questions.L3
the FairwrightsThe way that makes the watching: builders of workshop-theaters, market-rings, and the Round-Fair itself, whose founding telling is the Moth Fair — kept not for the fiddler but for the four hundred, and for whoever raked the ground that held them; no work wakes thin in a room a Fairwright raised.L3
the Round-FairThe fair that walks the Round: a few dozen wagons perpetually a week ahead of autumn, Fairwright-built and everybody-kept, arriving like a season with opinions — its commerce priced in wakefulness and nothing else, its dusk a mingling of all six gleam-grains.L3
the fair-answerThe custom for contested askings: two ways answer side by side before the whole town, and the verdict is read as which work wakes and the slow tilt of the town's woken things — no one presides, nothing counted decides, and the flagstones before the warmer bench are found a shade richer next morning, never watched.L3
red-thread-daysThe season the country enters when a seventh ring closes and a combing is found: six ways converge on one valley — seams read, word run, ring raised, staging fired, materials courted, last lightings kept — around the maker the asking warmed to; 'the weather picks the hands; the road makes sure they don't stand there alone.'L3
the gathered benchThe working-theater raised from all sides around the answerer of a great asking, with no one presiding — because a countryside that combed a great asking into being is the same countryside that wants to watch it answered.L3
road-marksThe gift-graffiti of the Rounds — knotted thread on stiles, chalk birds on gateposts, spirals copied forward — the six ways' shared shorthand for what the next walker needs to know; no one keeps them and everyone keeps them up, and leaving a false one is the nearest thing the Rounds have to a sin.L3
the quiet porchThe deep benches under wide eaves on every Round — kept by whoever is sitting on them — where Quiet Walkers are met as honored audience: a maker gone dim is the finest watcher alive, and work done before a porch of old hands wakes deep.L3
the mending questionThe one quarrel on the Rounds with real weight (the Untold against the Gleaners): answer a need early and small, or let it ripen into a poem? Settled town by town — the town is asked, and its answer, chalked on the gatepost, becomes part of its character.L3
fired-or-fedThe doctrine argument of the two persistences (the Kilnfast against the Eveners): is light best given set, held in a work for centuries, or best given back, fed to the table to come home in everyone's dawns? Two true readings of one conservation law, unsettled on purpose.L3
the even-morrowThe equinox festival held where a Gleaner circuit and an Evener circuit cross nearest the turning day: the two ends of the breath trade hours at one long table at first grey light, two lanterns at its ends, one held high, one held low — and underneath the comedy, the day the Paling's seasonal rate turns.L3
double-lit dayThe small festival made wherever a sunwise Gleaner circuit and a widdershins Evener circuit cross at a town — dawn-work and dusk-work in one turn of the sun, held to be lucky for weddings and first apprenticings.L3
season-shadowingHow craft crosses between the ways: a learner simply falls in behind a master of another way and keeps their hours for a season — begun without arrangement, ended with one journey-piece, given, because giving is how it moves.L3
waymeetThe ground where the great circuits cross — held by no town, tended by whoever is camped on it — where the six gaits mix, journey-pieces change hands, and young makers come to watch the ways go by and choose one.L3
turning your coat to the weatherWhat it is called when a maker changes their way of walking — grounds for a party, never a grudge.L3
the courtingThe acquisition law of the whole material world: nothing is mined, everything is asked, through two gates kept in strict order — the vouching (will the stuff state its terms to these hands at all, gleam's business and no coin's) and the terms (the material's own, always performable, always legible).L4
the vouchingThe courting's first gate: a proud material's consent to negotiate at all, granted only to hands the world is currently holding in mind — read in behavior, a lean or a ripple, never spoken.L4
the apprentice-stuffsStraw, dough, river clay, withy, tallow, chalk, coarse wool — the gregarious tier that answers any hands, dim or bright; every newborn's first courtship, every Quiet Walker's way back, and the stock from which handsels are whittled: anyone can make money, only circulation can make it worth anything.L4
singing-silverThe proud metal that will not take a shape unless sung to, one unbroken song from first heat to last pour; its lodes are common — what is rare is the singing, and the vouching before it. Sold with the last bars sung into the buyer's keeping, so the courtship travels live.L4
laughing-riverwoodThe green riverwood grown where the living come to be glad, which opens only for people who are laughing — clean splits under genuine laughter, ragged tears under a forced one — willing for about a season once cut: used glad and used soon, never hoarded.L4
festival-glassGlass blown before a crowd, which keeps its crowd — flecks of bunting-color drifting in it forever, warming a room like company — while glass blown alone clouds fog-grey in the annealing, every time; the whole craft is venue-bound, and its scarcity is the scarcity of gathered watching itself.L4
bell-bronzeA local proud stuff that will only pour while a crowd holds one breath.L4
hearth-slateA local proud stuff quarried sweetest while a story is being told over the work.L4
introduction-jarA jar of proud stock already courted — carried to you by a Mannerly or pressed on you in a glad-load — among the most valuable things a pack can hold, because it collapses weeks of courtship into a gift.L4
bright-shavingsThe warm leavings of attended work: the room's loose-returned regard settles thickest at the bench and what fell from it, and the custom of telling whoever receives one where it fell binds the sheen — a shaving told-of is a handsel already begun, the held home entered early, lapsing in a season if nobody delights.L4
the shavings-jarThe squat jar in a maker's pack where warm leavings are kept against whittling-nights; the finest coins in circulation began as the floor-sweepings of famous work, and everyone who receives one is told so.L4
the open purseThe Rounds' purse-craft: money carried open-work — nets, loops, coins strung at the belt — so every handsel rides the day in the world's attending; an open purse keeps a coin's waking the way walking keeps warmth — longer, never forever — while a shut box dulls a coin in a season.L4
the glad-priceHow prices are read on the Rounds: no stall posts a number — handsels are laid down until the pile in the keeper's palm sings; a grudged trade wakes nothing, and glad change comes back a shade brighter than the price strictly wanted. Haggling is a delight-craft of making the other's coins brighten.L4
the glad-loadWhat follows an answered asking, since no town pays a maker: delighted hands press the pack full at the gate — handsels rung bright by the square, a taught journey-piece, an introduction-jar, bread and wax and wool, road-marks chalked a day ahead — scaling with the gladness, which scales with the need.L4
the waymeet tableThe plank table under the fingerboard sign where the circuits cross, fed by whatever the last hundred walkers chose to leave — coins and held things because idleness dims them, fired pieces because a piece unperformed delights no one — never owed anything, only fed, the way a fire is fed.L4
the twice-benchedTimber and iron from a lovingly retired work: the old loose wash went to the table at the last lighting, but the fired sub-uses of beam and shaft — bearing, turning, taking weight — never unfire, so twice-benched grain wakes at nine parts the attention fresh grain wants. Sold with its stories; plenty downstream of tenderness, never of ruin.L4
last-redThe stubbornest pigment in the world, gathered in the left-behind where the hearth-line red — the last wash still bound — lets go seasons behind the rest; its patience is the firing's, not the powder's: it takes root only when laid as part of a woken re-making, and fades on schedule anywhere else.L4
the gleaningThe pale roads' harvest-craft, walked with soft brush, scraper, hazel, and chalk, under one strict manner: glean the left-behind, never the lived-in — a hearth still lit keeps its red. A thread minds what it touches, and only that: one thing, one threshold, one knack, held a season for the walker's return; it marks what is minded, never what must be done.L4
lane-glimmersThe small unowned brightenings a later walker gathers from a lane where work went dark: a lane's whole glimmer gathers less than the least of a maker's shine — a sip, never a cup, and never for the spiller, whose slack lines of cause the new color will never again answer.L4
the walking lightThe pace law of incoming shine: tellings walk one fireside a night, and only tellings move gleam or raise lantern readings — the murmur-net is the same net felt locally (pack-pieces tilt when a telling is a hearth or two out), and word (runners, road-marks) aims eyes, binds nothing, and moves no reading.L4
stillingThe rarest weather on the Rounds — three in living memory — a stretch of country whose Paling simply stops: nothing pales, nothing returns, color curdles waxy, mornings come thin and grey. It clears only at an equinox, and the first dawn returns the withheld season entire and exact, not a dram more.L4
the fresh verseThe ruling on the long forgetting: every re-spark is a fresh maker walking a fresh verse — the song keeps the verse and forgets the singer — so nothing of a past maker persists but what was given: fired works, road-marks, porch elders, woken things, and the glimmers in the lanes.L4
wander-yearWhat a run is: one maker's turn of one Round, walked from the morning after the spring even-morrow to the first still dawn of winter — the year trimmed the circuits to its own length long ago, so escalation is the season's own rising seep walking in step with the lantern ratchet.L5
the setting-out / the first-giftHow a run begins: a fresh maker makes some small true thing on waymeet ground before whoever happens to be there, the returning thread of shine is the whole starting stake, and the nearest lantern takes its first small fired reading — no rite, no roll, nothing conferred.L5
walking out of seasonThe road's name for the Quiet Walk's timing: Standing exhausted ends the year where it stands, in any season, because the Quiet Walk keeps no calendar — the one journey in Roundelay the year does not shape.L5
the four lightsThe legs of the walked year, named by light and weather: the Green Going (spring, seep barely waking), the Long Light (high summer, the biggest working days), the Deep Gold (late summer, the out-breath leaning in), and the Red Walk (autumn, combing weather) — with the Wintering, unwalked, when whatever a maker still carries is carried to one fire.L5
high daysThe year's fixed lights everyone walks toward — the two even-morrows, the Long Day of midsummer, the first-frost fires, the Fair's passage — which stitch the legs together and gather the year's great audiences, because a crowd is an occasion and the watchers live on the calendar.L5
lightshedThe basin of light: as rain has watersheds, light has lightsheds — the country whose seep runs downhill to one gloaming-table and out of whose table all its dawns rise; shed-lines are inked on every round as faint crown-ridges.L5
crown-countryThe rims of the lightsheds — shrines, beacon-stones, high pastures — first to pale and poor by valley measures, rich by three of its own: the weather-glass (when the crown goes grey, the floor is saving up), the readings (three sheds in one sight), and the shrine-custom; never gleaning ground, for no hearth ever bound a red up there.L5
the dawn-ladderThe elevation law: dawn climbs back up the channels the seep cut down and settles where the wash runs thinnest — paleness sets the size of a node's dawn, height sets its length and lateness, and the shed's own table sets the total, exact to the dram.L5
the inked roundA maker's kept map: oiled canvas inked at each night's fire, usually woken and attending (never acting), showing the world in its own notation — grey wash, chalk spirals, shed-ridges, red thread — with route-light brightening the walked line behind and pale pencil ahead.L5
threadingHow a route is planned: beads chosen and strung in the order the year will meet them, committed an arc at a time at each waymeet, never the whole year at once — and the thread can lace, figure-eighting back through a crossing, so returns are scheduled on purpose; skipped beads ripen at the chalked rate, so choosing a route quietly authors your own autumn.L5
the remembering roadsRoads have names, manners, and memories, so a route is a relationship: road-memory accrues to how you travel — marks kept up, tables fed, doorsteps answered — folding miles and shallowing fords on the return leg; an unwalked road is never punished, it simply stays a stranger.L5
the lantern-mileThe last mile into any town, which reads the whole town: marks thickening toward the gate, the spiral confirmed ring for ring, dawn-color reporting the table, rooftops greying crown-first — and the lantern itself legible from the ridge, with exactly one tier above tonight's showing previewed in silhouette before the rafters keep their dark.L5
the two paces of lightThe speed of regard: light is quick in the room and on foot on the road — present overflow lands the instant it is caused (the Moth Fair condition), and distant rises exist only as tellings walking one fireside a night, landing when seen landing; two speeds, and no third courier ever.L5
the traveling lanternThe Fair's asking-lantern on its tallest wagon, the only lantern in the world that moves — a town's tier of asking that rides, whose fired readings travel with it, making it the one lantern a maker meets twice in a year and the road's most personal ratchet.L5
