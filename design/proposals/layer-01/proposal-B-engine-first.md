# Layer 1 — Core Loop Pressure · Proposal B (ENGINE-FIRST)

**Proposer:** B-engine-first
**Layer:** L1 core_loop_pressure (FOUNDATION)
**Framing:** Lead with the attention engine as the heart of a turn. Contracts *frame goals* for the engine; Standing is the resource the engine's success/failure *moves*.
**Gate honored:** intuitiveness ≥ 0.8 — every core mechanic legible from its name + the world's own physics. No napkin, no depth floor at L1.

---

## 0. The one-sentence loop

> **You wake made things by paying attention to them — and every morning the world hands you back the attention it stopped using, so you can spend it re-making the country before it forgets itself.**

Everything below is that sentence turned into a turn. The engine is not "a combat system attached to a deckbuilder." The engine **is** the cosmology's one law — *nothing is noticed away; attention is conserved and circulates* — run at the speed of a single worked morning. Contracts point the engine at a target. Standing is the overflow the engine throws onto your skin, and the run ends when the world stops throwing it.

This is the whole reason the spine (D-001: escalating contracts + a depletable Standing) is *discovered, not installed*. We do not add a contract system and a health bar on top of an attention engine. We build the attention engine correctly, and contracts + Standing **fall out of it** as its input and its overflow.

## 1. The heart of a turn — the attention engine (ENGINE-FIRST)

A **turn is one worked morning at a node.** The engine drives it in five beats, and every beat is a direct reading of `the-round-of-light`:

### Beat 1 — Dawn accrues (income)
At turn-start, attention arrives as **the morning-draw**: a flat universal base **plus** a draw from the *local table* (the gloaming-table under this node). This is `the-unclaimed-morning` exactly — dawn is spent regard reissued, free to every eye that opens. **Pale nodes give richer dawns** (their table is fuller from everything the Paling collected there), so traveling faded country is a *lawful reward*, not just a risk. You also draw a hand of **journey-pieces** (cards) from your pack.

*Legibility:* the name says it. Morning = income. Pale places = big mornings. A player predicts this before it is ever explained.

### Beat 2 — The room gathers (the attention pool)
Made things turn toward the bench. Your starting attention pool for the turn = **morning-draw + the gathered room**: woken audience-things you brought (persistent artifacts), the townsfolk present, and the venue's own woken things. This is `audience-of-things` — *attention is a force, not a courtesy; makers work measurably better inside it.* The dread of the empty room is real: a thin room caps how bright anything you play can wake.

### Beat 3 — You give regard to made things (the play chain)
This is the moment-to-moment engine, and the seat of **per-turn agency**. To play a journey-piece is to **perform it in front of the work** — to spend attention *onto* a made thing. Two forces are always live:

- **Spend.** Each play draws attention from the pool as a **multiplier on that play** — the more of the room you aim at one piece, the brighter it can wake.
- **Sustain.** Playing pieces **back-to-back keeps the room with you** (`audience-of-things`: "sustained by unbroken work"). A **work-chain** holds the pool high; a **stall** (a pass, a dead hand, an unplayable turn) lets the room cool and the pool sag. So every turn is a real decision: *pour everything into one enormous waking now, or keep the chain alive and build?*

This is where depth-by-default lives even at L1: attention is simultaneously a **budget** (spend it) and a **momentum** (sustain it), and those two readings pull against each other every single play.

### Beat 4 — The waking threshold fires (the turn's payoff)
A played piece **wakes if and only if the attention resting on it meets its threshold** — `waking-of-made-things`, the crisp trigger the world already promised: *things wake when watched.* Above threshold, the card **fires**: it becomes a **durable woken card for the rest of the run** (`the-held-and-the-fired` — fired waking never un-fires). Below threshold, the play still *works* once but stays **inert and paling-prone** — it did its job and will be forgotten first. Spam-playing under-attended pieces gets you a hand of matches, not a workshop.

Crucially, **a woken card becomes a new audience-thing**: it now adds to the room's pool on later turns. The engine compounds — waking builds the audience that makes future waking easier. That is the whole strategic arc of a run in one rule.

### Beat 5 — The bench fans the attention three ways (conservation)
When an attended play resolves, its attention **splits three ways and not one dram is lost** — this is "conservation at the bench" made mechanical:

1. **SET** → into the card, as waking (Beat 4). *The fired share.*
2. **OVERFLOW** → back along the line of cause to *you*, as **Standing/gleam** (`gleam-runoff`). A card holds only so much; **attention poured past a card's ceiling — overkill — converts to Standing at full rate.** The biggest Standing swings in the game happen *on stage, in public, at the top of the engine.*
3. **RETURN** → loose to the node, **re-coloring it** (a pale square blooms back like watercolor), and any turn-end **surplus seeps to the local table and comes back in tomorrow's morning-draw** (`the-round-of-light` / `attention-conservation`). A "where it went" readout replaces discard-to-void — **nothing in the UI ever just disappears.**

**Dusk (turn end)** is the check-your-Standing beat: the maker's shoulder-candle (`diegetic-standing-readout`) shows where you stand; surplus seeps table-ward; and any asking carried past its season dims here.

> **The engine in one breath: morning gives you attention → the room decides how much you can wield → playing a chain spends and sustains it → the waking threshold turns spent attention into permanent woken cards → and every play's overflow lands as Standing while its remainder re-colors the world and seeds tomorrow's dawn.**

## 2. How contracts frame goals for the engine

The engine above is a fountain that pours in three directions. **An asking is what aims the fountain.** Contracts do not *drive* the loop; they give the engine a *target worth pointing at*, and the world's reward structure hangs off hitting it.

**Accepting an asking** (`the-askings`) is done with the hands: you take the little crafted token — a tin spoon with a dry bowl, a stitched swallow with one grey wing — down off the town's **asking-lantern**. From then on it **rides your pack** and **warms as its season shortens** (a visible, tactile countdown, never a red timer bar). Each asking states a **demand in legible iconography**: e.g. *wake a joinery-piece of this grain*, *re-color this square past this line*, *hold the room to this attention peak*. The demand is always something **the engine already produces** — SET, RETURN, or a peak of the pool — so a contract is never a separate minigame; it is a *named score* on the engine you're already running.

**Working the asking** = running the engine (§1) with its demand in view. **Fulfilling** it = the engine delivering the demanded SET/RETURN/peak before the season runs out.

**Reward** fans across every sub-economy at once (`contract-payoff-bundle`, the glad-load): bright handsels, one taught card into the pack, one shy-material introduction, and map-intel chalked outward — **and reward scales directly off the gatepost spiral** (rings in = load out). One number sets both the size of the ask and the size of the gift.

### Escalation — the world hearing of you (§spine, D-001)
Escalation is **not a difficulty dial**; it is `need-takes-aim` read as a generator, and it is one-directional by physics, not by design:

- **Tier keys off peak-Standing-this-run** — the *brightest the road has heard you be* — read from your gleam's **grain**, fired and **never falling** (`two-filter-lantern-sizing`, filter 1).
- **Offers key off current Standing** — what the town believes your hands can hold *tonight* (filter 2). So a dimming maker literally **watches the offers walk down the ladder** — rafters-work, then kettles, then buttons, then the lantern's dark — while the great reading waits overhead, unlowered.

This is **the two-filter lantern** and it makes the failure spiral *telegraph in the fiction itself*: you can always see, in what the lantern shows you, exactly how the world's regard is moving. Next-tier silhouettes hang in the rafters' warm dark (`contract-preview-ui`) — never greyed-out or padlocked.

Underneath, **the Paling runs its arithmetic on anything you skip** (`the-paling-arithmetic`): pass a three-ring town in spring and by harvest it is a five-ring town — twice the work, twice the reward, twice the wonder. Route choice *is* the escalation dial, and it is chalked on the map in spirals a player reads like an almanac. The **boss tier** is `the-combing`: when a region's summed spiral-count crosses threshold, a **great asking** forms — always weather the player *routed*, telegraphed seasons ahead.

> *Contract-schema obligation carried from I-022:* the core loop must expose a **contract COUNT/PACE** pressure axis alongside tier, so the Untold archetype's "many small askings, fast" reads as genuine escalation. Concretely: unanswered askings in a region **age into the spiral together** — clearing five small ones late is the same rising weather as one large one. Escalation is measured in *summed rings*, which tonnage and tempo both feed. (Numeric proof deferred to game-architect per D-005.3.)

## 3. Standing (gleam) — the resource the engine moves

Standing is **not a bar the engine damages.** Standing **is the engine's overflow, worn on the skin** (`makers-gleam` / the gleam cycle). The engine's success and failure *are* the only things that move it.

**How it moves:**
- **Up:** every OVERFLOW from an attended play (Beat 5.2), every **overkill** past a card's ceiling at full rate, and every delight given in any coin the world honors — an audience moved, a hearth warmed, a laugh set loose. Some arrivals are **delayed**, walking the road-graph at a hearth's pace and landing source-stamped ("Wader's Ford still tells of the reel — +2"), so every gain traces to a deed (`delayed-tellings`).
- **Down, on one clock only — outcomes:** a **failed or abandoned asking**, an **unmoved room** (a performance that never crossed a waking threshold), an asking **carried past its season**. Loss slides off where you stand and **re-colors the node as small ambient boons for later visitors** (`the-spilling`) — failure composts, never punishes, and the spill is always worth less than it cost so a flop is never a strategy.

**What it gates (soft, so it reads as warmth not HP):**
- Which **askings the lantern shows** you (filter 2 above).
- Which **shy materials** will even state their terms (`two-gate-courting`, gate 1) — the market widens as you brighten, narrows to the apprentice-stuffs at the bottom.
- Small graces: prices, doors, the road's welcome.

**Grain, not a scalar:** Standing carries the *texture* of the delights that earned it (`the-grain-of-gleam`) — "bridge-grained" vs "song-grained" runs — which gates *which* askings and materials open to you, giving builds identity with **no class menu**.

**The fail-state — the Quiet Walk (`the-quiet-walk`):** at **Standing zero**, all but the apprentice-stuffs refuse your hands, the engine goes quiet, and the run ends — **not death, the world going shy.** A brief playable epilogue revisits this run's woken works; one persists into the meta-layer as a permanent audience-thing or starting boon for the next maker. The light didn't go out; it moved into the work.

**Grammar-of-light guard (binding):** the Paling attacks **board-state, resources, and routes only — never the Standing meter directly** (`the-three-settlings`). Places pale, people gleam. The player always knows which losses are the world's weather (board) and which are their own (gleam).

## 4. The three-way exchange gate — why the engine is the *only* economy

One rule holds the whole design together and is the deepest thing L1 locks: **node color, Standing, and card wakefulness never convert into each other directly.** Every transfer between the three settlings **must pass through a played card** (`play-as-exchange-gate` / `the-three-settlings`). Playing a journey-piece is **the world's one and only exchange gate** — mechanically and cosmologically at once.

You cannot spend Standing to re-color a node. You cannot cash a woken card into money. You *play a piece*, and the bench fans attention across all three (§1, Beat 5). This is what makes the moment-to-moment loop the entire game: **there is no bypass.** Every strategic goal, from waking your engine to answering the boss to keeping your Standing lit, routes through the same act of attentive play.

## 5. Run structure & win condition

**Nesting (all traced to `the-wander-year` / `the-inked-round`):**

| Scale | Name | What it is |
|---|---|---|
| Moment | a **play** | one journey-piece performed onto a made thing (the exchange gate) |
| **Turn** | a **worked morning** | dawn → gather room → play chain → dusk check; the engine's full five beats |
| Leg | a **road between high days** | travel a segment; road-memory buffs apply, the Paling ticks, askings warm & compound |
| **Run** | a **verse of a Round** (the wander-year) | one maker's seasonal circuit, **spring to the first still dawn** |

The **seasonal calendar track is the escalation clock**: the Paling's seep curve steepens toward autumn, so the run's back half is its hardest and brightest. The map is a **procedural circuit** — a loop with a home note, not a branching tree toward a boss (`run-map-circuit`) — and **route commitment is the core strategic layer**: which pale towns to answer now, which to trust to a compounded future.

**Two run-end triggers:**
1. **Standing → 0** at any time → **the Quiet Walk** (loss / meta-seed). §3.
2. **Calendar's end** (the first still dawn) → the finale.

**Win condition — the Crown of the Year (`crown-finale-scheduling` → `winter-telling-victory`):** the run's finale is a **guaranteed year-end great asking** whose tier is computed honestly from the player's *own* skip-arithmetic summed over the year (the boss you routed). **Standing the crown** — answering that finale with the engine and pack you built, before your Standing gutters — is the win. It resolves into **the Winter Telling**: a playable victory epilogue where you choose the wintering fire and the one bead that goes on walking into the next verse (new-game-plus). *You do not win by killing anything; you win by re-making something the size of weather, in public, and being bright enough at year's end to be asked.*

## 6. Named core-loop design elements

Nine elements. Each is a concretization of locked canon into the playable loop — not new invention. `id` (kebab-case) · one-line mechanic · **trace** (WORLD.md element / ledger id).

1. **`morning-draw`** — Turn-start attention income = flat universal base + a draw from the local table; pale nodes pay richer mornings; end-of-turn surplus returns in tomorrow's draw. **Trace:** `the-unclaimed-morning` / ledger `dawn-income`.

2. **`gathered-room`** — The turn's attention pool is seeded by the audience of woken things present (your fired cards, townsfolk, the venue); a thin room caps how bright anything can wake, a full room lifts the ceiling. **Trace:** `audience-of-things` / ledger `attention-engine`.

3. **`work-chain`** — Playing journey-pieces back-to-back *sustains* the room and holds the pool high; a stall lets it cool — attention is a budget you spend and a momentum you keep, in tension every play. **Trace:** `audience-of-things` (“sustained by unbroken work”) / ledger `attention-engine`.

4. **`waking-threshold`** — A played piece wakes (fires into a durable woken card for the whole run, and joins the room as a new audience-thing) iff the attention on it meets its threshold; under-attended play stays inert and paling-prone. **Trace:** `waking-of-made-things` + `the-held-and-the-fired` / ledger `waking-threshold-play`, `fired-vs-held`.

5. **`bench-fan`** — Every attended play splits its attention three ways with none lost: SET into the card (waking), OVERFLOW to the maker (Standing), RETURN loose to the node (re-color) with surplus seeding tomorrow's dawn. **Trace:** “Conservation at the bench” + `the-round-of-light` / ledger `attention-conservation`.

6. **`overkill-to-gleam`** — Attention poured past a card's ceiling converts to Standing at full rate, so pushing a performance past “enough” is never waste and the biggest Standing swings happen in public. **Trace:** `gleam-runoff` / ledger `overkill-to-standing`.

7. **`play-is-the-gate`** — Node color, Standing, and card wakefulness never convert directly; every transfer must pass through a played card, making the act of play the world's only exchange gate. **Trace:** `the-three-settlings` / ledger `play-as-exchange-gate`.

8. **`asking-frame`** — Accepting a crafted asking-token points the engine at a legible demand it already produces (a woken card of a grain / a re-color / an attention peak); it rides the pack, warms as its season shortens, and fulfilling it fans a glad-load reward across every sub-economy. **Trace:** `the-askings` / ledger `escalating-contracts`, `contract-payoff-bundle`.

9. **`two-lantern-sizing`** — An asking's TIER is fired to your peak-Standing-this-run and never falls, while its OFFERS are gated by current Standing — so escalation reads as the world hearing of you and the failure spiral telegraphs in the lantern itself. **Trace:** `need-takes-aim` / ledger `two-filter-lantern-sizing`.

10. **`standing-quiet`** — Standing is the engine's overflow worn on the skin: it rises on overflow/overkill/delight, falls only on failed askings and unmoved rooms (spilling boons into the node, never punishing), gates soft graces and which askings/materials open — and at zero ends the run into the Quiet Walk. **Trace:** `makers-gleam` + `the-spilling` + `the-quiet-walk` / ledger `gleam-standing`, `spill-boons`, `quiet-walk-runend-meta`.

*(Ten listed; §6 is the 6–10 band the layer asks for. If a tighter set is wanted, `work-chain` and `gathered-room` may fold into a single `attention-engine` element and `two-lantern-sizing` may fold into `asking-frame`, giving a clean 7.)*

## 7. Spine & canon fidelity check

- **D-001 spine — escalating contracts:** ✅ `asking-frame` + `two-lantern-sizing`; escalation is one-directional by fired reading + Paling compounding, never a decline-able system. Count/pace axis exposed for I-022.
- **D-001 spine — depletable Standing fail-resource:** ✅ `standing-quiet`; zero → Quiet Walk run-end. Standing is the engine's overflow, not an added bar.
- **Engine-first mandate:** ✅ the turn *is* the attention engine (§1); contracts frame its goal (§2), Standing is what it moves (§3).
- **Grammar of light** (places pale / people gleam / things wake): ✅ Paling touches board-state only; Standing is the maker's key-light; waking is one gold edge on a card. Guard in §3.
- **Attention conservation** (nothing noticed away): ✅ `bench-fan`'s three-way split + surplus-returns-tomorrow; “where it went” readout, no discard-to-void.
- **Play-as-only-exchange-gate:** ✅ `play-is-the-gate` (§4) — no bypass of the moment-to-moment loop anywhere.
- **Register bans** (no owed/debt/promise/penalty; failure is ecological & mnemonic): ✅ loss = the world growing shy + spilled boons; no punitive/transactional language.
- **Anti-generic / D-002 wonder:** ✅ no HP bar (shoulder-candle), no XP (grain), no shop (hearth-right gift), no discard-void (return-to-table); the engine's fireworks are the Moth-Fair overkill moment.

**No spine conflict found.** Nothing in this loop demands a change to locked canon, so there is no P0 to raise.

## 8. Intuitiveness self-assessment (the L1 gate)

Every core mechanic predicts itself from its name + the world's stated physics:
- *morning-draw* → “the morning gives you the day's light” — and pale places give more, because that's where the light collected. Predictable.
- *waking-threshold* → “things wake when watched” — the world says this in Layer 1; the mechanic is the literal sentence.
- *bench-fan* → “a full cup runs to the pourer; the rest returns to the room” — the conservation law, played.
- *overkill-to-gleam* → “push past enough and it runs to you” — Immy Ash at the Moth Fair.
- *two-lantern-sizing* → “the town shows you what it believes your hands can hold, sized to the brightest it's heard you be.”
- *standing-quiet* → “disappoint and the world grows shy; go dark and take the Quiet Walk.”

Effect matches metaphor in every case; no mechanic needs a menu to explain it. **Self-scored intuitiveness ≥ 0.8.**
