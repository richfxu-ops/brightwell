# Layer 4 — Acquisition Model · Proposal B · MARKET / DRAFT

**Stance committed:** *Acquisition is a place you walk to.* New content enters a run because the maker **routes toward the source of it** — town material markets whose width is gated by shine, the **Round-Fair** as the year's one great draft/trade hub, **waymeet** swap-tables at the crossings, and **teachers** on the ways who give pieces for a performance or a bright handsel. The glad-load still pays at every answered gate (canon, LOCKED), but the maker is not merely *paid*; the maker **shops, drafts, courts, and gleans**, and *which* new pieces, materials, and handsels reach the pack is authored by the route the maker threaded through the country. Season and the Paling gate *what a place has to offer* — pale/left-behind country offers **last-red and salvage-grade** stock and enormous glad-loads; bright kept towns offer **proud stock, proud on the shelf**; crowds (fairs, high days, red-thread-days) gate the venue-bound stuff. Thinning is by **`retire`** (last-lighting), by **gifting to the waymeet table**, and by **freshness reversion**.

Every mechanism below traces to a locked WORLD.md/ledger id and honors the grammar of light, the L2 resource square, and the L3 primitive set. No generic card-shop appears anywhere: nothing posts a number, proud materials are never *bought*, teaching moves by gift/performance, and the "hub" is a moving node you must intercept.

---

## 0. The thesis — supply is geography, not a menu

Canon already forbids the shop: *"scarcity in Roundelay is manners, not tonnage"* (L4), *"nothing is mined… everything worth having is asked"* (`shy-materials`), *"no stall on the Rounds posts a number"* (`the-glad-price`), *"the table is never owed anything, only fed"* (`the-waymeet-table`). So the acquisition model cannot be a catalog the player scrolls. It is a **map of faucets**, each one **living on a place, a season, and a shine-gate**, and the strategic act is **the mending question worn as a supply chain**: *which country will I let feed me this leg, and what will I have to have grown to accept what it offers?*

The model has exactly **three things that enter the pack** — the three settlings that *can* change hands (`WORLD.md`:543) — and each has its own family of place-sources:

| Enters the pack | = L2 resource | Canon class (what may change hands) | Never enters by… |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Journey-pieces** (new cards) | R5 deck | *the set* — "given and taught… by gift, teaching, trading, and winning" | purchase of a completed masterwork off a rack; every card arrives *storied* or *taught* |
| **Courted stock** (materials) | R6 | *the loose* — "courted… by manners, met on its own terms" | any coin doing the vouching's job |
| **Handsels** (money) | R2 | *the held* — "loose light bound by circulating delight" | a mint; money is *whittled* and *brightened by circulation*, never issued |

**One-sentence intuitiveness anchor (the L4 gate):** *walk to where the stuff lives, be bright enough that it will deal with you, and give something worth giving to take it home.*

**The four sourcing verbs the player already knows** (all diegetic, all canon): **the gate loads you** (glad-load, passive, on the fulfilment), **the market courts you** (active, gleam-gated, place-bound), **the Fair drafts you** (active, periodic, intercept-timed), **the road gives to you** (waymeet swaps, teachers, doorsteps, gleaning). No fifth channel — Roundelay keeps no office that could hold one (`Who hangs the need`).

## 1. The full acquisition model — every source of new content

Each source: **what it is · how it works · what gates/costs it · trace.** Grouped by the three things that enter the pack, then the shared cross-cutting nodes.

### 1A. New JOURNEY-PIECES (R5 — cards into the run-deck)

> **Framing law (LOCKED, L1/L2):** a card entering the deck is not a card *woken*. Acquisition puts an *inert, paling-prone* piece in the pack; it becomes a fired engine-part only by being **played at/above its waking-mark** (`the-waking-mark`, `fired-vs-held`). So no acquisition source can bypass `play-is-the-only-gate` — every one of these delivers *potential*, and the room still has to pay for it. This is the structural reason a fast draft cannot trivialize the run (§5).

**P1 · The gate-taught piece (the glad-load's card share) — passive, on fulfilment.**
*What:* every answered asking loads the pack with **one journey-piece, taught at the gate by whoever the town loves best**, arriving *with that town's name written on it* (`the-giving-line`/`deck-autobiography` — the deck is an autobiography of teachers).
*How:* fires automatically as one strand of `the-glad-load` when a need's-fill completes (`on-fulfil`). The taught piece's **grain and tier scale off the gatepost spiral** — a five-ring pale town teaches a prouder piece than a kettle-town.
*Gate/cost:* you must **answer the asking** — accept it by hand, work it, fulfil it under the season clock. The cost is the whole loop; the piece is the compliment.
*Trace:* `the-glad-load`/`contract-payoff-bundle`; `journey-pieces-deck`; `the-giving-line`. L1 §3; L2 System D.

**P2 · The Round-Fair draft — active, periodic, the year's great card hub.**
*What:* the one node that moves. For three days a pale square remembers every color it ever had and **journey-pieces change hands by teaching, winning, and plain delight** (`the-round-fair`). This is the run's **draft/trade hub**: a spread of offered pieces from all six ways laid across the wagons, of which the maker takes a bounded few.
*How:* three legible acquisition lanes at the Fair, each priced in its own canon coin, none in numbers —
  1. **Teaching lane** — press bright handsels into a teacher's palm through `the-glad-price` (a *read*, wares set out, coins laid until the palm sings). Handsels **may** buy taught pieces (L2 R2 sink) — but the price is a wakefulness *read*, and a grudged trade wakes nothing. Bounded by your bright purse.
  2. **Winning lane** — `the-fair-answer` and stage-contests: answer an asking beside a rival way, and **which work wakes** takes the full reward (the piece + the telling); the loser is gifted the smaller piece anyway (*"nothing made before a whole town is ever wasted"*). Bounded by contest slots and your engine.
  3. **Delight lane** — a piece pressed on a maker the crowd loves, the way the town loads a gate. Bounded by your standing with the Fair's rafters (the traveling lantern *remembers you*, `a-week-ahead-of-autumn`).
*Gate/cost:* **the intercept** — you must route to meet the wagons, which ride *a week ahead of autumn* on a track readable in spring (`a-week-ahead-of-autumn`). Miss the intercept and this whole draft is gone until the wheel brings it round. Plus, per lane: bright handsels / a contest-worthy engine / Fair-standing. **The Fair retells everything at twice size**, so drafting its stage is the fastest deck-growth *and* the fastest ladder-raise in one act (`the-round-fair` — escalation and reward in the same visit).
*Trace:* `the-round-fair`; `fair-hub-intercept`; `the-fair-answer`; `fairwright-multiplier-spike`; `a-week-ahead-of-autumn`; `the-glad-price`. L2 System B/D.

**P3 · The waymeet table — active, free, deck-*re-shaping* at the crossings.**
*What:* at every circuit-crossing, a plank table under the fingerboard sign holding *whatever the last hundred walkers left* (`the-waymeet-table`). **Leave a piece, take a piece** — and a taken piece *arrives already storied* (*"this whistle has crossed the country twice"*), which is a **head start on waking** (a lower effective first-wake cost, canon-legal because the piece carries part of its set already).
*How:* a swap node, no shopkeeper, no accounts. You take what you will keep bright and leave what you won't; the gifted piece's small blessing follows you a leg down the road (`the-waymeet-table` hook).
*Gate/cost:* **near net-zero card count** by construction (leave one to take one — this is *re-shaping*, not net inflation; see §5). Cost is the *give*: you surrender a piece you were carrying. Gated by *what previous walkers happened to leave* (route- and run-dependent supply; your own past runs seed it, per `road-marks` meta-persistence).
*Trace:* `the-waymeet-table`; `journey-pieces-deck`; `run-map-circuit` (crossings). L2 System B (swaps).

**P4 · Walking-with-masters / season-shadowing — active, on the ways' stretches.**
*What:* the way-affiliation card-pool acquisition. Fall in behind a master on their way's stretch and keep their hours (**season-shadowing**), or meet a walking master as a map-event; **it ends with one journey-piece, given, because giving is how it moves** (`the-walking-ways`). A shadowed segment exits with an **off-way card carrying that master's name** (every splash card has a master's name on it).
*How:* adopt a master's **constraint** for a map segment (keep dusk hours / give every handsel / play only fired cards) and exit carrying their gift piece; multi-way decks legal, grain bonuses reward focus.
*Gate/cost:* the constraint itself (a self-imposed play-restriction for the segment) — a tempo/route cost, not a coin. Gated by *which way's stretch you route through* (`the-walking-ways` stretches drawn in the map's marginalia).
*Trace:* `the-walking-ways`/`walking-ways-cardpools`; `the-giving-line`. L5 archetypes.

**P5 · The bench — active, off-turn, material → piece.**
*What:* the maker **works courted stock (R6) into new pieces at the bench** between turns (not an in-turn primitive — a between-turn craft action, like packing, per L3 §1 "what is NOT a primitive"). This is how proud materials *become* deckable cards.
*How:* off-turn, spend courted stock of a grade to craft a piece whose grain and waking-mark reflect the material (proud stock → prouder, higher-mark pieces). Twice-benched stock crafts pieces with a **discounted first waking-mark** (nine parts the attention fresh grain wants; `the-twice-benched`).
*Gate/cost:* consumes courted stock (which is itself gleam-gated + performed to acquire, §1B) and off-turn tempo. Naturally rate-limited by material supply and freshness.
*Trace:* `the-twice-benched`; `journey-pieces-deck`; `two-gate-courting` (feeds it). L2 System C→A.

**P6 · The setting-out first-gift — the starter loadout (once, at spring even-morrow).**
*What:* the run's opening pack. The fresh maker makes one small true thing before whoever is on the waymeet ground; the town then loads the side-loops with mostly-dull handsels, **someone's grandmother teaches one journey-piece at the gate**, and a child chalks the first road-mark (`the-wander-year` setting-out; L1).
*How:* the first-gift (what you make + who is watching) seeds **starting grain, way-affinity, and the first lantern reading**; the town's welcome seeds ~6–8 apprentice-tier pieces + a net-purse of dull handsels + a lump of apprentice stock.
*Gate/cost:* none — it is a gift and a right (the loadout). Out-of-season sparks (a Quiet Walker re-lit) are unlockable alternate openings.
*Trace:* `the-wander-year` (setting-out); `hearth-right`; `the-apprentice-stuffs`. L1; L5.

### 1B. New COURTED STOCK (R6 — materials, one graded class)

> **Framing law (LOCKED, L2 §8 P1 / L3 §5):** proud/courted materials are **NEVER purchased**. Acquisition is the **two-gate courting** — first **the vouching** (a *gleam gate*: will the stuff state its terms to hands the world holds in mind — gleam's business, *no coin's*), then **the terms** (a *performable* micro-condition, executed as the `court` primitive, a play). Handsels may buy the *already-courted jar* and the *apprentice tier*, never do the vouching's job. Freshness reverts untended stock (garden, not hoard).

**M1 · The town material market — active, place-bound, gleam-gated *width*.**
*What:* the proud-stock stalls of a town. The market **widens as Standing rises and narrows to the apprentice tier at the bottom** (`shy-materials`) — the vouching gate made spatial: at low shine the silver stall goes quiet as you pass (*"that silence is the tutorial"*, L4 arc), at high shine every shelf leans in.
*How:* stand at the stall; the vouching (gleam) decides *which grades will state terms this run*; you then **`court`** — perform the stated term (`singing-silver`: an unbroken play-chain, no stall; `laughing-riverwood`: a "room is laughing" token; bell-bronze: a crowd holding one breath; hearth-slate: a telling over the work) — and the stock enters the pack with its courtship *live*, to be kept warm or lost.
*Gate/cost:* **gleam gates width** (Filter-1-style vouching, read from grain, never spent); **the performed term costs a play** (attention/tempo, in-turn via `court`). The *supply differs by place* (M4).
*Trace:* `shy-materials`/`two-gate-courting`; `graded-material-market`; `grain-tagged-standing`; `singing-silver`, `laughing-riverwood`, bell-bronze, hearth-slate. L2 System C; L3 `court`.

**M2 · The glad-load introduction-jar — passive, on fulfilment.**
*What:* every answered asking loads **one introduction-jar of the local proud stuff, already courted** (`the-glad-load`) — *"among the most valuable things a pack can hold, because it collapses weeks of courtship into a gift."*
*How:* fires as one strand of the glad-load `on-fulfil`; the jar banks a courtship you can spend later without the vouching or the term.
*Gate/cost:* answer the asking (the whole loop). Jar grade scales off the spiral.
*Trace:* `introduction-jars-freshness`; `the-glad-load`. L2 System D→C.

**M3 · Festival-glass & the crowd-bound stock — active, venue-gated by season/crowd.**
*What:* materials whose scarcity *is the scarcity of gathered watching* — `festival-glass` ("no crowd, no glass"), and by the same law any crowd-bound proud stock (bell-bronze pours only while a crowd holds one breath).
*How:* acquirable **only at high-attention nodes** — fairs, festivals, high days, red-thread-days. The glassworks travel with the Round-Fair (venue-bound).
*Gate/cost:* **the calendar is the gate** — a crowd is an occasion, not a faucet (L4). You must be *at a gathering* to court it. Fairwright-affine; works made of it grant small passive attention.
*Trace:* `festival-glass`/`graded-material-market`; `the-four-lights` (high days as venues); `a-week-ahead-of-autumn`. L2 System C.

**M4 · The gleaning — active, pale/left-behind country, the salvage-grade tier.**
*What:* the **pale-route harvest**. On the long grey lived-then-left roads, the hearth-line red seeps last, and the lag is a harvest: walk the left-behind with brush and jar and come in *"rich in exact proportion to how far into the quiet you were willing to walk"* (`last-red`). Yields **last-red** (a catalyst, not deckable) and — from Evener wagons trailing chosen last-lightings — **twice-benched stock** (the eased-waking grade).
*How:* a scout-plus-gather action whose **yield scales with node paleness** (`pale-route-harvest`); last-red is spent only as a catalyst on **woken re-makings** (via `soothe`) to slow a node's future paling, and ground into red thread (the bookmark).
*Gate/cost:* **route risk** — you must thread the pale/grey country (highest route risk in the game; Gleaner-affine). Strict manners: **never triggers on inhabited or answered nodes** (*"you glean the left-behind, never the lived-in"* — the anti-salvage law, §5). Twice-benched keys **only to chosen last-lightings, never failure**.
*Trace:* `last-red`/`pale-route-harvest`; `the-twice-benched`; `red-thread-marker`; `gleaner-adversity-economy`. L2 System D/S5; L3 `soothe`.

**M5 · Apprentice-stuffs — the floor, zero-gate, always at every node.**
*What:* straw, dough, river clay, withy, tallow, chalk, coarse wool — *"the gregarious tier, the stuffs that answer any hands at all, dim or bright"* (`the-apprentice-stuffs`).
*How:* **bypasses the vouching entirely** — always courtable at every node, the comeback path out of the Quiet Walk and the daily bread-work between commissions.
*Gate/cost:* free of the gleam gate, but its works **wake thin and pale fastest** — a doorway, never a destination. *Also the raw stock of handsel-whittling* (→ H1).
*Trace:* `the-apprentice-stuffs`; `two-gate-courting` (bypass). L2 System C floor.

**M6 · Waymeet-table & Mannerly-lent jars — active, the gift-lane for courtships.**
*What:* introduction-jars and spare stock left on the waymeet table (idle held things go there because idleness dims them), and jars a Mannerly presses on you or lends (the notebooks lent and returned like hearts).
*How:* take a jar from the table (leave something bright-that-you-won't-keep in exchange); or receive a Mannerly's introduction as a map-event gift.
*Gate/cost:* the give (waymeet), or your standing with the way. Jars are *earned/gifted, never minted* (§5, anti-arbitrage).
*Trace:* `the-waymeet-table`; `introduction-jars-freshness`; `the-mannerly`. L2 System B/C.

### 1C. New HANDSELS (R2 — money)

> **Framing law (LOCKED, L2):** *"anyone can make money; only circulation can make it worth anything."* Handsels are **whittled** (faucet) and **brightened by circulation** (never minted). The only sink is idle-lapse → gloaming-table → tomorrow's dawn income; **the money's only sink is the world's only income** (closed, mint-clean).

**H1 · Whittling from apprentice-stuffs — the money faucet.**
*What:* every coin in circulation *"began as somebody's glad afternoon of small work"* (`the-apprentice-stuffs`) — a withy blank whittled into a near-dull handsel.
*How:* off-turn or as bread-work; consumes apprentice stock (M5, zero-gate) and produces near-dull handsels that only *circulation* brightens.
*Gate/cost:* apprentice stock + tempo. Produces *dull* money — worthless until moved.
*Trace:* `the-apprentice-stuffs`; `handsels`/`handsel-currency-decay`. L2 System B faucet.

**H2 · `the-shavings-share` — the bench→money coupling (in-turn, `whittle`).**
*What:* great work leaves warm leavings; the bench's own **third share** (the RETURN wash settling thickest where the work happened) is bound by *the telling* into a handsel-already-begun (`bright-shavings`).
*How:* the L3 `whittle` primitive carves **a bound sub-portion of this play's bench-RETURN** into handsels — *carved from the RETURN, never minted on top* (it competes with the node's local-table dawn; §5). `read(season)` may scale it (paleness → more money, never Standing — the Gleaner adversity economy, firewall-legal).
*Gate/cost:* play-gated; the share it takes is *taken from* tomorrow's node-local dawn — a genuine trade-off, not free money.
*Trace:* `the-shavings-share`/`bright-shavings-byproduct`; `attention-conservation`. L2 System A→B; L3 `whittle`.

**H3 · The glad-load's bright handsels — passive, on fulfilment.**
*What:* the town presses **handsels rung bright that very evening by the whole square's glad palms** into the pack at the gate (`the-glad-load`) — the single richest flow in a maker's economy, scaled to the spiral.
*How:* one strand of the glad-load `on-fulfil`; unlike whittled money these arrive *already bright* (circulated by a whole square's delight).
*Gate/cost:* answer the asking. Scales off the gatepost spiral (pale town = bright cart-load).
*Trace:* `the-glad-load`. L2 System D→B.

**H4 · The glad-price & haggling — brightening-by-circulation (not net mint).**
*What:* *change comes back a shade brighter than the price strictly wanted* when giving goes well; haggling is a delight-craft where each side brightens the other's coins (`the-glad-price`).
*How:* spending into delight returns brighter change; a good haggle leaves both purses more awake.
*Gate/cost:* **brightening-per-trade is bounded below its tempo/attention cost** (gladness must be *performed under attention*, spending the scarce current — the napkin obligation, §5). Not a purse-printer.
*Trace:* `the-glad-price`/`glad-price-read`. L2 System B.

**H5 · Doorstep askings — passive, small, at hamlets.**
*What:* the road's smallest tier of need on a household's door-nail, paying *"a handsel or two"* + rest + welcome (`the-doorstep-askings`).
*How:* first-come, unsized, no reading taken (no lantern present); the household's glad palms ring a handsel or two bright, and their telling walks like any other.
*Gate/cost:* sparse **by law** — one nail to a hamlet, none on between-roads, priced **below the apprentice-stuff comeback loop** so it is the road's small change, never its bread.
*Trace:* `the-doorstep-askings`. L1 §8; L2 System B.

**H6 · Hearth & waymeet gifts.**
*What:* hearth-side gifts and coins taken from the waymeet table (leave what is lapsing on you, take what you will keep bright).
*How:* `hearth-right` is rest+telling (never a coin — the teller is warmed, not paid), but glad hosts *do* press small handsels; the waymeet table converts idle held things.
*Gate/cost:* the give; gifts are gifts.
*Trace:* `hearth-right`; `the-waymeet-table`. L2 System B.

## 2. The acquisition arc — thin deck → mid engine → late power

The arc is the **run's economy arc worn as a supply curve** (`WORLD.md` "The arc of a run's economy") — and it rises on its own with the season, because *what the country offers* changes with the Paling, not because the player unlocks a shop tier.

| Leg (season) | Deck supply | Material supply | Handsel supply | The felt shape |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Green Going** (spring) | ~6–8 apprentice pieces (first-gift); +1 taught piece per answered kettle-asking; waymeet swaps thin. **The shy market:** proud stalls quiet as you pass. | Apprentice-stuffs (M5) only, mostly; a lump of clay, chalk. Vouching closed at low shine. | Mostly-dull whittled coins (H1); the odd doorstep handsel (H5); glimmers in the lanes. | **Scarcity as shyness.** The pack is light; income is the thin ordinary dawn. Your one reliable wealth is the morning. Deck grows slowly and every piece must be woken. |
| **Long Light / Deep Gold** (summer) | **The engine turns.** Glad-loads bring taught pieces + jars; the **Fair crosses your circuit** (P2 draft burst); first **last-lighting** yields twice-benched (eased wakings); walking-with-masters on the ways' stretches. | Vouching opens with rising gleam (M1); introduction-jars ride the pack-crown (M2); the glassworks know your face at the Fair (M3); gleaning country opens in the Deep Gold (M4). | Glad-loads chime bright (H3); the shavings-jar glows (H2); glad-price haggling brightens the purse (H4). | **The garden in full tending** — everything working or being kept warm. The deck reaches its mid-size engine; courted stock widens as shine rises. |
| **Red Walk** (autumn) | **Power to stand the crown.** Everything states its terms; the **Fair retells at twice size** (fastest draft + ladder-raise); red-thread-days deliver **proud materials pre-courted** as boss-modifiers. Deck at its fired-core peak. | Full width — proud stock leans from every shelf; pale country routed on purpose for **last-red + twice-benched**; the biggest glad-loads (five-ring carts, seven-ring *seasons*). | The purse sings and cannot sit; the richest glad-loads; abundance that must keep moving (*"rich the way a juggler is rich: only mid-air"*). | **Abundance under pressure.** Supply peaks exactly as the crown does — you acquire the standable engine *by having reached*, and the crown converges where you routed. |

**The arc's load-bearing property:** supply rises with the *same clock* as difficulty (the season-seep). The glad-load scales off the spiral (rings-in = load-out), pale country both *pressures* you and *feeds* you richest, and the Fair's twice-size draft *raises the ladder as it grows your deck*. So **you cannot out-acquire the curve by turtling** (a static engine gets kettle-loads all year and is out-grown), and you cannot trivially *over*-acquire (each source is intercept-, shine-, or route-gated). Growth tracks reach. Consistent with L1 §5's `the-reach` and L2 §5(b) on-curve-clearable.

## 3. Card thinning & removal — the pack is a garden, not a hoard

A market/draft model **must** thin, or the deck bloats past playability. Four canon removal channels, none punitive:

**T1 · `retire` (last-lighting) — the primary thinner.** The L3 `retire` primitive: **last-light a held/inert piece — perform it to destruction (permanent thin)** and RETURN its worth to *this node's* local table (richer dawn tomorrow) or the room now (`evener-sacrifice-velocity`; `the-twice-benched`). Retirement in Roundelay is *chosen, tended, festival-lit* — never scavenge, never a failure state. It is **generative removal**: a leaner deck draws better all run, the RETURN feeds income, and the last-lighting yields twice-benched stock (M4) that wakes easier next time. Trace: `retire`; `evener-sacrifice-velocity`; `the-twice-benched`; `attention-conservation`.

**T2 · Gifting to the waymeet table — thinning as generosity.** Leave a piece you won't keep bright (P3): *"a masterpiece in a locked pack earns nothing for anybody"* — the idle fired piece walks, its small blessing following you a leg. Net-neutral to the *table's* economy, a thinner for *your* deck. Trace: `the-waymeet-table`.

**T3 · Perform-to-destruction (exhaust / paper-bird one-shots).** Pieces given away or performed to destruction — the Untold's cheap fine-grained one-shots that exhaust and never repeat (`untold-ratchet-tempo`, paper-bird pattern). A tempo-thinning built into play. Trace: `journey-pieces-deck` (exhaust); `untold-ratchet-tempo`.

**T4 · Freshness reversion (material-side thinning).** Courted stock **reverts to ordinary if not kept warm within a season** (`introduction-jars-freshness`) — inventory-as-garden, freshness in place of weight limits. This thins the *material* side automatically, and a reverted stock's pieces pale fastest, pushing the upgrade path. Trace: `introduction-jars-freshness`; `graded-material-market`.

**Why thinning stays on-curve with acquisition:** the Evener archetype makes retirement an *engine* (self-thinning for conversion), so the fastest-acquiring builds are also the fastest-thinning; slower builds acquire slower and need less thinning. The two rates are coupled through the same `retire`/glad-load loop. No build is forced to bloat, and none can thin into an empty pack (apprentice-stuffs are always re-courtable, M5 — the comeback floor).

## 4. How season/Paling and gleam gate supply

Two independent gates, on two channels, deliberately non-substitutable (this is *why* supply is strategic, not a menu):

### 4A. Gleam gates market WIDTH (the vouching — "who will deal with you")
- **The two-gate courting's first gate** (`two-gate-courting`, `shy-materials`): gleam decides *which material grades will state their terms this run*. Low shine → apprentice tier only (the market narrows, stalls go quiet). High shine → proud stock leans in from every shelf.
- **Filter-2-style lantern gating** carries to the taught-piece tier: which glad-loads (and thus which taught pieces) are lit and offerable is gated by present gleam (`two-filter-lantern-sizing`).
- **Grain-tagged**: *which kinds* of delight earned your gleam decide *which* materials answer (`grain-tagged-standing`) — a bridge-grained maker courts different stock than a song-grained one. Build identity, no class menu.
- **Gleam gates, never spends** (LOCKED, `Standing-never-spends`): the vouching is *"a key that turns, never a coin that leaves."* You do not *pay* shine for stock.

### 4B. Season/Paling gates what a place HAS (the weather — "what the country is offering")
- **Bright kept towns:** proud stock proud on the shelf (singing-silver, laughing-riverwood), ordinary thin dawns, small askings & kettle glad-loads (a kept town's table runs thin). The place to court the proud tiers.
- **Pale inhabited towns** (grey roofs, one ember, ringed gateposts): **richer work, richer glad-loads, the richest mornings on the Round** (dawn-ladder). The place to *answer* for a big load.
- **The left-behind / long grey roads:** last-red gleaning country + Evener twice-benched wagons (M4). The salvage-grade tier — *the left-behind, never the lived-in*.
- **Crowds (fairs, high days, red-thread-days):** the *only* place for venue-bound stock — festival-glass, bell-bronze, the Fair draft (P2), the fastest everything. *A crowd is an occasion, not a faucet* — the calendar is a resource.
- **The season curve itself:** the Green Going's shy market → the Deep Gold opening gleaning country and the first enormous glad-loads → the Red Walk's full-width abundance. Supply *rises with the seep* (`escalation-is-the-weather`), so pale country compounds *both* the pressure and the payoff in one number (`paling-arithmetic-compounding`, rings-in = load-out).
- **The Fair's position on the wheel** gates *when* the big draft is reachable (`a-week-ahead-of-autumn`); **crown-paths** trade income/supply for map-intel (thin dawns, no lanterns, free reads of adjacent sheds — `the-lightshed`); **still-bowls** clamp all supply shut until the equinox windfall (`the-stilled-summer`).

**The intersection is the strategy:** *shine* decides whether a place will deal with you; *route+season* decides what that place has. A high-shine maker in the Green Going still finds only apprentice stock (place is shy of stock); a low-shine maker at the Red-Walk Fair finds a full square but no vouching (crowd present, shine absent). You need *both* gates open at once — which is the mending question worn as a supply chain.

## 5. The anti-degenerate story (`napkin_flags` claimed: 0)

The executable claim: **acquisition keeps the deck on-curve (grows enough to stand the rising crown, not trivially fast); no single acquisition source dominates; no degenerate acquisition loop (no infinite card/handsel/material generation).**

### 5a. No single acquisition source dominates
Each source is on a **different clock and gate**, so no one strategy optimizes all supply — you must run several:

| Source | Resource | Clock | Primary gate | Cannot be spammed because… |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Glad-load (P1/M2/H3) | all three | the fulfil | answer an asking | bounded by askings available + your capacity to fulfil under the season clock |
| Fair draft (P2) | pieces + money | the wheel-intercept | route to the wagons | periodic; the Fair moves on; twice-size *also raises the ladder* |
| Town market (M1) | materials | the season/place | gleam vouch + performed term | width capped by shine; the term costs a play; freshness reverts |
| Gleaning (M4) | last-red / twice-benched | the route | thread the left-behind | never triggers on inhabited/answered nodes; highest route risk |
| Whittling (H1/H2) | handsels | the rest / the play | apprentice stock + tempo | produces *dull* money; only circulation brightens it |
| Waymeet (P3/M6/H6) | pieces / jars / money | the crossing | the give (leave-to-take) | net-neutral card count; supply is what others left |

Because the glad-load pays *all three at once* but is gated by the loop, and every *active* source pays *one* thing on its own gate, the model has **no keystone faucet** — pull only one and you starve the others. The route *is* the portfolio choice.

### 5b. On-curve clearable (deck grows enough, not too fast)
- **Growth tracks the curve by construction:** the glad-load scales off the spiral (rings-in = load-out), and the spiral *is* the difficulty. A reaching maker's supply climbs with the weather; a static engine gets kettle-loads all year and is *out-grown* on schedule (L1 §5, L2 §5b). Supply and difficulty ride the same number.
- **Napkin sketch (structural, exact coefficients → game-loop):** early pack ~6–8; per worked-morning cluster net acquisition ≈ (glad-loads answered × 1 taught piece) + occasional draft/teaching − retire/gift thinning ≈ a *modest* net climb; Fair intercepts add a bounded burst (draft *k* of *N*, once or twice per run); by the Red Walk the fired-core is deep enough to field a boss-tier working. The **retire/freshness/give** channels (§3) counter-pressure the climb, so the deck settles to a *tended* size, not a runaway pile — *"the wealthiest pack is the best-kept one, never the fullest."*
- **A fast draft cannot trivialize the run** because acquisition delivers *inert* pieces (§1A framing law): every drafted card still costs the room to wake (`play-is-the-only-gate`, `the-waking-mark`), and the room is soft-capped (`the-gathered-room`). You cannot draft your way past the requirement to *perform*.

### 5c. No degenerate loop (no infinite generation)
- **Infinite cards — closed.** Glad-load requires answering (finite needs, capacity-bounded). Fair is periodic + intercept-gated + raises the ladder as it feeds. Waymeet swap is **leave-one-take-one (net-zero count)**. Teaching/shadowing is a consumed map-event. Bench crafting consumes gated, reverting materials. No source *mints* net cards without a gate that rises with it.
- **Infinite handsels — closed.** Whittling makes *dull* coin worth nothing until circulated; `the-shavings-share` is **carved from the RETURN** (competes with the node's dawn — conservation-clean, no mint on top, L2 §5c); glad-price brightening is **bounded below its tempo/attention cost** (H4 napkin obligation); the Handsel Round is closed & mint-clean (idle-lapse → dawn income is the *only* sink). *"Hoarding literally funds other people's mornings."* No money printer.
- **Infinite materials — closed.** Courting is gleam-gated (vouch) **+** performed-term (a play, costs the current); freshness reverts untended stock (can't hoard a monoculture); apprentice-stuffs are zero-gate but wake thin & pale fastest (a doorway). Jars are **earned/gifted, never minted** — jar-arbitrage is gated behind actually answering needs; traded jars still cost bounded handsels.
- **Salvage/paling snowball — closed (the carried obligations).** Gleaning **never triggers on inhabited or answered nodes**; last-red is a catalyst **only on woken re-makings** (`soothe`, capped 1 node/1 knack/1 season); twice-benched keys **only to chosen last-lightings, never failure states**; and per the carried napkin flag, **peak-gleam sizing — not paling-farming — must carry the pressure curve when both anti-paling tools are maxed** (verify → game-loop). No plenty is ever sourced from misfortune (`Salvage guard`).
- **Mint-ban backstop:** every windfall returns *exactly* what was withheld (a stilling's release) or spilled (a glimmer) — no flow creates or destroys regard, so no acquisition loop can run positive indefinitely (`WORLD.md`:723).

**Carried to game-loop (numeric bounds, not structural holes):** (1) glad-price brightening < tempo cost; (2) deck net-growth coefficient vs. crown-tier curve; (3) Fair draft width *k*/*N* and intercept frequency; (4) peak-gleam sizing dominates paling-farm supply with both tools maxed. `napkin_flags` claimed: **0**.
