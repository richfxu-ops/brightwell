# Layer 2 — Resource Types · Proposal A · FLOWS-FIRST

**Proposer:** A-flows-first
**Logline:** *Five clocks, one regard that opens them, one gate that is the only door between them.* Model Roundelay's economy exactly as the L1 loop and the L4 canon already frame it — a **timing economy** in which nothing is a pile and everything is a flow with its own tempo. The five flows are the five scarcities canon already names; **gleam** is the cross-cutting regard that *opens* them without ever being spent; **the played journey-piece** is the world's single exchange gate, so no flow ever converts silently into another.

---

## 0. Framing — why flows-first is the canon-native model here

L4 states the thesis outright: *"the maker's economy is a timing economy — attention, coin, consent, crowd, and season, all of them flows, none of them hoardable, every one of them paintable at dawn."* The world was already designed as clocks, not stockpiles, under one law a child tests on a windowsill — **what stops moving, dims.** A flows-first resource model is therefore not an imposition on ROUNDELAY; it is a transcription of it.

So this proposal does **not** invent a resource lattice and then look for flavor. It takes canon's **five named scarcities** and treats each as a *flow with a source, a tempo, and a sink*, then shows the **regard (gleam)** that gates the flows without entering them, the **deck (journey-pieces)** that is the sole exchange gate between them, and the **need's fill** that is the sink pulling the whole engine. The five scarcities, verbatim from L4:

| Canon scarcity (L4) | The flow | Its clock |
|---|---|---|
| "A morning can only be stood in once." | **Attention** (the morning → the room → the returned table) | per-turn (one dawn) |
| "A bright coin must keep moving." | **Handsels** (the held / money) + bright-shavings | per-rest (idle-dim tempo) |
| "Proud stuff consents, and consent is gated by shine." | **Courted materials** (the loose) | per-season (freshness lapse) |
| "A crowd is an occasion, not a faucet." | **The room** (audience, the attention *pool*'s ceiling) | per-turn / per-venue |
| "A season only ripens once." | **The wander-year calendar** + the **Paling's spiral** | per-run (the escalation clock) |

Two things cut across all five: **gleam** (the regard that opens flows, spent by none of them) and **journey-pieces** (the deck — the only object through which any flow touches any other). Both are resources in their own right. That is the whole model, and it satisfies every gate below because the world already satisfied it.

## 1. The resource-type list

Each entry: **role · source · sink · transform · grammar-of-light channel · trace.** Flows are grouped by their clock. A resource's *state-forms* (e.g. attention's morning/room/returned-surplus) are listed inline because flows-first cares about the states a quantity passes through, not just its name.

### FLOW 1 — Attention (the per-turn engine current) · *"a morning can only be stood in once"*

The medium the whole economy is made of, and the L1 loop's per-turn engine resource. Modeled as **one current in three states**, never a pile:

- **the morning** (income state). **Source:** dawn issues a flat guaranteed base **+ a draw from the local table** (paler node → richer draw) **+ yesterday's node-local returned surplus.** The base alone always wakes the cheapest piece — no decision-free turn.
- **the room** (spendable state — the working *pool* this turn). Seeded by the morning-draw and by **the woken audience present** (see Flow 4); an unbroken work-chain **holds** the pool high, a stall lets it **cool.** Budget and momentum in one, pulling against each other every play. **Soft-capped** (`the-gathered-room`: "a listening span, loops not a number") so the personal room-floor plateaus.
- **the returned surplus** (banked state). By `the-returning-table`, an attended play's attention **splits with nothing lost**: the SET share works the piece; the RETURN share **seeps to *this* node's local table** and comes back in *this* node's next dawn. **Node-bound — no caravanning.**

**Sink / where it goes (never destroyed — `attention-conservation`):** SET into a piece (→ waking, Flow 7); RETURN to the local table (→ tomorrow's morning here); **OVERKILL past a card's ceiling → converts to Standing** at full rate (Flow 2), the only place attention leaves as anything but attention, and it does so **through a played piece** (the exchange gate). **Un-tradeable by canon** ("the current cannot be traded... no stall, no jar, no substitute").
**Grammar-of-light channel:** **the light itself** — attention is *"one thing — attention made visible,"* the medium under all three channels, not an object in any of them. It has no working-edge, no key-light, no paling-grey; it is what those three are read *in*.
**Trace:** `attention-engine`/`audience-of-things`; `dawn-income`/`the-unclaimed-morning`; `the-gathered-room`; `the-returning-table`/`attention-conservation`/`the-round-of-light`; GDD L1 §2, `the-worked-morning`.

### FLOW 2 — Standing (gleam) — the depletable fail-resource · *the regard that gates, spent by none*

The D-001 fail-resource, and the economy's cross-cutting **key.** Worn as warmth, never an HP bar.

- **Source (RISES):** **overkill** in performance at full rate (`full-cup-overflow`, "a full cup runs to the pourer" — the biggest swings, on stage); **glad palms** (doorstep askings, gifts, laughter — kettle-scale); **delayed tellings** (`delayed-tellings`/`the-walking-light`) — distant delight walks the road-graph one fireside a night, source-stamped, **crediting only on witnessed landing** (nothing off-screen).
- **Sink (FALLS — outcome-only, never spent):** the **spilling** — an **unmoved room** (a performance that crosses no waking-mark) or an **asking carried past its season.** The lost gleam re-colors the node as `lane-glimmers` worth *less* than it cost, claimable only by later visitors — **lawful compost, EV-negative to trigger on purpose.** **Zero → the Quiet Walk** (run-end, meta-seeding). **No ambient/within-run decay** (canon: *"a working maker's gleam moves only as work delights or disappoints"*).
- **What it GATES (soft — never a coin that leaves):** lantern **offers** (Filter 2); the shy-material **vouching** (Flow 6, *"a key that turns, never a coin that leaves"*); the glad-price read (Flow 4); and **build identity via grain** (`grain-tagged-standing`) — a *tagged* resource, so the kind of delight that earned it decides which askings size up and which materials answer.
- **Derived state — the fired reading (peak-Standing, grain-tagged):** the **high-water mark** of gleam, read by the lantern's Filter 1. **One-way — it never falls** (*"the compliment is permanent"*). Not a stock you can spend or lose; a *reading* the road keeps of the brightest you have been. It is the escalation ratchet's player-driven input.

**Grammar-of-light channel:** **people gleam** — a key-light on the **maker only.** Never on a place, never on an object.
**Never interacts with:** the Paling (Flow 10). Board-state can never touch the gleam meter.
**Trace:** `gleam-standing`/`makers-gleam`; `overkill-to-standing`/`gleam-runoff`; `grain-tagged-standing`; `spill-boons`/`lane-glimmers`; `delayed-tellings`/`the-walking-light`; `two-filter-lantern-sizing`; `quiet-walk-runend-meta`; GDD L1 §4–§5, `the-reach`.

### FLOW 3 — Handsels (the held — the money) · *"a bright coin must keep moving"*

Currency in **wakefulness denominations** (dull / warm / **singing**), not counts. A juggling, never a pile.

- **Source (faucet):** whittled from **apprentice-stuffs** blanks (Flow 6) + **bright-shavings** (Flow 5). *Anyone can make money; only circulation makes it worth anything.* Brightened by circulation — the `glad-price` trade, the `glad-load`, `the-waymeet-table`, hearth-side gifts.
- **Transform:** spent **into delight** → **change returns a shade brighter** (`the-glad-price`, a tempo-for-value read — gladness must be performed under attention). Held in **the open purse** (soft cap: loops, not a number).
- **Sink (the only one — and it *is* the world's income):** an **idle / boxed** handsel **dims** each rest (open-purse quality slows, never stops, the lapse) → its lapsed light **seeps to the gloaming-table → returns as somebody's dawn income** (Flow 1). *Hoarding literally funds other people's mornings.* **No fees. No debt. No wage.** (Commerce-vocabulary ban honored.)
**Grammar-of-light channel:** **held light** — a full-surface **breathing sheen** on a made thing that is *held, not woken* (the-two-golds: sheen, never the crisp fired gold edge). A "thing," but in the *held* register, not the *waking* one.
**Trace:** `handsel-currency-decay`/`handsels`; `the-open-purse`; `glad-price-read`/`the-glad-price`; `fired-vs-held`/`the-held-and-the-fired`; `two-golds-read`; `the-glad-load` (payout).

### FLOW 4 — The room (audience / crowd) · *"a crowd is an occasion, not a faucet"*

Modeled as a **flow that raises the ceiling of Flow 1**, distinct because its tempo is *occasion*, not *dawn*. Deep waking needs gathered watchers, and watchers gather **only** at fairs, hearths, fair-answers, red-thread-days — *the calendar is a resource.*

- **Source:** **woken audience-things you own** (a fired piece joins the room on later turns), townsfolk, venue, `festival-glass` rooms (passive attention). A woken bell rung before a crowd wakes other things.
- **Transform:** raises the **pool ceiling** of Flow 1 — a full room lifts how bright anything can wake; a thin room caps it. **Soft-capped** (each new audience-thing's contribution decays as the room grows — anti-snowball).
- **Sink / lapse:** transient crowd tokens (Fairwright venues) **expire when the fair moves on**; the room is an *occasion*, so it cannot be stockpiled between venues.
**Grammar-of-light channel:** **people gleam** for the living watchers (crowds shine, they do not wake) and **things wake** for the woken audience-objects (gold working-edge). The *pool* they produce is Flow 1's **light** (the medium). No object gets a person's key-light; no crowd gets an edge.
**Trace:** `attention-engine`/`audience-of-things`; `festival-glass` (venue material); `fair-hub-intercept`/`the-round-fair`; `fairwright-multiplier-spike`; GDD L1 §2 (`the-gathered-room`).

### FLOW 5 — Bright-shavings (the byproduct bridge)

The lawful bridge from the bench economy into the money and deck economies.
- **Source:** big **attended** plays yield shaving tokens (the bench transaction's third share, settling loose on what fell from the work).
- **Sink / transform:** converted at rests/fairs into **fresh handsels** (Flow 3) or **minor pieces** (Flow 7); **season-scale lapse** if left unturned (held light lapses everywhere).
**Grammar-of-light channel:** **held light** (breathing sheen, like banked embers — never the fired edge).
**Trace:** `bright-shavings-byproduct`/`bright-shavings`.

### FLOW 6 — Courted materials (the loose) · *"proud stuff consents, gated by shine"*

Acquisition as **two visible gates in strict order** — never a purchase. *Scarcity is manners, not tonnage.*

- **Source (the courting):** **Gate 1 — the vouching** (gleam's business, no coin: will the stuff state its terms to hands the world holds in mind?) → **Gate 2 — the terms** (the material's own, always performable, always legible: a song sung, a chain unbroken, a crowd present, a dusk kept). Also acquired pre-courted as **introduction-jars** (via `the-glad-load`, Mannerly lending — a courtship collapsed into a gift).
- **The grades (each a distinct upkeep clock):**
  - `the-apprentice-stuffs` — **floor, zero-gate**, courtable at every node; the raw stock of handsel-whittling; wakes thin, **pales fastest** (the felt upgrade path); the Quiet-Walk comeback material.
  - `singing-silver` — unbroken **play-chain** term; Mannerly-affine; reverts if not kept warm.
  - `laughing-riverwood` — **"the room is laughing"** mood token; use-by a season.
  - `festival-glass` — **venue-bound** (high-attention nodes only); grants passive attention (feeds Flow 4).
  - `the-twice-benched` — **retirement yield** (chosen last-lightings *only*, never failure): a **discounted waking threshold** (9 parts the attention fresh grain wants). Anti-salvage, mechanical.
  - `last-red` — **pale-route pigment** (Flow 10): gleaned from the left-behind, spent only as a **catalyst on woken re-makings** to slow a node's future paling.
- **Sink / lapse:** **freshness** replaces weight limits — untended stock **reverts to ordinary** ("the pack is a garden, not a hoard"); consumed into pieces at the bench.
**Grammar-of-light channel:** **the loose** — carries the wash of where it lay; **becomes fired** only when worked into a woken piece. **Materials never speak** (register): terms read like a cat's, through behavior.
**Trace:** `two-gate-courting`/`shy-materials`; `graded-material-market`; `apprentice-floor-materials`; `introduction-jars-freshness`; `the-twice-benched`; `last-red`.

### FLOW 7 — Journey-pieces (the set — the deck) · *the one exchange gate*

The deckbuilder's deck, and the **only object through which any flow touches any other.**
- **Source:** taught / traded / gifted / won — the `glad-load`'s taught card (arriving with that town's name), the `walking-ways` card-schools, `the-waymeet-table` swaps; crafted from materials (Flow 6).
- **Transform (the exchange gate):** a piece **played** draws attention as a **multiplier**; rested at/above its **waking-mark** it **wakes** — takes the thin gold edge, is **fired to the run-deck permanently** (`fired-vs-held`, the fired never unfires), **pays woken delight into the need's fill** (Flow 8), and **joins the room** (Flow 4). Below its mark it resolves once and stays **inert, paling-prone.**
- **Sink:** **exhaust** — pieces given away, performed to destruction, or **last-lit into local-table income / twice-benched grain** (Flow 6); inert pieces the Paling takes first.
**Grammar-of-light channel:** **things wake** — one thin gold **working-edge** on the piece, and nothing else (the-two-golds: line, versus held light's sheen — colorblind-safe by construction).
**Trace:** `journey-pieces-deck`; `waking-threshold-play`/`the-waking-mark`; `fired-vs-held`; `two-golds-read`; `walking-ways-cardpools`; `deck-autobiography`; GDD L1 §2.

### FLOW 8 — The need's fill (contract demand — the sink that pulls the engine) · *"a season only ripens once"*

Not a currency but a **demand-quantity** — the sink every other flow is aimed at, and the D-001 escalating contract's mechanical body.
- **Source:** the **asking**, sized by the **two-filter lantern** — **Filter 1 (TIER)** fired to peak-Standing (Flow 2's fired reading) over the **rising seasonal-seep floor**; **Filter 2 (OFFERS)** gated by *current* gleam. Its demand is always something the engine already produces (wake this grain / raise the fill past this line / hold the room to this peak) — never a separate minigame.
- **Sink:** consumes **woken delight** from plays (Flow 7). On completion the node is **re-made** and the **`glad-load`** pays across **every other flow at once** (bright handsels → Flow 3, one taught piece → Flow 7, one introduction-jar → Flow 6, map-intel chalked out).
- **Clock:** an accepted asking **warms as its season shortens**; carried past its season it goes **stale** → **spills** Standing (Flow 2). Unanswered askings in a region **age into the spiral together** (I-022: count/pace = tier pressure).
**Grammar-of-light channel:** **places pale** — the demand is a board reading; the **node re-colors** (watercolor strokes) **only on fulfillment.**
**Trace:** `escalating-contracts`/`the-askings`; `two-filter-lantern-sizing`/`need-takes-aim`; `contract-payoff-bundle`/`the-glad-load`; `contract-preview-ui`; GDD L1 §3.

### FLOW 9 — The wander-year calendar (the master flow — the escalation clock) · *"a season only ripens once"*

The run's **un-buyable master resource**, and — the load-bearing fact — **the same clock as the escalation curve.**
- **Source:** fixed at set-out (spring even-morrow → first still dawn).
- **Sink:** **advanced by worked mornings (dawns) only** — a stationary camp-in-place maker still spends dawns, so still walks into the rising out-breath; **no forcing function is travel-keyed.** Un-hoardable, un-substitutable.
- **Transform:** the **seasonal seep-rate curve** (near-still winter → strongest late-summer/autumn) sets the **minimum need a lantern can hang** (Flow 8's floor) and the Paling's tempo (Flow 10) — *escalation is the weather.*
**Grammar-of-light channel:** **places pale** / the world's weather — it moves board-state and never the gleam meter.
**Trace:** `wander-year-run-frame`/`the-wander-year`; `escalation-is-the-weather`; GDD L1 §1, §5.

### FLOW 10 — Paling pressure (the spiral rings — board-decay as economy) · *the weather with an arithmetic*

The D-001 escalation made a **legible board resource.**
- **Source:** unanswered towns pale — **one spiral ring per season, deterministic** (`the-spiral-clock`: 3 = plea, 5 = poem, 7 = a great asking born).
- **Transform:** more rings → **bigger askings + richer dawns** (fuller local tables → Flow 1) **+ last-red abundance** in the left-behind (Flow 6); **7 rings → the combing → a great asking** (the boss tier), telegraphed seasons ahead.
- **Sink:** **answered** → node re-colors + `glad-load`; **last-red** catalyzes woken re-makings that hold their wash longer (slows local seep); **red thread** bookmarks one knack for the return (one node, one knack, one season — capped).
**Grammar-of-light channel:** **places pale** — the Paling attacks **board-state, resources, routes ONLY, never the Standing meter** (so a player always knows which losses are the weather).
**Trace:** `paling-clock`/`the-paling`; `paling-arithmetic-compounding`; `combing-boss-tier`; `pale-route-harvest`/`last-red`; `red-thread-marker`; GDD L1 §3, `the-spiral-clock`, `escalation-is-the-weather`.

> **Meta note (out of L2 scope, flagged for continuity):** persistent gifts — the walking bead, wintering-fire seed, and the Quiet Walk's one woken work — are **gifts-only, sized to the tier the run engaged** (`winter-telling-victory`, `porch-meta-home`, I-013 the fresh verse). No between-run currency; nothing of a maker is kept, only what they gave. Not part of the run's resource web below.

## 3. Grammar-of-light channels + play-is-the-only-gate

Three channels that never cross: **places pale · people gleam · things wake.** Every resource is assigned exactly one channel (or is declared the *medium*), and no arrow above performs a **silent cross-channel conversion** — every cross-channel transfer passes through **a played journey-piece** (`play-as-exchange-gate`).

| Resource / flow | Channel |
|---|---|
| Attention (F1), the room's pool (F4) | **the medium** — the light itself (attention made visible); read *in*, never *as* a channel object |
| Standing / gleam / fired reading (F2), living watchers (F4) | **people gleam** — key-light on maker & crowd |
| Journey-pieces woken (F7), woken audience-things (F4) | **things wake** — thin gold working-edge |
| Handsels (F3), bright-shavings (F5), glad-load glow | **held light** — full-surface breathing sheen (a thing, *held* not woken) |
| Courted materials, loose (F6) | **the loose** — carries wash; *becomes* fired only when worked (channel-crossing happens *through the bench play*) |
| The need's fill (F8), the wander-year (F9), the Paling spiral (F10) | **places pale** — board-state / weather |

**The three cross-channel transfers, and how each is play-gated (never silent):**

1. **light → people (attention → Standing).** Happens **only** as **overkill through a played piece on stage** (`full-cup-overflow`). There is no menu button that turns attention into gleam; you must *perform* past a ceiling. Play is the gate.
2. **people → the loose (gleam → materials).** Gleam **opens the vouching** — it **gates, it does not convert.** *"A key that turns, never a coin that leaves."* Gleam is not spent, and no material is *bought with* gleam; the material still requires a **performed term** (a play) to enter the pack. Play is the gate; gleam only unlocks the door.
3. **the loose → things (material → woken work).** A material becomes fired **only when worked into a piece that wakes** — i.e. through a play at/above the waking-mark. Raw stock never wakes on the shelf.

And the two *within-medium* returns are conservation, not conversion: attention's RETURN share (F1) stays attention (seeps to the local table); the Paling's seep (F10) and idle-handsel lapse (F3) return as **next-morning attention** — the mint ban holds (*"the table mints nothing and loses nothing"*). **No flow creates or destroys regard.**

## 4. Anti-degenerate story (the napkin obligations, pre-answered)

Flows-first makes non-degeneracy structural: **you cannot hoard a flow, and you cannot convert one flow into another without paying the play/tempo toll.** Concretely:

- **No dominant resource.** The five flows are **non-substitutable** — play-is-the-only-gate blocks silently cashing one channel for another, so no single resource can be over-farmed and spent everywhere. Each has a *different clock* (dawn / rest / season / occasion / run), so no one accumulation strategy optimizes all five.
