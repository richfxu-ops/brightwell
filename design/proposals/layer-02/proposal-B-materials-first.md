# Layer 2 — Resource Types · Proposal B · MATERIALS-FIRST

**Proposer:** B-materials-first
**Framing:** model the economy around the world's **courted-materials web** as the deck-fuel economy — what you gather, court, transform, and spend to make woken cards — layered over attention (the per-turn engine) and gleam/Standing (the gate). Emphasize the transformation chain **harvest → court → wake → weather → seep** as the interacting-systems spine.

**Logline:** *The economy is a tended garden of courted stuffs. You harvest and court raw materials, wake them into journey-pieces under attention, and let what you cannot keep warm seep back into the world's dawn. The wealthiest pack on the Rounds is the best-kept, never the fullest — so every resource is a flow, not a pile, and the deck is the crop.*

**Binding read before writing:** DECISIONS D-001/D-002/D-003/D-005; WORLD.md Layer 4 (economy & materials) + the grammar of light + the three settlings/held-fired canon; ledger.json; GDD Layer 1 (LOCKED). This proposal invents no resource the canon did not already imply, and contradicts no L1 mechanic.

---

## 0. The materials-first thesis, in one paragraph

Roundelay's L4 already states that **"the rarity of a material is exactly the difficulty of its asking"** and that **"scarcity is manners, not tonnage."** That makes materials the ideal *primary* economy for a deckbuilder: your deck is not bought with coin — it is **grown** from raw stuff you court, wake, and tend. So this model puts the **courting web at the center** and reads the whole economy as one crop-cycle: you **harvest** raw material (the loose wash of a place), **court** the proud grades (a gleam gate + a performable term), **wake** the material into a fired journey-piece by playing it at or above its waking-mark under attention, **weather** it (fired holds for the run, unwoken pales fastest), and let the leftovers **seep** — offcuts to bright-shavings, exhausts to twice-benched, spills to lane-glimmers, idle coin to the gloaming-table and back as tomorrow's dawn. Attention is the daily water; gleam is the sun that decides which stuff will answer you at all. Nothing here is a pile; everything is a garden being tended or going ordinary.

## 1. The full resource-type list

Every resource states **role · source · sink · trace · grammar-of-light channel**. Channels honor the world law (`WORLD.md#grammar-of-light`, binding): *places pale, people gleam, things wake* — three phases of one light, and the phase belongs to the holder. Raw materials are **the loose** (regard lying loose on the unliving — the places phase); a material becomes a **thing** (the waking phase) *only by being played*, which is the one lawful cross-phase seam (`play-as-exchange-gate`).

### Tier 0 — The engine resource (per-turn water)

**R0 · attention — "the room."**
- **Role:** the per-turn engine resource you spend to work; the multiplier on every play and the gate on every waking. LOCKED by L1 §2 as the room.
- **Source:** the dawn reissue (flat base + local-table draw, richest over pale country) + the gathered woken audience + unbroken work-chain. `the-worked-morning`, `the-gathered-room`, `dawn-income`.
- **Sink:** *set* into a card (works it), *overflows* to gleam (overkill), or *returns* loose to this node's local table for tomorrow's dawn — **none lost** (`the-returning-table`, conservation).
- **Trace:** `attention-engine` (`audience-of-things`); `dawn-income` (`the-unclaimed-morning`); `attention-conservation` (`the-round-of-light`).
- **Channel:** **the current** — living light, *never a pool, never traded* ("nobody has ever sold a morning's regard"). It is the quiet floor everything else is made of. Being non-hoardable by nature is why it can be the engine without becoming the dominant *bankable* resource (§4).

### Tier 1 — The materials web (the deck-fuel — this proposal's spine)

**R1 · apprentice-stuffs** — the floor material.
- **Role:** zero-gate raw stock for starter and comeback cards; the money faucet's feedstock; the Quiet-Walk re-entry loop's fuel.
- **Source:** courtable at **every** node by any hands, dim or bright — straw, dough, river clay, withy, tallow, chalk, coarse wool. No vouching gate.
- **Sink:** worked into starter/comeback journey-pieces (which **pale fastest** — the upgrade path made felt); **whittled into handsels** (the money supply's origin — feeds R8).
- **Trace:** `apprentice-floor-materials` (`the-apprentice-stuffs`).
- **Channel:** **the loose** (raw), crossing to **things** only when played and woken.

**R2 · graded shy-materials** — the courted engine tier (`singing-silver` · `laughing-riverwood` · `festival-glass`, plus local characters bell-bronze / hearth-slate).
- **Role:** the high-power deck-fuel; each grade carries a distinct **upkeep term** that gives builds identity, and reverts to ordinary if not kept warm.
- **Source:** the **two-gate courting** (`two-gate-courting`): first the *vouching* (a gleam gate — will the stuff state its terms to these hands at all), then a *performable term* — singing-silver: an unbroken play-chain, no stall; laughing-riverwood: a "the room is laughing" mood token, use-by season; festival-glass: venue-bound, acquirable only at high-attention nodes, and its works grant **passive attention** (a small tap back into R0).
- **Sink:** worked into high-tier woken cards; **reverts to ordinary stock** if its term goes un-kept for a season (freshness, not a fee — `introduction-jars-freshness`); a courting-term accepted is itself an asking that can go stale → disappointment → the spilling (feeds R10 loss).
- **Trace:** `graded-material-market`; `two-gate-courting`; `mannerly-condition-puzzle`.
- **Channel:** **the loose** (courted), crossing to **things** only by play. Gleam **gates** the vouching but is **never spent** to court it — "a key that turns, never a coin that leaves" (L1 §5, binding).

**R3 · introduction-jars** — banked courtship (the acquisition-skip).
- **Role:** collapses weeks of courting into a gift — a proud material arriving already vouched-and-termed; among the most valuable things a pack can hold.
- **Source:** the glad-load (`contract-payoff-bundle`), Mannerly lending, and trade at fairs/the waymeet-table.
- **Sink:** consumed to **skip the vouching+term** and put a courted material straight into the pack (→ feeds R2's working chain); tradeable, so it also flows into R8's exchange.
- **Trace:** `introduction-jars-freshness`; `the market's grammar (shy-materials)`.
- **Channel:** **the loose** (a courted material, banked). Tradeable because it is set-stuff-in-waiting, not a current and not a person.

**R4 · last-red** — the pale-route harvest pigment.
- **Role:** the stubbornest pigment in the world; the reward for routing the grey; a **catalyst** that makes a re-making hold its color longer (slows a node's future paling); the dye that becomes red thread.
- **Source:** **the gleaning** (`pale-route-harvest`) — a scout-plus-gather action on **left-behind** pale nodes whose yield scales with paleness (richest where askings compounded). **Binding:** never triggers on inhabited or answered nodes ("glean the left-behind, never the lived-in").
- **Sink:** spent **only as a catalyst on a woken re-making** (never bare scenery) to slow that node's seep; or dyed at a fireside into **red thread** (R5).
- **Trace:** `pale-route-harvest` (`last-red`).
- **Channel:** **the loose / places** — it is the *last wash still bound* on paled scenery; harvested as loose stuff, it re-enters the **places** channel when laid into a woken re-making. The color takes root only in the *woken* work, so even this cross is a play-gated seam.

**R5 · red thread** — place-marking consumable + contract carrier.
- **Role:** a bookmark of regard — delay one **named knack's** forgetting at one node until your return; and the literal carrier of contract tokens riding the pack UI.
- **Source:** spun dooryard flax dyed in last-red (R4) at firesides.
- **Sink:** tie one thread at one node to mind one knack for one season (consumed on placement; a lane holds one thread, a thread holds one season); also spent as the physical mount for accepted askings.
- **Trace:** `red-thread-marker` (`red thread / last-red / red-thread-days`).
- **Channel:** **the loose (a minding)** — thread minds what it *touches*, never what must be done. Binds no one; a thread untied is a thought moved on. (Register-safe: not an obligation token.)

**R6 · twice-benched stock** — retirement-yield material with a discounted waking-mark.
- **Role:** the sacrifice-economy's fuel: grain that comes to a new bench with part of its **set already fired in**, so it wakes at ~9/10 the attention fresh grain wants — the easiest first-waking in the game.
- **Source:** **chosen last-lightings** and card-exhausts only. **Binding (anti-salvage):** keys to lovingly-retired works, **never to failure states** — "retirement is chosen, tended, and festival-lit."
- **Sink:** worked into eased-threshold journey-pieces at the bench (feeds R11 the deck at a discount); the Evener's self-thinning conversion loop.
- **Trace:** `the-twice-benched`; `evener-sacrifice-velocity`.
- **Channel:** **things** (part-fired), but still needs a **play** to complete the waking — the discount is on the mark, not on the gate.

**R7 · bright-shavings** — the crafting byproduct (bench → money/deck bridge).
- **Role:** the offcuts of great attended work; a "handsel already begun"; the premium mint beside the apprentice-stuff blanks.
- **Source:** big **attended plays** yield shaving tokens (the bench transaction's own loose third-share settling on the leavings).
- **Sink:** converted at rests and fairs into fresh **handsels** (R8) or minor pieces (R11); **lapses in a season if unturned** (told-of keeps it bright; untold lets it seep).
- **Trace:** `bright-shavings-byproduct` (`bright-shavings`).
- **Channel:** **the held** — loose light bound by the telling that named where it fell; a full-surface breathing sheen, never a fired edge.

### Tier 2 — The money (the held light)

**R8 · handsels** — the currency, in wakefulness denominations (dull / warm / singing).
- **Role:** the medium of exchange; worth = how **awake** it is, not a metal count.
- **Source:** whittled from apprentice-stuffs (R1) + bright-shavings (R7); **brightened by circulation** — the glad-price read, the glad-load, hearth gifts, the waymeet-table.
- **Sink:** **spent into delight** (returns change a shade brighter); or, if boxed/idle, its binding lapses → **ordinary seep → gloaming-table → returns as tomorrow's dawn income** (R0). The money's only sink is the world's only income: hoarding literally funds other people's mornings.
- **Container:** `the-open-purse` — a soft cap (loops, not a number); idle dimming per rest, slowed (never stopped) by purse quality; purse upgrades are fired artifacts.
- **Trace:** `handsel-currency-decay`; `glad-price-read`; `the-open-purse`.
- **Channel:** **the held** — loose light bound by circulating delight. **Not places, not people, not fired.** *No debt/fees currency* (D-003, binding): a handsel is never owed, only awake or dim.

### Tier 3 — The gate resource (the sun)

**R9 · gleam (Standing) + grain** — the depletable fail-resource **and** the vouching key.
- **Role:** the world's trust worn as a key-light on the maker; **gates** (never spends): which askings the lantern shows, which shy materials will state terms, prices, and — via **grain** (tagged Standing) — *which* materials and *which* askings answer your particular build. The fail-resource: zero → the Quiet Walk.
- **Source:** **overflow** (`full-cup-overflow`, full rate — the big on-stage swings); glad palms (kettle-scale); **delayed tellings** walking the road-graph at hearth-pace, landing only when seen (`delayed-tellings`).
- **Sink:** **NONE by spending** — gleam is never a coin that leaves. It **falls only via the spilling** (`spill-boons`): an unmoved room or an asking carried past its season withdraws regard, which slides off as lane-glimmers (R10). Outcome-only; no ambient decay within a verse (canon: "a working maker's gleam moves only as work delights or disappoints").
- **Trace:** `gleam-standing`; `overkill-to-standing`; `grain-tagged-standing`; `delayed-tellings`; `diegetic-standing-readout`.
- **Channel:** **people gleam** — key-light on the maker only. **Never touched by the Paling** (the grammar's hard wall). Its readout is the shoulder-candle, not a bar.

### Tier 4 — The board (the crop's ground) & the outputs

**R10 · node color / need's fill** — board-state; the output the whole economy exists to produce.
- **Role:** a node's knack-and-color (the loose wash of scenery) and the accepted asking's demand (`the need's fill`). Producing woken delight into a need re-makes the node.
- **Source:** **woken delight paid by a played card** into the need's fill (§2). Re-color happens **only on fulfillment**, never per-play.
- **Sink/attack:** the **Paling** compounds it — `the-spiral-clock` chalks one ring per skipped season (3=plea, 5=poem, 7=great-asking-born), attacking board-state and route **only**. Loss via the spilling seeds **lane-glimmers**.
- **Trace:** `paling-clock`; `paling-arithmetic-compounding`; `spill-boons` (`lane-glimmers`).
- **Channel:** **places pale.** The Paling lives entirely here and never crosses to R9.

**R11 · journey-pieces — the deck.**
- **Role:** the deckbuilding substrate; a run's engine is literally a workshop of things you've woken.
- **Source:** playing a material-card (R1/R2/R6/R7) at or above its **waking-mark** under attention → **fired to the run-deck permanently** (`fired-vs-held`, the fired never unfires) and joining the room as a new audience-thing.
- **Sink:** **exhaust** — given away, performed to destruction, or **last-lit into twice-benched** (R6); unwoken pieces stay inert and paling-prone; deck-thinning as gifting at the waymeet-table.
- **Trace:** `journey-pieces-deck`; `waking-threshold-play`; `fired-vs-held`; `deck-autobiography` (each card quotes its giver).
- **Channel:** **things wake** — one thin gold working-edge, and nothing else.

### Tier 5 — The master clock

**R12 · the wander-year calendar** — the run's master resource.
- **Role:** un-buyable run length **and** the escalation clock (the seasonal seep-rate curve **is** the escalation curve). LOCKED by L1 §1.
- **Source:** fixed at setting-out (spring even-morrow).
- **Sink:** **one dawn per worked morning** (season-time, never travel legs); ends at the first still dawn.
- **Trace:** `wander-year-run-frame`; `escalation-is-the-weather`.
- **Channel:** **none — it is time/weather.** Advances board-side pressure (Paling floor) and never touches gleam.

**The five systems, stated as transformations (each traces to an L4 system):**

- **M2 · The Making Chain** *(L4 S2, the bench chain).* materials (from M1) + attention (from M0/R0) → played at the **waking-mark** → **fired card** (R11) which **pays woken delight → M5 (R10)**, **overflows → M4 (R9 gleam)**, sheds **offcuts → M3 (R7 shavings)**, and on exhaust/retirement yields **twice-benched (R6) back into itself.** The literal **harvest→court→wake→weather→seep** cycle. Feeds M3, M4, M5; fed by M1, M0, M5 (taught cards, last-red).
- **M3 · The Handsel Round** *(L4 S3, closed money).* apprentice-stuffs (R1) + bright-shavings (R7) → whittled handsels (R8) → circulate → brighten → **spent to buy courted materials & introduction-jars → feeds M1**; idle handsels **seep → M0 dawn.** Fed by M2 (shavings) and M5 (glad-load handsels); feeds M1 (buys fuel) and M0 (idle seep is dawn income).
- **M4 · The Gleam Cycle** *(L4 S2 overflow branch + the gleam cycle).* overflow (from M2) + tellings → gleam (R9) → **gates the vouching (opens M1)** + **gates the lantern showings (M5)** + grain tags identity; falls via the spilling → **lane-glimmers on the board (M5).** Fed by M2; gates M1 and M5.
- **M5 · The Paling Market** *(L4 S6, pressure as economy).* unanswered nodes pale → **spiral rings compound** → bigger askings + richer dawns (→ M0) + **last-red abundance → the gleaning → R4 feeds M2**; answered asking → **the glad-load pays across ALL systems** (handsels→M3, taught card→M2, introduction-jar→M1, map-intel); seven rings → the combing → great asking → the **largest overflow event → M2/M4.** Fed by M0 (seep) and M4 (gleam gates which lanterns show); feeds M1, M2, M3, M0.

**Interacting-systems count claimed: 5** (M1–M5), plus the canon master loop M0. Every one transforms or sinks into ≥2 others; none is an isolated resource. Gate `system_count ≥ 4` — **met.**

## 3. Grammar of light — channel per resource, and the one exchange gate

The world law is **three channels that never cross** (`WORLD.md#grammar-of-light`): *places pale (board/scenery), people gleam (the maker only), things wake (objects only).* Under the three-settlings floor these are **three phases of one light** — loose (places), current (the living), set (things) — plus one lawful **binding** (loose light held by ongoing regard: gleam on a maker, held light on a coin).

| Resource | Phase / channel | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| R0 attention (the room) | **the current** (living light) | never traded, never pooled; the floor everything is made of |
| R1 apprentice-stuffs | **the loose** (places) | crosses to *things* only by play |
| R2 shy-materials | **the loose** (courted) | gleam gates but never spends; crosses to *things* only by play |
| R3 introduction-jars | **the loose** (banked) | set-stuff-in-waiting; tradeable |
| R4 last-red | **the loose / places** | last wash of paled scenery; re-enters *places* only in a woken re-making |
| R5 red thread | **the loose (a minding)** | binds no one; register-safe marker |
| R6 twice-benched | **things** (part-fired) | still needs a play to finish the waking |
| R7 bright-shavings | **the held** (bound by telling) | breathing sheen, never a fired edge |
| R8 handsels | **the held** (bound by circulation) | not places, not people, not fired; no debt/fee |
| R9 gleam + grain | **people gleam** | maker only; Paling can never touch it |
| R10 node color / need's fill | **places pale** | the Paling's sole home |
| R11 journey-pieces (deck) | **things wake** | one gold working-edge |
| R12 calendar | **none (time/weather)** | advances board pressure only |

**Play-is-the-only-gate (the one cross-channel seam, `play-as-exchange-gate`):** the three *readings* — node color (places), Standing (people), card wakefulness (things) — **never convert directly.** Every cross-phase transfer passes through **a played journey-piece**, and there is no bypass:

- **loose → thing** (material wakes into a fired card): only by playing it at/above its waking-mark under attention (M2). A material never wakes on the shelf.
- **thing/current → people** (overflow → gleam): only via `full-cup-overflow` on a played card (M2→M4). No silent material-to-Standing conversion.
- **thing → places** (woken delight → node re-color): only on **fulfillment** of a need worked by play (M2→M5), never per-play.
- **held ↔ loose** (handsels buy materials): a *trade* is not a channel-cross — both are loose-phase (held light and courted loose stuff), and the exchange still happens by performing gladness under attention (the glad-price read), which spends the scarce per-turn current. **No material is ever converted straight into Standing, and no Standing is ever spent as coin.** Gleam gates the courting; it is "a key that turns, never a coin that leaves."

This is why the game is structurally a **deckbuilder**: play is the world's only door between the phases, so building and playing the pack is the only way the economy moves at all.

## 4. Anti-degenerate story (napkin_flags ≤ 0)

**No dominant resource — every resource is a flow, not a pile.** This is the materials-first model's structural virtue: the *primary* economy (materials) is a **garden that reverts if untended** (`introduction-jars-freshness`: shy stock goes ordinary in a season), so a stockpile decays into floor-stuff. Cross-checking each candidate for dominance:
- **attention (R0)** cannot be banked — it is a current, the return share is **node-locked** (`the-returning-table`, no caravanning), and the room is **soft-capped** (`the-gathered-room` plateaus). No attention pile.
- **shy-materials (R2)** revert to ordinary if the term lapses → no hoard.
- **handsels (R8)** dim when idle and seep to the gloaming-table → no coin pile; the only sink is other people's dawns.
- **gleam (R9)** is **never a bankable currency** — it gates and is lost only on outcomes; you cannot "spend it all" or hoard-then-dump it as money.
- **twice-benched (R6)** keys only to chosen last-lightings, so its supply is throttled by your own retirement tempo.
- **last-red (R4) / red thread (R5)** are hard-capped by fiction (catalyst on woken re-makings only; one node / one knack / one season) so **peak-gleam sizing, not paling-farming, carries the pressure curve** (L4 handoff #3).

**On-curve clearable.** The **seasonal seep curve is the escalation clock** (L1, LOCKED): the minimum need rises with the year regardless of play. The materials market **widens as gleam rises**, so fuel-supply tracks the rising need on the same curve; the **glad-load scales with the spiral** (rings-in = load-out), so reward tracks difficulty in one number. A maker who reaches grows on-curve (peak rises → bigger fuel → bigger fills); a maker who turtles is out-grown by the weather and cannot stand the calendar-floored crown (L1 §5–§6). The curve is clearable precisely by engaging the making chain, and un-clearable by opting out — which is the intended tension, not a degenerate wall.

**No degenerate loop — the four L4 napkin obligations, discharged in-model:**
1. **Friendly-trade money printer (glad-price bound, handoff #2):** brightening-per-trade is bounded **below its tempo/attention cost** — gladness must be *performed under attention* (spends R0, the scarcest resource), so two makers cannot loop trades to print awake handsels. Circulation makes money *worth* something; it does not *create* regard (mint ban, L4).
2. **Deliberate-failure farming (lane-glimmers, handoff #1):** spill-boons total **well under one gleam point's marginal value**, are **claimable only by later visitors (never the spiller)**, and "you would be spending brightness to gather dimness." Deliberate flop is always EV-negative.
3. **Salvage-from-ruin (handoff #4):** **gleaning never triggers on inhabited or answered nodes**; **twice-benched keys only to chosen last-lightings, never failure states.** No plenty is sourced from misfortune — the salvage guard is mechanical, not just prose. (Also answers D-003's banned "salvage-from-collapse.")
4. **Anti-paling-tool ceiling (handoff #3):** red thread + last-red are capped so the run's pressure is carried by peak-gleam lantern-sizing, not by paling-compounding exploitation.

**Materials-first-specific degenerates, pre-closed:**
- **Single-material spam / one-crop monoculture:** freshness reverts untended stock, and a card wakes **only at its waking-mark** (needs an attention budget you cannot infinitely raise — soft-capped room), so you cannot flood the board with one cheap material. **Grain** rewards focus but multi-way decks are legal, so no single material *dominates* — focus is a build identity, not a solved loop.
- **Hoard-then-dump the deck:** overflow has **diminishing returns past a band** (`full-cup-overflow`), the return-table is node-bound, and the capstone multiplier is bounded by the room's current pool. The biggest swing stays biggest as flavor, but cannot trivialize the meter.
